# Alternative look for Triss

[![Triss in alternative outfit](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f4/Tw3_Alternate_look_for_Triss.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20160503184351)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f4/Tw3_Alternate_look_for_Triss.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160503184351) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_Alternate_look_for_Triss.jpg)

Triss in alternative outfit

**Alternative look for Triss** adds a new (and more revealing) outfit for [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss "Triss"). It is one of the 16 [free DLCs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_DLC "Category:The Witcher 3 DLC") for _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , released on June 16, 2015. The outfit can be disabled in the Downloadable Content tab of the main menu. 

## Official Statement

    _Love to play dress up? Now you can with this exclusive new look for Triss Merigold._

## Trivia

  * Some fans speculate that the outfit was in fact the clothing of the [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") [sorceress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorceress "Sorceress") [Francesca Findabair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Francesca_Findabair "Francesca Findabair"), who was supposed to appear in the game together with other members of [Lodge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses") according to early interviews.

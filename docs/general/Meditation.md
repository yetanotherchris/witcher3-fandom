# Meditation

In the [novels](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_saga "Witcher saga"), while it is mentioned that [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") meditates, this is not a huge part of the narrative. 

## _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_

[![Meditation screen](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/61/Meditate.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20081115171807)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/61/Meditate.png/revision/latest?cb=20081115171807 "Meditation screen")Meditation screen

In _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ , Geralt does not need to sleep when tired, but he _must meditate_ in order to allocate accrued [talents](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Character_development "Character development") as well as for any creation of [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bombs "The Witcher bombs"), [oils](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_oils "The Witcher oils") or [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_potions "The Witcher potions"). In order to meditate, he must either light a [campfire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Campfire "Campfire"), a fireplace, or choose to [meditate via conversation](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_character_interaction "The Witcher character interaction") (typically with an [innkeeper](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Innkeeper "Innkeeper"), but some other [NPCs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Characters_in_the_games "Category:Characters in the games") also offer this convenience). 

The slider to the right of the hourglass is used to select how long (in number of hours) Geralt should meditate. Alternatively, one of the four "time of day" icons can be selected to meditate "until dawn", "until midday", "until dusk" or "until midnight". 

[![slider](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/04/Slider.png/revision/latest?cb=20081115172020)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/04/Slider.png/revision/latest?cb=20081115172020 "slider")slider

Clicking on the hourglass in the center initiates the period of meditation. 

Meditation will typically restore your health and endurance, as well as cleanse you of toxicity and intoxication. If you meditate outdoors, one hour is enough to restore you to full vigor; meditating indoors will heal you much more slowly. 

**Note** : If Geralt is brewing potions (or making other alchemical items), or allocating talents, the hour necessary for brewing potions or allocating the talents is automatically added to the number of hours remaining until the desired time of day. So be careful when trying to make a specific appointment. 

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

To enter meditation mode, use [![the Ctrl key](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/4b/Tw2_control_ctrl.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232544)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/4b/Tw2_control_ctrl.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232544 "the Ctrl key") to open the Quick Menu, then press [![the Left mouse button](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Tw2_control_leftmouse.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232611)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Tw2_control_leftmouse.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232611 "the Left mouse button") on the center symbol. 

Unlike the original game, Geralt can meditate anywhere as long as there are no enemies about. However, he must enter meditation mode to drink [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_potions "The Witcher 2 potions"). 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

In the third game, Geralt can meditate anywhere to regain vitality, as long as he is outside of combat. To meditate, hold [![the Tab key](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/9e/Tw2_controls_tab.png/revision/latest?cb=20160514105705)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/9e/Tw2_controls_tab.png/revision/latest?cb=20160514105705 "the Tab key") and then press the [![the Space key](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/1a/Tw2_control_space.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605233257)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/1a/Tw2_control_space.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605233257 "the Space key"). You could also just press the [![N key](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a4/Tw2_control_n.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/24?cb=20160514105822)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a4/Tw2_control_n.png/revision/latest?cb=20160514105822 "N key") key without going into the quick menu. He does not have to meditate to level up or create potions. 

Geralt's [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions") and [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bombs "The Witcher 3 bombs") will also be restocked after meditating for at least one hour, and if he has alcohol in his inventory. [White Gull](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Gull "White Gull") will be consumed first, then [alcohest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alcohest "Alcohest"), then [dwarven spirit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarven_spirit "Dwarven spirit"). _Note:_ if playing on [Blood and Broken Bones!](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Difficulty_level#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "Difficulty level") or [Death March difficulty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Difficulty_level#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "Difficulty level"), vitality will not be restored upon meditation.

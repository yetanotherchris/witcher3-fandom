In _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ , the player character, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), advances or develops as accumulated [experience points](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP "XP") earn him [talents](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Talents "Talents") which can be allocated to various attributes. There are four main attributes: Strength, Dexterity, Stamina and Intelligence. These four in turn allow advancement of skills like signs and the mastery of steel and silver swords. The Hero panel displays each attribute and ability in 'tree' format. 

Progress through the game (as represented by experience points/levels and the consumption of [unique potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_potions "The Witcher potions")) earns talent points of the Bronze (attribute levels I-II), Silver (levels III-IV) and/or Gold (level V) varieties. ### Mechanics

[![Character development panel](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/ec/Tutorial_character_development.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20170504220858)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/ec/Tutorial_character_development.png/revision/latest?cb=20170504220858 "Character development panel")Character development panel

  * Select the category you would like to enhance from the list on the left side of the **Hero** screen, then choose from among the highlighted enhancements in the tree on the right side of the panel.
  * To commit your choices, left-click on the hourglass. As long as you do not meditate, you can still change your mind about the allocation of talents. To cancel and start over, left-click on the **Erase' icon, or right-click on an allocated (but uncommitted) talent to free it up again.**

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

[![The Witcher 2 skill tree](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b1/TW2_skill_tree.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20110628202229)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b1/TW2_skill_tree.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110628202229) [![The Witcher 2 skill tree](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b1/TW2_skill_tree.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20110628202229)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b1/TW2_skill_tree.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20110628202229) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:TW2_skill_tree.jpg)

_The Witcher 2_ skill tree

Similarly to the original game, Geralt earns talent points whenever XP adds up to a new level. These points may be distributed among the four branches of the skill tree: 

  * [Training](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_skills "The Witcher 2 skills") (uppermost)
  * [Magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_skills "The Witcher 2 skills") (leftmost)
  * [Alchemy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_skills "The Witcher 2 skills") (bottom)
  * [Swordsmanship](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_skills "The Witcher 2 skills") (rightmost)

[Some of these skills](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mutagen_slot_skills "Mutagen slot skills") can be enhanced further with [mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_mutagens "The Witcher 2 mutagens"). 

### Considerations

  * The game has a level cap of 35; since you start at level 1, you will find yourself with 34 talent points at the end of the game.
  * You must spend six talent points in the Training path to unlock the other three paths.
  * The major branches (Alchemy, Magic, and Swordsmanship) each have 15 talents and each talent has two levels of mastery.
  * Therefore, even spending as much as possible in a single major path, you will either miss one talent or leave two half-mastered. Only the Training path may be fully mastered, notwithstanding an expansion or mod.

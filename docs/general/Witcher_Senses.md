**Witcher Senses** are a gameplay mechanic introduced in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ ; when activated, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") focuses his superhuman senses to see clues and tracks that no ordinary [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") can. In the game, [Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") Senses highlight interactive objects in red, and highlights objects that have already been examined yellow. 

Witcher Senses are used extensively throughout _The Witcher 3_ such as in one contract when tracking a [werewolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf") \- its tracks run up a tree, forcing Geralt to find another clue to keep on track; when he finds a tuft of werewolf fur, a scent trail becomes visible to his senses and the hunt resumes. 

Another example is a contract that has the player looking for a [leshen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leshen "Leshen"). Knowing that crows serve leshens, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") focuses his senses and follows the sound of crows to the leshen's totem. 

## Journal entry

  * _**Tracks and clues** : When you find tracks or other clues, walk close, then press [E] to examine them._
  * _**Witcher Senses** : Hold the right mouse button to use your Witcher Senses._
  * _**Witcher Senses – Important Objects** : When you activate your Witcher Senses, objects of interest will be marked and highlighted in a brighter color.  
You can also locate the source of noises (shown as visible sound waves). When you turn toward the source, you will be able to hear the noise better._

## From _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ to _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

When starting a new game of _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ , you can import a saved game from _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ by selecting "New Game" then "Import Witcher 1 Save" from the main menu. In order to be used, the saved game files should be in their original location (most likely  in Windows systems, or ~/.local/share/cdprojektred/witcher2/GameDocuments/Witcher 2/gamesaves in SteamOS/Linux systems) and have been created during the [Ice Plains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Plains "Ice Plains") or Order of the Flaming Rose [Cloister](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cloister "Cloister") portions of the Epilogue. 

Note for Non-English versions
    In case the game is unable to detect your saves from _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ despite them being located in the appropriate folder, create a new save file in the Ice Plains or the Order of the Flaming Rose Cloister areas using English language settings.

Several choices, items, and a small amount of money are transferred to the sequel and are accessible at the start of the game. 

Some of these items include: 

  * Steel Swords 
    * [Ard'aenye](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard%27aenye "Ard'aenye")
    * [D'yaebl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/D%27yaebl_\(sword\) "D'yaebl \(sword\)")
    * [G'valchir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/G%27valchir "G'valchir")
    * [Gwalhir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwalhir "Gwalhir")

  * Silver Swords 
    * [Aerondight](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aerondight "Aerondight")
    * [Moon Blade](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moon_Blade "Moon Blade")

  * Other Items 
    * [Raven's armor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Raven%27s_armor "Raven's armor")
    * A small percentage of your orens (about 1% of your saved game from _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ added to the standard ~ 150 orens of a fresh game).

Some of the choices: 

  * Chapter 3 
    * Kill or save [Thaler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thaler_\(spy\) "Thaler \(spy\)") during [All the King's Men](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/All_the_King%27s_Men "All the King's Men") (you will receive a message and a package from Thaler in [Flotsam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flotsam "Flotsam") if you saved Thaler – this is default game behaviour)
    * Romance with [Shani](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shani "Shani") (a journal entry based on why Geralt parted ways with Shani was added with the Enhanced Edition.)
  * Chapter 5 
    * Kill or save [Princess Adda](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Adda_the_White "Adda the White") during [Her Highness the Striga](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Her_Highness_the_Striga "Her Highness the Striga") (her fate is mentioned by [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") during the Prologue and later by other characters, as well as in a journal entry, and it may have a minor impact on the status of Temeria at the end of the game)
    * Kill or spare [Siegfried](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Siegfried_of_Denesle "Siegfried of Denesle") (Neutral/Scoia'tael paths only: Geralt is either attacked by the fellow knight or left alone by the order)
  * Epilogue (final path): 
    * Order path: 
      * Fewer issues with the [Order](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_of_the_Flaming_Rose "Order of the Flaming Rose") in the third chapter.
      * Geralt may meet Siegfried and [Polycarp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Polycarp_of_Rinde "Polycarp of Rinde").
    * Neutral/Witcher path: 
      * [Iorveth](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Iorveth "Iorveth")'s path: you cannot get through the front door in [Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne").
      * [Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roche "Roche")'s path: you have to kill an angry knight in the second chapter.
    * Scoia'tael path: 
      * Some dialogue changes with Iorveth.

### Possible bugs

  * The book titled "[The Temerian Royal Dynasty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Temerian_Royal_Dynasty "The Temerian Royal Dynasty")" tells the tale of how the Witcher slew Princess Adda, even if the player spared her in the first game.

### Without a Witcher 1 saved game

Choosing **not** to import a _Witcher 1_ save will apply a specific set of choices, as set by the game's developers. These are as follows: 

  * Remained neutral. The neutral path has been chosen.
  * Romance with Triss. Alvin was given to Triss.
  * Spared Thaler.
  * Spared Vincent Meis.
  * Killed Adda.
  * Killed Siegfried.
  * Killed Yaevinn.
  * Killed the King of the Wild Hunt.

## From _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ to _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

_For a full breakdown of decisions made in the second game and their effects in the third, see[The Witcher 3 decision checklist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_decision_checklist "The Witcher 3 decision checklist")._

When you begin a new game in _The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt_ you will be given the option to either "simulate Witcher 2 save" or "Import The Witcher 2 saved game". Choosing to simulate the save will allow for a dialogue at the end of the prologue in Witcher 3, that will allow you to answer questions regarding the events of Witcher 2 which will affect parts of the Witcher 3 storyline and game world. Selecting to import your own Witcher 2 save will automatically fill in the choices with those you made in the previous game (This option is only available if your saved game file is in the correct location: "Users > [username] > My Documents > Witcher 2 > gamesaves" before launching the game). 

### Without a Witcher 2 saved game

Without save game files, selecting a simulated save option in the main menu enables a questioning of Geralt during his visit to the Royal Palace in Vizima, allowing for the selection of five decisions made in the previous game. No gear obtained in _The Witcher 2_ can be imported into the _The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt_. 

**Decisions from _The Witcher 2_**

  * Kill or spare [Aryan La Valette](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aryan_La_Valette "Aryan La Valette").
  * Keep or not the tattoo of the [Blue Stripes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blue_Stripes "Blue Stripes") from the quest [Hung Over](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hung_Over "Hung Over").
  * Side with either [Iorveth](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Iorveth "Iorveth") or [Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roche "Roche").
  * Kill or spare [Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt").
  * Aid them on their respective paths, or rescue [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss "Triss") (this ensures that only the conspirators for the "Lodge Of Sorceresses" are purged on "Loc Muinne").
  * Save [Síle de Tansarville](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/S%C3%ADle_de_Tansarville "Síle de Tansarville") or allow her to perish.
  * Kill or spare [Letho](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letho "Letho").

Choosing **not** to simulate a _Witcher 2_ save will apply a specific set of choices, as set by the game's developers. These are as follows: 

  * Spared Aryan La Valette.
  * Not keep the tattoo.
  * Sided with Vernon Roche.
  * Killed Henselt.
  * Saved Anaïs in Loc Muinne (given to [Natalis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/John_Natalis "John Natalis")).
  * Left Síle de Tansarville to die.
  * Killed Letho.

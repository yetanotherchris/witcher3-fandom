# Damage

Generally speaking, **Damage** is the amount of hit points deducted from a target by an object or an non-playable-character (NPC). Mostly we are concerned with damage inflicted by objects wielded by the player character ([Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia")), but of equal consideration is the damage received from objects wielded by his opponents. This damage amount can range from 0 to the amount of [vitality](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vitality "Vitality") a target has remaining in [the game](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Games "Category:Games"). 

The player, or Geralt, can inflict damage by the using [weapons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Weapons "Category:Weapons") such as a [steel sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Steel_sword "Steel sword"), a [silver sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword"), [signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign") or miscellaneous other [weapons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Weapons "Category:Weapons") he finds, and even his fists.

### Stacking of Damage

Damage in _The Witcher_ stacks by multiplication. This is best explained with the use of examples. 

**Case 1:**

  * Geralt wields a steel sword which boasts **+50%** damage.
  * He then applies a whetstone (**+10%** damage) to it.
  * The amount of damage he will do then is:

    _(base damage) × 1.5 (Weapon damage bonus) × 1.1 (whetstone) = (base damage) ×**1.65**_

**Case 2:**

  * Geralt wields a steel sword which boasts **+50%** damage.
  * He then applies [necrophage oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage_Oil "Necrophage Oil") (**+100%** damage _vs_ [necrophages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage "Necrophage")) to it.
  * The amount of damage he will do against necrophages then is:

    _(base damage) × 1.5 (Weapon damage bonus) × 2 (necrophage oil) = (base damage) ×**3**_

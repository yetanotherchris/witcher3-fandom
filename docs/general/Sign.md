# Sign

The witchers adapted the spell, making use of the fact that it does not require knowledge of a magical formula—concentration and the gesture are enough. That's why they call it a Sign.

\- [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") discussing signs with [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), pg. 309, _[Blood of Elves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_of_Elves "Blood of Elves")_ (U.S. edition)

Though they are not warrior [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") who employ powerful [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"), [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") can cast simple magic spells that can prove effective when used properly. Witchers call these spells **signs** and usually use them against [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster"), though they also have non-combat applications. Because of the signs, witchers prefer single-handed weaponry since it leaves their other hand free to cast. Some of the signs are cast by arranging the fingers in specific way, while the others have to be drawn on a solid surface. 

It is possible to make the sign more powerful by enforcing it with the spell.[1][2] [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") utilised this method at least once, during his fight with [Vereena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vereena "Vereena").[3][4]

### Sign modifiers

[Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia")'s skill at using these signs is governed by the allocation of talents as he levels up. This can affect the following: 

  * **Sign intensity** — a multiplier for the severity of the sign cast.
  * **Sign duration** — used with signs like Quen and Yrden where the length of time a spell lasts is useful.
  * Whether or not a sign can be "powered up".

### Cost of casting

The cost of casting a sign is measured in [endurance](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_character_statistics "The Witcher character statistics"). Casting signs uses up endurance and proficiency at casting signs reduces the amount of endurance required. 

### Notes

  * [Leo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leo "Leo") tells Geralt in the Prologue that he can not use signs since he broke his fingers on the [gauntlet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen_training_equipment "Kaer Morhen training equipment") during training.
  * The signs included in the game are based on [Classical Elements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_element "wikipedia:Classical element"), both in their nature and by their symbols.
  * The sign intensity for Igni does increase as you increase its level, despite not being mentioned in the tooltip.
  * One sign which was considered, but not implemented, for the game was the _Aagni_ sign, said to be a powerful combination of the Aard and Igni signs.

### Mechanics

Press [![CTRL](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/4b/Tw2_control_ctrl.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232544)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/4b/Tw2_control_ctrl.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605232544 "CTRL") key to select one of five icons on the left of the circle menu. Then press[![Q](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a6/Tw2_control_q.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605233123)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a6/Tw2_control_q.png/revision/latest?cb=20110605233123 "Q")to cast it. The sign will strike the nearest opponent in front of Geralt. When you enhance a sign spending talent points in the relative [magic skills](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_skills "The Witcher 2 skills"), it will hit more enemies at once with an area effect. 

### [![Vigor](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/76/Tw2_icon_vigor.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/48?cb=20110606151754)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/76/Tw2_icon_vigor.png/revision/latest?cb=20110606151754 "Vigor") Casting cost

Every time you cast a sign it consumes some vigor. The vigor bar is on the top left corner of the screen, under the wolf head [medallion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_medallion "Witcher medallion") and the red bar of [vitality](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vitality "Vitality"). When the vigor bar is zero, Geralt is not able to cast any sign or parry an opponent's hit. However some [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_potions "The Witcher 2 potions"), using dedicated [Places of Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Place_of_Power "Place of Power") or enhancing some specific skills like [Vigor Regeneration](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vigor_Regeneration "Vigor Regeneration") and [Fortitude](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fortitude "Fortitude"), can increase the vigor regeneration in and out of combat.

### Differences

Name  |  Changes   
---|---  
Aard | This sign allows players to force enemies back by manipulating kinetic energy. Its alternate form is an area of effect blast of energy that pushes everyone around the player away. Excellent when you need some breathing room.   
Igni | This sign remains the same from its predecessor, however, upon upgrading its alternative form, the player can then shoot a continuous jet of flame from his hand. Ideal for handling giant monsters and groups of opponents.   
Yrden | This sign was altered slightly. When employed it slows down all enemies that enter its range. Upon upgrading, the sign can cause damage.   
Quen | This sign creates an active shield that disappears after a hit (or several when upgraded) its alternate form is a bubble shield that can be maintained as long as the player has vigor to spare.   
Axii | Unlike in _The Witcher 2_ , Axii doesn't turn an enemy into a temporary ally right away; you need to unlock this ability as you level up in _The Witcher 3_. Until then this sign only leaves enemies in a confused state while standing in place.

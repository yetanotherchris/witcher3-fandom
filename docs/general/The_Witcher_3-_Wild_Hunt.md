_**The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt**_ is the third installment in the series of games developed by [CD PROJEKT RED](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/CD_PROJEKT "CD PROJEKT") featuring the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"). The game was originally scheduled for release in late 2014, then pushed back to 24 February 2015, and finally released on 19 May 2015.[2] During the first two weeks since release it had sold more than 4 million copies worldwide,[3] more than doubling the total sales of its predecessor, _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_.[4]

Unlike the previous games, _The Witcher 3_ is set in a multi-region open world, featuring over 100 hours of content.[5] Several early articles mentioned that the developers planned to have an open world similar to that of [Skyrim](https://elderscrolls.fandom.com/wiki/The_Elder_Scrolls_V:_Skyrim "w:c:elderscrolls:The Elder Scrolls V: Skyrim"), but 20% larger.[6][7] The developers were quick to point out that while there was much to appreciate in [Bethesda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bethesda_Softworks)'s open world concept, they were also making an effort not to repeat what they perceived as the flaws in-game. As with earlier games, the consequences of Geralt's actions will impact how the narrative progresses. The game has been built using the new [REDengine 3](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/REDengine "REDengine") and was designed with player feedback from fans of previous _Witcher_ games in mind.[8]

For fans of the series, the online event "[Killing Monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Killing_Monsters "Killing Monsters")", a weekly giveaway of wallpapers and audio files that ran from 10 June 2013 to 14 August 2013, culminated in the release of the "Killing Monsters" cinematic trailer. 

On 14 May 2015, CD PROJEKT RED (working with Digic Pictures) released a pre-launch short story trailer titled "[A Night to Remember](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Night_to_Remember "A Night to Remember")". 

## Later releases

At E3 2019, a port for Nintendo Switch got announced. It was developed in collaboration with Saber Interactive and released on October 15, 2019 as a _Complete Edition_ with both expansions and 16 DLCs on a 32-GB cartridge (or as a single download, respectively).[9] On January 28th of 2021, CDPR announced that the base game and the expansions would be made available separately, and that the Complete Edition would eventually get phased out.[10] As of August 2025, the digital Complete Edition is still available alongside the base game and its season pass (consisting of the also separately available expansions). For the physical version, a new release with only the base game got released on November 1st, 2021, seemingly replacing the Complete Edition.[11] This new physical edition is not being mentioned on Nintendo's or CDPR's game pages.[12][13][14]

On September 4, 2020, it was announced that the game and its expansions would be getting a next-generation upgrade and would be released as a standalone purchase for the next-generation consoles (Xbox Series X and PlayStation 5) as well as PC. A free update would be given out to those who already own the game on the PC, Xbox One, and PlayStation 4.[15] While it was originally expected to be released in 2021[16] it was later announced that the [next-generation update](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)") had been delayed to 2022[17] before CDPR announced it'd be released on December 14, 2022.[1]

On November 15, 2023, CD PROJEKT RED announced a mod editor was being created for the game to be released in 2024 and would be available for free for all owners of _The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt_ on PC.[18] In May 2024, they released a new version of [REDkit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/REDkit "REDkit") for the game. 

## Story

    _For the main article, see[The Witcher 3 storyline](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_storyline "The Witcher 3 storyline")._

The world is in [chaos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos"). The air is thick with tension and the smoke of burnt villages. The fearsome [Empire of Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") has struck again, ravaging the helpless [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). The once mighty who tried to use [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") for their own gain are now gone. In these uncertain times, no one can say what fortune holds in store, who will bring peace to the world and who will cause it only misery. But a force darker and deadlier emerges. 

The petty men and women commanding tin-plated armies fail to understand that their conflict is child's play compared to the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt"), the otherworldly threat which now looms. These ghastly spectral riders have for ages plagued humankind, bringing misery to the world. This time the Wild Hunt seeks one person in particular: the one individual [Destiny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Destiny "Destiny") itself bestowed upon Geralt, the one soul Geralt considers kin. 

## Features

### General

  * The grand finale to Geralt's story
  * A standalone adventure easily accessible to new players
  * Meaningful choices with consequences that change the story and the game world
  * Quests and main story threads that can be resolved in any order or completed in parallel
  * Unforgettable quests deeply linked with the core storyline and designed with care to draw players in
  * A breathtaking cinematic introduction illustrating the game background
  * Unique atmosphere, memorable characters, and gritty dialogue

### Open world

  * A vast open world - 35 times larger than that in The Witcher 2
  * Multiple ecosystems and cultures that players can move between at will
  * Regions with different inspirations and deep cultural references, each with a distinct feel
  * Free exploration unspoiled by loading times

### Living world

  * A world shaped by the player's decisions, but which, left to itself, goes on living
  * A realistic day and night cycle
  * A dynamic weather system
  * A living economy: The new in-game economy system varies the price of goods based on surrounding conditions or their place of origin relative to Geralt's current whereabouts in the world: the price of fish might differ depending on distance from water, and a village lying on a trade route for trappers and hunters might have many tanneries and leather workers, affecting the price of crafting components and armor.
  * A unique, deep, consistent and expansive game world based on that described by [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")

### Combat

  * Combat complexity based on a variety of actions at the player's disposal, not on correct attack sequencing
  * A [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses") system bringing new tactical depth to combat
  * [Monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary"), each with their own lore, posing unique hunting challenges and responding to changes in the environment

### RPG

  * A tutorial built into the game, allowing players to dive into the world of the Witcher with ease
  * Involving investigations and rewarding hunts through the Witcher Senses mechanic
  * A completely rewritten RPG mechanic allowing players to see the influence of statistics on gameplay
  * An expanded [Sign](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign") casting system: each of the 5 Signs has two different modes of use, creating even more possibilities in combat and challenging players to use magic in creative ways
  * A highly-intuitive [Alchemy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") system
  * An expanded character development system
  * Advanced [Crafting](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_crafting_components "The Witcher 3 crafting components") mechanics

### Visuals

  * Fully dynamic light and shadow effects throughout the game world, generating superior atmosphere and ambiance
  * Detailed, realistic locations through a significant increase in polygon numbers
  * A deep role-playing experience through visually attractive, film-like presentation

## [Expansions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_expansions "Category:The Witcher 3 expansions")

[![Expansion](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/42/Expansion.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150707114457)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/42/Expansion.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150707114457) [![Expansion](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/42/Expansion.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150707114457)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/42/Expansion.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20150707114457) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Expansion.jpg)

On April 7, 2015, [CD PROJEKT RED](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/CD_PROJEKT_RED "CD PROJEKT RED") announced two major expansions for _**The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt**_ , entitled _Hearts of Stone_ and _Blood and Wine_.[20] CD PROJEKT RED said that both expansion packs included new enemies, characters, and gear. Players would also be reunited with some "dearly missed" characters from the series. _Hearts of Stone_ was released on October 13, 2015, and _Blood and Wine_ on May 31, 2016, both are available on all three major platforms. Players can purchase both expansions at once by getting the Expansion Pass, now on sale through several retailers. 

On August 30, 2016, a Game of the Year edition (for PC) and Complete edition (for consoles) was released, containing the base game, both expansions, and all downloadable content. 

### _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion

    _**Hearts of Stone** is a major expansion that adds over ten hours of additional content. Geralt will adventure into the wilds of [No Man's Land](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen") and the nooks and alleys of [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"), where he will try to complete a contract from the mysterious [Man of Glass](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Man_of_Glass "Man of Glass"). Caught in a thick tangle of deceit, Geralt will need all his cunning and strength to solve the mystery and emerge unscathed._

### _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion

    _**Blood and Wine** is a major expansion that adds over twenty hours of additional content. A tale that will introduce the all-new in-game region of [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), Blood and Wine will take Geralt to a land untainted by war, where an atmosphere of carefree indulgence and knightly ritual masks an ancient and bloody secret._

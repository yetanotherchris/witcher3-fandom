## Bestiary entry

    _It is often said the darkest recesses of caves play home to the[Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent")'s most fearsome monsters against which there is little of hope of victory or escape. The long solution, then, is to seal them off from the world with the hope that they shall forever remain buried._
    _A certain priest of the[Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire"), however, learned that hope and prayer is sometimes not enough. The cavernous mind within [Devil's Pit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Devil%27s_Pit "Devil's Pit") holds a specter whose very existence is believed to breed pestilence. With the threat of its release now imminent, the cleric seeks a new solution – the aid of a [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher").  
The being that haunted Devil's Pit turned out to be a red miasmal. Whereas a [plague maiden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Plague_maiden "Plague maiden") is thought to be a damned spirit—once [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") but now cursed to spread the pestilence—a miasmal, by contrast, is considered an evil force born from the concentrated collective suffering of [plague](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Catriona_plague "Catriona plague") victims. It has the ability to possess human bodies thus granting it corporeal form and a means to wreak havoc. This process, however, irreparably corrupts and damages the host, causing the miasmal to continuously seek out stronger, more durable bodies. What cruel irony, then, that a witcher's immunity makes the monster slayer a perfect shell for the specter to invade and control._
    _Fortunately, a red miasmal's power is not so absolute that it cannot be defeated by an experienced witcher – though even then only with considerable effort. The specter, once weakened, can be trapped with with the[Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") [Sign](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign") then expelled from the host's body with [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard"), thus creating an opening in which to defeat it. Consuming [Reinald's Philter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reinald%27s_Philter "Reinald's Philter") offers an advantage by increasing the intensity of witcher Signs._

## Combat tactics

The miasmal uses the basic template of a [hym](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hym "Hym"), but in combat is more similar to a [leshen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leshy "Leshy"), but instead of summoning [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)"), it summons undead plague victims.

# Kikimore warrior

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What role do kikimore warriors play in their colony?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Kikimore warriors are vital for the defense of their colony. They protect the queen and workers by attacking any intruders. Their aggressive behavior ensures the colony's safety, as they eliminate threats. Warriors also command workers, who follow their orders. This structure resembles a termite colony, with the queen laying eggs that hatch into soldiers and workers. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why are kikimore warriors considered abominable creatures?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Kikimore warriors are seen as abominable due to their spider-like appearance and aggressive behavior. They protect their colonies by spitting acid to blind and harm intruders. Their areas are devoid of animal life, indicating their lethal nature. In 'The Witcher,' kikimore warriors are part of the kikimora lore, often viewed as an affront to the gods. They are vulnerable to silver and insectoid oil, despite their fearsome reputation. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How can you identify a kikimore warrior's territory?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Kikimore warriors mark their territory by defending nests and tunnels where their queen and workers reside. They communicate using scent and are immune to many effects, making them effective guardians. The presence of these nests and tunnels is a key indicator of their territory. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How do kikimore warriors protect their queen?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Kikimore warriors protect their queen by defending the nest from attackers. They are organized like a termite colony, with soldiers defending and workers building and gathering food. Kikimore warriors are immune to most effects and communicate through scent, ensuring the safety of the queen and workers. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What makes kikimore warriors different from spiders or cockroaches?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Kikimore warriors are distinct from spiders and cockroaches due to their colony structure and physical traits. Unlike the solitary nature of spiders and cockroaches, kikimores operate in a termite-like hierarchy with a queen, workers, and soldiers. Kikimore warriors resemble beetles, featuring thick chitinous armor, horned heads, and thorny legs. They defend their colony by spitting acid and are immune to many effects, including blinding. Their role is to protect the queen and workers, making them more organized and dangerous than typical spiders or cockroaches. 

Provided by: Fandom

## Information

### Class

[Insectoid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Insectoid "Insectoid")

### Occurrence

Kikimore warriors protect their queen and [kikimore workers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kikimore_worker "Kikimore worker") as they dig tunnels or look for food

### Immunity

Immune to most effects, including [blinding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blinding "Blinding"); resistant to [knockdown](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knockdown "Knockdown"); they use scent to communicate with other kikimores

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver") and [Insectoid Oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Insectoid_Oil "Insectoid Oil")

### Tactics

During combat warriors spit acid, blinding their opponents and causing crippling pain

### Alchemy

[Tracheae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tracheae "Tracheae")  
[Venom glands](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Venom_glands "Venom glands")  
[Toxin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toxin "Toxin")

Kikimore warriors defend [workers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kikimore_worker "Kikimore worker") and their [queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kikimore_queen "Kikimore queen") from intruders. They are very dutiful in this, killing anything that comes near the colony. Luckily, their territory is easily recognized as it is devoid of all animal life and littered with skeletons picked clean. Keep your eyes open when exploring the [swamp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swamp "Swamp")! 

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") [Bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bestiary "The Witcher bestiary") entry

    _"A more abominable beast is indeed hard to find. Not quite a cockroach nor a spider, it makes ladies faint and gentlemen feel disgusted. The kikimore warrior reeks of the swamp. For this reason, I insist that exhibit no. 88 be removed from the university assembly hall. And anticipating the question "what should be done with it then?", I answer: throw it to the compost heap._
    _From a request submitted to the president of[Oxenfurt University](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/University_of_Oxenfurt "University of Oxenfurt")"_

### Source

  * In Chapter III, Geralt can speak with a neutral (blue) Armored Guardsman (north of Town Hall entrance or next to Triss' house) near the Guard Knight in the [Trade Quarter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Trade_Quarter "Trade Quarter") who will tell him of a terrible spider-like monster. The conversation unlocks Kikimore Warriors, [Tracheae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tracheae "Tracheae"), and [Venom Glands](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Venom_Glands "Venom Glands"). At other times, Armored Guardsmen will tell him of [Kikimore Workers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kikimore_worker "Kikimore worker") for a Bestiary entry or [fleders](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fleder "Fleder"), but the fleder conversation does not give their Bestiary entry.
  * [The Wonderful World of Insectoids](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Wonderful_World_of_Insectoids "The Wonderful World of Insectoids")

### Notes

  * In the Prologue, [Lambert](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lambert "Lambert") claims the [Strong Style](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_fighting_styles "Witcher fighting styles") is best to use against "kikimores", but the conversation does not result in a Bestiary entry.
  * True to their description, the kikimore warriors will engage [drowners](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner"), [drowned deads](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowned_dead "Drowned dead"), [bloedzuigers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bloedzuiger "Bloedzuiger") and others in battle on sight, not just [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia").

### Bestiary entry

    _Then the kikimore princess summoned all her loyal sisters and off they went! Together they charged the enemy trying to harm their queen.  
– "The Kikimore Princess and the Mountain Giants"  
Cornelius Briggs (dwarven children's fable, fragment)_
    _It is unknown how exactly it is kikimores communicate with each other. Autopsies are hampered by the high toxicity of their subjects. Scholars have established that kikimores do not seem to possess any detectable auricles. One amateur researcher, Count di Salvaress, put forth a theory in his treatise "A Microscope Among Monsters" suggesting kikimores have a highly developed sense of smell and use airborne particles undetectable to humans to transmit information. This theory has yet to be proved or disproved._
    _Kikimore warriors defend their nests from attackers. They attack somewhat slower than kikimore workers. They are able to spew streams of caustic venom a great distance and leap to attack. This venom is highly unique in that it reacts with a witcher's body to raise the level of his potion toxicity. They are covered with thick, hard armor which easily deflects blows from even the sharpest sword._
    _Kikimore warriors are protected by swarms of workers which obey their orders until they are themselves eradicated._
    _They are completely immune to the effects of the Axii Sign, but vulnerable to Igni, oils and bombs harming insectoids and blows dealt by a silver sword._

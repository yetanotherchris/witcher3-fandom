**Ulfhedinn** are a type of [werewolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf") only found around [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"). They have stronger combat skills on their own, but unlike common werewolves can't summon [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)") to their aid[1], and only appear during the night (after 10pm). 

A [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entry can be added by reading [In Beast's Clothing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/In_Beast%27s_Clothing "In Beast's Clothing"). 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What makes the Ulfhedinn different from common werewolves?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Distinct from common werewolves, Ulfhedinn are found only in Skellige and are known for their superior combat skills. However, they lack the ability to summon wolves. They are nocturnal creatures, appearing only after 10pm. Their vulnerabilities include Moon Dust, Devil's Puffball, Cursed oil, and Igni. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why can Ulfhedinn only appear during the night?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Ulfhedinn, a werewolf variant from Skellige, manifests exclusively after 10pm. This night-time appearance is a species trait, not a limitation. In specific quests, daytime encounters occur within a magic tower where it's perpetually night, irrespective of the actual time. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of the Ulfhedinn in Skellige folklore?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In Skellige folklore, Ulfhedinn are unique werewolves known for their superior combat skills and nocturnal activity, particularly during full moons. Unlike common werewolves, they do not summon wolves for assistance. Skellige ballads commemorate those who have defeated Ulfhedinn, hailing them as heroes. The name Ulfhedinn originates from Icelandic, with 'Ulf' meaning 'wolf' and 'Heðinn' signifying a 'jacket of fur or skin'. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What happened to Professor Artibus Joannes Rack during his expedition in Skellige?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Geographer Professor Artibus Joannes Rack disappeared while on a surveying expedition in Skellige. His disappearance is linked to an encounter with an ulfhedinn, a powerful breed of werewolf native to Skellige. These creatures, known for hunting humans due to Skellige's harsh conditions, are stronger than other werewolves. Historically, only a handful of Skellige warriors have successfully defeated an ulfhedinn.

## Bestiary entry

    _Ulfhedinn? What's that, some kind of[fish](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fish "Fish")?  
– Professor Artibus Joannes Rack, geographer, disappeared during a surveying expedition in Skellige_
    _Ulfhedinn are a breed of werewolf found mainly in Skellige. The harsh and barren conditions of the isles might explain why they primarily hunt men and are stronger than their continental brethren. Older and particularly dangerous ulfhedinn are called vorefs. Only a few daring warriors in Skellige history have managed to defeat an ulfhedinn, and each of them is commemorated in ballads as a hero to this day._
    _**Unobtainable without reading the book:**_

    _Like werewolves, ulfhedinn and vorefs are active at night, particularly when the moon is at its fullest. Fast, strong, and amazingly resilient, these creatures kill with disturbing ease.[Silver blades](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword") should be brought against them, as should [Devil's Puffball](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Devil%27s_Puffball "Devil's Puffball"). Take note that when near death the ulfhedinn becomes particularly dangerous and will attack with doubled fury, while calling on wolves to come to its rescue._[1]

## Combat tactics

These breeds of [cursed ones](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cursed_one "Cursed one") are some of the fastest and most lethal. They can leap great distances at such speeds that it can be a challenge defending against them. [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") is of great use in avoiding these attacks. Counterattacks against a werewolf or ulfhedinn can be difficult to time. It is best to use footwork and evade them, using dodges and roles. When possible, use [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni"). 

Setting these foes alight can force cursed ones to stay still long enough to score critical strikes on their flanks. Fire, bleeding, and poison effects can be used to slow the regeneration of these creatures. Avoid fighting werewolves in tight spaces if at all possible, since the speed of their assault can quickly overwhelm even high skilled [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"). Werewolves have incredible reach, and you want to have space to work with while fighting them. 

[Exploding bolts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Exploding_bolt "Exploding bolt") for a [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow") can be useful at dealing fire damage, should the werewolf be caught at range. Watch for the dual overhead claw swipe; it cannot be parried and causes grievous wounds and bleeding. Near death, ulfhedinn become extremely dangerous, striking with increase strength. They can even call upon a pack of wolves for assistance.

## Notes

  * In _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ , there exists a large wolf called [Voref](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Voref "Voref"), which unlike other wolves is weak against silver.
  * There are only three ulfhedinn in the entire game and all are at quest locations as mentioned above. For unknown reasons, despite having a separate bestiary entry, they also all appear with the "Werewolf" label.
  * The first paragraph of the bestiary entry is also automatically added when [Morkvarg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morkvarg "Morkvarg") is first seen, at the [Freya's Garden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar") main gate. Despite this however, Morkvarg himself is coded as a regular werewolf and not ulfhedinn.

## Trivia

  * The name is probably the Icelandic name Úlfhéðinn, which derives from two names: Ulf, meaning "wolf" and Heðinn, meaning "jacket of fur or skin".
  * Historically, the Ulfheðnar (singular: Ulfheðinn) were Norse warriors who fought dressed in wolf clothes in a state of trance, after having ingested cocktails of beer and _[amanita muscaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amanita_muscaria)_. They were akin to the [berserkers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Berserker_\(creature\) "Berserker \(creature\)").

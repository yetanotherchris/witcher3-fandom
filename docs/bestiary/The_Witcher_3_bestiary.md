# The Witcher 3 bestiary

[![Tw3 icon monsters](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a4/Tw3_icon_monsters.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/160?cb=20160331170001)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a4/Tw3_icon_monsters.png/revision/latest?cb=20160331170001) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_icon_monsters.png)

A **bestiary** is usually a volume containing information on different creatures, either living or mythical, depending on the focus of the treatise. In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and its expansions, _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion and _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, there are many beasts to discover and slay in your adventures as [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"). As you come across new creatures all of the information you know about them is entered into this book. This Information can be found out by simply finding and engaging the [monster](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") or by reading certain books. Be aware that all animals and beasts Geralt kills have an actual scent that attracts other monsters in the area.[1]

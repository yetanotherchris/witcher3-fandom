The "**Bovine Defense Force Initiative** " or "**Bovine Defense Force** " is a game mechanic implemented into the game with [Patch 1.05](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.05_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.05 \(The Witcher 3\)") in response to an exploit which allowed players in [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard") to quickly gain [crowns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") by killing [cows](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cow "Cow"), looting [their hides](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cow_hide "Cow hide"), meditating for the cows to respawn, and repeating the process an indefinite number of times. 

This meant that players could accumulate large amounts of cow hides and sell them for large gain with relatively little effort. However, in Patch 1.05, and all game versions since, a level 27 [chort](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chort "Chort") was made to recurrently spawn a short distance away from the cow pen in White Orchard village after the player had killed six cows. If the player managed to kill a chort, a new one would spawn, and this process continued indefinitely. 

Ironically, this turned out to be a more efficient crown farming method for higher level characters who are able to handle the barrage of chorts, as the base value of a [chort hide](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chort_hide "Chort hide") is more than double that of cowhide. However, this has since been partially patched out and only one chort will appear at a time. Once killed, it may not respawn until [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") leaves White Orchard and returns again. 

## Notes

  * In _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion, the Bovine Defense Force Initiative was referenced by the addition of two new gwent cards: [Cow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cow_\(gwent_card\) "Cow \(gwent card\)") and [Bovine Defense Force](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bovine_Defense_Force_\(gwent_card\) "Bovine Defense Force \(gwent card\)"). Whenever the 0-strength Cow card is removed from the battlefield, the 8-strength BDF card is summoned to take its place. 
    * The exploit that led to the implementation of this mechanic is also referenced in [The Taxman Cometh](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Taxman_Cometh "The Taxman Cometh") quest, in which [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") is asked by taxman [Walthemor Mitty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Walthemor_Mitty "Walthemor Mitty") whether he engaged in the wholesale trade of rawhide in the White Orchard area.
    * Oddly, the card art depicts it as a [fiend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fiend "Fiend"), but in game it's actually a chort.

## Trivia

  * The chort being represented as a stronger bovine in this regard bears some resemblance to and may be inspired by [The Cow King](https://diablo.fandom.com/wiki/The_Cow_King "w:c:diablo:The Cow King") in [Diablo II](https://diablo.fandom.com/wiki/Diablo_II "w:c:diablo:Diablo II"). Coincidentally, the Cow King's domain has also been nerfed due to excessive farming.

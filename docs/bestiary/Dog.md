# Dog

**Dogs** are the carnivorous decedents of [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)") that were domesticated by [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human"). While generally docile, they may become wild, cursed, unnaturally ravenous, or all of the above. In these instances, they are treated as predators. 

After the [Northern Wars](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Wars "Northern Wars") in the [13th century](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#13th_century "Timeline/Games"), mass numbers of dogs died due to famine and their dead owners.[1]

## Information

### Occurrence

Dogs are domesticated animals; even wild dogs stay close to [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") settlements.

### Immunity

None

### Susceptibility

Susceptible to fear and knockdown attempts; experienced witcher use the Group Style while fighting multiple dogs.

### Tactics

Dogs attack in groups, taking advantage of numerical superiority.

### Combat tactics

As with all non-[magical](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") creatures, a [steel sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_steel_swords "The Witcher 3 steel swords") is most effective against dogs. One known weakness is simply by coating your sword with [beast oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beast_oil "Beast oil"), however the [sign](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign") [aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") is also good for pushing back a singular, or even pack of, dogs, and because a the creature is lightweight, this works really well. They are also fairly susceptible to [igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni"), catching [fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire "Fire") due to their fur. Generally, one-on-one with a single dog is not much of a threat at all; it's a pack of them surrounding you that is the problem. 

You have to drop each one quickly while simultaneously dodging out of range of the others coming at you from the sides and behind. Dogs are also particularly fast on their feet, and while they aren't all that strong, their speed and numbers can more than make up for it if you caught unprepared.

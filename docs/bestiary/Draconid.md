**Draconids** is a diverse order of quasi-reptiles that often cause trouble to humanoid races. 

## Characteristics

While the creature most commonly associated with draconids is [dragon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon"), the most populous members of this order are actually the so-called **lesser draconids** , which include [dracolizards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dracolizard "Dracolizard") (slyzards), [forktails](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forktail "Forktail") and [wyverns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wyvern "Wyvern"). They may be easily confused with dragons, as they share scaly skin or armor made up of bony plates, bat wings, and reptilian maws, legs, and tails. They are smaller and less intelligent than dragons, and often possess atrophied forelegs, to the point that certain species have none at all. Dragons, in turn, are larger, possess two pairs of well-developed legs, huge wings and tail, and obvious intelligence.[2][3][4] However, mutations from this scheme exist.[5][6]

A significant part of draconids possess no wings, such as [dracoturtles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dracoturtle "Dracoturtle")[6] or [vigilosaurs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vigilosaur "Vigilosaur").[7] At least one species, [sea serpent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sea_serpent "Sea serpent"), has no limbs. 

## Draconids in culture

[![COA Zerrikania](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/8/87/COA_Zerrikania.svg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/100?cb=20200503163837)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/8/87/COA_Zerrikania.svg/revision/latest?cb=20200503163837) [![COA Zerrikania](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/8/87/COA_Zerrikania.svg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/100?cb=20200503163837)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/8/87/COA_Zerrikania.svg/revision/latest?cb=20200503163837) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:COA_Zerrikania.svg)

The danger draconids present to herds and communities has led to conflicts with human and nonhuman races. [Witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") and other specialized hunters such as [Crinfrid Reavers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crinfrid_Reavers "Crinfrid Reavers") hunt some or all of these creatures, even though the danger is not always lethal.[4][2]

There are however notable exceptions. In [Zerrikania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikania "Zerrikania"), [dragons are venerated](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikanian_dragon_cults "Zerrikanian dragon cults") and warrior orders such as _[Faithel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Faithel "Faithel")_ have emerged in order to protect these sacred beings.[2][3] Even in [Northern Realms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Realms "Northern Realms") and [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") dragons are sometimes perceived as more than vile beasts, and are displayed on noble banners and coats of arms.[8][9][10]

Draconids are sometimes bred as pets or for guarding purposes. An example is wyvern, bred by certain [druid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid") circles[11] and crime bosses.[12] [Mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") of [Rissberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rissberg "Rissberg") have even created entirely new species of draconids, such as vigilosaurs.[7]

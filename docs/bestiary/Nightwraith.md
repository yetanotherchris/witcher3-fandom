# Nightwraith

When the sun sets, the fields become the domain of midnight [demons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Demon "Demon") who draw their power from moonlight. Dark as the night itself and filled with grief, they roam the fields lit by silver light. Woe to those who are spotted by a **nightwraith**.

## Information

### Class

[Specter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter "Specter")

### Occurrence

Nightwraiths appear on fields and meadows; they can be found after dark, when the moon is high; they are [specters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghost "Ghost") but at the same time they maintain a strong connection with the natural world, they see the living but cannot understand them, because the dead cannot hear the living

### Immunity

Immune to [blinding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blinding "Blinding"), [poison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison"), [pain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pain "Pain") and [bleeding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bleeding "Bleeding") attempt; they are [fearless](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fear "Fear")

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver") and [Specter Oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter_Oil "Specter Oil")

### Tactics

They are able to grasp the moonlight and blind their enemies with it

### Alchemy

[Death Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Dust "Death Dust")  
[Shadow Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shadow_Dust "Shadow Dust")  
[Ectoplasm](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ectoplasm "Ectoplasm")

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") [Bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bestiary "The Witcher bestiary") entry

    _Nightwraiths are born of moonlight, wind and the earth cooling after the heat of the day. They rise above the ground and whirl in a mad dance, which should not be seen by any mortal. If caught peeping, the mortal is blinded by moonlight, then taken into the circle and forced to dance until he expires, at times becoming a nightwraith himself._

### Bestiary entry

    _Nightwraiths exude this immense sadness, this helpless wraith... I fear them, same as anyone. But most of all I feel sorry for them.  
– Aelline Altsparr, [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") [trobairitz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bard "Bard")._
    _Compared to other creatures of the night –[katakans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Katakan "Katakan"), [nekurats](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nekurat "Nekurat") and [werewolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf"), for example – nightwraiths (and their rarer cousins, duskwraiths) might not seem all that dangerous. After all, one might ask, how much harm could a pale, withered woman in a tattered dress do? The answer: quite a bit. Instead of finding this out the hard way, avoid crossing fields and meadows at night at all costs._
    _Like[noonwraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith "Noonwraith"), nightwraiths are only found in rural areas. Travelers fall victim to them most often, but if legends are to be believed they also sneak into huts at times and murder peasants in their sleep._
    _When they attack nightwraiths remain immaterial for most of the time, meaning physical blows pass right through them. They only take on more tangible form for the brief moments in which they strike. When weakened, they will create several projections of themselves which cannot attack directly but act as transmitters of sorts through which the nightwraith can sap her victim's vital energy._
    _Nightwraiths can turn immaterial and are at such times very difficult to wound. In order to force one to take on corporeal form, trap it with the[Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") Sign or hit it with the blast of a [Moon Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moon_Dust "Moon Dust") bomb._
    _Do not believe the old wife tales and think yourself completely safe from nightwraiths during the day. They appear under the light of the sun as well - but are much weaker then than after dusk._

### Combat tactics

Nightwraiths favor the dark. If you seek to claim a bounty on a nightwraith, you have to meditate until the moon hangs in the sky. Unfortunately, this is also when the nightwraith is at its strongest. If you are patient, you can find a nightwraith during the day, when it is at its weakest. Mostly incorporeal, a nightwraith must be made corporeal, either through Yrden, or Moon Dust bombs. When forced to become corporeal, [silver swords](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword") can be employed to finish the wraith off. They are susceptible to [Specter Oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter_Oil "Specter Oil"). 

When weakened, a nightwraith can split into three copies of itself. Destroy these with all haste, during this state the nightwraith saps vital energy from nearby sources. Bombs and crossbow bolts can destroy nightwraith doppelgangers. Upon entering a cloud released by a Dimeritium Bomb, they will immediately dissolve. Destroying the copies forces the real nightwraith to reveal itself. 

## Notes

  * The name "Nightwraith" is actually a misnomer. In the Polish (original) version they are called "Północnica" where "północ" means "midnight" thus the proper name would be "Midnightwraith" which mirrors the "Noonwraith", the name was probably changed for convenience. However, a creature called "Nocnica" which in English would be "Nightwraith" is mentioned in the books like _[The Tower of the Swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Tower_of_the_Swallow "The Tower of the Swallow")_. It is possible that both creatures are actually one and the same.

Apart from [dragons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon"), **cats** are the only creatures known to absorb [magical](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") [energy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos"), but no one knows what they do with it.[1] They are also instantly able to recognize [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") and exhibit an obvious disdain for them, like hissing at them.[2] Cats can also see through invisibility.[3]

* * *

The following is considered comics canon only and may contradict [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")'s works.

* * *

In the [Japanese-inspired land](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Japanese-inspired_land "Japanese-inspired land"), some cats when growing old transform into [yōkai](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Y%C5%8Dkai "Yōkai") \- powerful and mysterious entities and spirits - such as [nekomata](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nekomata "Nekomata"), whose tails split into two and they gain powers, and [maneki neko](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maneki_neko "Maneki neko"), which become extremely lucky to be around. These spirits are worshipped by the population, with shrines built in places like Mount [Kaijinzan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaijinzan "Kaijinzan").[4]

## Notes

  * There are also [werecats](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werecat "Werecat"),[7] large creatures that are half-[human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") and half feline with powers to bind other similar beasts, such as [panthers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Panther "Panther"), to themselves.[8]
  * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , using the [potestaquisitor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Potestaquisitor "Potestaquisitor") (quest item) to find magical energy will lead [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to a nearby cat, leading to him uttering the lines "ah, there you are" or "damn cat, ffwshhht!".
  * Like in certain real world cultures, seeing a [black cat crossing one's path](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_cat) is considered a bad omen in the Witcher lore as well.[2]

### Species

Equus ferus

A **horse** is a domesticated animal, often used as a mount or pack beast. In the [novels](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_series "Witcher series"), it is the primary method of transportation. Distances are also often measured in multiples of a "day's ride", which is said to be between 35 and 50 miles per day for very good specimens and perhaps closer to 20 miles per day for "average" animals. 

In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can use [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") on wild horses to then ride them. Interestingly enough, this will cause the horse to be permanently ridable assuming it doesn't de-spawn. It can also be used on multiple horses, so Geralt could essentially "tame" an entire herd. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What is the name of Geralt's horse in The Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Geralt's horse in The Witcher series is named Roach. He names all his horses Roach, usually preferring mares. Roach is equipped with saddles, saddlebags, and blinders to aid Geralt's journeys. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does Geralt use Axii on horses in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Geralt uses the Axii sign to tame wild horses, making them permanently rideable unless they de-spawn. This allows him to ride horses other than his usual steed, Roach. Axii can be used on multiple horses, letting Geralt tame an entire herd if needed. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What factors determine a day's ride distance in The Witcher?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher, the distance a horse can travel in a day depends on its quality. Exceptional horses cover 35 to 50 miles, while average ones manage around 20 miles. Terrain, weather, and rider skill also affect the distance. In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Geralt can use Axii to tame wild horses, making them permanently rideable unless they de-spawn, offering flexibility in mount selection. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Which horse should you pick in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Geralt always names his horse Roach. Roach can be equipped with saddles, saddlebags, and blinders to improve performance in horse racing and combat. Saddles extend galloping time, saddlebags boost inventory, and blinders lower fear. Horse racing is a key activity where Geralt can bet and earn crowns. Roach is a dependable choice for these tasks. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How are horses used as transportation in The Witcher novels?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher novels, horses serve as the main form of transportation. Characters travel by riding horses or using horse-drawn carts and wagons. Distances are measured in 'day's rides,' typically 35 to 50 miles for top horses and about 20 miles for average ones. While magical portals exist, they are uncommon and used by powerful mages. 

Provided by: Fandom

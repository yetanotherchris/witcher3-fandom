**Devil by the Well** is a unique [noonwraith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith "Noonwraith") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that is found in the the abandoned village of [Hovel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Abandoned_Village_\(White_Orchard\) "Abandoned Village \(White Orchard\)"), [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard"). 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**How does the Devil by the Well haunt White Orchard?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, the Devil by the Well is a noonwraith haunting the abandoned village of Hovel in White Orchard. This specter, born from a young woman's tragic death, becomes immaterial and materializes to attack. It is vulnerable to Moon Dust, Specter oil, and the Yrden sign. Geralt feels sympathy for the noonwraith due to its sorrowful backstory. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What makes Devil by the Well unique in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Devil by the Well, a unique noonwraith in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, is found in Hovel village. It is the focus of the first contract quest, 'Contract: Devil by the Well.' This specter is susceptible to Moon Dust, Specter Oil, and the Yrden sign. It can become immaterial and materialize during attacks. Defeating it yields items like Amethyst dust and Noonwraith mutagen. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How can players defeat the Devil by the Well in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3, to defeat the Devil by the Well, players should investigate the well to learn about the Noonwraith. Use the Yrden sign to trap it, then attack with a silver sword and specter oil. The Noonwraith can become intangible, so timing and dodging are key. Completing this contract yields a gold reward. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the backstory of the Devil by the Well in Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In Witcher 3, the Devil by the Well is a Noonwraith haunting a well in a ruined village. This is the first contract in the game, set in White Orchard. The Noonwraith is the spirit of Claer, a woman who died tragically. Players investigate the area, especially an eastern hut, to find Claer's diary and skeleton. The spirit is tied to the well due to her untimely death and must be defeated to lift the curse. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why is the Devil by the Well significant in White Orchard?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The Devil by the Well is a key quest in White Orchard in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt. It involves a noonwraith haunting an abandoned village, resulting from a young woman's tragic death. Known as the Devil by the Well, this quest is the first contract Geralt undertakes, requiring him to lift the curse by understanding her backstory. This quest provides rewards and experience, highlighting its significance in the game. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## Bestiary entry

    _Wears a dirty dress, all rags, its skin flakin' off of its bones. And it howls... like it's sufferin'.  
–[Odolan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Odolan "Odolan") of White Orchard_
    _The residents of White Orchard have suffered more than their fair share of misfortunes. Passing armies had trampled their spring planting, a[griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)") had begun abducting their livestock, and, the rotten icing on this painful cake, a noonwraith haunted a nearby well. No wonder the locals called this final evil a 'devil'. The witcher could sense something bound her to this place, and that in order to send her off into ethereal realms he would first need to learn her secret._
    _Once that was done, he would have to prepare for battle - and a noonwraith is a demanding opponent. She can disorient her victim by blinding him and creating mirror images of herself. These copies slowly suck their victim's life energy - while their mistress remains untouchable. In order to defeat her, the witcher could not rely on his silver sword alone - the[Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") sign would also be needed to trap her and force her to take on material form._
    _Witchers don't normally feel a limited range of emotions regarding the monsters they slay. They despise the particularly cruel ones and are repulsed by the disgusting ones, but rare indeed is the monster for which they feel sympathy. Yet that is exactly what Geralt felt for the noonwraith of White Orchard. For a long time he was haunted by thoughts of the[young woman](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Claer "Claer") whose horrible death and powerful emotions had transformed her into that terrible monster._

# Erynia

**Erynias** are a species of [harpy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harpy "Harpy"). 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What distinguishes Erynias from other harpies in the Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Erynias stand out from other harpies due to their larger size, armor, and unique crystal bracelets. They exhibit swift, flying attacks and quick retreats, typically near cliffs and mountains. They are vulnerable to Grapeshot, Hybrid oil, and Aard. Erynias drop various loot, including the Erynia eye. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How can a player obtain a bestiary entry for Erynias in the Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

To acquire a bestiary entry for Erynias in The Witcher 3, players must either encounter these creatures during gameplay or learn about them via folktales and books. This information, encompassing their appearance and key traits, is subsequently logged in the bestiary, accessible through the Journal. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the connection between Erynias and the sorceress Lytta Neyd in the Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Lytta Neyd, known as Coral, compares erynias unfavorably to harpies. These creatures, found in Skellige, are deemed repulsive and dangerous by the sorceress due to their predilection for fresh meat, including humans. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the use of Erynias eye in the Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, the Erynia eye serves as an alchemy ingredient. It is essential in the crafting of Enhanced and Superior hybrid oils. Additionally, it can be dismantled to yield a monster eye and monster feather. This ingredient is a confirmed drop from erynias. 

Provided by: Fandom

### Bestiary entry

    _A merchant once told me I reminded him of an erynia. Naturally, I immediately[teleported](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Teleportation "Teleportation") him to one of their nests, so he could see for himself how ill-fitting a comparison it was.  
– [Lytta Neyd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lytta_Neyd "Lytta Neyd"), the sorceress also known as Coral_
    _Hard as it is to imagine, the erynias found in[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") are even more repulsive - and dangerous - than [harpies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harpy "Harpy"), their close relatives. Though well-rotten carrion is their food of choice, they will not turn up their noses at fresh meat, man flesh included._
    _When they spy a potential victim, erynias, like harpies or shishigas, will try to make full use of the strength of their numbers and their ability to control the skies. They will circle above their prey then attack from several directions at once, striking with razor-shark[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] talons and tearing their targets to shreds._
    _While attacking they aim for the neck, eyes and other vital organs, often causing their prey to bleed to death as a result. In this way a small flock of erynias is able to make quick work of larger and better-armed victims, who often are not able to defend themselves effectively from several opponents attacking at once._

### Combat tactics

Harpies, erynias, and all their varied sub-species, favor flying and hunting in packs. This could mean a handful of five or six individuals, upwards of twenty. Approach known nesting grounds with extreme caution. When provoked, harpies and erynias begin circling them from above, swooping alone or in groups of two or three. If they are grouped close enough during a dive, you can catch multiple harpies with area-of-effect [abilities](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_abilities "The Witcher 3 abilities") or [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_bombs "Category:The Witcher 3 bombs"). 

[![Tw3 fighting harpies](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/61/Tw3_fighting_harpies.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/280?cb=20160410190314)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/61/Tw3_fighting_harpies.png/revision/latest?cb=20160410190314) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_fighting_harpies.png)

Igni is effective against harpies, able to light them on fire and bring them out of the air. [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") is good for situations where a witcher is surrounded by many harpies in close proximity. Harpies and erynias often come to a hover in front of humanoid victims before lashing out with their talons. Use that moment to prepare a counterattack, or Aard to bring them to the ground. Like many flying creatures, a bolt from a [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow"), or a good blast of Aard, can be enough to ground a harpy or erynias. They can also be knocked out of the air with a well timed counter-attack. 

If a witcher is close to one of these grounded creatures, they should take advantage of this moment of weakness and execute them quickly, before they recover. If many individuals in a flock are converging around a witcher, they should use Aard to blast a path to freedom. 

[Grapeshot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Grapeshot "Grapeshot") can also be used, but one should be mindful of the blast radius. Erynias are more vicious than their harpy cousins, able to cause bleeding wounds that can overwhelm even a witcher.

# Drowned dead

**Drowned dead** (Polish: _Topielec_), is a more dangerous [drowner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner") variant. Like their cousins, they are found in and around bodies of [water](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water"). It's commonly believed that drowners and drowned dead are criminals who met their ends by drowning, but this is false. In actuality, they're [post-Conjunction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Conjunction_of_the_Spheres "Conjunction of the Spheres") [creatures](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Creature "Creature"), making them entirely separate entities from [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") and [non-humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Non-human "Non-human").

## Information

### Class

[Necrophage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage "Necrophage")

### Occurrence

The drowned dead are creatures of the night; they appear on the banks of ponds, lakes and rivers

### Immunity

Immune to [bleeding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bleeding "Bleeding"), [blinding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blinding "Blinding") and [poison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison") attempts; they are [fearless](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fear "Fear"), immune to [stun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stun "Stun") attempts and the [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") sign

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver") and susceptible to [knockdown](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knockdown "Knockdown") attempts; the [Fast Style](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fast_Style "Fast Style") is most efficient against the drowned dead; experienced witchers use the [Group Style](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Group_Style "Group Style") while fighting multiple drowned dead

### Tactics

These creatures poison their victims with [cadaverine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cadaverine "Cadaverine"); they often attack as a group

### Alchemy

[Cadaverine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cadaverine "Cadaverine")  
[Ginatz's Acid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ginatz%27s_Acid "Ginatz's Acid")  
[drowned dead tongue](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowned_dead_tongue "Drowned dead tongue")

### Notable drowned dead

  * [Nadir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nadir "Nadir"), a [trophy quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_trophy_quests "The Witcher trophy quests") in the [Outskirts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Outskirts "Outskirts") of [Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima").
  * [Zephyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zephyr "Zephyr"), a former thief featured in a secondary [quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Habits_Die_Hard "Old Habits Die Hard") in Chapter IV of _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_.

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") [Bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bestiary "The Witcher bestiary") entry

    _Particularly strong and dangerous drowners are known as the drowned dead. Simple people see no difference between the drowner and the drowned dead – encountering either of them is equally deadly. We might suppose, though, that the most gloomy legends concern the drowned dead rather than drowners._

### Notes

  * Their remains will not contain their [tongues](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowned_dead_tongue "Drowned dead tongue") until you have this bestiary entry in your journal.
  * An old townswoman in Chapter I will tell Geralt a tale of Drowned Dead in exchange for food. However, the conversation gives a journal entry for Drowners, not Drowned Dead.
  * A drowned dead has about twice the amount of hit points of a regular drowner; 50 instead of 25.

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

**Drowned dead** return in _The Witcher 2_ , however they are very rare and usually appear only at night near the [site of Sabrina Glevissig's execution](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Site_of_Sabrina_Glevissig%27s_execution "Site of Sabrina Glevissig's execution") or near the [bandit hideout](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bandit_hideout "Bandit hideout"). They are also classified as a subspecies of drowners. Drowned dead cease giving XP when killed from Level 15 onwards. 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Drowned dead** return in the third installment. They look largely the same in appearance with their counterparts in the second game and usually appear among five or more drowners. 

They are found in [Kaer Morhen valley](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen_valley "Kaer Morhen valley"), [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), and [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"). 

## _[The Witcher: Monster Slayer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher:_Monster_Slayer "The Witcher: Monster Slayer")_

In addition to the drowned dead, which is a rare monster, there is also its stronger, legendary variant - the **Water Devil**. It differs in appearance from the drowned dead by body coloring and fins on its head. Both monsters are vulnerable to steel and fire and are found in wetlands. 

## Trivia

  * The original name of the creature, _Topielec_ , is a Polish word for [kelpie](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kelpie "Kelpie").

**Therazane** is a powerful [earth elemental](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Earth_elemental "Earth elemental") which had been imprisoned by its previous owner in a [magical prison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Force_Field "Force Field"), located inside [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") ruins found just underneath [Moldavie Residence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moldavie_Residence "Moldavie Residence"). 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What powers does Therazane possess as an earth elemental?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Therazane, an earth elemental, is highly resilient and immune to fire attacks like Igni and mental manipulation such as Axii. It is vulnerable to Dimeritium bombs and Elementa oil. Therazane's powers include control over earth and stone, making it a formidable force. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of Therazane in the world of elementals?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Therazane, a powerful earth elemental in The Witcher universe, is imprisoned in a magical prison. It references the elemental lord of Earth in World of Warcraft, also named Therazane. In The Witcher 3, players face Therazane in the 'Contract: Doors Slamming Shut' quest, where they deactivate a barrier, defeat the elemental, and collect a trophy. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why was Therazane considered a threat to its previous owner?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Therazane, an earth elemental, posed a threat to its previous owner because of its significant power. To manage this threat, it was confined in a magical prison, indicating the owner's concern over Therazane's potential for destruction or dominance. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## Bestiary entry

    _I do not believe in[ghosts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghost "Ghost") \- it is that simple. But my men say the house shakes at night, walls crumble, why, even the floors move about...  
– [Kurt Dysart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kurt_Dysart "Kurt Dysart"), [Count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") of [Anchor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Anchor "Anchor")_
    _There's no such thing as a risk-free real estate investment. It might turn out your new home has a leaky roof, structural rot in the attic, a scratched floor in the living room... or elven ruins beneath its foundations, and within them an enormous earth elemental just waiting for a chance to break free of its chains._
    _Fighting such a monster is no easy task. Its tough outer husk deflects all but the strongest blows. It can liquefy a man's bones with one crushing blow from its fists - thus, one should never near it without first casting the[Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") Sign. One should have the [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") Sign at the ready as well, whereas the other signs - [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni"), [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") and [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") \- are completely ineffective against it._
    _In the end the[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") triumphed over the powerful earth elemental, and in doing so won gratitude of, and a reward from, Count Dysart._

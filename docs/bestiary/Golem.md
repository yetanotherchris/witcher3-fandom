**What is the weakness of a golem in the Witcher universe?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

A golem in the Witcher universe can be incapacitated by its creator or a significant electric shock. Its other weakness is acid, and a blade coated in acrid oil can be effective against it. When threatened, a golem may shield itself, leaving its back exposed for effective strikes. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does a Witcher defeat a golem?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

To defeat a golem, a Witcher employs combat tactics like dodging and striking. The Yrden sign is effective in slowing the golem, but caution is needed as the golem can still retaliate. Golems crouch and protect themselves when vulnerable, exposing their backs for a Witcher to strike effectively. A combination of a Whirl attack, Thunderbolt potion, and elementa oil can significantly damage a golem. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How is a golem animated by magic in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher universe, a golem is a magical entity made from stone or wood, animated by a mage. The animation involves summoning a being from the Plane of Fire. The golem's animation source is a magical obsidian heart, placed within its chest by the mage. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why are golem contracts considered frustrating jobs for witchers?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Golem contracts pose challenges for witchers due to the golem's physical properties and abilities. Golems, being magically animated solid rock, are impervious to regular sword attacks. Their superhuman strength allows them to stun and crush adversaries. The primary golem weakness is a significant electric shock, which can incapacitate or destroy them.

## Information

### Occurrence

A golem is a creature made of stone or wood, brought to life by a mage and animated with the use of magic

### Immunity

Its tough body is immune to sword blows and most [effects](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_critical_effects "The Witcher critical effects")

### Susceptibility

A golem can be stopped by its maker; usually the giant is set in motion by a lightning bolt, so a major electric shock might incapacitate or even annihilate it

### Tactics

A golem uses its superhuman strength to [knock down](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knockdown "Knockdown"), [stun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stun "Stun") and crush opponents

### Alchemy

[Golem's obsidian heart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Golem%27s_obsidian_heart "Golem's obsidian heart")

Frustrating jobs happen in every profession. **Golem** contracts are just such a job for [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"), as they involve slashing through solid rock animated by [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"). Luckily, what is brought to life by a lightning bolt can very well be destroyed by one. This is something we all truly wish the golem, from the very bottom of our hearts. 

In the game, Geralt finds an old golem cemetery in the [swamp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swamp "Swamp") with four or five of the beasts "frozen" in place. As it happens, he must re-animate and then kill one of them to complete the main quest in Chapter II.

## [Monsterbook](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monsterbook "Monsterbook")

[![The earth-toned golem, placed in the swamp, resembles an unfinished sculpture](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Golem_ConceptArt.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20081205143625)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Golem_ConceptArt.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20081205143625) [![The earth-toned golem, placed in the swamp, resembles an unfinished sculpture](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Golem_ConceptArt.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20081205143625)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Golem_ConceptArt.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20081205143625) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Golem_ConceptArt.jpg)

The earth-toned golem, placed in the swamp, resembles an unfinished sculpture

_Developer[CD PROJEKT RED](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/CD_PROJEKT_RED "CD PROJEKT RED")'s characterization of the golem taken from the monsterbook, which was enclosed with the Collectors Edition of the computer game _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)") _for Poland, Hungary, the Czech Republic and Slovakia:_

The golem is often portrayed as a clumsy, faceless figure made of clay or as a perfect effigy of a man. Neither of these ideas was suitable to the world of The Witcher. Ultimately, our golem became a piece of roughly hewn rock animated by a magical obsidian heart placed inside the beast's chest by its maker. The mage who created it was no sculptor; he cared little for aesthetics and focused on functionality. This creature was meant to work and defend its master's property. And the golem performs these tasks (especially the latter) perfectly, with ruthless efficiency. 

The hero encounters the golem in the swamp forest, near the Golem Burial Ground. The creatures stands still, frozen in mid-step, having expended all the power which animated it. Geralt's task is to bring the creature to life, which he does by summoning a storm with the help of friendly druids. We obviously drew the concept of a thunderbolt giving life to inanimate matter from the story of Doctor Frankenstein's monster. Having Geralt yell _"It's alive!"_ was too tempting to pass up. The golem is thus inspired both by the legend of a rabbi from Prague who created an artificial human being and by Mary Shelley's novel. This combination fits our world of grim and gritty fantasy.

### Combat tactics

Do not attempt to parry the punches delivered by a golem. The sheer weight and power behind them renders such thinking suicidal at best. A charging golem cannot be easily stopped, but a clever witcher can use this to their advantage. Roll out of the golem's path, then strike. 

Yrden is useful when going toe-to-toe with a golem, but remember that the golem is merely slowed, not helpless. They can still fight despite [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden")'s effects, and can still interrupt a witcher's swordplay with a single crushing blow. 

Like other elementals, a golem can pound the ground with such ferocity, anyone standing near it is knocked prone. Also similarly, when golems feel vulnerable, they may crouch and protect themselves with their arms, becoming invulnerable to frontal blows. However, that also exposes their back, allowing a witcher to freely strike at it with great effect. 

Surprisingly enough, a fully developed [Whirl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Whirl "Whirl") attack combined with the effect of [Thunderbolt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thunderbolt "Thunderbolt") potion and [elementa oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elementa_oil "Elementa oil") can in fact devastate a golem, as the creature's size prevents it from evading Geralt's attack, and its vulnerability to the knock-back effect mean that once caught inside the whirling storm of silver blade, it cannot back out or retaliate as long as Geralt has the stamina to spare.

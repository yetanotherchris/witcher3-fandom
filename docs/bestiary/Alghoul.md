# Alghoul

**Alghouls** are a sub-species of [necrophage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage "Necrophage"), and the biological cousins of [ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul"), distinguished by their darker visage, larger size and strength, colourings, and intelligence. After the [Northern Wars](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Wars "Northern Wars"), their numbers spread rapidly through the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"), due to lack of control and widespread famine which attracted the corpse-eaters.[1]

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What distinguishes an Alghoul from a regular ghoul in terms of physical characteristics?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Alghouls, a sub-species of necrophage, are biologically related to ghouls. They are identifiable by their darker color, larger size, and greater strength. The key distinction lies in their intelligence, as alghouls, unlike ghouls and graveirs, can plan and strategize, making them a greater threat. They typically lead ghoul packs, posing a danger to lone travelers, caravans, and farmsteads. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What caused the rapid spread of Alghouls through the Northern Kingdoms?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The swift proliferation of Alghouls in the Northern Kingdoms resulted from the aftermath of the Northern Wars. The ensuing famine and lack of control attracted these necrophages, leading to their rapid spread. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What makes human flesh irresistible to Alghouls?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Alghouls develop an insatiable craving for human flesh after years of consuming corpses. This makes human flesh irresistible to them, leading them to prey on the living. They are often found in areas with fresh bodies like cemeteries and battlefields. Alghouls also exhibit greater intelligence than ghouls, capable of planning and leading ghoul packs. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does an Alghoul's diet evolve over time?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

As a sub-species of necrophage, Alghouls are primarily corpse-eaters. Their diet evolves in response to the availability of corpses in their environment. For instance, their population surged after the Northern Wars, when widespread famine led to an increase in available corpses. 

Provided by: Fandom

## Information

### Class

[Necrophage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage "Necrophage")

### Occurrence

Alghouls appear in old necropolises and crypts.

### Immunity

Immune to common [poisons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison") and resistant to [knockdown](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knockdown "Knockdown") attempts.

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver"); the [Strong Style](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Strong_Style "Strong Style") is most efficient against alghouls; experienced witchers use the [Group Style](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Group_Style "Group Style") while fighting multiple ghouls.

### Tactics

Alghouls try to knock down their victims and eat them alive.

### Alchemy

[Cadaverine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cadaverine "Cadaverine")  
[white vinegar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_vinegar "White vinegar")  
[alghoul marrow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alghoul_marrow "Alghoul marrow")

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") [Bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bestiary "The Witcher bestiary") entry

    _Alghouls are ghouls which had been devouring corpses for so many years that human flesh becomes irresistible and they begin to prey on the living. They are seen in crypts and on battlefields, frequently surrounded by ghouls. Simple folk do not notice the differences between these two types of scavengers - unlike[witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"), who know that the alghoul is a more aggressive and challenging opponent._

### Bestiary entry

    _An alghoul's basically a badarse ghoul  
– [Yarpen Zigrin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yarpen_Zigrin "Yarpen Zigrin"), dwarven warrior_
    _Alghouls differ from normal ghouls in size, strength, coloring and, most importantly, intelligence. Whereas ghouls and graveirs are primitive creatures unfit to plan even the simplest ambush, alghouls and their kindred (such as[cemetaurs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cemetaur "Cemetaur")) are capable of forethought, and are thus much more dangerous._
    _Ghouls seem to possess wits enough to at least know a brighter mind when they see it, and so let alghouls and cemetaurs lead their packs. A pack so led will terrorize all in its path, attacking not only lone travelers but also caravans and even farmsteads. When encountering such a pack the alghoul should be eliminated as a first priority, leaving the other beasts for once their leader is gone. One be particularly careful when fighting alghouls around dusk and at night, when they fight with doubled strength._
    _During combat alghouls and cemetaurs try risky maneuvers aimed at knocking their opponents to the ground so the others can finish the job by tearing them to shreds. Like a normal ghoul, an injured alghoul can fall into a frenzy and attack with blind fury. An experienced witcher knows to get out of its way on such occasions and strike from behind, while for an inexperienced witcher, such a turn of events often marks the end of his Path._

### Combat tactics

[![Tw3 killing alghouls](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b9/Tw3_killing_alghouls.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/221?cb=20160502061259)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b9/Tw3_killing_alghouls.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160502061259) [![Tw3 killing alghouls](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b9/Tw3_killing_alghouls.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/221?cb=20160502061259)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/b/b9/Tw3_killing_alghouls.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160502061259) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_killing_alghouls.jpg)

Alghouls are smarter and more powerful than [ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul"), making them the greatest threat in a ghoul pack. An otherwise coordinated pack becomes less so once the alghoul has been dealt with. [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") forces the spines on an alghoul to retract after a few seconds, with the added benefit of temporarily stunning the creature. 

An alghoul can emit a piercing scream to stun nearby victims, as well as send nearby ghouls into a raging frenzy. When enraged, an alghoul grows spines on its back. This makes attacking it a very dangerous proposition, as the spines are harmful if you use melee attacks.

## Trivia

  * The name of the alghoul could be a reference of the star [Algol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algol) in the [Perseus constellation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perseus_\(constellation\)), also called the _"Demon Star"_. _Algol_ derives from the Arabic _"raʾs al-ghūl"_ , meaning "head of the ghoul" (meaning "head of the demon" or "head of the ogre" in Western translations). In Greek Mythology, the star represents the head of the [gorgon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gorgon "Gorgon") Medusa in the hand of the hero Perseus.
  * According to [John of Brugge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/John_of_Brugge "John of Brugge"), alghouls are compared to the "panthera tigris that in [Zerrikania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikania "Zerrikania") dwells". _Panthera tigris_ is the binomial nomenclature for the tiger.[1]

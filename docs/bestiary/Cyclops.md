**Cyclops** is an [ogroid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ogroid "Ogroid") creature in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

A [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entry can technically be obtained by reading [An Ancient Ballad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Ancient_Ballad "An Ancient Ballad"), which however never appears during gameplay.

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Cyclopses are ogroid creatures found in Kaer Morhen, Skellige, Velen, and Toussaint. They yield unique loot like Cyclops' eye, monster blood, and old bear hide. Susceptible to Ogroid oil, Axii, and Quen, their bestiary entry references Homer's Odyssey. They charge at opponents, delivering unblockable punches and slaps.

**What is the tragic story of Odess Thaka with a Cyclops in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, traveler Odess Thaka met a tragic end on Spikeroog due to a Cyclops. Thaka's plan involved killing the Cyclops by driving a sharpened stake into its single eye and escaping the cave disguised as sheep. However, Thaka's plan failed, leading to his tragic death, likely caused by the Cyclops, an ogroid creature known for its size, strength, and hatred for humans. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of the single eye of a Cyclops in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The Cyclops' single eye in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt serves as an alchemy ingredient. It can be dismantled into a monster eye and monster blood, although it doesn't craft anything directly. The eye is a high-probability drop from cyclopses and the Cloud Giant. The monster eye, derived from the Cyclops' eye, is essential for crafting various Witcher gear such as Feline boots, Griffin armor, and Wolven steel sword. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Is there a connection between Cyclops and the ancient ballad in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Indeed, Cyclops and 'An Ancient Ballad' in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt are connected. The ballad offers bestiary entries for cyclopses and ice giants. However, it's unobtainable during gameplay due to a bug, only accessible via console commands. The Cyclops' bestiary entry in the game references Homer's Odyssey.

## Bestiary entry

    _How about this... we take a big stake, sharpen its tip, jam it in the cyclops' eye – then sneak out of the cave disguised as sheep. How's that not a good idea?  
– Odess Thaka, traveler. Died tragically on [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog")_
    _Cyclopses can easily be recognized by the single eye located in the center of their foreheads. If for some reason that is not visible, other tell-tale signs are their enormous size, incredible strength and a seething hatred for all humans._
    _**Unobtainable without reading the book:**_

    _Evidence suggests cyclopses can live for an incredibly long time. The one well-preserved specimen whom scholars have examined and documented – a body found in 1112 and taken to[Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt") specialists – was over three hundred years old at the time of its death. The remains of this specimen could until recently be seen in the [Academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Academy "Oxenfurt Academy")'s zoological museum, but they were removed from display after students tried to dress the cyclops in a toga, damaging it significantly in the process._
    _As in the case of[giants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Giant "Giant"), one can only guess at the strong and weak points of a cyclops. Its heft alone precludes effective use of the [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") [Sign](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign"), while its enormous physical strength would make its blows particularly deadly. Cyclopses are most likely vulnerable to [ogroid oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ogroid_oil "Ogroid oil"). As [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") with relatively well-developed minds, they might also fall under the influence to the [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") Sign._

## Combat tactics

Cyclopses go where they want; even a witcher should move to the side if a cyclops is charging forward. The strongest witchers cannot parry or block any punch or slap delivered by a cyclops, and a mere hop backwards may not be enough to stay out of reach. Roll to the side or behind a cyclops to evade their attacks and reach their vulnerable flanks. 

Do not stand in one place when fighting a cyclops. They rarely flinch from anything a witcher can do to them, so they keep trying to fight no matter how many cuts from a silver blade they take. If a cyclops raises both hands in the air, avoid being anywhere close to the front of it. The resulting ground-pound flattens even the toughest warriors. 

If you're at a longer range Cyclopses can also leap into the air, knocking over anyone or anything too close to where they land. Alternatively they may do a "wild rush" charging at you swinging their arms madly while roaring; this in particular is almost impossible to avoid as the Cyclops _will_ change its direction mid-charge to try to intercept you if you try rolling out of the way. Axii can temporarily confuse a cyclops, giving the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") time to recuperate while the creature rages at something else. Getting close to a cyclops is dangerous, but still a necessity for slaying one. The [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") Sign can help witchers do more work every time they get within sword range of these monsters. Overall, [Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") is probably your best choice for handling them.

## _[The Witcher: Monster Slayer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher:_Monster_Slayer "The Witcher: Monster Slayer")_

Aside from the standard cyclops, there are 2 other variations of the monster: **Farbaut** and **Grimnir**. They differ only in appearance and difficulty level. Farbaut's head is covered by a cloth, while Grimnir wears a metal helmet. The cyclops is a common monster, Farbaut is rare and Grimnir is a legendary one. According to the [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_Monster_Slayer_bestiary "The Witcher Monster Slayer bestiary"), Grimnir is rather intelligent for a cyclops. All three kinds of the cyclops are vulnerable to fire and steel.

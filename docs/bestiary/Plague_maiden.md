**Plague maiden** , also called **Pesta** , is a spirit personifying disease and pestilence. Plague maiden will be added to the Bestiary by reading [Of Sweat and Blood](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Of_Sweat_and_Blood "Of Sweat and Blood"). 

### Quick Answers

**What is the Plague maiden also known as?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Also known as Pesta, the Plague maiden is a spectral entity embodying disease and pestilence. This character originates from European folklore, typically portrayed as a pale woman in white, traversing through plague-ridden towns and villages, resulting in death and devastation.

**What symptoms do patients experience when they hallucinate the Plague maiden?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Hallucinations of the Plague maiden, or pesta, involve a woman with scabs and boils, surrounded by rats. These visions cease with henbane and poppy extract treatment. The Plague maiden is depicted as a rotting woman trailed by rats, associated with causing or being attracted to plague and suffering. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What treatments are used to alleviate the hallucinations of the Plague maiden?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The hallucinations associated with the Plague maiden, also known as pesta, are treated with henbane and poppy extract. This method has been effective in reducing the vivid visions of a diseased woman surrounded by rats, as recorded in Joachim von Gratz's notes from Vilmerius Hospital in Novigrad. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What triggers the appearance of the Plague maiden spirit?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The Plague maiden, also referred to as Pesta, manifests during times of severe epidemic. This spirit, depicted as a diseased woman with decaying flesh and often followed by rats, is thought to either cause or be attracted to the pox. The specific cause of its manifestation is unclear, but theories suggest it could be due to the intense emotional energy linked to certain death scenarios, like a death following a prolonged, painful illness. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## Bestiary entry

    _Patients seem to have hallucinations of a woman covered in scabs and boils, with[rats](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rat "Rat") scurrying about all around her. These ravings subsided after an administration of henbane and poppy extract.  
– [Joachim von Gratz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Joachim_von_Gratz "Joachim von Gratz")'s notes, [Vilmerius Hospital](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilmerius_Hospital "Vilmerius Hospital") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad")_
    _When[plague](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Catriona_plague "Catriona plague") ravages a region, a spirit will sometimes walk its lands, a [ghost](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghost "Ghost") resembling an ill woman whose flesh rots off her bones and in whose wake crawls a cavalcade of rats. No one knows whether this spirit brings the pox with her or is merely drawn to it like a moth to a light. Yet it is certain that she delights in dealing pain and suffering, in hearing the howling and moaning of men._
    _Many have called into question the very existence of plague maidens, or pestae, as they are sometimes called. Only two sightings of such a creature have ever been recorded, both during times of raging epidemic._
    _As the name "plague maiden" suggests, these wraiths take the appearance of females, though exactly why that is remains a mystery. Some speculate they, like other such[specters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter "Specter"), arise from the powerful emotional charge associated with certain circumstances of death, such as death preceded by a long and particularly painful illness._
    _Not much is known about how to fight a plague maiden, though one can assume they possess many traits in common with other phantoms and[wraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith "Wraith"). They undoubtedly pose a great danger, though a [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher")'s immunities should at least prevent him from catching the contagious illnesses they carry._

## Combat tactics

Also known as a pesta, a plague maiden is one of the more insidious wraiths a witcher may come across. When provoked, a pesta calls forth clouds of plague-ridden insects that seek out their foes. The insect clouds can be destroyed with [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni") or a slice of a [silver sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword"). [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") can push the clouds back. 

Plague maidens have fewer overt weaknesses compared to other wraiths, but are still vulnerable to [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden"). This can help keep the maiden still while you strike with a silver sword, but it also means you will be swarmed by the plague clouds.

## Behind the scenes

[![Tw3 concept art Plague Maiden artbook](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/97/Tw3_concept_art_Plague_Maiden_artbook.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/170?cb=20160514162201)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/97/Tw3_concept_art_Plague_Maiden_artbook.png/revision/latest?cb=20160514162201) [![Tw3 concept art Plague Maiden artbook](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/97/Tw3_concept_art_Plague_Maiden_artbook.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/170?cb=20160514162201)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/97/Tw3_concept_art_Plague_Maiden_artbook.png/revision/latest?cb=20160514162201) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_concept_art_Plague_Maiden_artbook.png)

The plague maiden, a spirit personifying disease and pestilence, also has roots in European folklore. In those legends she is most often depicted as a deathly pale woman dressed in a simple white peasant dress, who walks across plague-stricken towns and villages leaving dead, corpse-strewn streets in her wake. The name Pesta is from Norwegian folklore, where Pesta was a personification of the Black Death plague. She was commonly said to be a withered old woman carrying either a rake or a broom. If she used the rake, some in the household would survive. If she used the broom, there would be no mercy. 

The concept drawing created for our game shows the plague maiden in her true, more horrific form. Her rotten, worm-ridden flesh is covered in a ragged shroud seemingly woven from miasma and foul vapors. Crawling out from under it are dozens of plague-bearing [rats](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rat "Rat").[1]

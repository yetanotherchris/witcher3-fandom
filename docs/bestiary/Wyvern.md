# Wyvern

In a grossly small cage, curled up in a ball, lay a lizard covered in dark, peculiarly adorned scales. When the beaked one struck the cage with his rod, the reptile thrashed about, running its scales across the bars. It stretched its long neck and emitted a piercing hiss, demonstrating sharp, white, conical teeth that contrasted starkly with the nearly black scales around its maw.

pg.75 _[Time of Contempt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Time_of_Contempt "Time of Contempt")_ (U.K. Edition)

**Wyverns** ([Elder Speech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Speech "Elder Speech"): _Vyverne_) are an endangered species of [ornithosaur](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ornithosaur "Ornithosaur") with snake-like necks and long tails that end in a venomous trident, adorned almost fully in dark scales. Their menacing teeth allow the creature to swoop down from the sky, easily snatch their prey and carry it off to their nest, not caring if it is a [sheep](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sheep "Sheep") or a [man](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human").[1]

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What makes the Wyvern species endangered in their universe?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The endangerment of the Wyvern species, or Vyverne in Elder Speech, is attributed to their dangerous nature. These dark-scaled ornithosaurs possess snake-like necks and venomous trident-tipped tails. Their teeth enable them to snatch prey from the ground and transport it to their nests. The royal wyverns are notably deadly, with a toxic stinger on their tails that inflicts intense pain, even to witchers. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of the Wyvern's venomous trident tail?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The Wyvern's venomous trident tail is a crucial part of its combat strategy, equipped with a stinger that delivers potent toxins. It uses this tail to strike its prey, often diverting attention with its tooth-filled beak before landing a venomous blow. The tail's bright colors suggest its venomous nature, and its aggressive use in combat makes it a lethal weapon. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Where can Wyverns be first encountered in the game?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the game, the first encounter with Wyverns occurs in Chapter II. Their initial location is restricted to Wyvern Island, situated in the northern section of the swamp. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What kind of prey do Wyverns typically snatch and carry off to their nests?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Wyverns, an endangered species of ornithosaur, use their menacing teeth to swoop down from the sky and snatch prey, carrying it off to their nests. They are indiscriminate in their choice of prey, targeting both sheep and humans alike. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the role of Wyvern Island in the game?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In 'The Witcher', Wyvern Island is a notable location in the north-central swamp forest outside Vizima. The island, located between the druids' grove and the mage's tower, is home to a colony of wyverns. These creatures, appearing from Chapter II, are resistant to stun and poison but vulnerable to silver and Ornithosaur Oil. They employ a combat strategy of diving at opponents and using venom.

### Class

[Ornithosaur](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ornithosaur "Ornithosaur")

### Occurrence

Wyverns live in the wilderness or near human settlements; they are a natural element of an ecosystem; they are prized by [druids](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid"), who tame them

Resistant to [stun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stun "Stun") and [poison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison") attempts

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver") and [Ornithosaur Oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ornithosaur_Oil "Ornithosaur Oil")

    _A wyvern will usually attack from the air, aiming to knock its prey down with its wings. After landing it will attack with its tooth-filled beak, trying to draw attention away from its most dangerous weapon: the stinger found at the end of its long tail. A strike from this carries the risk of poisoning from powerful toxins. Even a[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher")'s body experiences wrenching pain when injected with wyvern venom, and so it is worth preparing oneself with suitable potions ahead of time._

### Combat tactics

Wyverns are quite venomous, they are able to spit their venom from a distance and even inject it directly into its victim through the stinger on the end of its long tail, the use of [Golden Oriole](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Golden_Oriole "Golden Oriole") can prevent it from incapacitating you. They will fly around their prey, well beyond [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow") range, before they will swoop down at break neck speeds in an attempt to claw at their victim. 

[Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") and [Grapeshot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Grapeshot "Grapeshot") can bring a wyvern to the ground, though they are tough enough to be resistant to crossbow bolts, but other more lethal munitions can force it to the ground. 

Once on the ground a wyvern will prefer to strike with the talons on its feet, its teeth and its tail. It is possible to parry the bites, but if unsuccessful the consequences can be deadly. The don't appear to be very good at dodging while on the ground; an unending series of Fast attacks has been seen to keep them off balance so they don't attack right to the point that they die.

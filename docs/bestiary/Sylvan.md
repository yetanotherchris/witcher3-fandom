**Sylvans** , called **willowers** or **pucks** ,[N 1] and labeled superstitiously as **devils** ([archaic Common](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Speech "Common Speech"): _deovel_ ; [Elder Speech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Speech "Elder Speech"): _d'yaebl_ ; [Dwarvish](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarvish "Dwarvish"): _duvvel_), are an exceptionally rare, intelligent [race](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Race "Race") native to the [woodlands](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forest "Forest") of the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). They are distinguished by prominent [goat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Goat "Goat")-like visage and delight in pranks, feasting, and music. 

Sporadically, sylvan may also be referred to as a "**faun** ".[N 2]

## Characteristics

### Physical description

Sylvans resemble a [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") with goat's horns, soft lips with yellow, [horse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse")-like teeth, and a goatee. Their lower body features a tasseled tail and hairy legs with cloven hooves. The horns earned sylvans the label of "devils", which they dislike,[1] and occasionally cause confusion with the beastly [chorts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chort "Chort") for humans who have never seen the latter.[2] Their voice resembles goat's bleating.[1] Their life-spans reached up to more than 100 years.[2]

Though miles away apart in behavior, sylvans are related to the [Aen Seidhe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Seidhe "Aen Seidhe") with whom they share the blood of the [Elder Race](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Races "Elder Races").[4] It's therefore unsurprising that many sylvans are quite familiar with [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") defenses[5] and that the two peoples have no difficulty to cooperate.[1][2] Some scholars include sylvans among the so-called sapient monsters.[6]

### Mental traits

[![a sylvan with reed pipes](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Gra_Wyobrazni_sylvan.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20160505182835)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Gra_Wyobrazni_sylvan.png/revision/latest?cb=20160505182835) [![a sylvan with reed pipes](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Gra_Wyobrazni_sylvan.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20160505182835)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Gra_Wyobrazni_sylvan.png/revision/latest?cb=20160505182835) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gra_Wyobrazni_sylvan.png)

a sylvan with reed pipes

Most sylvans are merry, enjoying tricks and riddles more than anything else. They also love music and are quite apt at playing reed pipes. Mischief aside, they can be helpful by fertilizing fields, scaring birds away, voluntarily watching over turnips, and eating caterpillars. Their diet is largely herbivorous.[1] However, they can also become very comfortable and spend days in idleness, puffing on pipes, overeating, and downing stiff drinks.[2]

As for magical prowess, many sylvans can learn [mental spells](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mind "Mind").[4] Others, it seems, found a way to breathe [fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire "Fire") in combat.[2]

### Procreation

[Dryads](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dryad "Dryad"), with whom they live in [Brokilon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brokilon "Brokilon"),[7] value sylvans as lovers guaranteeing a vigorous daughter. In theory, sylvans may procreate with elves and humans but this doesn't occur.[4] Indeed, fauns bedding [nymphs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nymph "Nymph") are a popular motif in art[1] and sculpture.[8]

    _**Unobtainable without reading the book:**_

    _Sylvans and yakshas, resemble men not just in appearance, but also in behavior – though it must be said they have adopted more of our vices and weaknesses than strengths. Lazy by nature, they would gladly spend every day in idleness, puffing on pipes, gorging on rich foods and downing strong drinks._
    _Since sylvans and yakshas enjoy partaking of the fruits of others' labors without asking permission, men do not take to them kindly and at times, fooled by their awkward appearance, will try to solve this problem using force. This often ends in tragedy, for sylvans, despite their corpulent frames, are astonishingly strong and agile. They also emit a powerful and extremely unpleasant scent which can cause even a[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"), whose line of work accustoms him to foul stenches, to gag and feel faint. Watch out in particular for yakshas, which are fiercer and keener on fighting than normal sylvan._

### Combat tactics

Melee combat against a sylvan is difficult, even for a witcher. One has to evade the physical strikes from a sylvan, not attempt to parry or counterattack. [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") can gain time to bring a silver sword into play, but a sylvan can counter this by breathing intense flames that melt through the best armor. If close-quarters combat isn't going well, one should be prepared to make use of long-ranged options. A crossbow loaded with good bolts can keep the witcher relatively safe, whilst being effective. 

## _[The Witcher: Monster Slayer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher:_Monster_Slayer "The Witcher: Monster Slayer")_

There are four types of the monster in the game: Sylvan, Sylvan Dearg, Harrower, and Mulcher. They differ with strength level and appearance. Sylvan drops [powdered monster tissue](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_Monster_Slayer_ingredients "The Witcher Monster Slayer ingredients"), while the other 3 drop [relict remains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_Monster_Slayer_ingredients "The Witcher Monster Slayer ingredients"). They are all vulnerable to strong attacks and steel. 

### Sylvan

It is a mid level common monster, and it is virtually the same as its _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ counterpart, when it comes to appearance. 

### Sylvan Dearg

It is a rare monster, slightly stronger than a common Sylvan. 

### Harrower and Mulcher

They are both legendary monsters, appearing only during [Lammas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lammas "Lammas"). According to the [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_Monster_Slayer_bestiary "The Witcher Monster Slayer bestiary"), Harrower is said to deliver exteremly strong blows and Mulcher is know to scatter bones around fields.

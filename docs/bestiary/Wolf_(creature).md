# Wolf (creature)

[![Gwent cardart monsters wolf](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/30/Gwent_cardart_monsters_wolf.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180529205314)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/30/Gwent_cardart_monsters_wolf.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20180529205314) [![Gwent cardart monsters wolf](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/30/Gwent_cardart_monsters_wolf.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/250?cb=20180529205314)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/30/Gwent_cardart_monsters_wolf.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20180529205314) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gwent_cardart_monsters_wolf.jpg)

**Wolves** are an aggressive and carnivorous species of wild animal, related to the domesticated [dogs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dog "Dog") found in [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") settlements. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**How are wolves depicted in The Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher series, wolves are portrayed as aggressive, carnivorous creatures related to dogs. They appear in various locations such as swamps and fields. In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, they are included in the bestiary, offering insights into their combat tactics and related quests. Wolves are a significant part of the game's wildlife. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What role do wolves play in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, wolves are aggressive creatures found in swamps, caves, and fields. They are related to dogs but remain wild and dangerous. Wolves lack specific immunities and are part of the game's bestiary, featuring in several quests. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why are wolves hunted by the king's foresters?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Wolves are hunted by the king's foresters because they threaten livestock and can attack horses during harsh winters. Known for their intelligence and independence, these creatures are seen as enemies due to their predatory nature. In royal forests, hunting is restricted to the nobility. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How are witchers connected to wolves?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Witchers are linked to wolves through the Wolf School, a prominent witcher school. The Wolf School trains witchers skilled in monster hunting, with the wolf symbol representing their cunning, strength, and teamwork. Wolves, as creatures, are aggressive and often hunt in packs, reflecting the traits valued by the school. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What makes wolves a threat in The Witcher universe?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher universe, wolves are a threat because of their aggressive nature and pack tactics. They attack in groups, leveraging their numbers to overpower victims. Notably dangerous are wargs and the snow-white wolves of Skellige. Although lacking magical powers, they can still be lethal to travelers and hunters. Witchers combat them using the Fast or Group Style, exploiting their susceptibility to knockdown attempts. 

Provided by: Fandom

### Combat tactics

Wolves hunt in packs and coordinate with one another in order to surround their choice of prey. Beyond this group coordination, wolves are similar to dogs in the manner in which they attack, where occasionally the leader will be a warg as opposed. 

There are multiple schools of thought about engaging a pack of wolves. On one hand, you can parry all of their attacks as long as you hold down the parry button, and can counterattack with the proper timing. On the other, they'll happily attack so frequently and from so many directions that you won't have time to counterattack. Creating openings to halt their momentum is the key to efficiently defeating them, and your Signs are the best tools to make that happen. Which sign is the most effective is up for debate. 

You can choose [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") to slow down attacking wolves, which makes counterattacks easier to time and dodging and punishing more likely to succeed before being interrupted. The downside to this strategy is that it requires you to hold your ground, as getting forced out of your protective circle renders the sign useless. This can be done by simply parrying their attacks, but the more time you spend defending, the less time you'll spend attacking and making use of the slowdown. 

If one finds themselves surrounded, you can use [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni") and [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") to try and drive the pack off, even if just for a moment, which allows the you to recover some of your stamina and/or regain control over the fight. Wolves tend to have low resistance, which means they'll often catch fire or be knocked all the way to the ground by your Signs. With a few of the wolves momentarily disabled, you'll be able to either target their few remaining buddies, or strike a devastating blow to a downed wolf that can't defend itself. The downside to this tactic is that if enough wolves happen to resist burning or knockdown, or you fail to strike enough of them at once, you'll find yourself still overwhelmed by attacks and now unable to cast a Sign. 

Leshens are able to command entire packs of wolves to aid them in battle. The wolves that are summoned by a leshen must be fought with [silver swords](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword"). The wolves will be struck and killed by the leshen's own attacks, so if you see the leshen preparing to use the tree roots against you, try to roll in a direction that kites the wolves to the attack.

# Korred

**Korreds** , also known as korrigans, rutterkins, rumpelsticks, or mesmers, are creatures described as being from the family _Strigiformes_ , which would suggest they are owl-like. However, in the novels they are described more as "family monsters" typically found in basements, where they hibernate. 

They are described in the _"[Physiologus](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Physiologus "Physiologus")"_. [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") meets one named [Schweitzer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Schweitzer "Schweitzer") in the basement of the [Pomerol](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pomerol "Pomerol") estate in [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint").

Korreds were planned for The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt during its 2013-2014 development but were removed before release. These owl-like, human-sized creatures are intelligent and vicious, often found in caves or basements, using magical attacks from afar.

Korreds, also called korrigans or mesmers, are linked to the family Strigiformes, implying an owl-like nature. However, they are portrayed as "family monsters" that hibernate in basements. Geralt meets a korred named Schweitzer in the Pomerol estate's basement in Toussaint.

Korreds, referred to as korrigans or rutterkins, appear in the 'Physiologus' as creatures from the Strigiformes family, hinting at owl-like traits. Yet, they are portrayed as 'family monsters' that hibernate in basements. In the Witcher series, Geralt meets a korred named Schweitzer in the Pomerol estate's basement in Toussaint.

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Korreds** were supposed to appear in the third game during its development in 2013 - 2014 but were removed prior to the game's release.[1]

### Information from leaked materials

    _Owl-like monster of the size of human. Lives in caves or basements. It's an intelligent monster, but quite vicious.  
Usually occurs alone, it's a strong opponent._

  * _Role in the group: Individual_
  * _Preferred behavior: ranged - uses magical attacks_
  * _Special attacks and actions:_
    * _Strong attacks - his attacks are slow paced but deal high damage._
    * _Jump to the ceiling - Korred can jump to the ceiling of a cave (when fighting in a cave or cellar) and then fall down on his opponent, dealing damage. Player can dodge or use[Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") to protect himself. Korred uses this ability to deal damage to opponents blocked by his stone blockades._
    * _Spawn blockades - Korred can spawn magical stone blockades from the floor of a cave or basement and block movement of his opponent. Player can destroy a blockade by casting[Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") two times on it._
    * _Jump - the monster can jump to different places on the location. The maximum distance of a jump is 10 meters. Korred uses this ability to jump out from blocked areas._
  * _Defence:_
    * _Dodge - Korred can dodge strong attacks._
  * _Vital points:_
    * _Instant kill:_
      * _Piercing both lungs - causes the creature to suffocate and die slowly (cost: 3 points for each lung)._
    * _Protection: none_
    * _Critical effect/disable ability:_
      * _Cutting ligaments - disables the Jump to the ceiling, Jump and Dodge abilities (cost: 3 points)._
    * _Massive damage:_
      * _Piercing stomach - deals high damage (cost: 2 points)._
      * _Piercing a lung - deals high damage (cost 3 points each lung)_
  * _Immune to: Silver_
  * _Vulnerable to: Steel_

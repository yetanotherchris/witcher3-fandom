# Noonwraith

**Noonwraith** — These monsters appear in fields when the sun is at its highest. Swaying grains on a windless day announce their arrival. They dance in circles in the light of day and draw farmers in to join them. Since they are [ghosts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghost "Ghost"), no one who joins them leaves the circle alive.

## Information

### Class

[Specter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter "Specter")

### Occurrence

Noonwraiths haunt cultivated fields and meadows; they always appear when the sun is high; they are specters but at the same time they maintain a strong connection to the natural world, they see the living but cannot understand them, because the dead cannot hear the living

### Immunity

Immune to [blinding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blinding "Blinding"), [poison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poison "Poison"), [pain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pain "Pain") and [bleeding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bleeding "Bleeding") attempt; they are [fearless](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fear "Fear")

### Susceptibility

[Sensitive to silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sensitive_to_silver "Sensitive to silver") and [Specter Oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter_Oil "Specter Oil")

### Tactics

They are able to grasp sunrays and blind their enemies with them

### Alchemy

[Death Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Death_Dust "Death Dust")  
[Shimmering Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shimmering_Dust "Shimmering Dust")  
[Ectoplasm](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ectoplasm "Ectoplasm")

In the game, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") first meets noonwraiths in the seemingly idyllic [fields](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fields "Fields") surrounding the village of [Murky Waters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Murky_Waters "Murky Waters"). 

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") [Bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_bestiary "The Witcher bestiary") entry

    _Noonwraiths are born at high noon out of heat, sadness and the sweat of ploughmen. In the hot air above the fields, they gather to dance madly, creating air vortexes, but the specters dislike being watched. Those who peep are forced to dance with them._
    _Noonwraiths stop their dance when the sun goes down, once the abducted mortal is long dead from fear and exhaustion._

## [Monsterbook](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monsterbook "Monsterbook")

_Developer[CD PROJEKT RED](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/CD_PROJEKT_RED "CD PROJEKT RED")'s characterization of the noonwraith taken from the monsterbook, which was enclosed with the Collectors Edition of_ _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ ​ _for Poland, Hungary and the Czech Republic:_

Crops swaying in the fields on a windless day indicate that a noonwraith is nearby. These monsters kill [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") who venture into the fields at noontime, thus, farmers return home or seek shelter in the shade at this time of the day. 

Our noonwraith appears in picturesque location known simply as the Fields, where not a drop of rain has fallen in a very long time. We could not resist including this traditional Polish folk creature in our game, especially since a good part of the story plays out in rural areas. In a conceptual sketch, the noonwraith wields a sickle – a symbol of the harvest and of working in the fields. 

The noonwraith is a sun-burned old woman. Clothed in a linen, she hovers just above the ground, her hair white from constant exposure to the sun. Streaks of light drift about her, and when necessary she uses these to blind her opponents. The noonwraith's frock and shoes suggest she once was a young girl, who venturing into the fields, joined in a spectral dance and thus entered the spirit world as a [wraith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith "Wraith"). 

The final illustration depicts a [nightwraith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nightwraith "Nightwraith"), a creature that haunts the fields after dusk. The nightwraith resembles the noonwraith in many respects: she rules the Fields after dark and is associated with the moon. This creature is a dark reflection of the noonwraith, which is emphasized by colors chosen for the illustration.

### Bestiary entry

    _Despite what is commonly thought, peasants do not interrupt their labors at midday to get out of the sun – they do it to avoid noonwraiths.  
– Vlad Reymond, Peasants and Their Customs_
    _On particularly searing summer days, when the sun reaches its zenith, wraiths will at times appear, resembling sun-scorched women dressed in long, white robes. These are noonwraiths – the spirits of young women and girls who died violent deaths right before their weddings. Driven mad with pain and anger, they wander the fields searching for their unfaithful lovers or backstabbing rivals, though they will kill anyone who does not get out of their way in time. They are often held in this world by some object of intense emotional significance. That is why, if one ever finds a wedding ring or torn veil in the middle of a field, one should not pick it up, but instead back away as quickly as possible._
    _Noonwraiths are only known to haunt rural places, and usually stay near the place of their deaths. They prey on peasants working in the fields or children playing nearby._
    _Noonwraiths do not bleed and are for the most part immune to the effects of[Witcher Signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Signs "Witcher Signs"). They can create mirror images of themselves which circle their victims in a kind of morbid parody of a dance. This ghastly ritual drains their victims' life energy while adding to their own strength. Noonwraiths are also able to manipulate the physical world to a limited degree, kicking up clouds of dust which temporarily blind and disorient their opponents._
    _Noonwraiths can turn immaterial and are at such times very difficult to wound. In order to force a noonwraith to take corporeal form, one must first trap it with the[Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") Sign or strike it with a [Moon Dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moon_Dust "Moon Dust") bomb. Once the monster has regained physical presence, one can mount a fast attack with a [silver blade](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword"), preferably one coated in [specter oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Specter_oil "Specter oil")._
    _Contrary to popular belief, noonwraiths can also be encountered at night, but are much weaker then than during the day._

### Combat tactics

Noonwraiths are similar in nature to nightwraiths, save for their preference of the sunlight, and a greater mastery of physical attacks. They can blind foes with dust they kick up. 

Noonwraiths can also create copies of themselves when threatened, consuming the lifeforce of their victims until they are destroyed. Bombs and [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow") bolts can destroy noonwraith doppelgangers. Upon entering a cloud released by a Dimeritium Bomb, they will immediately dissolve. The Yrden sign or the Moon Dust bomb forces a noonwraith to become corporeal and vulnerable.

## Trivia

  * In Polish folklore, the _Południca_ , also called [Lady Midday](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Midday "wikipedia:Lady Midday") or the _Noon Witch_ , is a female supernatural figure that roomed in the roamed field bounds, scaring away the children from ruining the crops. They are said to bring people to madness, and they were used to explain cases of [heat stroke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_stroke "wikipedia:Heat stroke").

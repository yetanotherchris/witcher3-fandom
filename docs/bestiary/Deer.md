**Deer** are wild, passive mammals that inhabit most wooded areas throughout the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"). 

They are natural food for [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)"), [bears](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear "Bear"), [panthers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Panther "Panther"), and other predators. While deer aren't domesticated, some members of the [Elder Races](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Races "Elder Races") are able to ride them.[1]

Members of the [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") cultures tend to hunt deer for food and fame. They are though protected in some areas, like surrounding of [druid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid") circles or royal forests - where only the nobility have a permission to hunt. 

## Trivia

  * In the trailer scene for _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , killed creatures can have an actual scent that attracts other [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") in the area. This can lead to natural scenes occurring, like where a hungry bear walks into a pack of wolves busy devouring a deer's carcass.[2]

## Item ID

q104_keira_mine_key

**Key to elven necropolis** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") key and [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

It is unknown what it was supposed to unlock and there are no [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") ruins with conventionally locked doors present in released versions of the game. However, its item ID suggests a link with the [Wandering in the Dark](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wandering_in_the_Dark "Wandering in the Dark") quest and thus possibly the ruins that [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") explore together. Within that is of course also the door unlocked via a statues puzzle during the [Magic Lamp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic_Lamp_\(quest\) "Magic Lamp \(quest\)") quest, behind which is [Lara Dorren](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lara_Dorren "Lara Dorren")'s sepulcher[1] and a crypt-like area guarded by a [golem](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Golem "Golem") \- a plausible possibility for something referred to as a necropolis. In that general area (but above ground) are also some [elven ruins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elven_ruins_near_Lake_Wyndamer "Elven ruins near Lake Wyndamer"), speculated to be the "elven burial ground whereabout [Midcopse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Midcopse "Midcopse")" that some peasants often mention around Geralt.

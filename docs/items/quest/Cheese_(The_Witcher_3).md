## Item ID

Cheese

**Cheese** is a medium-strength [food item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_food_and_drink "The Witcher 3 food and drink") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , found as random loot and sold usually by [innkeeps](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Innkeeper#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "Innkeeper"). 

Since [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"), in a hidden joke by the developers, it can also be dismantled from [The Emmentaler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Emmentaler "The Emmentaler") sword.

## Item ID

q401_cheese

This **Cheese** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

It was part of a deleted phase of the quest [No Place Like Home](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/No_Place_Like_Home "No Place Like Home"), where [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") was supposed to raid the kitchen and bring some food back to the table - something that is now only mentioned in dialogue right before [Eskel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eskel "Eskel") disappears, having gone for more vodka because Geralt already went for food earlier. He could bring this, [bread](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bread_\(The_Witcher_3\)#Quest_item "Bread \(The Witcher 3\)") and possibly [sausages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sausages "Sausages").

## Item ID

mq2048_guide_notes

This **Journal** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , found on a [small island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hemdall%27s_Finger "Hemdall's Finger") to the east of [Redgill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redgill "Redgill") and is part of the quest [From a Land Far, Far Away](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/From_a_Land_Far,_Far_Away "From a Land Far, Far Away"). 

### Journal entry

    _Five weeks have now passed since I was hired by that "Prince[Valgridovt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Valgridovt "Valgridovt") of a far-off land" to help guide his family safely to [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"). At first I merely suspected it, but now I am certain: our ship is being followed._
    _We've charted a course for shore. The captain wants to replenish supplies in a small village called[Ursten](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ursten "Ursten"), and then sail up the [Pontar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_\(river\) "Pontar \(river\)"). The masts of the ship which sails after us are constantly visible on the horizon._
    _The worst is upon us. We shall drown. The shore was within our reach when a horrible wind blew us back. The gale tossed our ship against the rocks. We're taking on water. The yard fell and crushed my legs. I don't know what will come to pass now. I have failed them, have failed the heirs to the royal dynasty whom I was charged to escort safely to Beauclair... The[sealed letters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letter_sealed_with_impermeable_wax "Letter sealed with impermeable wax") were in the chest which the sailors tossed overboard when the ship began to sink._
    _If they ever wash ashore, if anyone ever finds them and this journal, know that they need to be given to a woman answering to the name of[Viki](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Viki "Viki"). She does not know the [local tongue](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Common_Speech "Common Speech"), but if you read the words "Viedhog latrut alame hoir," she will know what to do. I hope she has survived - luckily, she and the child sail in another ship. The elder insisted we travel separately. He was right. I don't know who chases us... I only know that I shall soon die..._

## Item ID

q704_ft_syanna_journal

This **Journal** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine_quest_items "Blood and Wine quest items") in the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion that is found during [The Night of Long Fangs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Night_of_Long_Fangs "The Night of Long Fangs"). 

If one agrees to find [Syanna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sylvia_Anna "Sylvia Anna"), they must search the playroom at [Beauclair Palace](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair_Palace "Beauclair Palace") and pick up this book, containing the journal entries of Isabelle de Roquefort, the governess that'd been in charge of the girls when they were young. [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") will then read aloud the entries that give both the next step to take as well as insight into Syanna's relationship with her family. 

Only the third entry is required to be read to continue the game, but the first two are necessary to be able to reunite the sisters later. 

### Journal entry

    _-I-_
    _Syanna gave me a card today with headless blood-stained people on it. I asked why she had drawn it. The princess answered that it was a scene from nightmares that have been haunting her since she can remember. It is true that Syanna awakes each night with a scream - unless she is sleeping in the same bed as[Anarietta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Anna_Henrietta "Anna Henrietta")._
    _-II-_
    _The girls quarreled today. Unfortunately, I must note that they did not behave in a manner becoming for young ladies: there were fisticuffs! Anna Henrietta was first to hit her sister, but Syanna didn't wait long to reply. Before I could break them apart, little Anarietta had lost two milk teeth, and she ran straight to her parents to complain. His Grace the Duke couldn't believe his ears when I told him what had happened. Syanna got the whole blame and was suitably punished. Anarietta then tried to ease the situation, but the Duke and Duchess were resolute._
    _-III-_
    _Today for the first time I accompanied the girls to the[Land of a Thousand Fables](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Land_of_a_Thousand_Fables "Land of a Thousand Fables"), where we spent half a day. First we played with [Thumbelina](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thumbelina "Thumbelina") and then with Redbeard, who was extremely gallant for a highwayman. I am thoroughly impressed by what the master [Artorius Vigo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Artorius_Vigo "Artorius Vigo") has achieved. He has proved that he has no equal in magical mysteries. The girls said that if I wish to go there by myself, all I have to do is open the book Land of a Thousand Fables and say out loud the spell: Expecto ludum!_
    _-IV-_
    _Today, Syanna was tested by the[mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") sent by the [Chapter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chapter_of_the_Gift_and_the_Art "Chapter of the Gift and the Art"). I don't know what conclusions they drew, but His Grace the Duke is clearly worried. I asked the Duke what awaits the princess. He didn't reply._
    _-V-_
    _I tutored the girls in[Nilfgaardian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") today. Syanna applies herself so, though she has great difficulty memorizing new vocabulary. Lady Anarietta seems more gifted in this regard, yet also prone to impish behavior. When she thought me out of earshot, she called me a 'bloede kusse.' She and Syanna laughed so hard they almost choked._

## Item ID

lore_journals_from_urskar_2

This **Journal** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

It is the 2nd of 7 books collectively known as "journals from [Urskar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Urskar "Urskar")", detailing what led to [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik")'s destruction. Small bits from that still remain in dialogues during [The Lord of Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Lord_of_Undvik "The Lord of Undvik") quest, but the overall story and a lot of other content related to the island have been removed from the game. 

### Journal entry

    _More and more movement around Undvik.[Port](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marlin_Coast "Marlin Coast")'s full of longships and smaller vessels. News about the [forge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tordarroch_Forge "Clan Tordarroch Forge") has spread all over the [isles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") like grubs on on a dead [bear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear "Bear"). Folk're coming here with their [mounts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse"). That's good for our isle. Perhaps good times are on their way._

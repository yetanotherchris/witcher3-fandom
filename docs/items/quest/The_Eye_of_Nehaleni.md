## Item ID

Illusion Medallion

**The Eye of Nehaleni** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

This item is an artifact tied to the deity [Nehaleni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nehaleni "Nehaleni"), and allows [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to [dispel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dispel "Dispel") magic [illusions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Illusion "Illusion"). It is given by [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") during the quest [Wandering in the Dark](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wandering_in_the_Dark "Wandering in the Dark") to clear an illusion blocking the exit from the [elven mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Mysterious_Elf "The Mysterious Elf")'s laboratory. Subsequently, the Eye is useful for clearing illusions blocking secret entrances, passageways, cave entrances, or rooms.

### Other locations

  * In [The Bits](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Bits "The Bits") of [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), there is an entrance down into the the sewers that leads to an outside path on the cliffs near the Passiflora. In that section of the Novigrad sewers, there is a hidden room behind a brick wall illusion that is guarded by a [water hag](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water_hag "Water hag") and several [drowned dead](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowned_dead "Drowned dead").
  * To the east of the actual amphitheater at [Seidhe Llygad Amphitheater](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Seidhe_Llygad_Amphitheater "Seidhe Llygad Amphitheater"), there is a large cave doorway on the cliffs down near the shoreline of the lake. There is an illusion that can be dispelled to reveal a small room. ![79?cb=20160527184953](https://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3b/BAW_English_logo.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/79?cb=20160527184953)
  * In [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") at the ruins of [Kaer Dhu](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Dhu "Kaer Dhu"), there is a monster den entrance. This entrance connects through a series of caves to another entrance on the other side of the mountain range. Inside this series of caves is an illusion wall. This hidden wall only stops Geralt from going from one end of cave to the other end. This illusion doesn't hide any loot.
  * During [La Cage au Fou](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/La_Cage_au_Fou "La Cage au Fou") in the [Mère-Lachaiselongue Cemetery](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/M%C3%A8re-Lachaiselongue_Cemetery "Mère-Lachaiselongue Cemetery"), Geralt will head underground into a crypt. Hidden behind an illusion wall at the end of a hallway, there is a tomb with the unique sword [Casus Foederis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Casus_Foederis "Casus Foederis") and a [letter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Knight%27s_Oath "A Knight's Oath"). ![79?cb=20160527184953](https://vignette2.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3b/BAW_English_logo.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/79?cb=20160527184953)
  * During [The Battle of Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Battle_of_Kaer_Morhen "The Battle of Kaer Morhen"), if Geralt convinced [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") to travel to [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") at the end of [For the Advancement of Learning](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/For_the_Advancement_of_Learning "For the Advancement of Learning"), she will be inside the castle on the right hand side of the entry hallway. Kiera will be enveloped in a transparent spherical illusion sitting in a chair looking at a mirror on a table brushing her hair. There is an interaction point on the sphere where Geralt will attempt to use the Eye to dispel the illusion that Kiera has created. The illusion will begin to break down like other illusions Geralt has interacted with and she will chastise him for trying to dispel her illusion. The animation will then stop and the illusion stays.

## Trivia

  * The name Nehaleni could be derived from the goddess "[Nehalennia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nehalennia)" , a first century goddess of the Germanic or Celtic tribes living in western Europe.

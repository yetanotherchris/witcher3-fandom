## Item ID

q309_key_orders

**Warrant** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , dropped by three of the [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter") encountered during the [Novigrad, Closed City](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad,_Closed_City "Novigrad, Closed City") \- the two ambushing [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") in the house [Lussi](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lussi "Lussi") leads him to, and one of the two attacking [Wallter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wallter "Wallter") behind [Crippled Kate's](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crippled_Kate%27s "Crippled Kate's"). 

Reading it provides the clues necessary for Geralt to know what to do with the keys, and is one way to start or resume the quest as: 

  * If Lussi is encountered first, killing the witch hunters marks all quest objectives as complete (not ending the quest, but causing it to not show as active).
  * If Wallter is encountered first, the quest doesn't start at all even after his [key](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Key_\(quest_item\)#Key_\(Wallter\) "Key \(quest item\)") is obtained.

Even if you somehow missed getting the warrant though, don't worry - the [letter about treasure](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letter_about_treasure "Letter about treasure") serves the same purpose and isn't missable.

**The Merry Adventures of Muriel the Lovely Harlot, Illustrated Edition** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. This is one of the books with a red cover that [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can find during the quest [Message from an Old Friend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Message_from_an_Old_Friend "Message from an Old Friend"), but it doesn't do anything. 

## Journal entry

    _On one occasion[Muriel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Muriel "Muriel") went on a journey to see her auntie in [Maribor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor_\(city\) "Maribor \(city\)") accompanied by her nursemaid. Their path took them through a forest, and in this forest lived a raucous troupe of bandits. This infamous group was led by Flynn Selms, and all the king's men had been unable to bring them to justice. Alas, such was Muriel's great misfortune that these bandits chose to attack her carriage._
    _Muriel's nursemaid was old, blind and deaf. She did not wake when a tree fell in front of their carriage with a loud thud, nor when the bandits fought a fierce battle against their guardsmen. When Flynn ripped open the carriage door with his muscular arms and stepped inside, Muriel had to deal with the danger herself. "Make our guest comfortable, young lady," the old nursemaid muttered in her sleep. Muriel obediently carried out her instructions._

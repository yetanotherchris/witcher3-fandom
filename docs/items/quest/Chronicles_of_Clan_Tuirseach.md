## Item ID

th1007_journal_bear_part7

**Chronicles of Clan Tuirseach** is a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be found in the chest by the old throne in [Castle Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Castle_Tuirseach "Castle Tuirseach") along with [The Ballad of Witcher Gerd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Ballad_of_Witcher_Gerd "The Ballad of Witcher Gerd") and the 4 standard [Ursine armor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear_School_Gear "Bear School Gear") diagrams. Reading it directs [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to the ruins of [Fort Etnir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fort_Etnir "Fort Etnir") and the [sirens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Siren "Siren")' cave on [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog") (where [Melusine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Melusine "Melusine") is) during [Scavenger Hunt: Bear School Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Bear_School_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Bear School Gear"). 

## Journal entry

    _... and so there was much rejoicing in the castle at the appearance of an itinerant[monster](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") slayer in the person of the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") [Gerd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gerd_\(witcher\) "Gerd \(witcher\)"). Jarl [Torgeir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torgeir_the_Red "Torgeir the Red"), whose face in the months since the death of his battle brother, Ingmar, had worn a listless and saddened frown, came alive with renewed strength. Jarl and witcher fast found common tongue, and soon were venturing on hunts together and hosting feasts at which there was no end to laughter nor bluster... [illegible]_
    _...then Jarl Torgeir told Gerd of the hideous[striga](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Striga "Striga") which prowled the ruins of [Etnir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fort_Etnir "Fort Etnir"), desecrating that once-proud fortress, which in happier times had stood a steadfast guardian of the northern expanses of [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"). The jarl's lament moved the witcher, who announced he would slay the beast. Yet witcher Gerd was a craftsman ever diligent and true, and so explained he first had to complete a prior contract and rid a cave on [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog") of sirens...[illegible]_
    _And so the jarl gave Gerd a full pouch of coin and the services of Olven, Org's son, as a guide to take him safely to Spikeroog and, if the danger grew fierce, to fight the sirens at the witcher's side. His heart filled with gratitude, Gerd entrusted an[armor diagram](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Diagram:_Ursine_armor "Diagram: Ursine armor") to the jarl, one he had long carried and held most dear, as a token of his sincere intent to return as soon as he finished his allotted task..._

# Bill of exchange

**Bill of exchange** is a special [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , originally intended to be exchanged for [crowns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") at the [Vivaldi Bank](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vivaldi_Bank "Vivaldi Bank") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). Many of them were supposed to appear throughout the game, possibly even as forms of payment for [contract](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_contracts "The Witcher 3 contracts") quests. However, the entire concept was [cut](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") and (functional) bills of exchange don't appear during gameplay, even though the mechanic is fully implemented. There is still one document bearing the same name that is obtainable, but it can't be cashed in.

## Item ID

q110_bill_of_exchange

This is the still obtainable document, received if [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") accepts payment from [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") after bringing [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") to him in the quest, [Blood on the Battlefield](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_on_the_Battlefield "Blood on the Battlefield"). 

While it says that it can be exchanged for 100,000 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") if/after Nilfgaard wins the war, it can't actually be cashed in.

    _The war shall be deemed over once all belligerent parties have signed a peace treaty or the Empire's enemies have all been annihilated and their lands occupied in totum._

## Item ID

vivaldis_bill_of_exchange

This is a version that was cut from the game. It still exists in the game files, but can only be obtained through console commands. The item is fully functional and, if present in the [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory"), an extra dialogue option with Vimme Vivaldi will be available: _"Got a bill of exchange to redeem."_ When chosen, Geralt has a short line saying almost exactly the same thing, to which Vivaldi replies with _"I'll need a spell to verify that the issuer's credit worthy. But it shouldn't take long."_ 100 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") are then received. 

It is yet unclear if there was a specific reason why it is tied to [Vimme Vivaldi](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vimme_Vivaldi "Vimme Vivaldi"), or if that's simply because his bank is where it is exchanged. Also, despite looking like a readable document (and actually flagged as such), it doesn't have a journal entry and can't be read. 

## Notes

  * In the _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion, a [summons to payment](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Summons_to_payment "Summons to payment") has the opposite effect, requiring Geralt to pay his taxes at the bank.

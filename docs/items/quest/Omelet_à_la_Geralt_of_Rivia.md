## Item ID

yennefers_omelette_fantasie

**Omelet à la Geralt of Rivia** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

If [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") is romantically involved with [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") during the main quest [Va Fail, Elaine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Va_Fail,_Elaine "Va Fail, Elaine"), she would ask him to prepare a meal for her and this is one of the possible options for it. It has been claimed[1] that 16+ food items on top of egg, onion and black pepper are required to get this, but as of [Patch 4.04](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.04_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.04 \(The Witcher 3\)") that doesn't seem to work, so it's possible that a specific yet unknown ingredient is required. Regardless, when presented with it, Yennefer will respond with a rather underwhelming _"Not quite what I asked for... But it looks appetizing enough."_ but the dialogue after that makes it clear that she meant that in a rather pleasantly surprised manner, only she looks very tired while delivering the line. 

The other three meal options are [scrambled eggs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scrambled_eggs "Scrambled eggs"), a [rather unappetizing meal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rather_unappetizing_meal "Rather unappetizing meal") and [omelet for Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Omelet_for_Yennefer "Omelet for Yennefer"). 

All of this is still present in the game and can be enabled via this console command, used at the start of the quest: addfact(q401_cooking_enabled)

**Fisstech** or **White Death** is a drug similar in appearance and effect to [heroin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heroin). It is a powder which is typically snorted or rubbed on the gums, but as with opiates and other narcotics in our society, it can also be used in medicine as an anesthetic. A more powerful version of fisstech was also used by the [Aen Elle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Elle "Aen Elle") and [Auberon Muircetach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Auberon_Muircetach "Auberon Muircetach").

_"Fisstech is a popular narcotic among affluent youth and members of Temeria's underworld. Powerful crime organizations jealously guard the secrets of its production. Although the laws of the kingdom forbid the drug's sale, fisstech is a source of huge profits for the bosses of the criminal underworld."_

The [Salamandra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Salamandra "Salamandra") control the production and the trading of this drug in [Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima"). It is usually consumed through the nose which leads to cold like symptoms, mostly sneezing. Another way of consuming the drug revealed in the game is to rub it under the foreskin: [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") describes this to [Jethro](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jethro "Jethro") as the most effective way: _"...where you can fold back your skin, Jethro."_

### [Glossary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Glossary_entries "Glossary entries") entry

    _**Fisstech**_
    _Fisstech is an illegal narcotic that is used widely in[Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria"). It takes the form of a white powder and is highly addictive. It can be manufactured only by professionals with the use of complicated alchemical equipment._

### Notes

  * Further information about this drug can be gleaned during the [Anatomy of a Crime](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Anatomy_of_a_Crime "Anatomy of a Crime") quest.
  * The [Vodyanoi](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vodyanoi "Vodyanoi") accept it as a tribute.
  * If you "experiment" and create fisstech without first having the [recipe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Recipe "Recipe"), it is one potion that does not require consumption to add the [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") entry.
  * You can produce fisstech with a dominant ingredient (such as [Rubedo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rubedo "Rubedo")), for which at least the [Order armorer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Armorer_of_the_Order "Armorer of the Order") will pay 40 Orens instead of 20. 
    * However, using such fisstech to get the effect will not work, as Geralt will only pass out like with the regular version.

## Notes

  * While mostly used as a recreational drug, it's known to have a beneficial use as an anesthetic for medical purposes.
  * Some fisstech users include the [Rats](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rats "Rats") and [Angoulême](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Angoul%C3%AAme "Angoulême") (the latter having only been seen using it once). [Leo Bonhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leo_Bonhart "Leo Bonhart") also drugged [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") with it when he forced her to fight in the [Claremont](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Claremont "Claremont") [arena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arena_in_Claremont "Arena in Claremont"). 
    * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , [Eskel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eskel "Eskel") admits he tried fisstech while bedding a [succubus](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Succubus "Succubus").
  * In _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ , during the quest [The Scent of Incense](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Scent_of_Incense "The Scent of Incense"), Geralt is asked by a shady alchemist, [Vencel Pugg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vencel_Pugg "Vencel Pugg"), to get the formula for some "special incense" being manufactured in [Flotsam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flotsam "Flotsam") by [Vilmos Bartok](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilmos_Bartok "Vilmos Bartok"). Later it transpires that the formula is actually for fisstech.
  * In the _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion it's shown to be manufactured like a real world meth lab.
  * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , if one pays for the services of a [prostitute](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prostitute "Prostitute"), it may show a random cutscene where she takes out a vial and snorts the substance, with the substance likely being fisstech.

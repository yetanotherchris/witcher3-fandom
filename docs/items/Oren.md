## Item ID

Orens

The oren is no longer the main currency in _The Witcher 3_. Instead, the [crown](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") takes its place. May sometimes be referred to as gold or money or coin. During Geralt's journey throughout Temeria he may sometimes acquire a few orens from looting various containers or corpses. These will be kept in his inventory like an ordinary item and may be exchanged for crowns at the [Vivaldi Bank](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vivaldi_Bank "Vivaldi Bank") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") for a one-to-one conversion. In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, orens may be exchanged for crowns at the [Cianfanelli Bank](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cianfanelli_Bank "Cianfanelli Bank") in [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"). 

### Bugs

  * Orens may not appear in quantities larger than 100 on the [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen")/Novigrad map, or 103 on the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") map, due to a leftover artificial limitation from a game mechanic that was never fully developed, but also never cleaned up. The intended logic was likely meant to simply reduce larger quantities to the mentioned numbers, but instead whenever the limit is reached they get outright removed. This is actually a global mechanic, but luckily in the case of orens it only happens to affect a couple of quests, while the only other item affected globally is [balisse fruit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Balisse_fruit#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "Balisse fruit"), although in a different way.

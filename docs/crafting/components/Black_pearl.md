## Item ID

Black pearl

**Black pearl** is a [crafting component](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_crafting_components "The Witcher 3 crafting components") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. It is not needed to craft anything but can be dismantled into [black pearl dust](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_pearl_dust "Black pearl dust"). 

It can be obtained by dismantling [sea shells](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_junk "The Witcher 3 junk"), found as random loot, or purchased from the following [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"):

## Notes

  * Harvesting black pearls is one way a player can exploit the game for [crowns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"). As sea shells are very cheap, a player can buy them, have them dismantled, then sell the pearl for a profit. In the _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion, the [tax collector](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Walthemor_Mitty "Walthemor Mitty") mentions this as one way of exploiting taxes.
  * Even if you have some in your inventory, the [Black Pearl quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Pearl_\(quest\) "Black Pearl \(quest\)") will still make you meet [Nidas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nidas "Nidas") in [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), dive in and retrieve one, then return to him in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") to get payment.

## Item ID

Pearl

**Pearl** is a [crafting component](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_crafting_components "The Witcher 3 crafting components") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. It is not needed to craft anything but can be dismantled into [powdered pearl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Powdered_pearl "Powdered pearl"). 

It can be obtained by dismantling the [junk items](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_junk "The Witcher 3 junk") shell and gold pearl necklace, found as both random and guaranteed loot in many places, or purchased from the following [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"): 

  * All [armorers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Armorer "Armorer") and [blacksmiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blacksmith "Blacksmith"), except the one at [Ardaiso Quarry](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ardaiso_Quarry "Ardaiso Quarry")

  * [Merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(boatyard\) "Merchant \(boatyard\)") in the [boatyard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Abandoned_site_\(boatyard\) "Abandoned site \(boatyard\)") south of [Refugees' Camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Refugees%27_Camp "Refugees' Camp")

## Notes

  * Harvesting pearls is one way a player can exploit the game for crowns. As shells are very cheap, a player can buy them, have them dismantled, then sell the pearl for a profit. The [tax collector](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Walthemor_Mitty "Walthemor Mitty") mentions this as one way of exploiting taxes.

## Item ID

Draconides vol 2

**Death From Above** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that, when read, provides [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entries for [forktails](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forktail "Forktail") and [wyverns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wyvern "Wyvern"), as well as [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Forktail_decoction "Torn-out page: Forktail decoction") for [forktail decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forktail_decoction "Forktail decoction") and [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Wyvern_decoction "Torn-out page: Wyvern decoction") for [wyvern decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wyvern_decoction "Wyvern decoction"). 

It should be available as random loot, but due to a randomization bug never appears as such, and can only be obtained through console commands. 

## Journal entry

    _Forktails, like wyverns, are often mistaken for[dragons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon"). This is not at all surprising, given that the witnesses to these [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster")' appearances are usually simple shepherds frightened witless by the monstrous winged creature shrieking above them. Yet that does not make it any less of a colossal and profound error._
    _Both forktails and wyverns are much, much smaller than dragons. True, they hunt animals in open fields, cattle in particular, just like those nobler beasts, but their behaviors and methods for dealing with potential prey could not be more different._

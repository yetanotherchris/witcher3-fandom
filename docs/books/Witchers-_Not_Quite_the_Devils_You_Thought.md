**Witchers: Not Quite the Devils You Thought** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Copies of it can be found in the [Crones](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crones "Crones")' house at [The Orphans of Crookback Bog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Orphans_of_Crookback_Bog "The Orphans of Crookback Bog"), on a [witch hunter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter")'s corpse by a [drowners](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner") [nest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster_nest "Monster nest") far west from there, and next to the [rose of remembrance](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rose_of_remembrance "Rose of remembrance") in [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold")' [ransacked house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss%27_house_\(Novigrad\) "Triss' house \(Novigrad\)"). It can also be purchased from a [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(halfling\) "Merchant \(halfling\)") in the [Silverton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silverton "Silverton") district. 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can also be found as themed loot during [Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Grandmaster_Griffin_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear"), in a chest along with [Moreau's journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moreau%27s_journal "Moreau's journal"). 

## Journal entry

    _A conviction has arisen amongst both[mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") and the hoi polloi that [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") barely rise a hair's breadth above animals, that they are deformed and bloodthirsty [mutants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mutant "Mutant"), and that in slaking this thirst they do not limit themselves to the fluids of [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster"). Meanwhile the research and observations of Virgil of [Ban Ard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ban_Ard "Ban Ard") paint an entirely different picture of the caste._
    _Of course, it is an indisputable fact that the[mutations](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Trials "Witcher Trials") witchers undergo influence their temperament, immune system, strength and endurance, yet one cannot consider them unthinking beings forged only for killing as a result. In fact, witchers cultivate a unique culture, a specialized body of knowledge, an effective training system and even a code of honor. They know an incredible amount about the effects of ingesting various organic and inorganic substances, and also a small amount, but nevertheless something, about the fundamentals of [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") (by which I mean the simple spells they call "[Signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign")"). On the surface the witchers appear to be a homogeneous social entity, but in truth their fighting styles, training regimens and world views differ significantly depending upon the school to which they adhere. The best-known [witcher schools](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_schools "Witcher schools") on the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent") are the [School of the Wolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Wolf "School of the Wolf"), the [School of the Bear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Bear "School of the Bear"), the [School of the Griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Griffin "School of the Griffin"), the [School of the Viper](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Viper "School of the Viper"), and the [School of the Cat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Cat "School of the Cat")._

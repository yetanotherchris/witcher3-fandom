**Forgotten priest's testament** is a document in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that was introduced with the next-gen update, [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"). 

It's found in the [Devil's Pit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Devil%27s_Pit "Devil's Pit") mine system, in a laboratory while following the [School of the Wolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Wolf "School of the Wolf") symbols, and opens an optional dialogue at the end of the quest with [Reinald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reinald "Reinald"). 

## Journal entry

    _From the moment I first learned of our martyrs, I dreamed I would one day become one. I thought, nothing is more beautiful than making the ultimate sacrifice in the name of your faith._
    _I know now I was wrong. Woefully, naively wrong. For in this moment, I would gladly forfeit my life of "sanctity" for a single cup of water so I might help that poor boy curled up against the wall, suffering a misery none should ever endure._
    _I can die here, knowing that I departed of my own free will. By caring for the afflicted, I knew that I, too, would eventually succumb to the scarlet fog. Yet I did not know my brothers would inflict such suffering to those under our care._
    _Father Edgar tortured leaders of the rebellion and decided to close the mine. I asked, in turn, who then would care for weak?[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] "They will die regardless, all." - the smug sod... I resolved to stay, to help ease the suffering of their final days however I could. Father Edgar locked them in here all the same, and me with them._
    _I now find myself under my own care._
    _I hear their wails and whimpers, witness their desperate attempts at escape, but I've no strength left. My only hope of deliverance now is prayer for a quick death, for I know it will not be merciful._
    _Should anyone ever read my account of these atrocities, I humbly ask you share them with the world. In my waning hours, I wish for nothing more._

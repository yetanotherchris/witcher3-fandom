## Item ID

lore_summit_of_loc_muinne

**Diary of a Fire Swallower** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that provides an account of a fire swallower who was at the [Loc Muinne summit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Summit_at_Loc_Muinne "The Summit at Loc Muinne"). 

It can be found as random loot, usually on bookshelves, and is one of the most commonly found books throughout the entire game. It can also be purchased from the books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\)#Merchant_\(blue_outfit\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square") and, due to a randomization bug, always appears in the treasure chest during [A Dog's Life](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Dog%27s_Life "A Dog's Life"). 

## Journal entry

    _I was at[Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne") \- that's right, the summit of summits itself. We'd gone there, my troupe mates and I, knowing there'd be a meeting of the mighty in the ruins. You see, whenever big shots gather to chew some particularly gristly fat, there's coin to be had for performing men like us - tired jaws need to relax with some laughing and indulging in other sorts of simple delights. I learned this as a lad from my old man, who'd drag me around to juggle outside courts and conventions all over the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"). And so now, plying my own trade as a fire swallower, I told the lads up soon as I caught word of what was brewing in Loc Muinne and we turned our wagons thataway at once._
    _I'm not much one for politics, but I couldn't help but listen to some of it this time - you see, we[Temerians](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") had just lost our [king](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest"), and our future was to be decided at that very summit, with [John Natalis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/John_Natalis "John Natalis") representing our side in the matter. [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") was there, too, a young pup then, though emanating strength like a grey-haired alpha [wolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)"). It seemed this wolf was licking his chops over Temeria like it was ripe bit of carrion..._
    _The rest was dull - a lot of talk about the uprising in[Aedirn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn") and its leader, [Saskia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Saskia "Saskia"), who they called the Virgin - though I never got the chance to check personally whether that was accurate. The [Nilfgaardians](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") had a delegate, some [Shilard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shilard_Fitz-Oesterlen "Shilard Fitz-Oesterlen") fellow, and [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") were there, talking about restoring the [Conclave](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers") and [Supreme Council of Sorcerers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Supreme_Council_of_Sorcerers "Supreme Council of Sorcerers")._
    _I didn't listen to much of that - my eyes were glued to the unusually large number of armed troops present at these "peaceful" talks. Steel-plated grunts clenched pointy weapons and paced the ruins' courtyards everywhere you looked. Radovid had even brought the[knightly](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knight "Knight") [Order of the Flaming Rose](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_of_the_Flaming_Rose "Order of the Flaming Rose"), those arrogant pricks ready to bash anyone who doesn't think the same as them. Though the coin was coming in amply, we didn't wait around to see how things would develop, just loaded our stuff onto our wagon and left at a trot. As we mounted the pass, we looked back to see pillars of smoke, black as pitch, rising above Loc Muinne..._

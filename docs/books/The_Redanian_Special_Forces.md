**The Redanian Special Forces** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It should appear as random loot in some treasure chests and two bookshelves in [Dijkstra's bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse"). However, due to a randomization bug none of the possible containers ever select it, and it can only be obtained through console commands. 

## Journal entry

    _Which[Northern Realm](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms")'s special forces are the greatest of them all? Once upon a time, the answer was clear: [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania"). Its network of spies and informers was so vast that [Tretogor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tretogor "Tretogor") knew which route the [Nilfgaardian armies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") would take before they even left their barracks. No ruler kept any weakness or compromising secret from these astute spooks. [Redanian intelligence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redanian_Secret_Service "Redanian Secret Service") had vast resources at its disposal, including the use of great [sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") like [Philippa Eilhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Philippa_Eilhart "Philippa Eilhart") and shining stars from the world of culture. With their innovative interrogation techniques perfected in [Drakenborg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drakenborg "Drakenborg") Fortress, its agents could extract vital information from even the tightest-lipped [Scoia'tael](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scoia%27tael "Scoia'tael") guerilla, allowing them to reduce that organization on their soil to a hounded, desperate few._
    _The golden days of the Redanian spy service ended, however, along with the disappearance of its head,[Sigismund Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra"). His fate still remains unknown to this day. According to some, he departed for voluntary exile after falling out with King [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V"). According to others, he was captured by enemy agents. Still others hold that Dijkstra was murdered by sorceresses, and his body disintegrated into a fine dust._
    _After Dijkstra's disappearance, the royal council ordered a thoroughgoing audit of the spy corps. Radovid appointed a commission with a remit to search for abuses of power, root out double agents and suggest remedial measures. As usual, however, inserting civilians into the affairs of spies had the opposite of the desired effect. Shortly after the work began a list of agents and their sources leaked. Many on it were subsequently murdered or fell off the face of the[earth](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World"). A few days sufficed to reduce to ashes an organization built over long years of hard work._

## Item ID

**Monstrum, or a Portrayal of Witchers. Volume 1** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

One copy of this volume can be found in the morgue during [Carnal Sins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Carnal_Sins "Carnal Sins") and it should also on rare occasions appear as random loot. Due to a randomization bug however, instead it always appears in a chest at the cave nearest to [Temple of Lilvani](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Lilvani "Temple of Lilvani"), connected to the larger cave system outside [Toderas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toderas "Toderas") in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"). It can also be purchased from the [halfling merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(halfling\) "Merchant \(halfling\)") in [Silverton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silverton "Silverton"). 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can also be found as themed loot during [Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Grandmaster_Griffin_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear"), in a chest along with [Moreau's journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moreau%27s_journal "Moreau's journal"). 

## Journal entry

    _In truth, there is naught more repulsive than these monsters that defy nature and are known by the name of[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"). They are the offspring of foul sorcery and witchcraft. They are veritable scoundrels without conscience and virtue, unscrupulous creatures off hell able only to take lives. They have no place amongst decent and honest folk._
    _And this keep, this[Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") where these villains nest and practice their foul rituals must be wiped from the earth, all traces of it treated with salt and [saltpeter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Saltpeter "Saltpeter") to complete the deed._

## Item ID

lore_bells_of_beauclair

**Toussaint, a Duchy Out Of Tales of Fantasy and Wonder** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found as random loot, usually on bookshelves, throughout the entire game. It can also be purchased from the books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can be found as random loot inside rich houses around [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"), and oddly can also randomly appear alongside the [fragment of a letter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fragment_of_a_letter "Fragment of a letter") in one of the endings for [Wine Wars: The Deus in the Machina](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wine_Wars:_The_Deus_in_the_Machina "Wine Wars: The Deus in the Machina"). 

## Journal entry

    _When a traveler from the[Northern Realms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Realms "Northern Realms") first crosses the border into [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), he feels at once as though he has stepped into a land ripped straight from the pages of a fantastic fairy tale. He will know no inclement weather there, for even the winters in Toussaint are mild and sunny, with only gentle, calming breezes and not a hint of gale. He will know no hunger, for the trees and bushes of that land burst with ripe and juicy fruits all the year long. He will know no loneliness, for each and every soul he encounters will treat him like a long-lost friend. He will not find a single backwater of dullness, boredom or inquietude in this overflowing stream of marvel. In Toussaint, the wine rages in torrents, music plays ceaselessly and everywhere the air is filled with the sound of birdsong and the twittering of beautiful maids, who are never stingy with their ample charms when a handsome [knight](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knight "Knight") comes a-calling. The capital of the duchy, [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"), is an architectural gem, full of glorious [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") monuments: delicately soaring towers, masterfully carved reliefs and atmospherically mysterious ruins. Only the rare sun in a field of black, the odd gold and dark stain on an otherwise pristine edifice, reminds one that this land, this fable incarnate, is a vassal of [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire")..._

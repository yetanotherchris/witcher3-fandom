## Item ID

Cursed Monsters vol 2

**The Bear Legend** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that, when read, provides [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entries for [berserkers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werebear "Werebear") and [botchlings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Botchling "Botchling"), as well as the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Doppler_decoction "Torn-out page: Doppler decoction") for [doppler decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Doppler_decoction "Doppler decoction"). 

It should be available as random loot, but due to a randomization bug can only and always be found in a chest at the [elven mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Avallac%27h "Avallac'h")'s hideout during the quest [Wandering in the Dark](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wandering_in_the_Dark "Wandering in the Dark"). It can also be purchased from [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") in his [shop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Books_and_Scrolls "Books and Scrolls") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). 

## Journal entry

    _The berserkers do not constitute a separate clan in terms of blood or lineage. They are instead a family forged by warriors who have heard the "call of the[bear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear "Bear")" and answered it by abandoning their loved ones and joining the community of mountain dwellers._
    _Not every man who seeks to join it is accepted into the brotherhood: the candidates must first pass difficult trials which usually end in their death. The last trial is the hardest. It is called the "Trial of the Bear's Triumph," but few outside the brotherhood have ever witnessed it and none know what those who undergo it must endure._
    _Quite curiously, the inhabitants of[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") themselves know very little about the berserkers, or "[vildkaarls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vildkaarl "Vildkaarl")," as they call them. They do not like to talk about these man-bears, not with outsiders and not even amongst themselves._

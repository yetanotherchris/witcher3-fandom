**Necromancy, the Forbidden Magic** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can always be found near [Johnny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Johnny "Johnny")'s spot during the main quest [Bald Mountain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bald_Mountain_\(quest\) "Bald Mountain \(quest\)"), and can appear as random loot, but is very uncommon as such. It's also a "possible" loot item off [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") if [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") kills her during the secondary quest [For the Advancement of Learning](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/For_the_Advancement_of_Learning "For the Advancement of Learning"), and she always drops it due to a randomization bug. 

## Journal entry

    _It is commonly said that[magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") is neither good nor evil, that it transcends the moral criteria of men, that it depends on the [mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") whether it will be used for worthy goals or for shameful and immoral deeds. There is, however, one key exception to this principle - [necromancy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancy "Necromancy")._
    _Proponents of this branch of magic once supposed that one can also do good by reviving corpses. Dead men can, after all, hide secrets which may save the living. It is better to send an army of reanimated corpses into battle than to squander the life of those in whose veins hot blood still flows. Moreover, a revived soldier may be reused many times, until such members are chopped off so as to make his flesh unusable._
    _Though it is hard to refute the logic of these arguments, the practice of necromancy is still forbidden. This is not at all based on the rabble's superstitions about the living dead, nor the resistance of the powerful afraid those they have murdered could be forced to confess against them, nor the moral scruples of the older members of the[Conclave](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers"), who were, in fact, laughed down as reactionaries when it was discussed. The fate of necromancy was sealed by the observation of [Hen Gedymdeith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gerhart_of_Aelle "Gerhart of Aelle"), which was later confirmed by manifold experiences proving the revived dead are always unpredictable and reviving them always involves entirely negative side effects. In other words, no matter how noble a mage's motives might be, necromancy will always lead to evil. It seems that in this way the gods let us know that we should not transgress the laws they have given us. And even the Conclave must respect the will of the gods._

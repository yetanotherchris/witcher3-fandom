**His Imperial Majesty, Emhyr var Emreis** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Copies of it are guaranteed to be found on a soldier's corpse to the left of the [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard") [griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)")'s nest, and on a small table inside the [Var Attre Villa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Var_Attre_Villa "Var Attre Villa") during [Broken Flowers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Broken_Flowers "Broken Flowers"). It also appears (technically) as random loot on two bookshelves, in [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold")' [ransacked house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss%27_house_\(Novigrad\) "Triss' house \(Novigrad\)") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square") and [Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra")'s office at the [bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse"), but due to a randomization bug it is also guaranteed there. 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can be found as actual random loot inside rich houses around [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"), but one is also on a bench in the southwestern part of [Castel Ravello](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Castel_Ravello "Castel Ravello")'s gardens. 

## Journal entry

    _[Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") rules to expand. He has trained his strategic brilliance on a singular vision - to conquer the [world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World"), though he rightly calls this not conquest, but salvation. Yes. The [emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") of Nilfgaard sets the world alight with the torch of civilization._
    _Trampling the still-warm corpses of his[father](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fergus_var_Emreis "Fergus var Emreis")'s [usurpers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Usurper "Usurper"), the emperor seized the throne and began to assimilate the [Northern Realms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"), one after the other. Terror engulfed the North. Only the [Battle of Brenna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Brenna "Battle of Brenna") slowed the [Imperial Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army")'s march toward victory. A temporary peace then took hold, though none believed this mirage would last. Emhyr's aim, to make the entire North a vassal of Nilfgaard, remained unchanged. Emhyr used peace as a cover to intensify his political efforts. Now all can see their results - chaos and disorder devour the [Nordlings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nordling "Nordling")' states. Ridden with internal bickering and backstabbing, they near collapse. Only a strong ruler can grapple this dysfunction into function. Only a wise ruler can give vision to their blindness. Only Emhyr var Emreis can save the North. So our armies have again crossed the [Yaruga](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yaruga "Yaruga") \- for the [third](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_III "Northern War III") and final time..._

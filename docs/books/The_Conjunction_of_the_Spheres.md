**The Conjunction of the Spheres** is a [book](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Booklist "Booklist") in _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_. 

### Journal entry

    _**The Conjunction of the Spheres**_
    _by[Adam Nivelle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Adam_Nivelle "Adam Nivelle")_
    _"The cataclysm commonly known as the Conjunction of the Spheres happened one and a half millennia ago. A cosmic collision of several parallel universes, this disaster left numerous creatures not native to our reality trapped here. For example,[ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul") and [graveirs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Graveir "Graveir"), which lack their own ecological niches are simply relics of the Conjunction._
    _The[elves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") claim that [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Humans "Humans") also arrived in this world during the Conjunction. This occurred soon after they managed to destroy their own world. The elves claim that it was during the Conjunction that humans learned to use [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic")._
    _Of course, these are all vile lies and foul fabrications circulated by[nonhumans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nonhuman "Nonhuman"), who will resort to the most malicious slander to justify their claims."_

A work devoted to the famous magical cataclysm. Describes the appearance of monsters, the origins of humans and human magic.

Because of this event, creatures never seen before entered our world, and still do not have their own ecological niche here. Among others, graveirs and ghouls are relics of the permeation of the spheres, though elven tradition has it that we, humans, are also newcomers from that time. The sorcerers claim that time humanity received both the wondrous gift and the terrible curse that they consider magic to be at that time._

## Item ID

The book can be found as random loot on almost any bookshelf throughout the third game, with at least one copy usually found in a chest by the entrance to [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold")' [ransacked house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss%27_house_\(Novigrad\) "Triss' house \(Novigrad\)") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). It is also sold by [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") and the [halfling merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(halfling\) "Merchant \(halfling\)") in [Silverton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silverton "Silverton"). 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can also be found as random loot on bookshelves in houses around [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), or purchased from the [Ducal Camerlengo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rafael_de_Surmann "Rafael de Surmann"). 

### Journal entry

    _Scholars have many vices. Possibly the worst among them is a tendency to describe the simple in unnecessarily complex terms, to dress the plain the garb of false learning.  
"The Conjunction of the Spheres" might serve an an excellent example of this. This name, so mysterious to a commoner's ear, could be replaced with a much simpler alternative: When the Worlds Collided. The phenomenon itself can also be explained in terms simple enough for a child to understand.  
Imagine, dear reader, that our [world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World") is a ship sailing on a great sea. From its deck we can see other, distant vessels - those are the stars. These vessels each bear their own goods and their own crews. They usually pass us at some distance, barely visible specks, even viewed through a spyglass. Once every few thousand years, however, a storm breaks above this [cosmic sea](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Multiverse "Multiverse"), a storm so strong it tosses the ships towards one another, making them sail cheek by jowl. Part of the crew of one ship can, at such times, move to another, and some of the cargo from one ship's hold can spill onto a neighboring vessel. When the weather calms, the ships separate once again and sail their separate, invariably different ways.  
The so-called "post-Conjunction beings," namely [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") such as ghouls and [basilisks](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk"), are precisely such passengers from another vessel. And we humans are castaways, flung against our will from somewhere far away onto a world previously inhabited by the [Elder Races](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Races "Elder Races"). Once here, we learned the arcane mysteries of magic. unbeknownst to us before.  
Could the worlds collide once more? Perhaps. Can this cataclysm be avoided, or thet oppposite - hastened? Some scholars believe there are beings who have mastered this skill, who possess rare genes allow which[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] some to seize the helm of our vessel and steer us... to safer waters, or to our doom._

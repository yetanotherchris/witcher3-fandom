**The Northern Wars, Volume II** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but were never attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. 

## Journal entry

    _The[Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") and the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms") crossed swords again in [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline") to [1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline"). Following an eruption of [mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") [treachery](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup") on [Thanedd Island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island"), Nilfgaard could not sit idly by while the North let [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") sow destruction freely. The [Imperial Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") took [Lyria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lyria "Lyria") at lightning speed, meeting with little resistance. After short sieges, our forces marched into [Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rivia_\(city\) "Rivia \(city\)") and [Vengerberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vengerberg "Vengerberg"), greeted each time by jubilant crowds thirsty for peace and civilization. We then moved against [Aedirn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn")._
    _(...) King[Demavend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Demavend_III "Demavend III")'s attempt to stop the Golden Sun's triumphant march failed, and his forces were pushed back to the [Pontar Valley](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_Valley "Pontar Valley") and [Dol Blathanna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dol_Blathanna "Dol Blathanna")._
    _(...) Queen[Meve](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meve "Meve") began to format guerilla bands made up of bandits and outlaws, of which the North has never been short._
    _(...) In a display of typical Nordling treachery, King[Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt") signed a truce with the Empire behind his allies' backs, resulting in Aedirn being promptly partitioned._
    _(...)[His Imperial Majesty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis"), in an act of supreme benevolence, created an [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") state in Dol Blathanna, thus forging a bulwark of tolerance against the abhorrent racist laws of the [Nordlings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nordling "Nordling")._
    _(...) King[Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") of [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") signed a truce with the Empire. Yet this Nordling's word proved to have as much value as the snow melting in the fields that spring. The truce held a mere sixteen days before it was violated. Foltest's stupidity led to the Slaughter of [Brugge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brugge "Brugge"), and then to a number of bloody battles in the [Yaruga](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yaruga "Yaruga") Valley. Faced with senseless and uncontrolled violence, the Imperial Army's leadership took it upon itself to tamp the bloodshed and ordered a retreat to [Sodden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sodden "Sodden")._
    _Sadly, Foltest's greed meant that hostilities resumed with the coming of spring. They culminated with the[Battle of Brenna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Brenna "Battle of Brenna"), where our heroic regiments faced a combined Temerian-[Redanian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") force. To this day the North considers said battle a turning point in [the war](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_II "Northern War II"). There is no point debating this conviction. Far more important is the fact that this battle saw two strategies confronted: an honorable one, presented by the Imperial Army's leadership, and one of trickery and subterfuge, conducted by the Nordlings._
    _(...) His Imperial Majesty realized the direction the conflict had taken. The Northern armies employed scorched-earth tactics, caring not at all for the pain they inflicted on civilian populations. Disgusted and unwilling to take part in this type of war, Nilfgaard withdrew its armies south. The Nordlings mistakenly saw this as an admission of defeat._
    _(...) Through the[Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor")'s mediation, an armistice was drawn up and eventually [signed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_of_Cintra "Peace of Cintra") in [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra"), putting an end to the Second Northern War._

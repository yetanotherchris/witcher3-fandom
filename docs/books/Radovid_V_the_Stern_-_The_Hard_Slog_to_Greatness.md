**Radovid V the Stern - The Hard Slog to Greatness** is a tome roughly describing [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V")'s childhood, way to throne and most recent reign. 

It is guaranteed to appear on a bench near the fountain at the [Var Attre Villa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Var_Attre_Villa "Var Attre Villa") gardens, and should also be available as random loot in some treasure chests and select few bookshelves. However, all such containers seem to have bugged randomization, some of them "favorably" always picking this book, while others in ways that prevent them from selecting it. The only bookshelf known to have it is in [Sigismund's Bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse"), while of the chests one is in the cellar where [Allgod](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Allgod "Allgod") is during [A Greedy God](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Greedy_God "A Greedy God"), and another in one of the locked rooms in [Freya's Garden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar"). 

## Journal entry

    _(...) Which brings us to the epithet of the current ruler of Redania - "the Stern." Allow me to diagnose the causes and consequences of this appellation._
    _Firstly, the seeds of Radovid's Sternness were surely planted in his childhood. When Radovid was born to King[Vizimir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II") and Queen [Hedwig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hedwig_of_Malleore "Hedwig of Malleore") in [1255](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1255 "Timeline"), his care was immediately entrusted to [Philippa Eilhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Philippa_Eilhart "Philippa Eilhart"), the court sorceress. While none dared question if she was fit for the role of tutor, few now envisage her as having been a warm caregiver to a young child - and whatever harsh lessons she gave Radovid clearly left a lasting mark._
    _The next step on Radovid's path to becoming the Stern? The death of his father when Radovid was but 13 years of age. Though rumor has it father and son barely knew one another, the mere fact of being thrust into one's inheritance at such an early age and in such murky circumstances must have had a hardening effect._
    _Finally, the regency finished schooling Radovid towards becoming the Stern. Led by[Sigismund Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra") of [Tretogor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tretogor "Tretogor"), the [Regency Council](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Regency_Council "Regency Council") imparted upon Radovid a style of politics seeped[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] in Dijkstra's aggressive, no-holds-barred, reason-of-state-first principles._
    _Such are the causes of Radovid's temperament - now on to the consequences. Most manifestations of his fierce manner are well known, however, a few illustrative examples might prove useful here. They include his summoning of the[Order of the Flaming Rose](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_of_the_Flaming_Rose "Order of the Flaming Rose") to be his armed enforcers, his later support for the [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter") in their zealous cause, and finally, there is the [massacre](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Summit_at_Loc_Muinne "The Summit at Loc Muinne") at [Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne")..._
    _Yet it is in the fight against[Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") that Radovid has proven himself truly the Stern. While the forces of other realms disintegrated in the face of the empire's onslaught, he has strengthened his own [realm](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") and taken command of the joint armies of the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). Whatever their opinion of him before, all clear-eyed observers now agree: Radovid V, known also as Radovid the Stern, is the North's last bulwark against [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis")'s expanding power._

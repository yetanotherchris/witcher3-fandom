**The First War against Nilfgaard** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but were never attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. 

## Journal entry

    _War with[Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") first broke out in [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1263 "Timeline/Games") \- its immediate cause a clear act of aggression by Nilfgaard against [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra"). The [Imperial Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") crossed the [Amell](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Amell "Amell") Mountains and attacked Cintra's southern border posts near the forest known as [Erlenwald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Erlenwald "Erlenwald"). There are unverified claims of [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") having a special attachment for this place - their import, however, remains unknown. What is certain is that he long ago staked an unfounded claim to control over all Cintra._
    _The decisive battle of the conflict took place in the[Marnadal Valley](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marnadal_Stairs "Marnadal Stairs"). There Nilfgaard surrounded and decimated Cintra's forces led by the royal couple. King [Eist Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eist_Tuirseach "Eist Tuirseach") fell in [the battle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Marnadal "Battle of Marnadal"). The survivors, led by Queen [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe"), sought shelter in the [capital](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra_\(city\) "Cintra \(city\)"), yet none was to be found there. The city was overrun, its population [massacred](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Slaughter_of_Cintra "Slaughter of Cintra"), and Queen Calanthe preferred to take her own life rather than surrender to the invaders._
    _The theater of war then shifted to[Upper Sodden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Upper_Sodden "Upper Sodden"), where acts of terror and barbarous cruelty were perpetrated. Historical consensus holds that the Imperial Army's bestial acts here propelled the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms") to victory during the so-called [Second Battle of Sodden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Sodden_Hill "Battle of Sodden Hill"). The atrocities had left the northern forces with only two options: to win the war, or to die with their families. Desperate determination thus drove them to defeat the seemingly invincible Nilfgaardians. The next year, in [1264](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1264 "Timeline/Games"), an armistice was signed which ceded control of Cintra to the empire, making it the northernmost province of Nilfgaard in all but name._

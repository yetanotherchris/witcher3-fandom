## Item ID

lore_velen

**Lands of the North: Velen** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found as random loot, usually on bookshelves, throughout the entire game. 

However, due to a randomization bug it appears less often than other books of no notable rarity. Because of the same bug, it also seems to consistently appear in [Margrave Henckel's house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margrave_Henckel%27s_house "Margrave Henckel's house"), even though almost all books found there should be random. 

## Journal entry

    _The land of[Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"), located in western [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") with its capital in [Gors Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gors_Velen "Gors Velen"), is one of the poorest provinces in the kingdom. Its territory encompasses the [isle of Thanedd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island"), home to the famous [magic academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aretuza "Aretuza"), which, along with Gors Velen, constitutes the commercial and developmental mainspring of the entire province. Velen is a stop on the [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") trade route running through [Cidaris](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cidaris "Cidaris"), [Vergen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vergen "Vergen"), [Brugge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brugge "Brugge"), [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") and other such southerly realms._
    _Veleners subside primarily on agriculture, crafting and animal husbandry. The province is practically deprived of all natural resources. It contains a great deal of forests, wetlands and cultivated woods, though the greatest part of it is covered in swamps and bogs (...)._

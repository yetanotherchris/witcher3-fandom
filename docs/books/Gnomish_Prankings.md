## Item ID

Horse vol 2

**Gnomish Prankings** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be found as random loot on most bookshelves and some wooden chests throughout the entire game, or purchased from the [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Putrid_Grove\) "Merchant \(Putrid Grove\)") in [Putrid Grove](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Putrid_Grove "Putrid Grove"). 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can be found as random loot inside rich houses around [Beauclair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauclair "Beauclair"). 

## Journal entry

    _Scrub the greasings from a[dog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dog "Dog")'s ear, soak into cotton twine, place in a new lamp of greenish hue and set said lamp betwixt an eager crowd - forsooth shall they swear that a dog's head they behold, and this shall be no [sorcery](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"), but good betidings._
    _For the[prince](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") of [Ellander](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ellander "Ellander")'s nuptials, a paltry [gnome](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gnome "Gnome") armed with a miniature cutlass behid himself in a pie. When guests partook of the princely banquet, anon jumped out of the besabred gnome, giving a terrible fright to all at first, then causing much merriment once the jest was figured._
    _A[cynocephalus](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cynocephalus "Cynocephalus"), or a doghead, in our tongue, a beast that in the wastes of [Zanguebar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zanguebar "Zanguebar") dwells, has the corpus of a man but the head of a dog. The Prince of Ellander did receive such a specimen from those far-off lands. The cynocephalus lets stream its urine with the tolling of every hour, both day and night, and this is why the Zanguebarians engrave its likeness on timepieces and compasses._

## Notes

  * In early development of the game, certain "perk" abilities were meant to be acquired by reading books such as this one. The idea was eventually [scrapped](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content"), but this particular book would've provided the ability to strike opponents while riding a [horse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse").

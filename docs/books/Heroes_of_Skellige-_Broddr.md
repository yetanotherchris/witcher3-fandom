**Heroes of Skellige: Broddr** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that describes the story of [Broddr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Broddr "Broddr"), the legendary founder of [Clan Dimun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Dimun "Clan Dimun"). 

It can only be found during the secondary quest [King's Gambit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King%27s_Gambit "King's Gambit"), if taking [Cerys an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cerys_an_Craite "Cerys an Craite")'s path and letting [Arnvald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arnvald "Arnvald") escape/die. This book can be found laying on his bed while searching his chambers as a later part of the quest. 

## Journal entry

    _Broddr, founder of Clan Dimun, whose members dwell on the Isle of[Faroe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Faroe "Faroe"), was an extraordinarily good and fair man, giving advice and help to all who came to him. He loathed looting and deception and never told a lie - in short, he was a paragon of virtue. He ruled his people wisely and lived to a venerable age. At the dusk of his life he was summoned before [Hemdall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hemdall "Hemdall"), but a whale devoured his longship and he never reached his journey's end. Ever since, descendants of his line have hunted [whales](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Whale "Whale") in search of the bones of their great and beloved ancestor._

## Notes

  * Upon finding the book, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and Cerys have unique dialogue about it, but it is completely mismatched, referring instead to the [song](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ballad_of_Bran_the_Conqueror "Ballad of Bran the Conqueror") of [Bran the Conqueror](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran_Tuirseach_\(the_Conqueror\) "Bran Tuirseach \(the Conqueror\)") and how he was a hero to his [clan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tuirseach "Clan Tuirseach") (as circumstantial evidence Arnvald to Clan Tuirseach). Hence, another book was likely meant to be in its place.
  * The book is also supposed to be sold by a [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Harviken\) "Merchant \(Harviken\)") in [Harviken](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harviken "Harviken"), who however never appears.

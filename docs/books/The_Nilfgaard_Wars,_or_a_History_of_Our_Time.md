## Item ID

lore_third_war_with_nilfgaard

**The Nilfgaard Wars, or a History of Our Time** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found on top of a cabinet inside [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite")'s room at the [Kaer Trolde citadel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_citadel "Kaer Trolde citadel"). 

It should also be available as random loot on some bookshelves, particularly in [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire")-related locations. However, likely due to oversight, only two such bookshelves were ever placed, not anywhere near [Nilfgaardians](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_\(people\) "Nilfgaardian \(people\)"), and due to a randomization bug they always select another specific book. 

## Journal entry

    _It came as a surprise to many when, a mere three years after the[Second War against Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_II "Northern War II") \- the war which, it was said, "would end all wars" - the empire's [forces](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") once again invaded the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). To the perceptive student of history, however, this development was not at all surprising - Nilfgaard's economy and political system have for decades relied on expansion and conquest for continued survival._
    _Attentive analysis shows that Nilfgaard never abandoned its plan to subjugate the North. Contrary to popular belief, imperial diplomats, and not[sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage"), were behind the death of [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest"). They also brought about the crisis during the [summit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Summit_at_Loc_Muinne "The Summit at Loc Muinne") of mages and monarchs at [Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne"). The current conflict is thus the result of cold calculation and long-term planning. Before the wax had time to dry on the [Peace of Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_of_Cintra "Peace of Cintra"), [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") had already begun to prepare for the [next war](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_III "Northern War III"), which ultimately broke out in the spring of [1270](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1270 "Timeline/Games")._
    _The war's first stage went exactly as the[emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") had planned. In a matter of months [Lyria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lyria "Lyria"), [Aedirn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn") and [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") found themselves in the shadow of the black banner. If not for the early frosts of December [1270](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1270 "Timeline/Games") and the daring of King [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") \- who, instead of concentrating his forces on the [Pontar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_\(river\) "Pontar \(river\)"), invaded [Kaedwen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaedwen "Kaedwen") thereby doubling his territory and strength - all the North would surely now be but another province in the vast Empire of Nilfgaard._

## Notes

  * The year mentioned in this book is inaccurate as the entirety of _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ takes place during [1271](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1271 "Timeline/Games"), and Nilfgaard declares war at the end of the game.
  * There is another book related to this one still in the game files, titled "The Wars against Nilfgaard" (ID: 'lore_wars_with_nilfgaard'), that was supposed to be found on Crach's nightstand. However, it's been either never fully finished or intentionally [removed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") from the game and its contents are empty. Judging by its item ID, it was likely supposed to summarize the contents of two other cut content books: "[The First War against Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_First_War_against_Nilfgaard "The First War against Nilfgaard")" and "[The Second War against Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Second_War_against_Nilfgaard "The Second War against Nilfgaard")".

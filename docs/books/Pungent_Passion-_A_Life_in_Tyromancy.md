**Pungent Passion: A Life in Tyromancy** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It should be available as random loot in approximately 15 locations, but due to a randomization bug, it never appears and thus can only be obtained through console commands. 

## Journal entry

    _Chapter 1._
    _More than curds!_
    _Contrary to popular belief,[tyromancy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tyromancy "Tyromancy") does not only consist of cutting cheese and examining the result. Adherents of this ancient but now forgotten and unfairly disregarded branch of the [divining arts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Divinations "Category:Divinations") know that the slice and peek, as we call it, is only the beginning._
    _Once a student has mastered the fundamentals of first degree tyromancy, he should be encouraged to better his olfactory capabilities, with the aid of[magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"), if needs be. Once he has made satisfactory progress in this regard, he should be confronted with a well-aged and thoroughly-ripe blue cheese, preferably in an enclosed space. The first days of this training might prove stormy. Discipline is especially vital at this stage - by no means should you respond to the student's pleas and open the larder doors._
    _Chapter 2._
    _On the superiority of tyromancy_
    _Though tyromancy's advantages vis a vis the other forms of divining are self-evident to me, I have gone to the trouble of a thorough comparison of its results and can with great certitude say that cheese-induced visions are much more reliable than, say, those brought about by chiromancy, aleuromancy or the current craze, rumpology (those interested in that may refer to "Rumpology – the Art of Divining from Buttocks. With Illustrations!" by[Artorius Viggo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Artorius_Vigo "Artorius Vigo")[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")])._

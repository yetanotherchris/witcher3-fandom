# The Northern Wars. Myths, Lies and Half-truths

**The Northern Wars. Myths, Lies, and Half-truths** is a critical treatment of the history of the [Northern Wars](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Wars "Northern Wars") written by [Restif de Montholon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Restif_de_Montholon "Restif de Montholon"). 

## Excerpt

The trial, sentence and execution of [Joachim de Wett](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Joachim_de_Wett "Joachim de Wett") is usually ascribed by most historians to the violent, cruel and tyrannical nature of [Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr "Emhyr"), and neither is there any shortage either–particularly amongst authors of a literary bent–of allusive hypotheses about revenge and the settling of wholely private scores. It is high time the truth were told: the truth, which for every attentive scholar is more than obvious. [Duke](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duke "Duke") de Wett commanded the [Verden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Verden "Verden") Group in such a way that the term 'inept' is much too mild. Having against him forces twice as weak as his own, he delayed the offensive to the north, and directed all his efforts towards a fight against the Verdanian guerrillas. The Verden Group committed unspeakable atrocities against the civilian population. The result was easy to predict and was inevitable. If, in the winter, the forces of the insurgents had numbered less than half a thousand, by spring almost the entire country had risen up. [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Ervyll](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ervyll "Ervyll"), who had been devoted to the Empire, was eliminated, and the insurrection was led by his son, [Prince](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") [Kistrin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kistrin "Kistrin"), who sympathised with the Nordlings. Having on his flank a landing force of pirates from [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), to the fore an offensive of Nordlings from [Cidaris](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cidaris "Cidaris"), and at the rear a rebellion, de Wett became entangled in piecemeal engagements, suffering defeat after defeat. In the process he delayed the offensive of the Centre Army Group–instead of, as had been planned, engaging the Nordlings' wing, the Verden Group tied down [Menno Coehoorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Menno_Coehoorn "Menno Coehoorn"). The Nordlings immediately exploited the situation and went on the counter-attack, breaking through the encirclement near [Mayena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mayena "Mayena") and [Maribor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor "Maribor"), and thwarting the chances of those vital forts being swiftly captured a second time. 

The ineptitude and stupidity of de Wett also had a psychological significance. The myth of Nilfgaard's invincibility was broken. Scores of volunteers began to flock to the army of the Nordlings ...

**The Wars of the North: Myths, Lies and Half-Truths** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found at [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen")'s inner courtyard, in an outhouse toilet south of the keep entrance, near where [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") was training in the first quest, [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen_\(quest\) "Kaer Morhen \(quest\)"). 

While this book will show in red and have white sparkles when using [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses"), and is technically associated with [The Battle of Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Battle_of_Kaer_Morhen "The Battle of Kaer Morhen"), it doesn't have a purpose other than [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") speaking about it when it is picked up. 

### Journal entry

    _Most historians ascribe the trial, sentencing and execution of[Joachim de Wett](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Joachim_de_Wett "Joachim de Wett") to the violent, cruel and tyrannical nature of [Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr "Emhyr"), although many authors with a predilection for sensationalizing propose loaded hypotheses about vengeance and the settling of thoroughly private scores. It is high time to tell the truth — a truth that is and has long been more than clear to every scholar._
    _Duke de Wett led the[Verden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Verden "Verden") Group. To describe the manner in which he did so as "incompetent" would be far too kind. Facing a force half as strong as his own, he delayed the northern offensive, turning all his attention to the fight against the guerillas of Verden._
    _The Verden Group brought unheard of cruelties to bear against the civilian population. The result proved as predictable as it was inevitable - whereas in winter the insurgents numbered a mere half thousand, by spring nearly the entire country had taken up arms against us.[King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Ervyll](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ervyll "Ervyll"), a loyal liege subject to the Empire, was killed, and his son, Crown [Prince](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") [Kistrin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kistrin "Kistrin"), who sympathized with the Nordlings, was made king._
    _With[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") raiders biting at his flanks, a Nordling offensive coming from [Cidaris](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cidaris "Cidaris") before him, and a rebellion at his rear, de Wett became bogged down in a chaotic struggle and suffered one defeat after another. This in turn delayed Army Group Center's offensive — instead of pinning down the Nordlings' flanks as it was to, the Verden Group tied down [Menno Coehoorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Menno_Coehoorn "Menno Coehoorn"). The Nordlings took advantage of the situation immediately. They mounted a counter-offensive, breaking through the ring of forces encircling [Mayena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mayena "Mayena") and [Maribor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor "Maribor"), and thwarting any chance for the quick recapture of these important fortresses. De Wett's incompetence ineptitude and stupidity also affected morale. The myth of the invincible [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") was shattered. New recruits began to pour into the armies of the North by the hundreds..._

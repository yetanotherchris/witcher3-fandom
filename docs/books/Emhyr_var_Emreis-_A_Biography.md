**Emhyr var Emreis: A Biography** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found on a nightstand in the [Var Attre Villa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Var_Attre_Villa "Var Attre Villa"), on the second floor. 

It should also be available as random loot on some bookshelves, particularly in [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire")-related locations. However, likely due to oversight, only 2 such bookshelves were ever placed, not anywhere near [Nilfgaardians](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_\(people\) "Nilfgaardian \(people\)"), and due to a randomization bug they always select another specific book. 

## Journal entry

    _Childhood and Early Manhood_
    _Though the Nilfgaardian throne was his by right of birth, it would be many long years before[Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") sat upon it. When he was but thirteen, an uprising engulfed the empire's [capital](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaard_\(city\) "Nilfgaard \(city\)"). A [usurper](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Usurper "Usurper") seized power, overthrowing Emhyr's father, [Fergus var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fergus_var_Emreis "Fergus var Emreis"), who then refused to grant legitimacy to the coup. The usurper then decided he would break Fergus by having [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") torture his son and heir, Emhyr. He failed in his plan, was forced to kill Fergus, and Emhyr escaped, presumably to a land beyond the empire's borders._
    _No reliable accounts of the emperor's actions in exile exist. Some claim he fell into the company of a[knight](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Knight "Knight") errant, serving as his squire. This seems plausible enough, though the tales about the eccentric knight's proclivity to charge at windmills must surely be storytellers' exaggerations. A few unreliable reports claim a connection between Emhyr and the fabled [Urcheon of Erlenwald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duny "Duny") who saved the life of King [Roegner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roegner_of_Ebbing "Roegner of Ebbing") of [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra"). This seems far less likely, for it is public record that when the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") lifted the Urcheon's curse, he was revealed to be a duke named Duny, who later wed Roegner's daughter, [Pavetta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pavetta "Pavetta"), and perished at her side when their ship went down at sea._
    _What is known for certain is that around the time of this great tragedy for the kingdom of Cintra, Emhyr reappeared in the empire and led a revolt to overthrow the usurper and restore his own birthright. Soon after he led the[empire's armies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") in their successful [conquest of Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Slaughter_of_Cintra "Slaughter of Cintra"), setting a tone of determined military expansion that would endure through the rest of his reign._

## Trivia

  * The "eccentric knight's proclivity to charge at windmills" is a reference to [Miguel de Cervantes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_de_Cervantes "wikipedia:Miguel de Cervantes")' [Don Quixote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Quixote "wikipedia:Don Quixote").

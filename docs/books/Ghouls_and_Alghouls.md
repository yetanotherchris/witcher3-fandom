## Item ID

Necrophage vol 1

**Ghouls and Alghouls** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. Reading it provides the [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entries for [ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul"), [alghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alghoul "Alghoul"), [drowners](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner"), and [rotfiends](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rotfiend "Rotfiend"), as well as the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Alghoul_decoction "Torn-out page: Alghoul decoction") for [alghoul decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alghoul_decoction "Alghoul decoction"). 

It can be purchased from the following [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"): 

  * [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") at [his shop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Books_and_Scrolls "Books and Scrolls") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square")
  * Books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in Hierarch Square
  * [Merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Fish_Market\) "Merchant \(Fish Market\)") at the [Fish Market](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fish_Market "Fish Market")
  * [Halfling merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(halfling\) "Merchant \(halfling\)") in [Silverton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silverton "Silverton")

The book should also be available as random loot, but due to a randomization bug never appears as such. 

## Journal entry

    _As for the genesis of ghouls, there are hypotheses aplenty. Some scholars claim these monstrosities arose from scoundrels who indulged in a taste for[human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") flesh and for this misdeed drew the wrath of the heavens down upon themselves. The gods punished them by taking away their souls, their minds and their human forms._
    _My experiments have shown, however, that ghoul anatomy displays far too little in common with that of humans for this thesis to seem at all probable. Any and all similarity in appearance between ghouls and men, such as their somewhat kindred shapes and the measures of their physiognomies, are pure matters of incidental circumstance. It thus follows that ghouls, like their vile cousins the alghouls, are post-[Conjunction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Conjunction_of_the_Spheres "Conjunction of the Spheres") creatures – that is to say, such beings as came to our universe in an abrupt cataclysm disrupting the normal laws of nature._

## Notes

  * The author, [John of Brugge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/John_of_Brugge "John of Brugge"), is highly regarded by [Vesemir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vesemir "Vesemir"). In the [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen_\(quest\) "Kaer Morhen \(quest\)") quest, he had [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") reading the book, but she ran off while he was asleep because the book was "horribly dull" as described by her. Vesemir makes the comment to [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") that "John of Brugge lacks flair, true, but he's reliable".
  * This book was also at some point intended to be sold by either [Tomira](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tomira "Tomira"), the roadside shrine [herbalist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Herbalist_\(Shrine\) "Herbalist \(Shrine\)") or possibly another [herbalist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Herbalist "Herbalist") in [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard"), for whom an entire store inventory list still exists in the game files, but is unused.
  * The bestiary entry for ghouls is superfluous as Geralt already has it at the start of [Lilac and Gooseberries](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lilac_and_Gooseberries "Lilac and Gooseberries").

## Trivia

  * Alghouls are compared to the "_panthera tigris_ that in [Zerrikania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikania "Zerrikania") dwells". _Panthera tigris_ is the binomial nomenclature for the tiger.

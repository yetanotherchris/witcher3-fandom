**The Second War against Nilfgaard** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but were never attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. 

## Journal entry

    _Unlike in the[First War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_I "Northern War I") against [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"), there was no clear aggressor at the start of the [second conflict](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_II "Northern War II"). In early [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline") the kings of the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms") met at [Hagge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hagge "Hagge") to prepare a plan for [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra")'s reconquest. Foreseeing their intentions, [Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") [var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") amassed a large expeditionary force on the [Temerian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") border. He then masterminded a series of events that left the North ripe for invasion - a [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage")' [revolt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup") erupted on [Thanedd Island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island"), and [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") descended into chaos after King [Vizimir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II") was assassinated. All Emhyr needed now was a pretext, which he got when supposedly [Aedirnian soldiers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirnian_Army "Aedirnian Army") attacked the Nilfgaardian frontier fortress known as [Glevitzingen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Glevitzingen "Glevitzingen"). Armed with this excuse, [Nilfgaardian forces](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") marched north and thus began the second war._
    _During the war's first phase, the imperial offensive quickly swept across the North. The empire's army started by occupying southern[Lyria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lyria "Lyria"). [Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rivia "Rivia") was next to fall, after a short siege. Then came the [Battle of Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/First_battle_of_Aldersberg "First battle of Aldersberg"), followed by [Menno Coehoorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Menno_Coehoorn "Menno Coehoorn")'s march into [Vengerberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vengerberg "Vengerberg"). King [Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt") of [Kaedwen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaedwen "Kaedwen") seized this opportunity to dismember his neighbor and promptly signed a treaty with Nilfgaard partitioning [Aedirn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn") between them. Opportunistic [elves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") also joined the empire's cause and for their trouble gained an independent realm in [Dol Blathanna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dol_Blathanna "Dol Blathanna"). King [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") was the next rat to scurry from the sinking ship, signing a truce with Nilfgaard and grabbing for himself a portion of western Aedirn. Abandoned by his allies and left with the rump of his kingdom, [Demavend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Demavend_III "Demavend III"), ruler of Aedirn, fled to seek shelter in Redania._
    _The war's first stage ended and its second stage began when Nilfgaard broke its truce with Temeria after a mere fortnight and two days. Its forces immediately entered[Brugge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brugge "Brugge") and [Sodden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sodden "Sodden"), and soon both lands were awash in blood. In short order Emhyr occupied all of southern Temeria and [shattered](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Maribor "Battle of Maribor") Foltest's [troops](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temerian_Army "Temerian Army") at [Maribor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor_\(city\) "Maribor \(city\)"). The shrewd emperor then retreated for the winter to Sodden, where he prepared for a renewed offensive come spring._
    _No war in history saw such fierce battles as were waged during the great Temerian offensive undertaken over those fair spring days. The forces of the North, united at last, proved a worthy opponent and slowed the Nilfgaardian advance. This provided Emhyr's internal enemies with an opening to set in motion a plot designed to depose him. Weakened by the infighting (known as the Conspiracy of Nobles), the myth of its invincibility shattered, Nilfgaard then saw its[mightiest army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Center_Army_Group "Center Army Group") [routed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Brenna "Battle of Brenna") by northern forces near [Brenna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brenna "Brenna"). The tide of war had clearly turned._
    _Thus began the war's fourth and final phase. The North embarked on a counter-offensive, forcing the Imperial Army into a slow and bloody retreat. It was only a matter of time before armistice talks began, temporary truce lines were drawn, and the war drew to a close. Delegates from all parties to the conflict convened in Cintra to draft a peace treaty and establish a new world order. On the 2nd of April,[1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline"), the [Peace of Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_of_Cintra "Peace of Cintra") was signed, bringing to an end the Second War against Nilfgaard._

**Monumenta Elforum** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be purchased from the [Scoia'tael merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Scoia%27tael\) "Merchant \(Scoia'tael\)") south of [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") and can be found as loot in only 2 locations - in Mad [Kiyan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kiyan "Kiyan")'s room during [Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Cat_School_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear"), and (guaranteed due to a randomization bug) in the [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") room occupied first by [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") and then [Avallac'h](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Avallac%27h "Avallac'h"). The chest that has it there only appears at the start of [The Battle of Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Battle_of_Kaer_Morhen "The Battle of Kaer Morhen") though. 

## Journal entry

    _To my beloved[Y](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg"). I still await your kestrel.  
[Istredd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Istredd "Istredd") of [Aedd Gynvael](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedd_Gynvael "Aedd Gynvael")_
    _Let us begin, dear reader, with a riddle. What links a town like[Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima") the proud capital of [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria"), or [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"), a center of learning for all the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/North "North"), with Aedd Gynvael, a provincial settlement at the end of the know world? Nothing, it would seem... After all, each of these places looks, sounds and even smells different. Their origins, however, are identical — all were founded atop ancient [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") cities. The ruins sprawling beneath their streets bear witnesss[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] to this fact.  
A tour of elven monuments is best begun in Vizima - in the [city sewers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima_sewers "Vizima sewers"), to be precise. A dangerous place, to be sure, so it is worth bringing along a well-armed guide. A branch of the sewers leads to the remains of an [Aen Seidhe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Seidhe "Aen Seidhe") city. One can still admire its masterfully carved plafonds, peer into its immaculately polished marble facings, find artifacts discarded by the Elder Race and hold ancient tomes that disintegrate in one's hands. Equally impressive monuments of elven culture can be found only in Oxenfurt — as well as somewhere in the wilds of [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"), though I have been unable to verify this information personally.  
What caused elven civilization to collapes? Plague? Cataclysm? No, dear reader. Their destruction was caused by us, by [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human"). We drove the elves from their cities and, instead of occupying their marvelous palaces, we dismantled them to build our meager and crooked huts. All that remains is that which we covered in debris and sludge, and then wiped from our memories for centuries._

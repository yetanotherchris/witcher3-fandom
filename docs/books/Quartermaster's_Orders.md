## Item ID

mq1051_orders_note

**Quartermaster's Orders** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") document in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It was likely intended to be a [quest item](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_quest_items "The Witcher 3 quest items"), obviously related to [Contract: Patrol Gone Missing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Contract:_Patrol_Gone_Missing "Contract: Patrol Gone Missing"). However, it's been removed for unknown reasons and doesn't appear during gameplay, though still in the game files and can be obtained through console commands. 

## Journal entry

    _From the office of the quartermaster of the[Imperial Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") Group 'Center' [camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army_Group_%27Center%27_Camp "Nilfgaardian Army Group 'Center' Camp")_
    _Order No. 22_
    _I. I hereby order immediate implementation of the solution to the[Redanian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") prisoners-of-war problem._
    _II. Extermination shall be carried out in keeping with established guidelines._
    _III. Within two day's time a report must be delivered verifying the execution of this order._
    _Quartermaster[Tavar Eggebracht](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tavar_Eggebracht "Tavar Eggebracht")_

## Trivia

  * The phrasing _"solution to the Redanian prisoners-of-war problem"_ , especially considering that Eggebracht is a distinctly Germanic name, could be drawing parallels to Nazi Germany's well-known "solution to the Jewish problem".

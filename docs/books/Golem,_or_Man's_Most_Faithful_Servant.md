**Golem, or Man's Most Faithful Servant** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. When first read, it adds a [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entry for [golems](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Golem "Golem"). 

It can be purchased from [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") or the books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"), and should also be available as random loot, but due to a randomization bug it never appears as such. 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can be found in the dungeons of [Mont Crane Castle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mont_Crane_Castle "Mont Crane Castle") during [Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Grandmaster_Griffin_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Grandmaster Griffin Gear"), in a chest along with [Moreau's journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moreau%27s_journal "Moreau's journal") and copies of [Witchers: Not Quite the Devils You Thought](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witchers:_Not_Quite_the_Devils_You_Thought "Witchers: Not Quite the Devils You Thought") and [Monstrum, or a Portrayal of Witchers. Volume 1](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monstrum,_or_a_Portrayal_of_Witchers._Volume_1 "Monstrum, or a Portrayal of Witchers. Volume 1"). 

## Journal entry

    _Despite what you might have heard, the first golem was created not by a powerful[mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") or an [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") [Sage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sage "Sage"), but by a simple [dwarf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf") from [Maribor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor "Maribor") named Bonaventura Sesto - a brickmaker._
    _The fateful act of creation took place over a century ago, when the persecution of[nonhumans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nonhuman "Nonhuman") was in full swing in Maribor. Elves and dwarves had been accused of bringing an epidemic to the [city](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor_\(city\) "Maribor \(city\)") \- though it was later discovered the outbreak had been caused by a man named Mattheo, a barkeep's dogsbody at the Golden Goose Inn. This Mattheo was charged with both serving meals and cleaning the latrines, and would only wash his hands once a month, and then not very thoroughly. The rest, as they say, is history._
    _The persecution of nonhumans was limited at first to robbing their shops, arson and the occasional beating, but the violence soon escalated into a bloody[pogrom](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pogrom "Pogrom"). As the city guard looked on without lifting a finger, [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") murdered elves and dwarves during three days and three nights of slaughter, killing over three hundred individuals, including the aforementioned Bonaventura Sesto's entire family._
    _Driven mad with grief and thirsting for vengeance, Bonaventura molded a ten-foot-tall humanoid out of clay, and then carved the names of the slain nonhumans onto it, along with a smattering of dwarven curses. Once finished, he slit his own throat - and when his blood splattered the gigantic statue, it came alive and started walking towards the city. Bonaventura had his sweet revenge on his persecutors - his creation killed almost five hundred humans, including the mayor and the entire city council, before the soldiers of the city garrison could take it out using their[ballista](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ballista "Ballista")._
    _[The Conclave](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers")'s later investigation found that Bonaventura had possessed hidden [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") talent. Though he had never had any training, the torrent of powerful emotions running through him at the time of his death flushed out his latent powers and created from them a new spell. After numerous alterations and improvements, one can now cast this spell without sacrificing one's life - and golems guard laboratories, libraries and palaces across the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent")._

## Trivia

  * Mattheo's story is likely a reference to the story of [Rabbi Judah Loew ben Bezalel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judah_Loew_ben_Bezalel "wikipedia:Judah Loew ben Bezalel"), a 16th century rabbi of Prague, who created a [golem to protect the Jewish people of Prague from a pogrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golem#Classic_narrative:_The_Golem_of_Prague "wikipedia:Golem").

**Brother Adalbert's bestiary** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Despite what the title might imply, reading this book doesn't add any entries to the [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary"). However, the excerpt about [griffins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)") is followed accurately during [The Beast of White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beast_of_White_Orchard "The Beast of White Orchard"), and the one about [forktails](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forktail "Forktail") is literally quoted during [To Bait a Forktail...](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/To_Bait_a_Forktail... "To Bait a Forktail...") There are, unfortunately, no drunk [trolls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Troll "Troll") seen in the game. 

A copy of it can always be found in the basement of the southesternmost house in [Mulbrydale](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mulbrydale "Mulbrydale"), and it can appear as random loot on many bookshelves. 

## Journal entry

    _Each beast herein described in two manners ye may hunt. Either seek ye tracks, follow and strike the foulness wherever in its wild ye find it, or lure it to a chosen place and engage it there in advantageous battle. The latter poses less risk, for the hunter exposes no chance for an unexpected fight. Note, however, that to lure a beast into a trap, first its tastes and behaviors ye must know. As for forktails, bait them thusly: pound a stake in the soil, bind a[goat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Goat "Goat") to it, then hide ye in nearto shrubbery posthaste. The beast, lured by the caprine odor, will without fail soon come. If it is a griffin ye seek to slay, take ye [buckthorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Buckthorn "Buckthorn") from deep water. The mighty stench of this herb is to griffins like fresh-roasted meat of the primest sort, and so come they will forthwith, though faraway they might be. Turning to [drowners](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner") and [water hags](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water_hag "Water hag"), it is known to all and sundry that fish and rotting mollusks are their loves, and as for trolls, common hooch is their weakness._

**Beliefs of Skellige: Druids** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found on the deck near lift downstairs from the [Bridge to Kaer Trolde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bridge_to_Kaer_Trolde "Bridge to Kaer Trolde"). It may also appear as random loot, but that is very rare. 

## Journal entry

    _The disciples of the Isles' local pantheon are not the sole spiritual devotees in[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), for some [druids](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid") also call the archipelago home. Yet this is no cause for conflict, for [Freya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Freya "Freya")'s [adherents](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Freya "Temple of Freya") and the [Druids' Circle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druids%27_Circle "Druids' Circle") coexist and treat each other with great respect. What welds these two [faiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Religion "Religion") together? A common worship of the divinity found in the earth, fertility, love and birth. To the druids, men form a part of nature and should treat the world around them with the utmost respect. They hold that since nature gave life, her most precious gift, to [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") and the other civilized [races](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Races "Elder Races"), they should live in harmony with her, yielding their will to hers._
    _Druids are seen as wise men and[priests](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priest "Priest") are held in high esteem by society. They act as spiritual guides, [herbalists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Herbalist "Herbalist") and [alchemists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemist "Alchemist"). Skelligers seek their council in times of trouble, summon them when ill and ask them to settle thorny conflicts. Like their [continental](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent") brethren, the druids of Skellige have a Circle - an area set aside in their [holy grove](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gedyneith "Gedyneith") where they assemble, meditate and study sacred tomes. Many druids are capable of wielding [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic")._

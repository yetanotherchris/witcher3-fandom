**The Aen Seidhe and the Aen Elle** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

One copy of it can be found next to the chest with [Morkvarg's journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morkvarg%27s_journal "Morkvarg's journal") in a locked room at [Freya's Garden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar"). If [Hjalmar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hjalmar "Hjalmar") and/or [Vernon Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vernon_Roche "Vernon Roche") and [Ves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ves "Ves") are recruited for [The Battle of Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Battle_of_Kaer_Morhen "The Battle of Kaer Morhen"), it can also be found on tables near them, suggesting that they're reading the book to prepare for facing the Wild Hunt. 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can also be found as random loot on bookshelves in houses around [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), or purchased from the [Ducal Camerlengo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rafael_de_Surmann "Rafael de Surmann"). 

## Journal entry

    _Sad as it may be, to many in our time the word "[elf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf")" is a synonym for pauper, bandit or layabout. "She-elf" is used by many to denote a prostitute or woman of loose morals. The adjective "elven", in turn, describes damaged, needlessly complicated or useless goods. Statistics, for their part, show that one in three elves living in [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") has spent time in prison, and a full one half of them have been fined at least once. The average lifespan of elves, though still thrice that of [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human"), grows shorter each year._
    _Given the above it is easy to forget that elves - or[Aen Seidhe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Seidhe "Aen Seidhe"), as they fashion themselves - were once a proud race that ruled the lands stretching from the banks of the [Great Sea](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Great_Sea "Great Sea") in the west to the [Blue Mountains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blue_Mountains "Blue Mountains") in the east, and from the [Dragon Mountains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon_Mountains "Dragon Mountains") in the North to the [Mahakam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mahakam "Mahakam") range in the south. The ruins of their cities scattered throughout the known world bear witness to their former might (cp. "[Monumenta Elforum](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monumenta_Elforum "Monumenta Elforum")" by [Istredd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Istredd "Istredd") of [Aedd Gynvael](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedd_Gynvael "Aedd Gynvael"))._  
_Many of the most outstanding mages, artists and poets of recorded history have been of this race. And we have elves to thank for dozens of ingenuous items we rely on each day, from screw pumps to cosmetics. Those elves who dwell amongst men have largely forgotten their history and culture - the sole sliver of elven identity left in them being a burning hatred for humans, whom they refer to as dh'oine. The elves of[Dol Blathanna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dol_Blathanna "Dol Blathanna"), that puppet vassal of [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"), and of the wild highlands of the Blue Mountains have retained much of the old knowledge and culture, though they, too, are condemned to perish. This death sentence was handed to them by biology - for men, though short-lived, are several times more fertile than elves. Thus, while the Aen Seidhe's numbers continue to dwindle, ours grow at an alarming and ever-increasing rate._  
_Some elves believe that the tide of events can be turned, that they can put a stop to human expansion and, ultimately, to their oppression by men. They look to their mythical cousins for salvation - to the[Aen Elle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Elle "Aen Elle"), the Alder Folk. The Aen Elle are said to dwell in [another world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tir_n%C3%A1_Lia "Tir ná Lia") or possibly another plane to which they traveled during the mythic [Age of Migration](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Age_of_Migration "Age of Migration"), and from which they at times journey to visit our world. Legend claims a gate between the worlds could be opened, allowing the Aen Elle to ride to their downtrodden brethren's rescue. These are, however, mere fables, naïve fairy tales this race condemned to extinction uses to provide itself small comfort in these, their last, sad moments._

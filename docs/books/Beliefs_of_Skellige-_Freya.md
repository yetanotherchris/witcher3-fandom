**Beliefs of Skellige: Freya** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found on the ground in front of the northwestern house in [Lofoten](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lofoten "Lofoten"), or purchased from the [Freya Priestess](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Freya_Priestess "Freya Priestess") at the [temple](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Freyja "Temple of Freyja") east of [Freya's garden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar"). 

It can also be found as random loot, but is quite uncommon as such, though a skeleton on the bigger of 2 isles just north of [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog") is known to often have it. 

## Journal entry

    _As is true of most realms, the predominant religion in the[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") Isles has its roots in the prehistoric cult of the Great Mother, Mother Nature. On the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"), such worship has taken as its object and namesake [Melitele](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Melitele "Melitele"). In Skellige, her counterpart is [Freya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Freya "Freya").  
Like Melitele, Freya is represented in three aspects - virgin, mother and old crone. That of mother is most common, and sculptors chose to depict her thus in her greatest sanctuary in the Isles. There she stands, a pregnant woman draped in loose robes, her face partially revealed, her head bent and her hands folded across her breast. A golden necklace hangs around her neck, and on it a large, pure diamond ([Brisingamen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brisingamen "Brisingamen")) shines like a clear summer sky.  
Freyja Modron, or Freya the Great Mother, is the goddess of fertility, love, beauty and abundance. She is also the patron of oracles, soothsayers and telepaths. Warriors pray to her before setting out on sea raids, and the wives they leave behind pray to her for their husbands' safe return. Only priestesses serve the goddess – men may worship Freya, but only women may do her work. Freya's priestesses, like the clerics of other cults, treat mages and sorceresses with great reserve.  
The center of Freya's worship is her [temple](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Freya "Temple of Freya") on the isle of [Hindarsfjall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindarsfjall "Hindarsfjall"), in the sacred grove called [Hindar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar"). Worshippers place offerings to the goddess before her statue, on an altar that incorporates a great stone basin surrounded by figurines of cats and falcons - her sacred animals. In addition to this temple, sprinkled throughout the isles are other, smaller places where one can worship the goddess and make offerings to her (...)._

## Item ID

lore_journals_from_urskar_6

**Warrior's notes** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

It is the 6th of 7 books collectively known as "journals from [Urskar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Urskar "Urskar")", detailing what led to [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik")'s destruction. Small bits from that still remain in dialogues during [The Lord of Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Lord_of_Undvik "The Lord of Undvik") quest, but the overall story and a lot of other content related to the island have been removed from the game. 

## Journal entry

    _Ill news has come in from the south. Hambald One-Eye's expedition was ambushed in Adaria Bay, off the northwestern coast of[Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"). Admiral Shirac's fleet surrounded two longships. But he took no prisoners. They all died. No one surrendered or asked for mercy. Witnesses claim it all went down in absolute silence. Only sound the clanging of weapons and whistling of arrows. Didn't even scream as they died._
    _I'm sure you're thinking, "If no one survived, you couldn't know all that." Aye, I can. Last week a patrol of our lads came across the[Black One](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army")'s ship. They couldn't attack, but at night they snuck aboard and loosened some tongues. Turned out the sailor they pushed up against the fire had taken part in the battle in the bay. Told them everything. Thanks to him the [skalds](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skald "Skald") will sing praises of Undvik's warriors' heroic deeds till the end of time._

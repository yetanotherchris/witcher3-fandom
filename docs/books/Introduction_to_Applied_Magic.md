## Item ID

lore_basics_of_magic

**Introduction to Applied Magic** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can only be found as random loot in a few locations associated with [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") users. 

However, due to a randomization bug, it only and always appears in two places - a bookcase in the [elven mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Avallac%27h "Avallac'h")'s hideout during [Wandering in the Dark](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wandering_in_the_Dark "Wandering in the Dark"), and in the room with [key to library](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Key_to_library "Key to library") during [The Tower Outta Nowheres](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Tower_Outta_Nowheres "The Tower Outta Nowheres"). It is also sold by [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") and the [halfling merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(halfling\) "Merchant \(halfling\)") in [Silverton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silverton "Silverton"). 

## Journal entry

    _[Magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") is the science of harnessing, subduing and making use of the [power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") of the elements. It is also an incarnation of [Chaos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chaos "Chaos"), which is why no one without proper training should ever dabble in any of its domains. Expert [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") make of magic an art, but the road to virtuosity leads through years of practice, study, and hard work - and requires a considerable amount of required inborn talent. Magic was implanted in our world through the [Conjunction of the Spheres](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Conjunction_of_the_Spheres "Conjunction of the Spheres"), and was discovered to be a living, changeable and controllable being shortly thereafter. The name of the first mage, the one who first discovered this talent within himself and grappled with its power, is unknown. We do know, however, that in a relatively short period of time after the discovery of magic groups began to form in which talented beings could work together on developing their abilities. This period is usually considered the beginning of magic as a science, and these groups – the prototypes of magic schools.  
The fundamental [planes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_plane "Elemental plane") from which one might draw magic power are: [earth](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Earth "Earth"), [water](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water"), [air](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Air "Air") and [fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire "Fire"). In the tome which you currently hold, each of these will have a separate chapter dedicated to it, ordered according to the level of difficulty and danger associated with the element discussed (...)._

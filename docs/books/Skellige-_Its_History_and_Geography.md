## Item ID

lore_skellige_island

**Skellige: Its History and Geography** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ describing the landscape, customs, and way of life on [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), as well as the titles of [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King_of_the_Skellige_Isles "King of the Skellige Isles") and Jarl. 

It can only be found in 2 locations: 

  * Under the ship wreck with the [siren](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Siren "Siren") [nest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster_nest "Monster nest") at the beach where you arrive in Skellige
  * By the fireplace inside [Donar an Hindar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Donar_an_Hindar "Donar an Hindar")'s house in [Larvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Larvik "Larvik")

## Journal entry

    _Skellige the political entity encompasses the archipelago of the same name, composed of twenty individual isles. The largest of them are[Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"), [An Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Skellig "An Skellig"), [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik"), [Faroe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Faroe "Faroe"), [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog") and [Hindarsfjall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindarsfjall "Hindarsfjall"). Originally these isles were inhabited by independent clans, but these later united to face the growing threat from barbarians as well as the rising powers of the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"). Skellige then became an elective monarchy, ruled by a king chosen from among the jarls (clan chieftains). The present leader of the Skelligers is [Bran](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran_Tuirseach "Bran Tuirseach") of [Clan Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tuirseach "Clan Tuirseach")._
    _It is worth noting that, in contrast to the situation in the kingdoms on the Continent, in Skellige each[inhabitant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skelliger "Skelliger") is a citizen and enjoys equal rights, regardless of birth, trade or the property they possess._
    _On account of the rocky and often barren terrain and inhospitable climate, the inhabitants of Skellige gain their sustenance primarily from fishing, maritime trade and armed raiding. Though this last is primarily the work of men, female warriors are not unheard of, and they equal their fathers and brothers in courage and skill. For Skelligers believe that each is master of his or her own fate, and when[Freya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Freya "Freya") determines a man's or woman's destiny, she looks only to inner predispositions, and cares not for outward traits of status or sex._

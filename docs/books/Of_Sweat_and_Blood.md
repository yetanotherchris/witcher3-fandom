## Item ID

Specters vol 1

**Of Sweat and Blood** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. When first read, it adds [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entries for [nightwraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nightwraith "Nightwraith"), [noonwraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith "Noonwraith"), and [plague maidens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Plague_maiden "Plague maiden"), as well as [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Nightwraith_decoction "Torn-out page: Nightwraith decoction") for [nightwraith decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nightwraith_decoction "Nightwraith decoction") and [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Torn-out_page:_Noonwraith_decoction "Torn-out page: Noonwraith decoction") for [noonwraith decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith_decoction "Noonwraith decoction"). 

One can be received as a reward from [Tomira](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tomira "Tomira"), if [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") decides to give her a [swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swallow "Swallow") potion for [Lena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lena "Lena") during the secondary quest [On Death's Bed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/On_Death%27s_Bed "On Death's Bed"). It can also be bought from the following merchants: 

  * [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") in his [shop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Books_and_Scrolls "Books and Scrolls") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square")
  * Books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\)#Merchant_\(blue_outfit\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in Hierarch Square

It should also be available as random loot, but due to a randomization bug never appears as such. 

## Journal entry

    _A Veil hangs between the[world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World") men see and the one they cannot. This Veil blocks the dead from the view of the living, and the living from the view of the dead. Some [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") can break through this Veil and communicate with the dead, or else summon them to the World of the Sun for short periods of time. This is the art known as [necromancy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancy "Necromancy")._
    _The dead can also break through the Veil and enter the world of the living on their own. Yet unlike the necromancers, in doing so they are not driven by reason and will, but by a thoughtless, irrational need. This need arises from powerful emotions such as regret, longing or wrath. Very often these emotions gain their power by being invested in a material object by the dead individual while he or she still knew life. The objects most frequently so endowed? Wedding rings, favorite toys - or the instrument used for the crime which sent the returned individual to the other world in the first place._

**Elemental Empires** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , written by Gianbattista. 

It is found on [Amos var Ypsis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Amos_var_Ypsis "Amos var Ypsis")' ship during [The Last Wish](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Last_Wish_\(quest\) "The Last Wish \(quest\)"). 

## Journal entry

    _None of the four[Planes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elemental_plane "Elemental plane") or Dimensions - those of [Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire "Fire"), [Water](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water"), [Earth](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Earth "Earth"), and [Air](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Air "Air") \- is accessible to mere mortals. They are inhabited, however, by creatures known as [genies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Genie "Genie"). There are four types, each corresponding to one of the four Elements which comprise their respective essences. Each type of genie also counts its antithesis among the others. Thus, the [marides](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maride "Maride"), aligned with the Element of Water, are opposed by the fiery [ifrits](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ifrit "Ifrit"). The Plane of Earth is inhabited by the [d'ao](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/D%27ao "D'ao") genies, and the Dimension of Air which opposes it is home for [d'jinni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Djinn "Djinn") \- whose name, incidentally, is the root of the word genie. This last term is often used by simple folk to refer to all creatures that inhabit the Elemental Planes, which is an obvious blunder..._
    _Unusually powerful[sorcerers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcerer "Sorcerer") can sometimes bind such beings and bend them to their will, thus acquiring tremendous might to the point of near omnipotence. For a genie, being the living personification of an Element's energies, is akin to an almost boundless reservoir of the [Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power"). Thus, its master can draw energy from the genie for spell casting, without the tiresome need to channel from traditional sources. However, those who are able to bind a genie are few and far between, for the strength of the inhabitants of the four Planes is matched only by the cunning which they employ to avoid such a fate._

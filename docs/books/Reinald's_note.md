**Reinald's note** is a document in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that was introduced with the next-gen update, [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"). 

It's found just inside the [Devil's Pit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Devil%27s_Pit "Devil's Pit") mine, on a table in the shack to the right. Besite it is also a [greater Glyph of Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Greater_Glyph_of_Yrden "Greater Glyph of Yrden")

## Journal entry

    _Dear O._
    _If hope this message finds you well. Beware the beings that lurk the mine. Took them for[plague spirits](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Plague_maiden "Plague maiden") of some type at first, but they behave all too [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human"), just stupefied in some strange way. A novel [ghoul](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul") species? [Wraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith "Wraith")? [A manifestation of concentrated suffering](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Red_Miasmal "Red Miasmal")? Whatever the case, they haven't tried to hurt me (much). Still, I advise caution._
    _Watch for the ones pierced by nails - they seem especially dangerous. Was forced to kill one when it attacked. I assumed it was a ghoul, but its viscera were fully and undeniably human. That said, most of the organs were badly damaged - symptoms of a blood fever. Couldn't understand how he was still alive. I suspect now it must have been some form of possession - something that twisted his form, and then - for reasons unknown - departed. I gathered samples our mentors ought to see. Have I discovered a new species? Curious to hear your thoughts._
    _[Reinald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reinald "Reinald")_
    _PS - I put Master Alger's hypothesis to the test on some of them. He was right. Scattered salt has no effect – only a complete enclosure seems to hold them in place. But it must be complete – pure salt, no gaps – for it to work properly._

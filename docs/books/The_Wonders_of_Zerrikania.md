**The Wonders of Zerrikania** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that can be found as random loot, usually on bookshelves, throughout the entire game. 

## Journal entry

    _During my many travels I have seen countless extraordinary places - the primeval wilds of[Brokilon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brokilon "Brokilon"), with trees so high their tips disappear in the clouds, [dwarven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf") chambers carved into the guts of the [Mahakam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mahakam "Mahakam") Mountains, with walls plated in pure gold, the ice palace of [Pont Vanis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pont_Vanis "Pont Vanis"), adorned with stained-frost windows - yet none of these made such an impression on me as did the rightly-famed [Zerrikania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikania "Zerrikania")._
    _Yet while I was traversing the[Fiery Mountains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fiery_Mountains "Fiery Mountains"), I feared disappointment awaited me on the other side. I had heard many a fantastic tale about Zerrikania - about its trackless sands, burnt white by the sun, its golden-scaled [dragons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon"), weaving their nests amidst the dunes, its hunch-backed [horses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse") able to survive weeks without even a swallow of [water](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water") \- yet none seemed to me at all plausible. I was sure all these sensations were but the figments of some [bard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bard "Bard")'s overactive imagination._
    _I know this will be as hard for you to believe, dear reader, as it once was for me - but all of the unbelievable tales are true. Not only that - during my many months of travel I came across wonders far surpassing those any prior travelogues mentioned. I saw temples dedicated to the worship of dragons. I heard their voice, almost[human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human"), but reverberating with a thousand echoes. I met warrior maids clad in leopard skins, tattooed from head to foot and giving no ground to [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") in mastery of the blade. I saw [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") who channeled [Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") from [fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire "Fire"). I saw seemingly harmless flies whose solitary bite would make a man fall into a deep slumber, never to awake save to die. In short, Zerrikania is a land where the fantastic is normal, and the impossible occurs daily..._

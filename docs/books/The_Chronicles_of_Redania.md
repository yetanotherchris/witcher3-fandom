## Item ID

Jacob of Varazze Chronicles

**The Chronicles of Redania** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be found quite often as random loot, but a few copies of it are guaranteed to appear during the secondary quest [Now or Never](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Now_or_Never "Now or Never"), in the underground library while going through the [sewers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad_sewers "Novigrad sewers"). It can also be purchased from the [books merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). 

## Journal entry

    _[Vridank](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vridank "Vridank") the [Elf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf")  
Despite what one might conclude from his moniker, not one drop of elven blood flowed in [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") Vridank's veins. They called him the Elf because of his exceptional beauty, and for the great admiration he felt for the [Aen Seidhe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Seidhe "Aen Seidhe"). This fascination, seemingly harmless, would have horrible long-term consequences. King Vridank, spitting in the face of all laws and customs, chose as his wife a [half-elf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Half-elf "Half-elf") \- and one of low status at that - known as [Beatrix of Kovir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beatrix_of_Kovir "Beatrix of Kovir"). The fruit of this regrettable and short-lived mésalliance was [Falka](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Falka "Falka"), who later fomented [bloody revolt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Falka%27s_rebellion "Falka's rebellion") against her own father. Though this uprising was ultimately extinguished and Falka herself burned at the stake, the young state was thereafter thrown into chaos for years to come._

## Notes

  * Upon reaching the underground library, [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold") actually refers to the book as _"Jacob of Varazze's chronicles"_ \- evidently its original name, altered for unknown reasons after the voice lines were recorded for the quest.

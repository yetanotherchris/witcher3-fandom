**Ithlinne's Prophecy** is a [book](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Booklist "Booklist") in _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_. 

The legendary prophecy of the [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") prophetess, foretelling the end of the world. By far the most ubiquitous book in the game. 

### Contents

    _**Aen Ithlinnespeath**_  
 _The Prophecy of[Ithlinne Aegli aep Aevenien](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ithlinne "Ithlinne")_
    _"I tell you that the time of the sword and axe approaches, the time of the Wolf's Blizzard. The Time of the[White Frost](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Frost "White Frost") and [White Light](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Light "White Light"), the Time of Madness and Disdain, Tedd Deireadh, the Final Age. The world will perish amidst ice and be reborn with the new sun. Reborn of the Elder Blood, of Hen Ichaer, of a planted seed. A seed that will not sprout but burst into flames!_
    _Ess'tuath esse! So shall it be! Watch for the signs! I will tell you what these signs will be — the earth will run with the blood of Aen Seidhe, the Blood of the Elves..."_

**Ithlinne's Prophecy** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

A copy of it can be found on the stone railing in front of the [Vegelbud Residence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vegelbud_Residence "Vegelbud Residence") house entrance, and another on the ledge where Geralt jumps off to find the [Defier's Oren](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Defier%27s_Oren "Defier's Oren") during [Bald Mountain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bald_Mountain_\(quest\) "Bald Mountain \(quest\)"). 

In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, it can also be found as random loot on bookshelves in houses around [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), or purchased from the [Ducal Camerlengo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rafael_de_Surmann "Rafael de Surmann").

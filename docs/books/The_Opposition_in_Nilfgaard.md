**The Opposition in Nilfgaard** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be found as random loot on most bookshelves and some wooden chests throughout the entire game, or purchased from the [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Putrid_Grove\) "Merchant \(Putrid Grove\)") in [Putrid Grove](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Putrid_Grove "Putrid Grove"). 

## Journal entry

    _While[Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire")'s [emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") wields absolute power, harshly crushing the slightest sign of disobedience, opposing forces continue to exist within the empire. By this I do not mean the disgruntled leaders of conquered provinces, but the magantes within the City of a Thousand Towers who are unhappy with the current leadership. This conflict between the emperor and the noble houses of [Nilfgaard the capital](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaard_\(city\) "Nilfgaard \(city\)") dates back to many years. All the [princes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") of the blood and magnates expected their ruler to wed one of their daughters and sire an heir with one of their own. The [emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis"), however, had other plans. This proved a slap in the face of all the great families from which he refused to take a bride._
    _The Nilfgaardian opposition patiently waits for the emperor to slip up, for some event to occur which will weaken his authority, be it an economic crisis or a defeat in battle. A secret conspiracy lies ready to seize such a moment to incite the disaffected, assassinate the emperor and carry out a coup d'état culminating with one of their own number ascending to the throne._
    _For obvious reasons, only a limited few know of this conspiracy, but any shrewd observer of Nilfgaardian politics can read the signs of its workings. So long as men are men and the[world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World") is as it is, certain dynamics will forever be the same, and the discontented will always form subversive societies with their secret signs and hidden agendas._

## Notes

  * In early development of the game, certain "perk" abilities were meant to be acquired by reading books such as this one. The idea was eventually [scrapped](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content"), and this particular book was repurposed (likely renamed and entirely changed contents) for lore instead, but it would've provided a [sailing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sailing "Sailing")-related ability, possibly to shoot a [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow") while riding boats.

**The Great Migration** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but were never attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. 

## Journal entry

    _We had a different home, once. Once we were one folk, the[Aen Undod](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Undod "Aen Undod"). But then came winter, death and destruction. We had to leave. The road was long and difficult. Many lost the path, others, their minds, and still others, their lives._
    _We came to a crossroads. One trail led to a world bursting with green, the other - to the mountainous isles the[dh'oine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") later called [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"). We could not agree where to go, where we would be safer, further from the [White Frost](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Frost "White Frost"). We split. Those who dwelt amidst the cliffs and rocks are called the [Aen Seidhe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Seidhe "Aen Seidhe"). Those who built new homes in the land of forests are known as the [Aen Elle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Elle "Aen Elle")._
    _We were the same, once. Now we are other. We look for each other in the stars. We speak to each other through the mouths of the[Aen Saevherne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Saevherne "Aen Saevherne"). We listen to ballads about the proud Aen Elle folk. We mourn the fate of the Aen Seidhe, enslaved by the dh'oine. We remember the olden days. We await the White Frost and the next migration - or our death._

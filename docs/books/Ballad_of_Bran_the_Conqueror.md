**Ballad of Bran the Conqueror** , is a poem by [skalds](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skald "Skald") of [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") dedicated to [Bran](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran_Tuirseach_\(the_Conqueror\) "Bran Tuirseach \(the Conqueror\)"), an early [konung](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King_of_the_Skellige_Isles "King of the Skellige Isles") and a hero to [Clan Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tuirseach "Clan Tuirseach"). 

It is the same in-game book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can potentially find in [Arnvald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arnvald "Arnvald")'s room during the quest [King's Gambit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King%27s_Gambit "King's Gambit"), in the event that Arnvald dies. Whether due to oversight or time constraints during development, it simply never got attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition, effectively making it a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") item. 

## Journal entry

    _From the peak of the mountain, Bran stared down at the isle.  
"This is my kingdom!" he cried to the sun.  
"Forged in fire and bathed in human blood  
It will be great once more, as it was in ages past.  
This oath I swear on the sun and the stars."_
    _"For the people of sea and mountain  
There is no other way but through fire and blood.  
Such is the fate the gods have written for us in the stars.  
And this fate I, Bran of Clan Tuirseach, shall bring to fruition.  
By lightning fires and spilling my own blood."_

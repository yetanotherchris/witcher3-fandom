**History of the Theater of Novigrad** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but aren't attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. A related cut content book also exists in the game files as "[Novigrad Theater Review](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad_Theater_Review "Novigrad Theater Review")". 

## Journal entry

    _One may place the crown of the King of[Novigradian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") Comedy on the deserving head of one [Boliere](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maxim_Boliere "Maxim Boliere"), who in his works pokes merciless fun at the vices of the bourgeoisie, in particular its exaggerated piety. Alas, this great playwright died while in the flower of his youth, in the dank cellars of the [Temple](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Isle "Temple Isle") of the [Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire"), whose priests have never been known for their sense of humor._

**Who's Who Among Koviri Sorcerers** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , which was meant to be found in the library during the secondary quest [The Tower Outta Nowheres](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Tower_Outta_Nowheres "The Tower Outta Nowheres"), where it can still be examined and [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") has a unique line for it, but it cannot be picked up. 

Its contents are still present in the game files, but were never attached to an [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") item definition. 

## Journal entry

    _According to legend, Gabriella Maurienn, the founder of the Maurienn dynasty, was lover to[Scarab](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scarab_Thyssen "Scarab Thyssen"), son of [Godamba Thaess'en](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Godamba_Thaess%27en "Godamba Thaess'en"), progenitor of the [Thyssenid dynasty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/House_of_Thyssen "House of Thyssen"). For this reason the Maurienns are considered kin to the royal dynasty, though no documents survive that confirm this blood tie with absolute certainty._
    _During its stormy history the Maurienn dynasty did not make very frequent use of its family ties to the court, though there were times when it did closely cooperate with it. Thus for example Franz Maurienn, grandson of Gabriella, was advisor to[Guido Thyssen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Guido_Thyssen "Guido Thyssen") and his wife [Klaudia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Klaudia_Starhemberg "Klaudia Starhemberg") of the Starhembergs. The chronicles tell us that while Franz dabbled mainly in healing and [elemental](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elementa "Elementa") [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"), he also conducted experiments in [necromancy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necromancy "Necromancy"). Guido Thyssen, who was fond of Franz's pleasant company and sense of humor, turned a blind eye to this. His detractors claim Franz repaid his patron several times over - as a result of the magic teas Franz would send on behalf of Guido to King [Gerard Troydenid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gerard_Troyden "Gerard Troyden")[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")], Guido was settled in the [Eastern March](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/East_March "East March") and given the title of [margrave](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Political_titles#Margrave "Political titles")._

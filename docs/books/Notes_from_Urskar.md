**Notes from Undvik** is a [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , still present in the game files, but only obtainable through console commands. 

It is the 1st of 7 books collectively known as "journals from [Urskar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Urskar "Urskar")", detailing what led to [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik")'s destruction. Small bits from that still remain in dialogues during [The Lord of Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Lord_of_Undvik "The Lord of Undvik") quest, but the overall story and a lot of other content related to the island have been removed from the game. 

## Journal entry

    _Spring was late in coming this year. Snows stuck around till after the first apple blossoms. And not just high up the hills. Won't be a bumper crop, that's for sure. Summer was short, and fall's just started and already we've had the first frosts. Got men digging in hardened ground. They plant millet or barley, but they won't harvest much of either. Panic's setting in on Undvik. "Winter's coming," or so say the old timers huddled around their fires. Everyone avoids saying "[Ragh nar Roog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ragh_nar_Roog "Ragh nar Roog")," as if the words themselves could bring disaster. But everyone senses the last battle's leaping ever closer towards us. To my mind, long as the [giants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Giant "Giant") still sleep, we've hope yet. Aye.... we've hope yet..._

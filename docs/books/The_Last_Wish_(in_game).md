**The Last Wish** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It is one of the most commonly found books throughout the entire game, though also scripted to appear during quests related to [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion"): atop a shelf in [Marabella](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marabella "Marabella")'s [School for Tots](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marabella%27s_School_for_Tots "Marabella's School for Tots"), visited during [Broken Flowers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Broken_Flowers "Broken Flowers"), and on the nearest bookshelf inside [Margrave Henckel's house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margrave_Henckel%27s_house "Margrave Henckel's house") during [Count Reuven's Treasure](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count_Reuven%27s_Treasure "Count Reuven's Treasure"). It can also be purchased from [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") and the books [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Hierarch_Square\) "Merchant \(Hierarch Square\)") in [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). 

### Journal entry

    _[Human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") life requires three things for its sustenance - food, drink, and gossip. It is thus no wonder that no matter where I travel, be it ice-bound [Poviss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poviss "Poviss") or ever green [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), everyone asks me about the passions that bind [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") the sorceress [Yennefer of Vengerberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg"). As a man both cautious and discreet, I refuse to betray their secrets - with one important exception. The history of their first encounter is so extraordinary, so romantic and moving, that it would be a veritable crime to hide its light beneath a [bushel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bushel "Bushel") basket. Indeed, had I not witnessed these events in person, I would never believe there was room in our grim and and dark world for such fantastic marvels._
    _It all began when Geralt and I were feeling a bit peckish and, unburdened by heavy coin pouches, decided to fish our supper out of a lake. No bites were to be had, but we did not leave empty-handed - my hook snagged quite a lovely little pot. Oblivious to my friend's warnings, I opened it - and in doing so freed a powerful[djinn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Djinn "Djinn")._
    _Without giving it much thought I set about proclaiming my wishes. Before I could get to three, however, the djinn - irritated, I see now in hindsight, at being issued demands so soon after waking - began to throttle me. Geralt was able to drive him off, but I was left in a sorry state. I acted, the[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") told me later, as though under the influence of some [curse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse"). Clearly, the help of an expert in the [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") arcana would be needed._
    _It was our good fortune that Yennefer of[Vengerberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vengerberg "Vengerberg") happened to be staying in a nearby village. Geralt went to her to ask that she heal his best friend, who happened also to be the brightest star in the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/North "North")'s poetic firmament. Yennefer, however, was more interested in the djinn (which she wanted to trap into magic servitude) than in its victim, and, it should be said, played the witcher like a well-strung mandolin. Yet rather than grow angry at being used in such a calculating manner, he fell white head over muddy boots in love with her._
    _What happened then... well, I won't go into details, suffice to say that Yennefer's plans hardly delighted the djinn, and without its cooperation she proved unable to tame it. The sorceress would surely have met a tragic end - and taken all of[Rinde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rinde "Rinde") along with her - had Geralt not rushed to her rescue. For once, he did not need to draw either of his blades: to send the djinn packing, he had but to pronounce the third and final wish. He could have asked for anything: wealth, fame, power... but instead he asked the djinn to bind his fate to that of the arrogant yet intriguing sorceress from Vengerberg._
    _[Julian Alfred Pankratz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion"), [Viscount](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Viscount "Viscount") of Lettenhove, born [1232](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1232 "Timeline/Games"). Award-winning poet, playwright and troubadour. Frequent performer at the courts of [Niedamir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Niedamir "Niedamir"), [Vizimir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II"), [Venzlav](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Venzlav "Venzlav") and many other nobles and notables. Alumnus of [Oxenfurt Academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Academy "Oxenfurt Academy")._

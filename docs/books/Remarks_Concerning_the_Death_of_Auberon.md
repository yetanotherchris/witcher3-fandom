**Remarks Concerning the Death of Auberon** is a document in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , written by [Avallac'h](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Avallac%27h "Avallac'h"). 

It can be found under the family tree inside Avallac'h's secret residence at [the Pali Gap Coast](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Pali_Gap_Coast "The Pali Gap Coast") cave, during the quest [Child of the Elder Blood](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Child_of_the_Elder_Blood "Child of the Elder Blood"). 

## Journal entry

    _When[Auberon Muircetach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Auberon_Muircetach "Auberon Muircetach") died, we lost not only our finest sage and leader, but also our best chance to renew the gene of the [Elder Blood](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Blood "Elder Blood"). Prior to his, success seemed possible, even imminent. I had interpreted [Cirilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri")'s willingness to attempt to beget his child - despite her earlier resistance - as a personal triumph and a sign there was more of [Lara](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lara_Dorren "Lara Dorren") in her than I had ever imagined possible.  
Now, with all my designs shattered and the benefit of hindsight, I have concluded that putting pressure on her then in [Tir ná Lia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tir_n%C3%A1_Lia "Tir ná Lia") had a very negative effect on our relationship. Yet I could not have predicted that Muircetach had so little time remaining, and would squander it so foolishly, even though Cirilla was his for the taking.  
Now my only hope lies in trying to regain her confidence and trust at any cost._

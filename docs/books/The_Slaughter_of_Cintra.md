## Item ID

Theatre Glossary vol 2

**The Slaughter of Cintra** is a book in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

It can be found as random loot on most bookshelves and some wooden chests throughout the entire game, or purchased from [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") in his [shop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Books_and_Scrolls "Books and Scrolls") on [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square"). 

## Journal entry

    _(...) The cavalcade of riders pounded across the blood-soaked courtyard. They looked at no one and asked no questions - they knew exactly where they were going and why. To kill the old[queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Political_titles "Political titles") and capture the [Cintran](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") [princess](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"). As to what would become of her after that - no one would say._
    _[Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe") and a group of her most loyal subjects had barricaded themselves inside the castle. They were protected by stone walls and a gate sealed by a spell - but they all realized neither would last long against a concentrated assault by the [Nilfgaardian army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") and the [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") supporting it. After four days, enemy soldiers forced their way inside (...)_

## Notes

  * In early development of the game, certain "perk" abilities were meant to be acquired by reading books such as this one. The idea was eventually [scrapped](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") and this particular book was repurposed for lore instead, with no clues as to what perk it would've provided.

# The Witcher 3 contracts

**Witcher contracts** in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ are optional quests that [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can be tasked by various NPCs, and through notice boards posted throughout the world. There are a total of 26 witcher contracts in the base game, with 3 additional ones included with DLC. 

_Achievement:_ see [Geralt: The Professional](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt:_The_Professional "Geralt: The Professional") for more information on contracts needed to get the achievement. 

## Overview

Most contracts in The Witcher 3 follow a set sequence; reading a notice and/or speaking with the contract poster, an optional opportunity to haggle with the contract poster, followed by an investigation of the threat. This investigation can vary from speaking with members of the local population, or using [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses") to investigate a path or the scene of a killing. After a certain point in most contracts, the [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") will update with an entry on the unidentified monster responsible for the killing. Reading through bestiary entries can help at higher difficulties, as the contracted monster will always share the same weaknesses to the standard version of the monster found in the open world. 

### Haggling

Typical contracts in The Witcher 3 provide an opportunity for Geralt to ask for additional pay, whether it is prior to the contract being fulfilled, half being paid before and after, or the total sum being increased after the contract is fulfilled. 

The annoyance level can vary based on the level of your player and other choices made throughout the quest. Typically, negotiating just beyond the halfway point can provide a safe first bid, but this can also result in some bids immediately being accepted. Additionally, a few quests will always accept a maximum negotiated rate. 

The majority of contracts in The Witcher 3 allow Geralt to haggle for upwards of 300 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") in pay, with higher level contracts allowing haggling in excess of 375 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"). However, there are exceptions, where a few have much higher or lower haggling limits. There's also one contract, [Contract: Swamp Thing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Contract:_Swamp_Thing "Contract: Swamp Thing"), in which one has the potential to receive double the negotiated pay if they give the character one week's time to come up with the money.

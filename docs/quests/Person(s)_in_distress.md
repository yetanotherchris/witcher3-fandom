# Person(s) in distress

[![Prima Guide person distress](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/7f/Prima_Guide_person_distress.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/315?cb=20160426182231)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/7f/Prima_Guide_person_distress.png/revision/latest?cb=20160426182231) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Prima_Guide_person_distress.png)

There are a number of **persons in distress** to be found and rescued throughout the [locations](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_locations "The Witcher 3 locations") and regions of _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**Who are the 'persons in distress' in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The 'persons in distress' in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt are high-value targets like blacksmiths, armorers, or merchants, found in various regions. They are typically locked in cages or attacked by bandits. Upon rescue, they return to their hometowns or villages on foot. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What happens when a 'person in distress' is rescued in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, rescuing a 'person in distress' involves freeing high-value targets like blacksmiths, armorers, or merchants. These individuals, typically found locked in cages or under attack by bandits, return to their hometown or village on foot after being rescued. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What kind of services do rescued 'persons in distress' provide to Geralt in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3, when Geralt rescues a 'person in distress', they often turn out to be high-value individuals like blacksmiths, armorers, or merchants. After rescue, they return to their home and provide Geralt with services related to their respective professions. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How can you find a 'person in distress' in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, a person in distress can be located in different regions, often locked in cages or under attack by bandits. These distressed persons are typically high-value targets like blacksmiths, armorers, or merchants. Once rescued, they return to their home town or village. If interaction is not possible at first, meditating for a few hours can resolve this issue. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What types of characters are typically 'persons in distress' in The Witcher 3?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt often features 'persons in distress' in precarious situations, such as being confined in cages or assaulted by bandits. These distressed individuals are typically high-value characters like blacksmiths, armorers, and merchants. Once rescued, they typically make their way back to their home town or village.

## Description

[![Tw3 icon persons in distress](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/94/Tw3_icon_persons_in_distress.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/21?cb=20170901043203)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Person\(s\)_in_distress "Person\(s\) in distress")

Person(s) in distress are usually found locked in cages, or being attacked by bandits. They are often "high-value" targets such as [blacksmiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blacksmith "Blacksmith"), [armorers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Armorer "Armorer"), or [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"), who will return to their home town or village on foot once rescued. Once they have returned back home, their services will be unlocked for [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to use, and some of them (at least those in the base game) will also give him a small reward.

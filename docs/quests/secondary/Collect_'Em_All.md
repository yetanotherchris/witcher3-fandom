## Additional Information

### Type

[Secondary quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_secondary_quests "The Witcher 3 secondary quests")

**Collect 'Em All** is a [secondary quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_secondary_quests "The Witcher 3 secondary quests") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. Since there is no way (in-game) to check exactly which [gwent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwent "Gwent") cards you do and don't have, this is regarded as one of the hardest, non-combative quests in the game. 

_Note:_ completing this quest will unlock the achievement [Card Collector](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Card_Collector "Card Collector"). 

## Walkthrough

To complete it, you must collect every single card of each type; a total of 120 cards, from the 199 available cards. The other 79 cards are duplicates (for example, there are three [Ghoul](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul_\(gwent_card\) "Ghoul \(gwent card\)") cards you can find, but you only need one for this quest objective).[1]

  * [Northern Realms Gwent deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Realms_Gwent_deck "Northern Realms Gwent deck") (40 cards)
  * [Nilfgaardian Empire Gwent deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire_Gwent_deck "Nilfgaardian Empire Gwent deck") (41 cards)
  * [Monsters Gwent deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monsters_Gwent_deck "Monsters Gwent deck") (44 cards)
  * [Scoia'tael Gwent deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scoia%27tael_Gwent_deck "Scoia'tael Gwent deck") (41 cards)
  * [Gwent neutral cards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwent_neutral_cards "Gwent neutral cards") (29 cards)

Gwent cards may be obtained by purchasing them from [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"), winning against others at gwent, and by winning against named NPCs in gwent-related secondary quests. After winning against a certain number of generic merchants, playing against merchants will no longer earn new cards. The book [A Miraculous Guide to Gwent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Miraculous_Guide_to_Gwent "A Miraculous Guide to Gwent") (added by [Patch 1.20](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.20_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.20 \(The Witcher 3\)")) can be used to keep track of the number of cards still necessary to obtain from each region, and of ones acquired from gwent secondary quests. 

### Missable cards

Throughout the game, some cards are easily missed if a player chooses one action over another, chooses to skip a side quest, or just by not exploring. Here's a list of such cases: 

  * Do the side quest [A Matter of Life and Death](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Matter_of_Life_and_Death_\(The_Witcher_3\) "A Matter of Life and Death \(The Witcher 3\)"): There'll be a small gwent tournament going on in the main courtyard where you'll need to win all the rounds to get all the cards.
  * Do [A Deadly Plot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Deadly_Plot "A Deadly Plot"): Turns out the one you're looking for is a big gwent player and has one of the best cards in the game.
  * Do [A Dangerous Game](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Dangerous_Game "A Dangerous Game") and take the cards as your reward.
  * Participate in [High Stakes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/High_Stakes "High Stakes"): You **must** win the tournament to get every card (see main quest page for tips).
  * Do [Shock Therapy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shock_Therapy "Shock Therapy") to get the [Iorveth card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Iorveth_\(gwent_card\) "Iorveth \(gwent card\)").
  * Free the [Claywich](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Claywich "Claywich") [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Claywich\) "Merchant \(Claywich\)") who is held captive southeast of the [Boatmakers' Hut](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Boatmakers%27_Hut "Boatmakers' Hut"): Be careful as this merchant is bugged. Make a save just before opening his cage, then [fast travel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fast_travel "Fast travel") to Claywich. If the talking option doesn't show up, reload until it does or [meditate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meditation "Meditation") until daylight. **Make sure to buy all the gwent cards he has for sale**.
  * Play [Lambert](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lambert "Lambert") as soon as you can (either at [The Nowhere Inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Nowhere_Inn "The Nowhere Inn") or at [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") when the witchers are drinking)
  * See [this list](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwent_players "Gwent players") for a breakdown in missable characters/cards, making sure to play (or buy from) each one as soon as you can to avoid missing their card later. [Patch 1.05](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.05_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.05 \(The Witcher 3\)") added in fail-safes if you missed a player's card.

## Journal entry

    _In his previous adventures,[Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") had gained fame as a master [dice poker](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_dice_poker "The Witcher dice poker") player. Yet having mastered all challengers in this arena, he had no choice but to find another in which to garner more laurels. He thus decided to take up gwent, and since he aimed to play at the highest levels, he felt he had to gather a complete collection of all the cards available – including some which could only be had by winning them off the best players around._

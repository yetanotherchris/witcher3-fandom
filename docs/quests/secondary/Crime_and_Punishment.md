## Walkthrough

Sail to the peninsula east of the [Ancient Crypt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ancient_Crypt "Ancient Crypt") to find a man chained to a rock being attacked by [harpies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harpy "Harpy"). 

After you kill off the monsters, you can talk to [Yorg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yorg "Yorg"), who claims he was framed for murder and begs you to free him. You can choose to free him or leave him to his fate. If you choose the latter, the quest will fail once you travel far enough away. 

If you choose to free him, he'll thank you and offer to help you out should they ever meet again. The quest will then complete. (10[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level")) 

While that completes the quest, you can learn more by heading to [Rogne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rogne "Rogne") where, on the northwest outskirts of the village, you'll find a group of mourners at the murdered man's grave, [Gretter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gretter "Gretter"), praising how good of a man he was and the vile criminal, Yorg, should have been punished more severely. A young girl in the group will then cry out that her brother was a good man before running a short distance away. 

Go over and talk to her to learn she's [Yorg's sister](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yorg%27s_sister "Yorg's sister") and that she's to blame: she didn't like their uncle so she lied and told Yorg that Gretter had done bad things to her, sending her brother into a flying rage and killing their uncle. If you freed Yorg, you can choose to tell her you freed him and she'll be happy and swear to never lie again, or you can coldly tell her that she learned her lesson. If you left Yorg to his fate, the latter is the only option. Regardless of what you choose, the conversation will end and the journal will update. 

## Journal entry

    _On a small beach on the shores of[Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik") [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") came across a man chained to the rocks, the tide threatening to engulf him. The man begged for mercy, swearing he had been framed for murder and unjustly punished._

    **If Geralt talked to Yorg's sister:**

    _In the village near the site of the man's punishment a group was preparing to bury the person the chained man had supposedly killed. Geralt's attention was drawn by a sobbing girl. He talked to her and learned the child blamed herself for her brother's death. When pressed for the reason why, she explained that her false accusations had caused her brother to fly into a rage and kill her uncle. In[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), there was only one punishment for killing a family member in cold blood: a slow death by the sea's embrace..._
    **If Geralt didn't believe Yorg and left him:**

    _Geralt did not believe the chained man's testimony and left him chained to the rocks, to meet his end at the mercy of the waves and the beasts of the sea._
    **If Geralt believed Yorg and freed him:**

    _Geralt was convinced by the accused man's testimony and freed him from his chains._

## Bugs

  * The journal incorrectly states that the man is found chained up on Undvik instead of [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig").

## Trivia

  * The quest may have been inspired from the legend of the immortal Titan [Prometheus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prometheus "wikipedia:Prometheus") from Greek mythology, who was chained to a rock for an eagle to bite a piece out of his liver every day as punishment.
  * The name of the quest may have also been inspired from [Fyodor Dostoyevsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fyodor_Dostoyevsky "wikipedia:Fyodor Dostoyevsky")'s book, [Crime and Punishment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_and_Punishment "wikipedia:Crime and Punishment"), where murder is also committed in pursuit of a higher purpose.

## Walkthrough

This quest is given by the herbalist [Tomira](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tomira "Tomira") in [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard"), if she is asked whether the [griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)") of White Orchard attacked the injured girl on the bed in her hut. This usually happens during the main quest [The Beast of White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Beast_of_White_Orchard "The Beast of White Orchard"), after [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") is directed to the herbalist by captain [Peter Saar Gwynleve](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peter_Saar_Gwynleve "Peter Saar Gwynleve"). 

You must then decide whether to give the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") potion [Swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swallow "Swallow") to the young peasant woman, [Lena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lena "Lena"), who was indeed mortally injured by the griffin. 

You will need to brew one which requires you to find the ingredients and then craft it in your inventory. The recipe calls for: 

  * 1 × [Dwarven spirit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarven_spirit "Dwarven spirit")
  * 1 × [Drowner brain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner_brain "Drowner brain")
  * 5 × [Celandine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Celandine "Celandine")

### Getting the ingredients

The dwarven spirit can be purchased from the tavern keeper in White Orchard or crafted if you don't want to pay for it. A Dwarven spirit can also spawn in a lootable sack in the western-most corner of the alchemist's garden. 

The [drowner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner") brain can be looted from the drops of drowners you kill. Search along the river coast for drowners, especially between White Orchard and the [Nilfgaardian Garrison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Garrison "Nilfgaardian Garrison") or near the [Ransacked Village](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ransacked_Village "Ransacked Village"). 

Tomira actually sells celandine so it can simply be bought from her, but it also grows in many of the fields surrounding White Orchard, especially between the village center and the herbalist's house. Once you have the alchemy ingredients, simply craft the potion in the alchemy section of your character menu. Alternatively, the quest [Precious Cargo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Precious_Cargo "Precious Cargo") can also provide the necessary celandine if you choose to keep the contents of the locked chest. 

### Take the potion to the herbalist

With the potion in your inventory, talk to Tomira and say "Got a potion for Lena." to complete the quest, for which she will award Geralt with the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Manuscript_page:_Cursed_oil "Manuscript page: Cursed oil") for [cursed oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cursed_oil "Cursed oil"), the book [Of Sweat and Blood](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Of_Sweat_and_Blood "Of Sweat and Blood"), and 2 vials of [venom extract](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Venom_extract "Venom extract"). Make sure to read the book, which will unlock several [bestiary](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") entries and a couple decoction formulae. 

## Journal entry

    _Once again Geralt had a near impossible choice to make - a choice between a greater and lesser evil. While preparing to hunt the griffin, he had come across one of the beast's victims - a simple peasant woman named Lena. She had been on her way to meet her lover when the griffin attacked. The beast had mortally wounded her. A witcher's potion could save her life... or cause her to perish in agony._
    **If Geralt helps her:**

    _The witcher decided to help Lena. Geralt then left White Orchard before he could learn the results of the risky treatment he applied. Soon afterwards, while visiting the[Nilfgaardian garrison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army_Group_%27Center%27_Camp "Nilfgaardian Army Group 'Center' Camp") in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"), he learned the woman had survived. The imperial infantryman with whom she was to meet the ill-fated evening of the griffin's attack had brought her to a nearby village. Sadly, none of this denoted a happy ending. Though the wounds on Lena's body healed, the toxins in the witcher's brew had melted her mind. This was not the first time a cure had proven worse than the disease._
    **If Geralt doesn't help out:**

    _They say witchers do not feel as humans do. That they are stripped of emotion, untroubled by doubts and dilemmas. This might be true for some of the caste-Lambert possibly being a case in point. But there is no doubt Geralt was different. He honestly debated what to do with Lena, considered what would be the lesser evil, and did so for so long that the woman finally succumbed to her wounds._

## Objectives

  * Brew a dose of the Swallow potion.
  * Take the potion to the herbalist.

## Notes

  * Oddly, if Lena is helped, the quest automatically updates to reflect information that Geralt doesn't learn until he's in Velen.
  * The quest will automatically fail if the player finishes up the prologue and leaves White Orchard before finishing this quest.
  * If Geralt chooses to use the potion on Lena, a Nilfgaardian soldier of her acquaintance will later speak to Geralt about her condition as he approaches the [Nilfgaardian Army Group 'Center' Camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army_Group_%27Center%27_Camp "Nilfgaardian Army Group 'Center' Camp") in Velen. If you go back to Tomira, she will also say that some Nilfgaardians took Lena away.
  * It is possible for Geralt to already have [buckthorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Buckthorn "Buckthorn") in his possession before the captain directs him to Tomira, but her door is still locked until he speaks to the captain.

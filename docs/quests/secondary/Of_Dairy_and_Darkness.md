## Walkthrough

This quest can be started 2 ways: 

  * find [Aeramas' Abandoned Manor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aeramas%27_Abandoned_Manor "Aeramas' Abandoned Manor")
  * find the [Gildorf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gildorf "Gildorf") district [shop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shop_\(Gildorf\) "Shop \(Gildorf\)") and buy the related quest item there

If you found the manor first, you can explore the residence if you wish, but won't be able to activate the [portal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Portal "Portal") to continue. Instead, you'll need to head to the shop in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") to get the related quest item. 

At the shop, the [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Gildorf\) "Merchant \(Gildorf\)") sells a number of quest related items, but the only one you need for this is the [lizard figurine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lizard_figurine "Lizard figurine") (40 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown")). Buy it, then head to the manor east of [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"). 

If you didn't already find the manor, you can look around a bit, finding the deceased mage's [notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_mage_Aeramas%27_last_journal_entry "The mage Aeramas' last journal entry") on the second floor, along with a lever that'll make everything dark for a small duration. _Witcher gear:_ a chest on the second floor contains [Diagram: Enhanced Feline armor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Diagram:_Enhanced_Feline_armor "Diagram: Enhanced Feline armor"). Once done, examine the magic circle on the main floor then activate the portal to find Aeramas' secret laboratory. Note you can leave and re-enter anytime, simply activate the portals where you entered to leave/return. 

As you enter the main chamber, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") will remark that Aeramas' must have been a [tyromancer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tyromancy "Tyromancy"), explaining the smell and all the cheese statues. You'll see below what is essentially a maze: however unlike other gases, you can't use [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") or [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni") on the toxic cheese fumes here. That said, it's pretty easy to navigate it to the other side. Follow the cheese pieces that have a glass case above them, the ones without glass are toxic, earning 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") as you go. 

Make your way through the stinky cheese maze until you reach some broken stairs, then climb up. Make your way to the next room on the higher level and interact with the floor tiles to activate another portal to another location. Head all the way down to the water, then jump in and take the northern passage (there's no worthwhile loot down the other path). Continue following and climbing up ledges until you reach Aeramas' laboratory. However, as soon as you jump across the broken bridge, a [foglet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foglet "Foglet") will attack if it spots you (otherwise you'll need to venture closer to it). 

Once it's dead, head into the final part of the laboratory here and loot the [bull figurine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bull_figurine "Bull figurine") and [Aeramas' notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aeramas%27_notes "Aeramas' notes") off the desk. If you have [The Eye of Nehaleni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Eye_of_Nehaleni "The Eye of Nehaleni"), use it on the nearby rock wall to uncover some more loot and possible random diagrams. Once ready, activate the portal near the fire brazier and enter to find the final room, earning another 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"). 

The final room has a magical barrier that you'll need to deactivate before you can proceed. Cast Igni on both of the pyramid shape stones in the walls to the left and right of the barrier. 

_Note:_ Prior to [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"), you didn't need The Eye of Nehaleni to show the small pyramid on the left: you can simply hit the one already exposed on the right with Igni and the barrier will drop. However, with the patch, The Eye of Nehaleni is required as you will need to cast Igni on both the left and right pyramids to drop the barrier. 

Once the barrier is down, loot the chest for 50 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") and the [snake figurine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Snake_figurine "Snake figurine") (the last portal key), then take the sword, [The Emmentaler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Emmentaler "The Emmentaler"), to complete the quest. (75[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level")) You can now exit the area through the final portal further behind the statue that had the sword. 

## Journal entry

    

    **If the merchant is spoken to first:**

    _A Novigrad merchant told Geralt about a mage named Aeramas. The poor man had, like so many of his colleagues, met a gruesome death on a pyre. With no surviving heirs, his expansive rural residence was now abandoned - and, if the rumors about this eccentric sorcerer were true, inside it awaited a great many curiosities and valuable secrets..._
    **If the manor is found first:**

    _An abandoned home, or even an abandoned village, was no rare sight in the Velen of those days, but a certain set of ruins attracted Geralt's particular attention. He decided to find out what exactly was responsible for the manor's sorry state._
    _Geralt investigated Aeramas' estate and discovered the mage had been a raring tyromancy enthusiast - tyromancy being, as you surely know, the art of divining the future from cheese. This is a very rare specialization among mages, one only practiced by those for whom a social life is not a high priority - there reason being that the cheese they use to divine with is, and pardon my blunt language, fouler smelling than a drowner's scrotum. No wonder Aeramas' home hadn't been looted._
    _Geralt searched the abandoned residence from top to bottom. Among the assorted worthless trinkets, he found some true treasures which Aeramas had cleverly hid from the world by setting murderous cheeses as guards. Tyromancers are not normally the cheery sort, but Aeramas certainly would have been delighted to know that his treasure had ended up in good hands._

## Objectives

  * **If found the manor first:**
    * Find out more about the run-down residence.
  * Use Aeramas' artifacts to activate the portal.
  * Search Aeramas' residence using your [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses").
  * Find a way to activate the portal.
  * Use Aeramas' artifacts to activate the portal.
  * Use the portal in Aeramas' residence.
  * Investigate Aeramas' dungeon.
  * Find a way to pass the tyromancy rooms. ## Bugs

  * Despite the quest being in the [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") region, in the quest log it's labeled as being in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen").

## Notes

  * The lever at the top of Aeramas' residence simulates a "light switch" and has no other functional purpose.
  * It is entirely possible to skip the statue maze by climbing the rocks along the wall on the left side of the room as you enter.
  * A [notice board posting](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aeramas%27_Shop:_the_Best_Goods_Around! "Aeramas' Shop: the Best Goods Around!") for this quest exists in the game files, but is [unused](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content").

**Redania's Most Wanted** is a [secondary quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_secondary_quests "The Witcher 3 secondary quests") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and is part of the Assassins' quests (the other two being [A Deadly Plot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Deadly_Plot "A Deadly Plot") and [An Eye for an Eye](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Eye_for_an_Eye "An Eye for an Eye")) needed for [Reason of State](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reason_of_State "Reason of State"). However, this could be allowed to fail without locking out Reason of State (see notes for further details). 

_Note:_ this quest will fail if not completed before starting [The Isle of Mists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Isle_of_Mists_\(quest\) "The Isle of Mists \(quest\)"). 

## Walkthrough

Go to [Est Tayiar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Est_Tayiar "Est Tayiar") to meet the [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter"). They will not be happy to see Geralt as they believe he will claim all the glory for himself after so many of them died trying to get in, but they will give him the [plate key](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Key_to_Philippa_Eilhart%27s_hideout "Key to Philippa Eilhart's hideout") anyway. Head on down to find a forcefield and use your witcher senses to examine the wall to the right and place the plate there. 

_Note:_ You can also find a breakable wall there behind which you can find the [Feline silver sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Feline_silver_sword "Feline silver sword") [diagram](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Diagram:_Feline_silver_sword "Diagram: Feline silver sword") for the [Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Cat_School_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear"). 

Continue forward to find 2 portals, the one to the left is open while the other is not, so use the left one. Inside you will find some [nekker warriors](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nekker_warrior "Nekker warrior") (mislabeled as [nekkers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nekker "Nekker")) and an inactive portal. Notice the power cell crystal nearby, if you examine it Geralt will mention that one of his signs may charge it, use [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") to do just that. Now that the portal is working, continue through it. 

### Courage above all

Once through you will meet a survivor, who will have another [power cell](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Power_cell "Power cell"), but refuses to give it up because he is too afraid of the monsters: 

  * You can use [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") (requires [Delusion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Delusion "Delusion") level 2 for a minimum of 35 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"))
  * Knock some sense into him, in which case you need to fistfight him

Regardless once you have the power cell continue forward. If you look inside the chests you can find a [Letter to Philippa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letter_to_Philippa "Letter to Philippa"). Now place the cell on the marked location and use Aard on it to open the next portal. After getting through, Geralt will be greeted by more Nekkers, kill them and go right to find another inactive portal. Geralt will remark that the nekkers may have the next power cell, so go back and take the path leading down. You will find a few more nekkers and the power cell there. Take it back to the portal and activate it like the previous. 70[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") is awarded once you go through the portal. 

### The Lair

Now you will be at the lowest level and encounter a [Fire Elemental](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fire_Elemental "Fire Elemental"), so use your [elementa oil](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elementa_oil "Elementa oil") and whatnot to get rid of it. Now enter through the gate at the far end to find Philippa's room, to the left you can find some [agate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Agate "Agate"). Search the rest of the room to find a broken [megascope](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Megascope "Megascope") and a [polished crystal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Polished_crystal "Polished crystal"). _Note:_ The crystal doesn't glow with [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses"), so it can be hard to spot - it's by the right-side column. Take it then go back to the last portal, however, there is no need to go back all the way, just go to the right and there will be another portal that is inactive. Use Aard on the nearby power cell to activate it and it will take you back to the beginning through the inactive portal that was to the right at the start. 

### The choice

Once you are back out the witch hunters will demand you give them the polished crystal. Agree and you get 50[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"), but no payment from [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") later. If you refuse, you will have to fight them. If you chose the latter, you can bring it back to Radovid, or if [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss "Triss") and/or [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") are available you can bring it to them. If you go to either of them, it will reveal the contents of the crystal, after which you can take it back and hand it over to Radovid anyway. 

If you don't give him the crystal, you will not receive payment. This includes the case if you gave it to the witch hunters, as they will have lied to him that they found it and he takes their word over yours. However, if you do have and give him the crystal, you'll receive 135[![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"). Regardless of your choice, the quest will end here. (50[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level")) 

## Journal entry

    _They say chess is the game of[kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King"), and [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") proved this point twice over during his meeting with [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"). First, by playing the royal game at the [Oxenfurt Chess Club](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Chess_Club "Oxenfurt Chess Club") while they met, and then by treating Geralt like a pawn he could send out to do his bidding. His requested move? [Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") takes [sorceress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorceress "Sorceress"): his spies had reported finding signs of [Philippa Eilhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Philippa_Eilhart "Philippa Eilhart")'s presence in the [elven ruins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Est_Tayiar "Est Tayiar") outside of [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). Geralt was to go there and retrieve her. Normally Geralt resisted attempts to command him around the board, but he owed Radovid a favor - and had his own reasons for wanting to find Philippa..._
    _Geralt found and searched Philippa's hideout. Though there were signs of her presence everywhere, the sorceress herself was long gone._
    _She had, however, left one thing behind that could prove of great value - a damaged crystal from her[megascope](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Megascope "Megascope"). The witcher knew such a gem could, if properly probed, reveal priceless information about her doings. He thus was faced with a choice - should he give the crystal to Radovid, or take it to one of his sorceress allies?_
    **If Geralt takes the crystal to Triss:**

    _Geralt took the crystal to[Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold"). A bit of [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") and she got it to play back a fragment from the last conversation Philippa had with [Margarita Laux-Antille](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margarita_Laux-Antille "Margarita Laux-Antille"). From it they learned the sorceress, whom Radovid had blinded, was trying to restore her vision, and that both were planning to bring the [Lodge of Sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses") back to life._
    _The witcher decided it would be best if such information never made it to Radovid's ears. He thus decided to leave the crystal with Triss._
    **If Geralt takes the crystal to Yennefer:**

    _Geralt took the crystal to[Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg"). A bit of magic and she got it to play back a fragment from the last conversation Philippa had with Margarita Laux-Antille. From it they learned the sorceress, whom Radovid had blinded, was trying to restore her vision, and that both were planning to bring the Lodge of Sorceresses back to life._
    _The witcher decided it would be best if such information never made it to Radovid's ears. He thus decided to leave the crystal with Yennefer._
    **If Geralt reports to Radovid promising only his word:**

    _Out of a sense of duty, or perhaps a desire to receive at least part of the promised reward, Geralt returned to Radovid to tell him what he had found in Philippa's hideout. The king was clearly not pleased with the outcome of the investigation and dismissed the witcher. As for compensation for his trouble, sadly, the witcher had to go without..._
    **Additionally, if Geralt gave the crystal to the witch hunters before:**

    _Geralt encountered Radovid's men at the entrance to Philippa's hideout. They demanded the witcher give them the magic crystal, with assurances they would deliver it to the king personally. Geralt reluctantly accepted their offer._
    **If Geralt reports to Radovid with the megascope crystal:**

    _As agreed, Geralt returned to Radovid and gave him the crystal from Philippa's megascope. Though the king was hoping to be handed a sorceress, not a cold, damaged lump of minerals, in the end he appreciated Geralt's effort and paid him his reward._

## Notes

  * In the Official Prime Guide, it was noted that to unlock Reason of State, all three Assassins' quests ([A Deadly Plot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Deadly_Plot "A Deadly Plot"), [An Eye for an Eye](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Eye_for_an_Eye "An Eye for an Eye"), and Redania's Most Wanted) had to be completed/couldn't be failed. However, it was later shown the latter two could be failed as long as one completed A Deadly Plot and bribed Dijkstra (as opposed to breaking his leg) at the end of Blindingly Obvious to get the quest to unlock. 
    * For this particular quest, the actual requirement is that you search Philippa's hideout and find the [Polished crystal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Polished_crystal "Polished crystal"). You can then safely fail this quest without locking out [Reason of State](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reason_of_State "Reason of State") by not returning to Radovid afterwards.
  * When you descend from the outside through the hole in the ground, before entering the hideout previously barred by a force field, use Aard on the cracked wall behind you. It will lead to a secret stash where you will find the [Feline silver sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Feline_silver_sword "Feline silver sword") diagram for the [Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Scavenger_Hunt:_Cat_School_Gear "Scavenger Hunt: Cat School Gear").
  * A number different circumstances can affect if you can show/give the crystal to the sorceresses: 
    * If you get the crystal after finishing [Now or Never](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Now_or_Never "Now or Never"), the option to give it to Triss won't appear, naturally because she can't be found anywhere at that point. In this case, if [The Last Wish](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Last_Wish_\(quest\) "The Last Wish \(quest\)") hasn't been completed yet, an option to show the crystal to either sorceress will never appear and unless given to Radovid or the witch hunters, the crystal becomes a sellable junk item.
    * If you got the quest before finishing The Last Wish, this quest will not show an option to give Yennefer the crystal. If you don't give it to Triss, it will appear that the only option is to give it to Radovid. But if you hold on to the crystal until you finish The Last Wish, you will get the option to give it to Yennefer, even if the quest has already ended.
    * If you held on to the crytal until it can be given to Yennefer and then pick the conversation option about wondering what to do with it, Geralt will automatically agree with Yennefer's suggestion that she should keep it. This will remove the item from your inventory and it will update the quest log to say that Geralt decided to give her the crystal. However, no objective mark will appear about it unless the quest is still active.
  * At the lowest level of the cave there are multiple lootable containers hidden among ruins and at least one chest can contain relic weapons.
  * You do not have to give the crystal to anyone to have completed all objectives—going straight to Radovid will mark the Triss objective as completed, despite never talking to her about it (and the one about Yennefer doesn't appear in the log).
  * There is a minor continuity error at the end of the quest when Geralt goes to report to Radovid. When first meeting the king, he is required to surrender his weapons before boarding the ship; at the end, he is allowed on board fully armed with no reference to his weapons being relinquished. (As it's only a cutscene and dialogue, it makes no difference to Geralt.)
  * Giving the crystal to Triss marks the Yennifer objective as failed, even if it hadn't yet appeared on the list.
  * There is an unknown amount of [cut content](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") related to this quest, including a [damaged eyes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Damaged_eyes "Damaged eyes") item which, along with the agate, was related to Philippa attempting to restore her eyes.

## Bugs

  * Showing the crystal to Triss may mark objectives for talking to both her and Yennefer as failed. There is no known fix for this.
  * Having Triss look at the crystal, but not giving it to her could cause a bug in [A Matter of Life and Death](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Matter_of_Life_and_Death_\(The_Witcher_3\) "A Matter of Life and Death \(The Witcher 3\)") where the Vegelbud servant won't spawn in the fish market in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). Should this happen, a fix would be to find Triss in her hideout, and give her the crystal (the option is there even if you already gave the crystal to Yennefer after [The Last Wish](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Last_Wish_\(quest\) "The Last Wish \(quest\)").
  * The nekkers at Philippa's hideout are actually mislabelled nekker warriors.

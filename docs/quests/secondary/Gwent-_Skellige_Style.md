## Walkthrough

_Note:_ While the quest can be picked up any time from the [notice board](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notice_board_\(Kaer_Trolde_Harbor\) "Notice board \(Kaer Trolde Harbor\)") at [Kaer Trolde Harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor"), it cannot truly be started until [Echoes of the Past](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Echoes_of_the_Past "Echoes of the Past") is completed. Additionally, [Practicum in Advanced Alchemy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Practicum_in_Advanced_Alchemy "Practicum in Advanced Alchemy") also has to be completed to unlock two of the matches. 

**[Ermion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ermion "Ermion")** is always the first player to face, but the earliest point to do that is while he walks away right after Echoes of the Past. You can later find him at [Gedyneith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gedyneith "Gedyneith"). Once beaten, he will award you the [Leshen card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leshen_\(gwent_card\) "Leshen \(gwent card\)") and direct you to [Gremist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gremist "Gremist") and [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite"). 

**Gremist** , just north of Gedyneith, cannot be played until [Practicum in Advanced Alchemy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Practicum_in_Advanced_Alchemy "Practicum in Advanced Alchemy") is completed. When beaten, he gives you the [Mysterious Elf card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mysterious_Elf_\(gwent_card\) "Mysterious Elf \(gwent card\)") and directs you to [Madman Lugos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lugos_Drummond_\(Madman\) "Lugos Drummond \(Madman\)"). 

**Madman Lugos** is found at [Kaer Muire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Muire "Kaer Muire"). However, unless [Svanrige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Svanrige_Tuirseach "Svanrige Tuirseach") becomes king, he cannot be played after you set sail for [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") at the start of [Battle Preparations](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_Preparations "Battle Preparations"). If you miss that last opportunity, an objective to loot his [Vampire: Katakan card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vampire:_Katakan_\(gwent_card\) "Vampire: Katakan \(gwent card\)") will appear. In this case, you will get a red mark for winning the match, but [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") is still awarded when the card is looted. 

_Note:_ At least as of [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"), Ermion and Lugos are still available in their usual places before asking Crach where the druid is for [The Sunstone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Sunstone "The Sunstone") \- even though Geralt supposedly doesn't know where Ermion is, he's not yet gone to Kaer Muire and is just chilling at Gedyneith, allowing to go through everything needed to unlock the match with Madman Lugos. 

**Crach** is usually found in his room at the [Kaer Trolde citadel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_citadel "Kaer Trolde citadel"). When beaten, he gives you the [Draug card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Draug_\(gwent_card\) "Draug \(gwent card\)") and directs you to [Sjusta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sjusta "Sjusta"). However, Crach can't be played after you start the battle during [On Thin Ice](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/On_Thin_Ice "On Thin Ice"). Should this happen, the objective to play him will be removed, you'll simply receive his card (no [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") though) and be directed to Sjusta. 

**Sjusta** is always at his place at [Kaer Trolde Harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor") and beating him awards the [Yaevinn card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yaevinn_\(gwent_card\) "Yaevinn \(gwent card\)"). 

Winning each match awards 25[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and when you're done with all of the above, you'll also be awarded the [Emhyr var Emreis: The White Flame leader card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis:_The_White_Flame_\(gwent_card\) "Emhyr var Emreis: The White Flame \(gwent card\)") as the quest completes. 

## Objectives

  * Win a unique card from [Ermion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ermion "Ermion").
  * Win a unique card from [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite").
  * Win a unique card from [Gremist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gremist "Gremist").
  * Win a unique card from [Sjusta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sjusta "Sjusta").
  * Win a unique card from [Madman Lugos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Madman_Lugos "Madman Lugos").
  * **If you couldn't play Madman Lugos:**
    * Search Lugos' room and find a unique card.

## Journal entry

    _Gwint[[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] is played wherever long evenings, warm fires and strong alcohol are present – meaning it is quite popular in the [Skellige Isles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"). Skellige players rank among the best there is, so [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") decided to try his hand against them and see if he could win their most powerful cards._
    _The Skelligers had even better decks than Geralt had expected – yet still he defeated them and added their prize cards to his collection._

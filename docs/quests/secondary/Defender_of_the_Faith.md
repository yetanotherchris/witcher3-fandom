**Defender of the Faith** is a [secondary quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_secondary_quests "The Witcher 3 secondary quests") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

## Walkthrough

To start the quest you'll need to find the old woman who can be encountered at 2 of the 3 shrine locations: 

  * between [Blackbough](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blackbough "Blackbough") and [Crow's Perch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crow%27s_Perch "Crow's Perch")
  * at [Duén Hen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Du%C3%A9n_Hen "Duén Hen")

Regardless how you find her, fix the shrine for her and she'll ask if you can fix the rest, earning 10 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"). 

However, two more shrines need to be fixed in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen") and while the level for this quest is 10, there are high leveled enemies between the two shrines in lower Velen, particularly a [griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)") if you stick to the main roads, so be mindful of this. 

Note that while it doesn't matter what order you fix them, as you'll earn 25 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") at the second, if you do the one at Duén Hen second, you'll encounter an [endrega warrior](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Endrega_warrior "Endrega warrior"), while if you do the unmarked location between [Byways](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Byways "Byways") and [Frischlow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Frischlow "Frischlow") there will be [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)"), so if you're looking to avoid as many fights as possible, it's best to find the woman at Duén Hen then do the one near Crow's Perch second (as it doesn't spawn anything). 

The last location is where you'll run into the vandals destroying the shrines. Letting them do as they please fails the quest, but beating them up (or killing them) completes it, earning 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"). 

## Journal entry

    _[Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") has a "live and let live" policy regarding the [gods](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Deities "Category:Deities") and their worshipers: if they don't bother him, he returns the favor. Sadly, not everyone follows this sage precept. A distraught woman in Velen informed him someone had been destroying the shrines that dot that region's roadsides. She begged him to repair the damage and, if possible, punish those responsible. Though he did not share the woman's devotion or outrage at this sacrilege, he decided to help all the same._
    _While following the trail of damaged holy sites Geralt encountered a group of young men who, he learned, were students at[Oxenfurt Academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Academy "Oxenfurt Academy"). Influenced by some radical philosopher, they had taken up arms in the battle against religion._
    **If Geralt stops the vandals:**

    _As is often the case with students, what began as a measured discussion of worldviews ended in a[brawl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_fistfighting "The Witcher 3 fistfighting")._
    **If Geralt doesn't interfere (fails quest):**

    _The[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") engaged them in a philosophical discussion which threatened to come to blows, but luckily merely ended with an exchange of profanities and insults._

## Trivia

  * A number of references exist in this quest to philosophical and religious figures and works: 
    * The title of the quest "Defender of the Faith" may be a reference to [Philip Roth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_Roth "wikipedia:Philip Roth")'s short story titled "[Defender of the Faith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goodbye,_Columbus "wikipedia:Goodbye, Columbus")", or to a title given to monarchs.
    * Many make a reference to [Friedrich Nietzsche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Friedrich_Nietzsche "wikipedia:Friedrich Nietzsche")'s works, including "[Overman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%9Cbermensch "wikipedia:Übermensch")", "[God is Dead](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_is_dead "wikipedia:God is dead")" quote, and _[Beyond Good and Evil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beyond_Good_and_Evil "wikipedia:Beyond Good and Evil")_.
    * "[Religion is the Opium of the Massses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opium_of_the_people "wikipedia:Opium of the people")" is attributed to [Karl Marx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karl_Marx "wikipedia:Karl Marx").

## Notes

  * If you don't draw your sword, the students won't draw their swords as well, so it becomes a fistfight, not a bloody massacre, though the result is the same - they all end up dead or unconscious.

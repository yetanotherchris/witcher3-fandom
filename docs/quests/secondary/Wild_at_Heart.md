## Walkthrough

This quest can be picked up from several [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen") [notice boards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notice_board "Notice board"), including from [Mulbrydale](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mulbrydale "Mulbrydale"), [Midcopse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Midcopse "Midcopse"), [Inn at the Crossroads](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Inn_at_the_Crossroads "Inn at the Crossroads"), and [Crow's Perch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crow%27s_Perch "Crow's Perch"). You just need to obtain one to read to then find Niellen in Blackbough. 

In Blackbough you'll find him standing in front of a hut talking to his sister-in-law, [Margrit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margrit "Margrit"). Talk to him and agree to look for his wife and the two will give you several people to talk to and a location to check out. 

There are a couple people [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can talk to: the butcher's wife and the blacksmith. In addition, the latter's children also give Geralt a bit more information about the morning Hanna disappeared. 

Now head out to the marker in the woods to find more clues about Hanna's whereabouts. Eventually Geralt finds a pack of [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)") gathered around a corpse. After they're dealt with, Margrit appears and attempts to bribe Geralt to lie to Niellen, saying she doesn't want to lose him and he deserves better than this. You can either accept the bribe or keep searching. If you accept, you'll earn 80 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and 55 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"), the search field disappears, and the quest can be finished by talking to Niellen back in Blackbough. However, even if you agreed to lie to Niellen, as long as you don't go back to talk to him, the clues still linger and Geralt can still investigate despite telling Margrit otherwise. 

_Note:_ Accepting Margrit's offer and then continuing the investigation despite that is the most profitable route. However, this will create and subsequently fail a mission objective to tell Niellen that Hanna is dead. 

If you wish to lie, head back to Niellen who'll offer you a reward. If you accept, you'll get 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and 10 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"), otherwise if you refuse you'll just get 30 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"). Regardless, the quest will then conclude here. 

### Find the werewolf's lair

If you continue to investigate, examine the dog corpse then, slightly further away, a bloody mess that must be Hanna, which makes Geralt conclude it's the work of a [werewolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf"). There are also claw marks around that should be clearly visible with [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses"). Follow the trail of marks to a scratched stone leaning against a tree. Obviously you can't climb up there, so look for a tuft of fur nearby, just around the tree, although it can be hard to spot. That when examined will allow Geralt to follow the werewolf's scent. 

Follow the trail which leads to some torn clothes and eventually to an [isolated shack](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Isolated_Shack "Isolated Shack"). While optional, you can enter the shack and pick up the [mysterious notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mysterious_notes "Mysterious notes") and read them to learn some additional information. There's also a locked chest that you can come back for later. Once done, exit the shack and use Witcher Senses to follow a trail of blood to the werewolf's lair entrance, hidden underneath the shack, then use [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") on the door or break it with a weapon. 

_Note:_ You'll likely notice a Bear just north of the [isolated shack](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Isolated_Shack "Isolated Shack"). There's no requirement to fight it, but it may be hard to avoid as it tends to see Geralt from afar and moves toward the shack - if not at first, then when you exit the cave or when you enter the shack to loot the locked chest after killing the [werewolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf"). 

### Fighting the werewolf

Once you enter the cave, or if you meditated/waited in the cave for nightfall, the werewolf and three weaker wolves will run in from the entrance. It's recommended to deal with the wolves separately or from a distance so the much stronger werewolf can be dealt with on its own. 

The werewolf swiftly claws at you, dealing moderate damage, but his attacks are very aggressive. This is best returned with highly active and equally aggressive tactics, as he stuns you when attempting to parry or countering his hits. Thus, dodging and rolling works much better as a defensive tactic. It's also worth using fast strikes with the [silver sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword") rather than attempting to set up heavy strikes or long chains of actions. Once the werewolf's health bar reaches half, with a howl he starts regenerating health. Take advantage of the howl as it leaves him vulnerable to a few heavy attacks. This is where you should make use of [signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign"), [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions"), and [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bombs "The Witcher 3 bombs") as switching to defensive tactics would only allow the werewolf to regain health. If you're having trouble and he's regenerating faster than you deal damage, one solid tactic is to drink a [Tawny Owl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tawny_Owl "Tawny Owl"), and then [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") him after a series of 3-4 fast attacks, then repeat. 

A cutscene will interrupt the fight once the werewolf's health is low enough and Margrit runs in. No matter what you pick, you learn she knew Niellen was a werewolf for a long time and secretly loved him regardless. She then decided to show her sister what Niellen was, thinking Hanna would be disgusted and leave him. However, this plan backfired and Niellen slaughtered his own wife in a blind bloodlust. Enraged at this, Niellen threatens to kill Margrit for what she's done. You're then given a timed reaction to tell Margrit to leave so you can finish Niellen off, or let Niellen have his revenge. 

If you allow Niellen to enact his revenge, the werewolf will slaughter Margrit without mercy, after which he returns to Geralt to ask for death and Geralt will oblige, killing Niellen without resistance. If you told Margrit to run, you'll need to finish killing Niellen. Whichever course is taken, the quest completes, earning 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"). You can also loot Niellen's body for [his key](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Niellen%27s_key "Niellen's key") which will open the locked chest in the isolated shack. _Note:_ if you let Niellen have his revenge, he'll only have the key on his body. If you killed him by protecting Margrit, the key will be there along with some werewolf ingredients. Furthermore, if Margrit survived, you'll find her crying in the isolated shack. 

## Journal entry

    _While muddling around the well-shaded village of Blackbough, Geralt happened across a notice. It stated a hunter named Niellen was looking for any help he could get in finding his wife, who had disappeared in the nearby woods a few days earlier. Geralt, already deep in a search-and-rescue vein, decided to help find the woman._
    **If Geralt agrees with Margritt:**

    _While Geralt was combing the woods for signs of the missing woman, he came across Margaret, Hanna's sister. The woman asked the witcher to abandon his search and tell Niellen his wife was dead. Margaret thought the sooner Niellen came to terms with his loss, the sooner he would be able to put it behind him and start living his life. Geralt decided this made sense and agreed._
    **If Geralt declines Margrit's offer and continues to search:**

    _The story of Hanna's disappearance had a tragic end. It turned out she had been killed by her own husband. He did not do this on purpose, however. Afflicted with the curse of lycanthropy, he would hide in the forest during his transformations in order to protect his friends and loved ones._
    _Hanna's sister Margaret knew this secret, and had one of her own - she was in love with Niellen and fiercely jealous of Hanna's life with him. She decided to take Hanna into the woods to watch her husband's transformation, hoping she'd then abandon him in disgust. Sadly, Hanna was careless enough to draw the werewolf's attention. Margaret was able to escape, but her sister was not so lucky. Niellen, transformed into a bloodthirsty beast, slaughtered the love of his life with his own hands._

## Objectives

  * Talk to the hunter named Niellen.
  * Ask the neighbors about Hanna.
  * Search for signs of Hanna in the woods using your Witcher Senses.
  * **Accept Margrit's bribe:** (80[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level")) 
    * Tell Niellen Hanna is dead.
  * **Continue to investigate:**
    * Find the werewolf's lair using your Witcher Senses.
    * Enter the cave.
    * Search the werewolf's lair.
    * **If it's not night time:**
      * Wait for nightfall.
    * Defeat the werewolf. (50[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level"))

## Notes

  * A few days later, you can run into a group of peasants in the village discussing the three's fate. 
    * If Geralt let Niellen kill Margarit (and thus killed Niellen) the villagers will mention how Margrit was found torn to shreds in the woods and Hanna likely died as well and speculate a depressed Niellen left the area as a result.
    * If you lied to Niellen, villagers will note that both Niellen and Margrit are dead, but don't elaborate on how they died.
    * If you spared Margrit, the villages will note she left as her sister and brother-in-law are dead.
    * There's also a single unmarked peasant, smoking a pipe behind the northernmost hut, who can be talked to and gives a more concrete account of how they found Niellen dead, if Geralt killed him.
  * If Niellen begs for Geralt to kill him, Geralt will remark for Niellen to hang himself with reins from a tree, a throwback to where Geralt himself was told similarly _[Sword of Destiny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sword_of_Destiny "Sword of Destiny")_ and in turn told [Istredd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Istredd "Istredd") in the same book.

## Bugs

  * The chest unlocked by Niellen's key is also supposed to have 100 [orens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oren "Oren"), but they never appear due to a bug.

## Trivia

  * The title of this quest is the same name shared by a [David Lynch movie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_at_Heart_\(film\)) starring Nicholas Cage and Laura Dern.
  * The [mysterious notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mysterious_notes "Mysterious notes") found in the shack are a callback to the quest [Beauty and the Beast](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beauty_and_the_Beast "Beauty and the Beast") from the first Witcher game. Where the same methods are used to cure [Vincent Meis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vincent_Meis "Vincent Meis") from Lycanthropy.

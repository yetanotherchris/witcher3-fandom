## Walkthrough

Go talk to [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson") inside his [bookstore](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Books_and_Scrolls "Books and Scrolls") just off [Hierarch Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch_Square "Hierarch Square") and he'll immediately tell you a red book was left with him years ago to one day be given to the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), but he can't remember where he placed it, so you'll have to look around for it. 

There aren't that many books here, and all of them are unique for this quest so feel free to pick them all up to read: 

  * [Moribundia: The Vampire's Last Likeness](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Moribundia:_The_Vampire%27s_Last_Likeness "Moribundia: The Vampire's Last Likeness")
  * [Tyromancy, or the Noble Art of Cheese Divination](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tyromancy,_or_the_Noble_Art_of_Cheese_Divination "Tyromancy, or the Noble Art of Cheese Divination")
  * [The Merry Adventures of Muriel the Lovely Harlot, Illustrated Edition](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Merry_Adventures_of_Muriel_the_Lovely_Harlot,_Illustrated_Edition "The Merry Adventures of Muriel the Lovely Harlot, Illustrated Edition")
  * [The Treatment of Furuncles Through Cauterization: A Study](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Treatment_of_Furuncles_Through_Cauterization:_A_Study "The Treatment of Furuncles Through Cauterization: A Study")
  * [My Manifesto - The Life of Jacques de Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/My_Manifesto_-_The_Life_of_Jacques_de_Aldersberg "My Manifesto - The Life of Jacques de Aldersberg")

However, it is only the last one that you need and it is upstairs, just before the third floor stairs. On picking it up, a [letter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letter_from_%22A%22 "Letter from "A"") inside of the book will also automatically be added to the [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory"). Once you read this letter the quest will complete, earning 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and 51 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"). 

## Journal entry

    _Geralt stopped by a certain[Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") book merchant whose owner recognized him at once. He said someone had left a book with him a long time ago with instructions to give it to Geralt if he ever got the chance. This naturally piqued the witcher's curiosity. Sadly, the book dealer did not know the book's exact present location, only that it had a red cover and that Geralt should look for it in his stacks of dusty tomes and manuscripts._
    _Geralt found the book and, inside it, a letter from someone he had once known. Though he gave no outward sign of it, this filled him with touching recollections of old times. To this day I don't know who the old friend was nor what he had written in the letter - and that's a shame, for judging by Geralt's behavior, therein lies a very interesting tale..._

## Objectives

  * Find a book with a red cover using your [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses").
  * Read the letter you found inside the book "My Manifesto."

## Notes

  * This letter is a reference to [Jacques de Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jacques_de_Aldersberg "Jacques de Aldersberg"), the main antagonist of the first game who was speculated to be the grown up [Alvin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alvin "Alvin"). This letter finally confirmed the theory.
  * Oddly, the book itself isn't red as described by the merchant.

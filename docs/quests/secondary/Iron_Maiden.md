## Walkthrough

East of [Harviken](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harviken "Harviken"), on the top of a hill, you'll see a laid out ring in which a woman fights and knocks out her opponent before demanding if there's really no one that can best her. Approach and she'll reveal herself as [Jutta an Dimun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jutta_an_Dimun "Jutta an Dimun"). However, she won't fight a nobody and thus asks [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to perform a worthy feat to fight her and will give one of the following tasks: 

  * defeat [Gundar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gundar "Gundar") in Harviken
  * recover [Horned Hoskuld's sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horned_Hoskuld%27s_sword "Horned Hoskuld's sword")

Note that certain feats will allow you to skip any tasks as she'll recognize your name: 

  * Defeat [Olaf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Olaf_\(bear\) "Olaf \(bear\)") ([Fists of Fury: Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fists_of_Fury:_Skellige "Fists of Fury: Skellige"))

If you've already found the sword or defeated Olaf, she'll acknowledge Geralt as a worthy opponent and fight him on the spot. If you didn't do it yet though, you must complete it to continue the quest. 

### The tasks

If asked to defeat Gundar, it'll be a sword fight and you must take care not to leave the arena to beat him. You can also use this to your advantage: if having a hard time defeating him, try to force him to back out of the ring (like near the nearby hut). 

If you were given the sword task, go to the far western part of [Faroe Isle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Faroe_Isle "Faroe Isle") to find the sunken treasure chest, and where a bunch of [ekhidnae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ekhidna "Ekhidna") are flying over. Deal with the nearby threats (if you're lucky, you'll only need to kill a couple) then dive down and retrieve the sword, then head back to Jutta. 

### Talk to Jutta

Once your task is complete, talk to Jutta and tell her, and she'll then fight you. If you instead tell her you refuse to fight, she'll refuse to fight you in the future. This can then lead to a bug: while the quest won't mark as failed, when you then try to talk to her about fighting, she'll give you another task, sometimes including the same one you just did, and the quest won't acknowledge it. 

If you do fight her, bear in mind not to use any [signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign"), [crossbow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crossbow "Crossbow"), [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bombs "The Witcher 3 bombs"), or [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions"), as the quest will fail owing to it not being a fair fight. Also note you only get one shot at this: if she defeats you instead the quest will fail and you won't get another chance to fight her. 

After knocking her health down significantly she'll yield (25 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level")) then ask where you learned to fight and regardless what you say, she'll insinuate meeting her at her place after dusk. You can take her up on her offer or not. _Note:_ if you choose not to take her offer right here, the quest will mark as failed. 

Head to her home (the earliest at 8pm) to talk with her and she'll reveal her vow to [Freya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Freya "Freya"): she swore she'd marry the man who could best her in a fight and have children with him, but turns out Geralt, a [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"), is sterile. If you tell her you can help with the other part of that, the two will have sex. If you instead tell her the wrong guy beat her, the two will just talk before ending the night with her commenting how it's time she left Faroe. _Note:_ sleeping with her will have no effect on your relationship with [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") or [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold"). 

Note that if Geralt sleeps with Jutta after the encounter he will be disrobed and unarmed and you'll need to reequip all his armor and weapons; this differs from most other encounters (i.e., Triss, Yennifer, Keira, prostitutes) which automatically restore Geralt's inventory after. 

## Journal entry

    _In one of Skellige's famed arenas Geralt met an undefeated warrior named Jutta and decided to try his strength against her. Before he could do so, however, he had to complete several tasks to prove he was a worthy opponent._
    **If Geralt loses to Jutta (fails quest):**

    _Geralt did everything Jutta asked and in the end was granted the right to face her in combat. Though Geralt put up a brave fight, the warrior woman defeated him. Forced to savor the bitter taste of defeat, Geralt left the arena._
    **If Geralt defeats Jutta:**

    _Geralt did everything Jutta asked and in the end was granted the right to face her in combat. Jutta proved a fierce and demanding opponent, but Geralt still was able to best her. Enchanted by his strength and manliness, Jutta asked the witcher to join her for a rendez-vous on the beach._
    **If Geralt declines her offer (fails quest):**

    _Yet Geralt had no desire to get to know her any better and so thanked her for a fight well fought and continued on his way._
    **If Geralt accepts and spends an intimate evening with her:**

    _Both Jutta and her proposition were to Geralt's liking, so he agreed to meet._
    _The warrior woman turned out to be just as deft with words as with a sword. It quickly became clear, however, that she had planned on doing more than just converse that evening. And you know our dear Geralt - he's never one to refuse a beautiful woman. They passed a very pleasant evening with a particularly spicy finale._
    **If Geralt accepts but just talks with her in the evening:**

    _Both Jutta and her proposition were to Geralt's liking, so he agreed to meet._
    _The warrior woman turned out to be just as deft with words as with a sword. Though she clearly was hoping for more than just conversation, Geralt had other plans, thanked her for the good time and continued on his way._

## Objectives

  * **The following are mutually exclusive and depends on the task given:**
    * Look for the sword Jutta requested in the sunken ship's wreck using your [Witcher Senses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Senses "Witcher Senses").
    * Defeat Gundar.
  * Tell Jutta you have performed the feat.
  * Defeat Jutta.
  * Meet Jutta at her home after dusk.

## Notes

  * Using the crossbow, throwing bombs, or casting signs other than [Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") during the fight with Jutta will fail the quest.
  * The use of potions is blocked during the fight, however, the effects of any potions imbibed prior to the fight will continue to be active.
  * Since you may still consume food/drink during the fight, the skill [Gourmet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gourmet "Gourmet") may prove useful.
  * Loading a save immediately before approaching Jutta will change the task she gives you.
  * Gundar never appears unless he is the given task.
  * Even if you are given the task to defeat Gundar, you can still retrieve the sword. Upon returning to Jutta, she will accept your feat as if that's what she asked and "Defeat Gundar" objective will be marked as successful, even if you never met him. You can also complete both tasks and then have a choice which one to say you did.
  * Regardless of which task you are given, if [Fists of Fury: Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fists_of_Fury:_Skellige "Fists of Fury: Skellige") is not yet completed, you can always defeat [Olaf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Olaf "Olaf") instead and return to Jutta. Simply ask _"Will you fight me?"_ again and she will recognize the feat, and whichever objective you were given will be marked as resolved too.
  * If you acquire the sword, but don't present it to Jutta during the quest, it will remain forever as a quest item in your inventory, and it is considerably heavier than most other quest items. The only way to remove it then is on PC via the console command removeitem('mq2038_headsman_sword')

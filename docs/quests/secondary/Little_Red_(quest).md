## Walkthrough

Head to [Yantra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yantra "Yantra") where, just on the western edge of the village you'll find a group gathered about, saying they'll protect one man among them. Talk to them and they'll reveal a bandit named [Little Red](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Little_Red_\(bandit\) "Little Red \(bandit\)") and her gang wish to attack the village soon. You can ask them more on this and they'll reveal [Bertram](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bertram_\(peasant\) "Bertram \(peasant\)") went and told the [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter") about her group, and the hunters attacked her gang. However, she found out about Bertram's involvement and now wants revenge. 

You can then agree to help or leave them to their own devices. If you agree, they'll run off to their huts to wait until it's all clear and you'll have to wait until dusk (7pm or later) for the bandits to appear. 

Once the proper time arrives, you'll see the bandits knocking on doors calling for Bertram to come out. However, unlike most they're neutral to you and won't attack while they look for their target. You also can't immediately attack them, but must talk to Little Red instead, who's standing to the side. 

_Important:_ After the bandits appear, you can't stray too far away, otherwise the quest will fail. Note if it's already evening, they can appear immediately after you talk to Bertram, so it is best to discover this quest during the day unless you intend to complete it immediately. 

Talk to her and, if you ask her why she wants Bertram specifically, she'll reveal that the peasant came to her with a deal half a year ago: he'd point out who's worth robbing and they'd give him a percentage of the spoils. However he got greedy, wanting more of the cut, and when Little Red refused he went to the witch hunters, who killed half her men. You now have two choices: stand aside or kill her. 

If you stand aside, as promised she'll just kill Bertram, rewarding you 25 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and 20 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"), before she and her gang leave. 

If you refuse to stand aside, she'll force you to fight her, and reveals herself to be a [werewolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werewolf "Werewolf"). _Important:_ if you haven't already, it's best to turn on manual sword drawing for this fight, as [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") will draw the [steel sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Steel_sword "Steel sword") rather than [silver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_sword "Silver sword"). As she's obviously the bigger threat at lower levels, switch to silver and kill her off quickly, doing your best to dodge or incapacitate her men in other ways (like using [bombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bombs "The Witcher 3 bombs") or [signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign")). Afterwards, remember to turn manual sword fighting off unless you prefer it on. If instead you find the bandits more troublesome than the werewolf, you won't need to bother changing this setting. 

Once Little Red and her men are killed, Bertram will thank you and give a lame excuse why he didn't tell you she was a werewolf. You'll then earn 50 [![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") and 10 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown"), completing the quest. 

Note that you can't reveal Bertram's role in the robberies to the villagers, whether you save him or not. 

## Journal entry

    _Geralt's travels took him to a certain village whose inhabitants were clearly anxious about something. It turned out they were expecting a gang of bandits led by a woman known as Little Red to attack any minute. She was out for the blood of a certain Bertram who she claimed had wronged her. Geralt promised to defend the villagers._
    **If Geralt spares Little Red:**

    _When Little Red's band attacked, it became clear the matter was not at all as clear as it had at first seemed. The bandits' leader assured Geralt she did not want to hurt anyone except for this Bertram. The man had worked with her at one point as an informer pointing out who was worth robbing, but then turned and reported them to the authorities._
    _Faced with the choice of killing several people or letting one guilty man be punished, Geralt decided to let Little Red have her revenge. For that, he received a reward._
    **If Geralt kills Little Red:**

    _When Little Red's band attacked, it was clear the villagers had bet on the right horse in hiring Geralt. He made quick work of the cutthroats, collected his reward and went off into the sunset._

## Objectives

  * Wait until dusk for Little Red's band to attack.
  * Defend the villagers.

## Trivia

  * Little Red's name is a reference to Little Red Riding Hood, though ironically Little Red is now the werewolf. 
    * The irony stems from the lack of a proper translation. In the Polish (Original) version her name is _"Wilczyca"_ which has no real English translation, as it simply means "female wolf" and there is no proper way to refer to a female wolf in English except "she-wolf" or sometimes "bitch" like with female dogs. Obviously neither would work well as a name or alias.
  * Little Red may be a reference to Laurie from the horror movie [Trick 'r treat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trick_'r_Treat) who dresses as Little Red Riding Hood for Halloween and is later revealed to be a werewolf as well.
  * Coincidentally, Bertram plays into the wolf motifs as well: he could be described as a wolf in sheep's clothing (someone who appears nice but in reality has ill intentions).

## Notes

  * You can find a [letter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_mother%27s_letter "A mother's letter") on Little Red's corpse from her parents.
  * Despite it requiring to be after dusk, once the group arrives you can meditate to another time and they'll still be looking for Bertram until you talk to Little Red and the quest won't fail (until you stray too far away).

## Bugs

  * Upon accepting this quest, the door to the eastern facing house in the middle of Yantra will remain permanently locked, effectively making the contents of this house inaccessible.

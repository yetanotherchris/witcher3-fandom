## Walkthrough

This quest is started by speaking to [Petter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Petter "Petter") and [Ulf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ulf "Ulf") at the [Grymmdjarr Monument](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Grymmdjarr_Monument "Grymmdjarr Monument"), on the road running between [Fayrlund](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fayrlund "Fayrlund") and [Fyresdal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fyresdal "Fyresdal") on [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"). Petter is badly injured, having fallen from a great height off a stone statue while trying to light a fire atop its shoulders. Though succeeding, the ferocity of the fire caused him to fall and break his leg. [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can offer Petter some [celandine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Celandine "Celandine") to help dull the pain or simply leave them be. 

Unlike other quests, this quests doesn't have a "fail" status if you leave them, it simply never appears in one's journal. 

## Journal entry

    _Age-old customs can be as dangerous as they are beautiful - a lesson learned the hard way by two young fishermen Geralt encountered on Ard Skellig. As youth of their line had done for ages, the men set out to light a fire on the shoulder of a certain gigantic statue. Sadly, one of them slipped on the plinth, fell and broke his leg. Geralt decided to help the injured man._
    _Ah, if only[human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") bodies could tolerate [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") potions! Sadly, Geralt could not administer such a toxic brew to the fisherman in good conscience, so the injured young man had to be content with some celandine. Not much, not much at all, but still, something._

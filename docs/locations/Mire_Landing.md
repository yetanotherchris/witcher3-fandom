## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Mire Landing** , also known as **Cestersover** , is an abandoned village south of [Byways](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Byways "Byways"). Once the bandits are killed off, villagers including a [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Mire_Landing\) "Merchant \(Mire Landing\)") will return and 120[![XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/f/f7/Tw3_icon_xp.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/25?cb=20170901051043)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/XP#The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "XP earned based on the Story and Swords! difficulty level") \+ 50 [![Oren3](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Oren3.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/18?cb=20160608063044)](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") is awarded. Despite its relatively large size, the village doesn't have its own signpost, but [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can [fast travel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fast_travel "Fast travel") to its [harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harbor "Harbor") while on a boat. 

## Map description

    _Not long ago, during the reign of[King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Political_titles "Political titles") [Medell](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Medell "Medell"), [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest")'s father, folk from far and wide would gather here to fish in the crystal-clear waters, which were exceedingly rich in crayfish. The region's most beloved dish was a creamy crayfish bisque spiked with hearty helping of dill. Alas, no more..._

# Guarded treasure

[![Tw3 icon guarded treasure](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a8/Tw3_icon_guarded_treasure.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/32?cb=20170901053622)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a8/Tw3_icon_guarded_treasure.png/revision/latest?cb=20170901053622)

There are a number of **guarded treasures** scattered about the various regions in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , and its expansions, _[Hearts of Stone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hearts_of_Stone "Hearts of Stone")_ expansion and _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion. In general, they consist of a single powerful or few weaker [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") that must be killed before a treasure can be looted, with a few exceptions where the enemy appears only after the treasure is looted. 

In that regard, they aren't much different from [bandit camps](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bandit_camp "Bandit camp"), but guarded treasures typically contain higher-quality loot and relic equipment in particular. Some have at least partially fixed loot (including all _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion locations), but a lot are randomized.

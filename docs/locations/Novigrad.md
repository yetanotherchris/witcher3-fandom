**Novigrad** is a free city within [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") and is therefore not subject to the rule of that kingdom. It is one of the major ports on the [continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent") and populated by nearly 30,000 inhabitants, making it one of the largest cities in the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/North "North"). 

Like any true metropolis, Novigrad has many factories and is home to all manner of craftsmen offering every ware possible and one can even find the occasional con-man or shady dealer. The city is also home to numerous banks and even has a zoo. The [Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire") is said to protect the city's inhabitants from all evil, monsters included. The thick city walls have never been breached, as they were tactfully designed by the architects of the [Oxenfurt Academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Academy "Oxenfurt Academy"). 

Novigrad is inhabited by an unusually colorful group of both permanent residents and those in town on long and short-term visits. Most eye-catching amidst the throngs of common townsfolk, stall-keepers, and craftsmen are those practicing the more roguish professions. There is no army in the city but it does have a [secret service](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad_secret_service "Novigrad secret service"), an ever-present [Temple Guard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Guard "Temple Guard"), and a powerful [Temple Fleet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Fleet "Temple Fleet").

## History

### First Landing

During the [Conjunction of the Spheres](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Conjunction_of_the_Spheres "Conjunction of the Spheres"), a [magical passage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Portal "Portal") opened at this site. Later, what began as a circle of [monoliths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monolith "Monolith")[4] turned into a small [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") settlement.[5] In this settlement, at the site of the passage, a temple dedicated to the moon was built.[4] When the first [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") ships arrived in the [Pontar Delta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_Delta "Pontar Delta"), the city was abandoned by the elves and it quickly became the capital of a new country created by King [Sambuk](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sambuk "Sambuk"), the ancestor of the first king of Redania.[6]

### Road to independence

During the reign of [Vestibor the Proud](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vestibor "Vestibor"), Novigrad was conquered by [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") during the [Seven Years' War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War "Seven Years' War"). The capital of Redania was then moved to Tretogor and Novigrad became a Temerian city for a long period.[7][8]

The situation changed during the reign of Vestibor's grandson, [Radovid III the Bold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_III "Radovid III"). After long negotiations, a compromise was reached and the metropolis was acclaimed as a free city.[8]

### Northern Wars

After the [First Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/First_Northern_War "First Northern War") in [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1263 "Timeline"), King [Vizimir II](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II") of Redania forced Novigrad into _ius stapulae_ , blocking all merchandise passing the city for inspection and sale within the city.[5]

### The Syndicate and Witch Hunts

[![Gwent Novigrad expansion](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a3/Gwent_Novigrad_expansion.jpeg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/300?cb=20190617204436)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a3/Gwent_Novigrad_expansion.jpeg/revision/latest?cb=20190617204436)

Although Redanian kings are officially recognized as "_kings of Redania and Novigrad_ ", real authority is vested in the hierarch by the [Electoral College](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Electoral_College "Electoral College"). By [1275](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1275 "Timeline/Games") the current hierarch was [Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cyrus_Engelkind_Hemmelfart "Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart"). 

However, it has long been known that the city is under the control of its criminal underworld, which even the Hierarch himself is not stranger to; by [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), the city was in fact under the control of the [Syndicate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Syndicate "Syndicate") and eventually later by the [Big Four](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Big_Four "Big Four").[4]

    _Once part of Redania, Novigrad now has the status of a free city. It is the world's largest city and, without a doubt, the richest as well. Cradle of the cult of the Eternal Fire, it is now home to the church of the same name. It is ruled officially by the head of the church, referred to as the hierarch, though its underworld crime bosses also have a great deal of say in matters._

## Trivia

  * Novigrad as seen in third game is inspired by medieval [Amsterdam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amsterdam) and [Gdańsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danzig).[9][10]
  * Novigrad, or Novi Grad, means _"Newtown"_ , _"Newcity"_ or _"Newcastle"_ in various Slavic languages, and it is the name of different cities in various Slavic countries (such as Novigrad, Croatia).
  * The concept of the Free City of Novigrad is mainly based on the historical Free City of Gdańsk, which was such a city-state twice between 1807-1815 and 1920-1939, but also had considerable independence as a royal city of Poland from the 15th to the 18th centuries. Like Novigrad, the city was also a large and rich port on the (Baltic) sea, with a very strong economical and cultural position. There were also witch trials in the town.

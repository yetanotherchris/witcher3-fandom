**Tir ná Lia** is the capital of the world of the [Aen Elle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Elle "Aen Elle") and was governed by [Auberon Muircetach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Auberon_Muircetach "Auberon Muircetach"). The river [Easnadh](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Easnadh "Easnadh") runs through it. According to [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), the city has a look similar to that of [Shaerrawedd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shaerrawedd "Shaerrawedd"). 

Its buildings are described as light, open on all sides (thus resembling gazebos or arbours), seeming so airy, delicate and ephemeral that they actually look not like buildings, but phantoms of buildings. They are built of marble, alabaster, and malachite, and adorned with stucco, terracotta, and mosaics. There are many bridges and terraces, peristiles, cloisters and balconies, sculptures and monuments. 

According to [Galahad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Galahad "Galahad"), this land is known by people of his world and have various names: Faërie (by [Britons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtic_Britons "wikipedia:Celtic Britons")), [Annwn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annwn "wikipedia:Annwn") (by Celtic druids) or [Elfland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elfland "wikipedia:Elfland") (by [Saxons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saxons "wikipedia:Saxons")).

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

During the third game, the city is visited one time, during the main quest, [Through Time and Space](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Through_Time_and_Space "Through Time and Space"). [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and Avallac'h go here to visit the [viceroy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Viceroy "Viceroy") at that time, [Ge'els](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ge%27els "Ge'els"), who resides in the [Palace of Awakening](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Palace_of_Awakening "Palace of Awakening").

## Trivia

  * The name Tir ná Lia is likely to be connected with "[Tír na nÓg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%ADr_na_n%C3%93g "wikipedia:Tír na nÓg")" (Land of Youth), which is the world of elves in Irish mythology.

## Notes

  * Contrary to description from the novel, the buildings in the game resemble those of a typical medieval town.

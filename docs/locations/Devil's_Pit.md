## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Devil's Pit** is a large abandoned quarry located east from the village of [Mulbrydale](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mulbrydale "Mulbrydale") in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen") that has been taken over by a group of deserters from the [Temerian army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Military_of_Temeria "Military of Temeria"). 

With [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"), the next-gen update, this location was given a quest that occurs within the cave system here, [In the Eternal Fire's Shadow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/In_the_Eternal_Fire%27s_Shadow "In the Eternal Fire's Shadow") and, depending on the outcome, Devil's Pit may become inhabited once more. If so, a [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Devil%27s_Pit\) "Merchant \(Devil's Pit\)") will also appear in the house outside the gates, which will be unlocked.

## Notes

  * Devil's Pit was originally a field hospital for plague victims but since the [Catriona](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Catriona "Catriona") questline was entirely cut, the area has been repurposed.[1]
  * Prior to [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"): 
    * It featured an extensive cave system that was inaccessible by any conventional means in-game, because it was unfinished. Inside was an isolated bed with candles around it - almost certainly the place of ritual involving [Martin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hector_Krafft_Ebbing "Hector Krafft Ebbing")'s body. Further into the cavern stands a huge stone object, likely a sigil that was connected to demonic presence.
    * Unlike other bandit camp locations in the game, enemies here respawn even after clearing the entire place of both enemies and loot. This and the swarm of [nekkers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nekker "Nekker") just south of the signpost made it a popular place to level up [Aerondight](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aerondight "Aerondight").
  * Since [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"): 
    * The fast-travel signpost is moved a bit to the east and there are no longer nekkers to the south.
    * If the deacon dies at the end of the quest, the pit is infested by respawning [ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul").

## Trivia

  * If the place is inhabited again after the quest, a dog named Kal can be seen around. This is in reference to [Henry Cavill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henry_Cavill "Henry Cavill")'s dog.

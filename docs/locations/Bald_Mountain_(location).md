## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Bald Mountain** ([Elder Speech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Speech "Elder Speech"): _Ard Cerbin_) is a low mountain in southern [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen") topped by an ancient oak. Every year the [Crones](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crones "Crones")' Sabbath takes place in and around the mountain, which is attended by those loyal to the Crones – mostly local peasants – as a form of tribute. At the foot of the mountain is a [small village](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Road_to_Bald_Mountain "Road to Bald Mountain"). Within the mountain itself, the Crones have a den in which they feast on the bodies of those they take. 

## Associated quest

  * [Bald Mountain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bald_Mountain_\(quest\) "Bald Mountain \(quest\)")

## Notes

  * If the Bald Mountain quest is not active, the village area will be occupied by high-level [foglets](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foglet "Foglet") who are lethal to intrepid low-level players exploring the map. Once the quest is active, the village will be safe, with a blacksmith and one or two merchants.
  * On the mountain there is one [Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") Sign [Place of Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Place_of_Power "Place of Power"). It cannot be accessed until a certain point in the Bald Mountain quest is reached.
  * This is the location [the Ghost in the Tree](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Ghost_in_the_Tree "The Ghost in the Tree") mentions to Geralt that the orphans from [Crookback Bog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crookback_Bog "Crookback Bog") were taken to (if Geralt met them before meeting the spirit).

## Trivia

  * Bald Mountain is a reference to [Łysa Góra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%81ysa_G%C3%B3ra "wikipedia:Łysa Góra") (Bald Mountain), a mountain in Poland known to be an ancient pagan place of cult activity and, in legends, as a place where witches had sabbaths.

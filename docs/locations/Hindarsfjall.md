**Hindarsfjall** is the smallest[1] island in the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") archipelago. Located closely behind [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik"), it has a conical shape, many fjords and the landscape is covered by a lush vegetation and forest.[2]

There lies the sacred grove [Hindar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindar "Hindar") and [Temple of Freya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Freya "Temple of Freya") which is tended by the high priestess. Located just off the coast is also another mythical place, a great rock called [Kaer Hemdall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Hemdall "Kaer Hemdall"). 

## History

In [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), when Ciri had gone missing, [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") convinced Jarl [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite") to accompany her to the island. There she spoke with the priestesses of the Temple of Freya and the then-high priestess, Modron [Sigrdrifa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigrdrifa "Sigrdrifa"), regarding a possibility of urgent rent of powerful [Brisingamen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brisingamen "Brisingamen").[2]

* * *

The following is considered game canon only and may contradict [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")'s works.

* * *

When Ciri teleported to the island in 1272 and was treated at a home in the village of [Lofoten](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lofoten "Lofoten"), she also briefly visited the local sauna before leaving. Upon leaving, however, the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") attacked the village. Ciri managed to flee on horseback with [Skjall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skjall "Skjall")'s help. 

Sometime later Yennefer and Geralt visited this village whilst searching for Ciri learning that she was there and in the presence of Skjall, who had since gone to sacred gardens to fight [Morkvarg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morkvarg "Morkvarg") and died. Geralt found his body and Yennefer performed necromancy on it to discover information about Ciri, but ultimately killed the energy of the place. For this, the priestesses declared that no Islander is permitted to feed, shelter or help Yennefer.[3]

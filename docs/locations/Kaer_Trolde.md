**Kaer Trolde** is a port city reigned over by jarls from [Clan an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_an_Craite "Clan an Craite") on the coast of [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"), the largest of the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") islands. It is usually compared to black and amorphous like a huge marine crustacean thrown on the shore by the waves.[1] It is built just under the island's [main stronghold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_citadel "Kaer Trolde citadel") whose highest towers make the entire archipelago visible. 

The [port](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor") serves as the docks for the [Jarl of Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jarl_of_Skellige "Jarl of Skellige")'s fleet and is accessible by longships and triremes.[2] It is home to many sailors, fishers, and warriors but also [alchemists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemist "Alchemist"), goldsmiths, glass blowers, and jewelers. The island branch of a [dwarven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf") [Cianfanelli Bank](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cianfanelli_Bank "Cianfanelli Bank") is also situated here.[1]

According to legend, [Grymmdjarr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Grymmdjarr "Grymmdjarr"), the founder of Clan an Craite, carved the keep that overlooks the city with his bare hands.[3]

## Trivia

  * A tapestry in the Kaer Trolde throne room depicts warriors battling the [Ice Giant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_Giant "Ice Giant"). Its style makes reference to an actual 11th century artifact, the Bayeux Tapestry, which shows [William the Conqueror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_the_Conqueror "wikipedia:William the Conqueror")'s successful battles for control over [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England "wikipedia:England").[4]
  * "Trolde" is the Danish word for "trolls".

## Notes

  * The city is downsized to a small settlement in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_.

## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Hierarch Square** is one of the main squares of [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), in the heart of the city center. Many nobles live here, giving them front row seats to the regular mage executions conducted by the [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter"). 

The square is littered with trading posts and market stalls, much like the nearby Fish Market. It hosts several merchants, including an herbalist, and an armorer in an alcove beneath one of the buildings on the western side. One of the merchants possesses sizeable amounts of [mandrake cordial](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mandrake_cordial "Mandrake cordial") and [cherry cordial](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cherry_cordial "Cherry cordial") \- two of the ingredients required for making [white gull](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_gull "White gull") which is used in many higher-level alchemical recipes. 

One could take out a loan, or convert [orens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oren "Oren") and [florens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Floren "Floren") into [Novigrad crowns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crown "Crown") at the Vivaldi Bank overseen by [Vimme Vivaldi](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vimme_Vivaldi "Vimme Vivaldi"). 

The stone steps on the square's eastern side lead up to the [Kingfisher Inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Kingfisher_Inn "The Kingfisher Inn"), known for hosting performances by the famous trobairitz [Priscilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priscilla "Priscilla"), better known by her stage name Callonetta. 

Triss' ransacked house can be accessed through a gate on the south side of the square. 

## Map description

    _Until quite recently a great many mages lived near Novigrad's main square. They fled when the witch hunters began their reign of terror, leaving many of the city's most beautiful townhouses abandoned and uncared for._

**Vizima** , **Wyzim** , or **Wyzima**[1] (Polish: _Wyzima_) is the capital city of [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria"), one of the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). 

## History

### First Landing

Vizima once was the location of an ancient [elven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf") settlement, which upon the great eastern expansion of [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") approximately five hundred years ago, was abandoned by the elves and in its place humans built this city.[1][2]

### Foltest's reign

Around [1266](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1266 "Timeline"), between the [First](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_I "Northern War I") and [Second Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Second_Northern_War "Second Northern War"), [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") introduced _ius stapulae_ to Vizima, blocking all merchandise routes with inspection sites around the city, and seizing any [Redanian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") or [Nilfgaardian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") goods to be sold within the city.[1]

## _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_

Located on the shores of a beautiful [lake](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lake_Vizima "Lake Vizima"), **Vizima** is a walled city and the main setting of _The Witcher_ computer game. The city is under a state of quarantine for the duration of the game, so movement about town in general is limited most of the time, and totally restricted, for some particular areas. 

### [Journal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_journal "The Witcher journal") entry

    _Vizima, the capital of Temeria, is the largest city in the kingdom. It is located on the shore of Lake Vizima, at the intersection of important trade routes, one of which is a waterway. Owing to a developed network of roads, the city draws considerable profit from trade. By King Foltest's order, the city is governed by Burgomeister[Velerad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velerad "Velerad"). Vizima is divided into three large districts. The poorest one is the Temple Quarter, where Saint Lebioda's Hospital and the Cloister of the Order of the Flaming Rose are located. The Trade Quarter is home to the wealthiest and most important inhabitants, and both the [town hall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Town_hall "Town hall") and the main [marketplace](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marketplace "Marketplace") are situated there. The oldest part of the city, Old Vizima, has been recently converted into a ghetto for [non-humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Non-human "Non-human")._
    _I acquired a pass which allows me to pass through the gates into the city in spite of the quarantine._

### Gates

[![ marked by Chapter](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/2b/Map_Vizima_major_locations.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/230?cb=20090120222552)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/2b/Map_Vizima_major_locations.png/revision/latest?cb=20090120222552) [![ marked by Chapter](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/2b/Map_Vizima_major_locations.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/230?cb=20090120222552)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/2b/Map_Vizima_major_locations.png/revision/latest?cb=20090120222552) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Map_Vizima_major_locations.png)

[Locations](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_locations "The Witcher locations") marked by Chapter

This restriction of movement is achieved through guarded gates which separate the various areas. There are nine gates in total. Six main gates: 

  * [Merchants' Gate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchants%27_Gate "Merchants' Gate"), locked for the duration of the game
  * [Maribor Gate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maribor_Gate "Maribor Gate"), locked for the duration of the game
  * [Miller's Gate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Miller%27s_Gate "Miller's Gate")
  * [Gate to Old Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gate_to_Old_Vizima "Gate to Old Vizima")
  * [Gate to New Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gate_to_New_Vizima "Gate to New Vizima")
  * [North Gate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/North_Gate "North Gate"), locked for the duration of the game

Three internal gates also restrict movement between areas of the city, at least for parts of the game: 

  * [Gates connecting the Trade and Temple Quarters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gate_to_the_Trade_Quarter "Gate to the Trade Quarter")
  * two gates to enter the cemetery, from the Trade or Temple Quarters
  * A map showing all the gates can be found [here](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Map_Vizima_gates.jpg "File:Map Vizima gates.jpg").

During the Prologue, Chapter I, and Chapter IV, the city proper is inaccessible, but the main story takes place within its confines. The city and surroundings are divided into the following locations: 

### City proper

  * [Temple Quarter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Quarter "Temple Quarter") — Chapters II, III and V
  * [Trade Quarter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Trade_Quarter "Trade Quarter") — Chapter III
  * Royal Quarter — never accessible
  * [Vizima cemetery](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima_cemetery "Vizima cemetery") — Chapters II and III
  * [Vizima sewers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima_sewers "Vizima sewers") — Chapters II, III and V
  * [Royal castle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest%27s_castle "Foltest's castle") — Chapter V

### Surroundings

  * [Outskirts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Outskirts "Outskirts") — Chapter I
  * [Vizima dike](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima_dike "Vizima dike") — Chapters II, III and V
  * [Old Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Vizima "Old Vizima") — Chapter V

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

[![Vizima](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/96/Tw2_journal_Vizima.png/revision/latest?cb=20110602134439)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/9/96/Tw2_journal_Vizima.png/revision/latest?cb=20110602134439 "Vizima")Vizima

Vizima is only mentioned in the _The Witcher 2_. 

### Journal entry

    _Lying where important trade routes converge, Temeria's capital sits picturesquely on the shore of Lake Vizima in the valley of the Ismena River, a tributary of the Pontar. The city is divided into four teeming quarters, which are home to thousands and host travelers from all corners of the world. The Royal Palace and court are both found here, and the city boasts many markets, temples, forges, shops, banks, workshops, inns and brothels. Since our story does not concern Vizima directly, I will end my description here and urge those interested to consult other sources._

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

[![TW3 castle garden](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/64/TW3_castle_garden.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/230?cb=20160210172259)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/64/TW3_castle_garden.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160210172259) [![TW3 castle garden](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/64/TW3_castle_garden.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/230?cb=20160210172259)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/6/64/TW3_castle_garden.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20160210172259) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:TW3_castle_garden.jpg)

Following the regicidal events of the previous game, [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") invaded a weakened Temeria, subjecting its capital city to occupation. Residency of the Royal Palace has been taken up by Emperor [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") himself, who personally invites Geralt to visit. Geralt can only access certain parts of the inner residence, namely, all the areas from the first game and one new area off the garden. 

  * [Royal Palace](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Royal_Palace_\(Vizima\) "Royal Palace \(Vizima\)") (occupied by Nilfgaard)

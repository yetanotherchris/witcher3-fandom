## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**White Orchard** is a region and village located in [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") famous for its fruit orchards, whose boughs burst out white blossoms come spring. The [Ismena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ismena "Ismena") river flows through the village and continues north. 

After the [Battle of White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_White_Orchard "Battle of White Orchard"), the village lost its independence and was annexed by [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"). 

There is a [Nilfgaardian Garrison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Garrison "Nilfgaardian Garrison") headquartered in the northwest point of the region near the mouth of the river that, in [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), was controlled by [Captain](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Captain "Captain") [Peter Saar Gwynleve](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peter_Saar_Gwynleve "Peter Saar Gwynleve"). In addition to the merchants listed below there are also six [Places of Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Places_of_Power "Places of Power"). Two are for [Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen") and one for each of the other four [signs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign"). 

## Developer comments

The game begins near a small Temerian village, in surroundings which at first glance seem almost idyllic. Clear skies abound and the orchards' trees are bursting with white flowers. It takes a moment to notice the tell-tale signs of war: collapsed roofs, turned-over carts littering the roadside, and abandoned possessions strewn about neglected farmyards. This juxtaposition of a warm, sunny spring and a landscape rife with destruction and conflict lends color and credence to the game's atmosphere. In this land, as is often the case in reality, war continues to upend human lives no matter the weather or the time of year.

## Notes

  * Depending on your choices in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , the final controlling force of this region will either be [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") (under [Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra") or [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V")) or [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") (as a vassal state under [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") or [Empress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Empress "Empress") [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri")). 
    * In particular, if Temeria is restored, Lord [Dagborg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dagborg "Dagborg") will rule over this region.

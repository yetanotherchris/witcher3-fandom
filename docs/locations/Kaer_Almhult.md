## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Kaer Almhult** is a large fortress found between the islands of [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig") and [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog"). After it fell into disrepair, a large group of bandits took over the ruins. One time the fortress was supposed to become the seat of the [king](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King_of_the_Skellige_Isles "King of the Skellige Isles"), but it never came into practice. 

## Map description

    _Built centuries ago to serve as the home keep for the kings of[Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"). In practice, however, each ruler preferred to keep his clan's seat, and Kaer Almhult was left unused. Eventually the decision was made to turn it into a prison, and today the fortress is a crumbling ruin._

## Notes

  * The pirates at the signpost marker do respawn until the entire fortress is cleared, so [fast travelling](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fast_travel "Fast travel") to it may be a dangerous action.
  * There is a hidden treasure marker on the south side of the fortress that may be hard to both find and resolve. 
    * It is located in the dungeon cells and you have to kill practically all enemies to reach it.
    * The [key to prison](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Key_to_prison "Key to prison") that is required to clear it unlocks two cells and multiple treasure containers within them, not just one.

## Trivia

  * There is a clear reference to the _[A Song of Ice and Fire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Song_of_Ice_and_Fire "wikipedia:A Song of Ice and Fire")_ series by George R. R. Martin within Kaer Almhult. The cells are built exactly like the "sky cells" in The Eyrie, and in one of the cells, you can find a [dwarf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf") that looks similar to Tyrion Lannister, to which [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") will comment, "_Sky cells... Nice idea for a prison without bars. Shame he didn't know how to fly_ ".
  * Within the fortress, you can also find a pirate with a [mysterious note](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mysterious_note_\(The_Witcher_3\)#Skellige "Mysterious note \(The Witcher 3\)") that reads: "_Keep an eye out for castaways. Lone travelers. Drunk warriors. Bring them to me, to the cellar. The Ancient One craves blood. Ph'nglui mglw'nafh Cthulhu R'lyeh wgah'nagl fhtagn._ ", a reference to the Cthulhu mythology of H.P. Lovecraft.
  * Kaer or Kêr means "city" in Breton, Caer means "fort" in Welsh, while Ker means "city" or "fortress" in Cornish.
  * Kaer Almhult is likely named after [Älmhult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almhult) in Sweden.

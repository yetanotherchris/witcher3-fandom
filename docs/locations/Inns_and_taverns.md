# Inns and taverns

**Inns and taverns** play a significant role in the [Witcher universe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"). The [very first story](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(short_story\) "The Witcher \(short story\)") about the witcher begins with [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") entering [Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima") and heading straight for [The Fox](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Fox "The Fox"), one of the two taverns in town. Many telling conversations between Geralt and the people he meets take place at the local inn, both in the novels and in the game. 

## _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_

[![New Narakort Inn, seen from the outside](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0f/Places_New_Narakort_exterior.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/260?cb=20081116173348)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0f/Places_New_Narakort_exterior.png/revision/latest?cb=20081116173348 "New Narakort Inn, seen from the outside")New Narakort Inn, seen from the outside [![Tutorial image](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Tutorial_inns_and_taverns.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/260?cb=20170504223821)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e9/Tutorial_inns_and_taverns.png/revision/latest?cb=20170504223821 "Tutorial image")Tutorial image

In _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ , inns and taverns are found throughout the game, except in Chapter V, the Epilogue, and "[The Price of Neutrality](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Price_of_Neutrality "The Price of Neutrality")." They are excellent places to store, or retrieve, items which are of no immediate use and therefore cluttering up precious [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_inventory "The Witcher inventory") slots and to buy or sell other items, like [alcohol](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Potion_bases "Potion bases") for [potion making](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_potion_preparation "The Witcher potion preparation"), or [food](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_food_and_drink "The Witcher food and drink"). They can also be used as places to [meditate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meditation "Meditation") which is also necessary in [potion making](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_potion_preparation "The Witcher potion preparation"). 

They are also social places where one can find [entertainment](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Entertainment "Entertainment"), [fistfighting](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_fistfighting "The Witcher fistfighting") and [gambling](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_dice_poker "The Witcher dice poker").

### [![Game Interaction icon storage](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/d9/Game_Interaction_icon_storage.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/35?cb=20170523040146)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/d9/Game_Interaction_icon_storage.png/revision/latest?cb=20170523040146) Storage

  * To _store_ an item at, or retrieve an item from, an inn or tavern, first speak with the innkeeper.
  * Left-click on the "storage" icon (sometimes the storage icon is not immediately visible, so a conversation thread must be followed until it is).
  * In Chapter V ([Old Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Old_Vizima "Old Vizima")) and the Epilogue, [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion") performs this function in a limited way, no other storage possibilities exist.

### [![Game Interaction icon sleep](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/28/Game_Interaction_icon_sleep.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/35?cb=20170523043447)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/2/28/Game_Interaction_icon_sleep.png/revision/latest?cb=20170523043447) Meditation

  * To _[meditate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meditation "Meditation")_ in inns, first speak with the innkeeper.
  * Left-click on the "meditation" icon.

### [![Game Interaction icon shop](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/ac/Game_Interaction_icon_shop.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/35?cb=20170523043526)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/ac/Game_Interaction_icon_shop.png/revision/latest?cb=20170523043526) Buying and selling

Typically these transactions are handled by the [waitresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Waitresses "Waitresses"). Just initiate conversation with the waitress and then either ask her using a conversation option what she sells, or just click on the buy/sell icon during dialogue mode. This will open a panel showing you what she has to sell. Also, items she is willing to buy will be highlighted in your own inventory panel on the same screen. 

Typical wares include [food](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_food_and_drink "The Witcher food and drink"), [drink](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drink "Drink"), as well as some [grease](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blade_coating_bases "Blade coating bases"). Just double-click on an item to either buy or sell. Happy transacting! 

### Notes

  * You can leave an unlimited number of items in a tavern's storage. You can also recover your stored items at any time, from any inn or tavern. **Take note** that Chapter V has _no inns nor taverns_ , and therefore no storage possibilities during the act. Only one opportunity is given, by your friendly sidekick [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion") at the beginning of Chapter V, but that is the _one and only time_ you can speak with him prior to the very final scene in the game.
  * It is advisable to always have one free slot when using storage to retrieve items. There was a bug which caused items to fall on the floor and become unusable (sort of embedded into the floor image).
  * There is an unofficial [Kalkstein innkeeper mod](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Storage_mods "Storage mods") available.

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

In the second game, there is always a [Notice Board](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notice_Board "Notice Board") in front, near the entrance of an inn. There are two proper inns and one area that functions as an inn since it doesn't even have a door or a roof. Inns and taverns offer [storage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_storage "The Witcher 2 storage") that carries over its content throughout all three chapters. 

### Locations

  * Chapter I: the [Flotsam inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flotsam_inn "Flotsam inn")
  * Chapter II: [The Cauldron](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Cauldron "The Cauldron") in [Vergen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vergen "Vergen")
  * Chapter III: Eternal Fire inn, area just to the southwest of the central marketplace right past the Notice Board in [Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne").

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

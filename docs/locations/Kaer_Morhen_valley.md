**Kaer Morhen valley** is an abandoned area of the mountainous region known as [Hertch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hertch "Hertch")[1] in northeastern [Kaedwen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaedwen "Kaedwen"), where the prehistoric sea was located many millennia ago.[2]

Currently, the only known "inhabited" structure in the valley is the limestone keep of [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen"), the home of the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher")'s [School of the Wolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Wolf "School of the Wolf"), and surrounding places. The [Gwenllech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwenllech "Gwenllech") river flows through the valley. 

## _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_

Kaer Morhen valley is the area immediately adjacent to and surrounding the fortress of [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen"). It is here that the [campsite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Camp_near_Kaer_Morhen "Camp near Kaer Morhen") of the unexpected visitors to [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") is situated in _[The Price of Neutrality](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Price_of_Neutrality "The Price of Neutrality")_ premium module. The valley itself is divided by a fast flowing river which can only be crossed at either of the two fords. The area is also infested with [archespores](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Archespore "Archespore"), [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)"), [basilisks](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk"), [giant centipedes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Giant_centipede "Giant centipede") and [wyverns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wyvern "Wyvern"). 

The area also boasts: 

  * two [circles of elements](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Circle_of_Elements "Circle of Elements") (one for [Aard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aard "Aard") and one for [Igni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igni "Igni"))
  * three [places of power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Place_of_Power "Place of Power")
  * an [old mine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Old_mine_outside_Kaer_Morhen "Old mine outside Kaer Morhen")
    * The entrance near Kaer Morhen fortress leads to the kikimore hatchery. It is where [Eskel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eskel "Eskel") is first found.
    * The more southerly entrance leads to a basilisk den.

One can also "see" [Beggartick](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beggartick_blossoms "Beggartick blossoms") outside, but it can not be picked. There is some that is "pickable" inside [Sabrina's tent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Camp_near_Kaer_Morhen "Camp near Kaer Morhen"), but Geralt does not have the journal entry, so trying to pick it is useless. 

There are also [Berbercane](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Berbercane_fruit "Berbercane fruit") trees here and there, but those, like the beggarticks, are just landscaping, too. 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

The valley of Kaer Morhen is a major region in the third and possibly final game of Geralt's adventures.[3] The geography of the area, while more fully fleshed-out, is faithful to the original game, including the interior of the keep, which has preserved the same floorplan. 

### Map description

    _Kaer Morhen, meaning "Keep of the Sea of Yore," is home to a school of witchers. Years ago the keep was destroyed and its inhabitants murdered during a massive pogrom. Currently only a handful of witchers winter here before setting out again on the Path._

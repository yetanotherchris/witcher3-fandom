## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Undvik** is one of the larger islands in the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") archipelago. It is described as lying east of the main islands of [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig") and [An Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Skellig "An Skellig"). 

## History

* * *

The following is considered game canon only and may contradict [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")'s works.

* * *

In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), the population of Undvik was forced to abandon the island after the [ice giant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_giant "Ice giant") [Myrhyff](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Myrhyff "Myrhyff") appeared. [Hjalmar an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hjalmar_an_Craite "Hjalmar an Craite") led an expedition to kill it, joined by fellow clan members. 

_If Geralt went to help Hjalmar:_ Geralt's actions influenced the events that took place on this expedition. The ice giant was slain and the isle of Undvik free from it's rule. 

Later, during their Skellige campaign, the [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") laid their fleet off the coast of Undvik and later made an encampment on the shore to assist [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") against the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt"). 

* * *

## Notes

  * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , it is located to the western side of Skellige, probably to make the map more square.
  * There was a lot of additional content related to Undvik that was [cut](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content") from _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. Some remnants can still be seen during The Lord of Undvik quest, but there's also plenty in a collection of "journals from Urskar": 
    * [Notes from Urskar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notes_from_Urskar "Notes from Urskar") (1st journal) alluding to the usual ill omens predicting the arrival of the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt"), which however [skelligers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") attribute to impending [Ragh nar Roog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ragh_nar_Roog "Ragh nar Roog").
    * [Memoirs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Memoirs "Memoirs") (3rd journal) detailing the start of an alliance between clans [an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_an_Craite "Clan an Craite") and Tordarroch and plans for the two clans to jointly raid [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"), but also explaining why the people of Undvik later fled specifically to [Kaer Trolde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde "Kaer Trolde").
    * [Guard captain's notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Guard_captain%27s_notes "Guard captain's notes") (4th journal) talking about strange signs and disappearances around [Tor Gvalch'ca](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tor_Gvalch%27ca "Tor Gvalch'ca"), heavily implying that the ice giant came through an unstable portal open at the tower.
    * [Notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notes#5th_journal_from_Urskar "Notes") (5th journal) accounting the birth of a two-headed lamb, chronologically implying more trouble coming from the tower.
    * [Notes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notes#7th_journal_from_Urskar "Notes") (7th journal) mentioning that [Madman Lugos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lugos_Drummond_\(Madman\) "Lugos Drummond \(Madman\)") attempted an attack on a village on the island's eastern shore, speculatively in retaliation after the an Crate-Tordarroch alliance, but most notably no such village exists on the game map. What is now the quite useless [Gull Point](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gull_Point "Gull Point") is likely where it was supposed to be, and makes logistical sense as one of the closest points to [Holmstein's Port](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Holmstein%27s_Port "Holmstein's Port") and the [Clan Drummond](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Drummond "Clan Drummond") domain.

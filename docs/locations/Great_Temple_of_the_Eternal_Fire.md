The **Great Temple of the Eternal Fire** is one of the nineteen[1] temples of [Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). It is linked with the market square by Main Street.[2]

The temple is also known for the sarcophagus of Prophet [Lebioda](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lebioda "Lebioda"), whose remnants are sometimes brought out of the coffin to be kissed by worshipers during some holidays.[2]

## _[Wiedźmin: Gra Wyobraźni](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wied%C5%BAmin:_Gra_Wyobra%C5%BAni "Wiedźmin: Gra Wyobraźni")_

Built of marble, the **Great Temple of the Eternal Fire** , commonly nicknamed as **Grand Picket** , lies in the center of the city. Before it had been the elven mansion and was extended by human settlers. 

According to a legend, when the first colonists were visiting the abandoned town, they saw a strange glow emanating from one of palaces. Inside they met an individual sitting near the great bowl of fire. Upon being asked who he was, he answered: _I guard the Eternal Fire; as long as it will flame in this place, so long this city and your kin will endure_ \- and then he disappeared. 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

The **Temple** is located on the [Temple Isle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Isle "Temple Isle") near [Electors Square](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Electors%27_Square "Electors' Square"). Though it dominates the view of Novigrad, the interiors are inaccessible.

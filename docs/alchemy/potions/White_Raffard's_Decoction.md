_"[White Raffard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Raffard_the_White "Raffard the White"), a famous [mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") of times long past, developed a healing potion for the human warriors who conquered the new world for their race ages ago. [Witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") have found new, more efficient ways to produce White Raffard's Decoction, using ingredients obtained from monsters. Classic witcher training suggests that the [Swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swallow "Swallow") potion be consumed simultaneously, for White Raffard's Decoction is highly toxic."_

**White Raffard's Decoction** immediately restores a majority of [Vitality](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_character_statistics "The Witcher character statistics") lost through combat. 

### Source

  * [Formula for White Raffard's Decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Formula_for_White_Raffard%27s_Decoction "Formula for White Raffard's Decoction")
  * There is one flask of this potion in a trunk in the [Cemetery crypt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cemetery_crypt "Cemetery crypt"), first accessible in Chapter II.
  * Receiving this potion from the druids in Chapter V, during "[The Green March](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Green_March "The Green March")" will unlock the formula if you did not have it already.

### Notes

  * The potion randomly restores between 50-60% of Geralt's current health. Meaning if Geralt's maximum HP is 100 it restores between 50-60 HP, while if he has 500 HP then between 250-300 HP.
  * This is one of the few potions that some [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_merchants "The Witcher merchants") will buy (and rarer still, sell):
  * [Vesemir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vesemir "Vesemir") looks in vain for White Raffard's Decoction to help [Leo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leo "Leo") in the Prologue.
  * Geralt does find some of these in his travels, before he knows what it is. Drinking it identifies it of course, but it is best to do so at the appropriate moment, or risk wasting a decent, if toxic, emergency potion.
  * You can create White Raffard's Decoction using only ingredients with [Albedo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Albedo "Albedo") and then sell these potions to the [Murky Waters blacksmith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Murky_Waters_blacksmith "Murky Waters blacksmith") for 60 (+20 more) orens.
  * You can create White Raffard's Decoction using only ingredients with [Nigredo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nigredo "Nigredo") and then sell these potions to [Kalkstein](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kalkstein "Kalkstein") or the [Murky Waters blacksmith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Murky_Waters_blacksmith "Murky Waters blacksmith") for 60 (+20 more) orens. Even if you buy all the ingredients (exchanging cheap food for [Mistletoe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mistletoe "Mistletoe") for instance) it can prove to be quite profitable (even moreso if you opt to only use ingredients obtained with effort and not gold).
  * You can create White Raffard's Decoction using only ingredients with [Rubedo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rubedo "Rubedo") and then sell these potions to the [Order Armorer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_Armorer "Order Armorer"), the waitresses at The Hairy Bear, The New Narakort, Country Inn, the [Hermit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hermit "Hermit") in the [Fields](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fields "Fields"), the [Murky Waters blacksmith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Murky_Waters_blacksmith "Murky Waters blacksmith") for 60 (+20 more) orens.

### Journal entry

    _Invented by a[famous mage](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Raffard_the_White "Raffard the White"), this potion helps the witcher survive wounds that would normally be lethal to him. The buzz from this powerful brew, however, adversely affects coordination. After consuming it, although he is fortified, the witcher deals reduced damage for a short time. This side effect can be alleviated by drinking the [Rook](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rook "Rook") potion._
    _This potion very significantly increases Toxicity when imbibed._
    _It should be consumed before fights that could prove particularly difficult._

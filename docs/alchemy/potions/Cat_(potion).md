_"Cat is said to have been the first potion created specifically for[witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"). The concoction allows witchers to pursue monsters into their lairs, including dark caves, ruins and crypts, because it augments vision to pick up additional wavelengths of light. To prevent blinding, the imbiber's pupils automatically narrow when in bright light. This is possibly the potion most commonly used by witchers."_

**Cat** potion grants vision in total [darkness](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dark_areas "Dark areas"), a very handy ability in crypts and caves, or just badly lit interiors. 

### Source

  * [Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss "Triss") gives this formula at the end of the Prologue when Geralt asks her about the sorcerer who attacked the lab.
  * [The Book of the Swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Book_of_the_Swallow "The Book of the Swallow")
  * Receiving this potion from the druids in Chapter V, during [The Green March](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Green_March "The Green March") will unlock the formula if you did not have it already.

### Notes

  * Cat is quite possibly the potion referred to in the [Alchemy section](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witchman "Witchman") of the in-game Journal entry for [Witchman](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witchman "Witchman").
  * Since the potion already has low toxicity [albedo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Albedo "Albedo")-dominant Cat becomes non-toxic.

Geralt begins the game with two Cat potions as well as the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Formula:_Cat "Formula: Cat"). 

### Journal entry

    _Cat is one of the potions witchers use most often. After imbibing it, the witcher is able to see in total darkness and will avoid being surprised by any nocturnal creatures. The potion allows its user to see both living creatures and those made of inanimate matter, even through walls._
    _Cat slightly disrupts perception, however, as a result of which its user deals less damage to monsters. The Rook potion can be used to alleviate this side effect._
    _Potion causes minor intoxication when imbibed._
    _The Cat potion is recommended before entering a cave or a crypt, and before a midnight stroll through dangerous terrain._

## Item ID

**Cat** returns as a [potion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions") in the third game. Three of these potions can be crafted at any one time and placed in the consumables slot. In addition to granting vision in the dark, allows you to see invisible monsters like [Ekimmara](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ekimma "Ekimma") or [foglets](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foglet "Foglet"). 

[Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") has the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Manuscript_page:_Cat "Manuscript page: Cat") to brew this potion from the moment he arrives in [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard").

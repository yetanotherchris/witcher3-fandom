# The Witcher 3 potions

This page is a list of all **potions and decoctions** which are found in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. 

Potions in _The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt_ are similar to the [potions](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2_potions "The Witcher 2 potions") in _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ , a major difference being that most potions can be upgraded to Enhanced and, in turn, Superior versions which increases some of the stats, whether it be duration, lower toxicity, or more restoration. [Potion recipes](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_alchemy_formulae "Category:The Witcher 3 alchemy formulae") can be [looted](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_diagram_locations "The Witcher 3 diagram locations") from chests, or bought from [herbalists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Herbalist "Herbalist") and alchemists in the world. 

Furthermore, potions may be consumed at any time either by selecting and activating them in the [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory"), or by assigning certain potions to hotkeys. [Meditation](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meditation "Meditation") is no longer needed to consume potions. However, meditation is still needed to replenish potions which uses up alcohol ([alcohest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alcohest "Alcohest") by default). Thus, herb ingredients or monster parts are used only in the initial creation of a potion, or when a potion is being upgraded to a better variant. 

_The Witcher 3_ also introduces decoctions which are longer-lasting than regular potions (30 minutes real time) and confer more powerful effects. However, decoctions also have high toxicity and may only be used once before they have to be replenished via meditation. 

The default maximum toxicity of Geralt is 100, with toxicity damage occurring above the default overdose threshold of 50%. This damage is 0.5% maximum health per second, and lasts until toxicity naturally drops below the overdose threshold or is reduced manually, for example by drinking [White Honey](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Honey "White Honey") or meditating. Note that toxicity from decoctions does not decay while the decoction is in effect, and is treated differently from potion toxicity for the purpose of some abilities ([Delayed Recovery](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Delayed_Recovery "Delayed Recovery")). 

_Note:_ the values listed in this table for duration, doses and toxicity are default values not affected by any [abilities](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_abilities "The Witcher 3 abilities"). Also note some stats change depending on if it's a brand new playthrough or a [New Game +](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/New_Game_%2B "New Game +"). 

_Bug:_ note that some potions, like the [ancient leshen decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ancient_leshen_decoction "Ancient leshen decoction"), are bugged.

# White Seagull

**White Gull** or **White Seagull** is an alcoholic beverage commonly used by [witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"). However, it is far gentler than its counterpart, [Black Seagull](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Seagull "Black Seagull"), and is often used as a mild remedy for pleasant dreams. Generally it is diluted with [water](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water "Water") as to not be too strong.[1]

**White Gull** is a potion that can be consumed as an alcoholic beverage, or used as a potion base. 

  * The **duration** of the effect is only long lasting if the potion is _consumed_ as a hallucinogenic beverage. This has no effect on any potion brewed using it as a base.
  * The **toxicity** of White Gull is medium but only when consumed as a hallucinogenic beverage. When used as a base, the toxicity is irrelevant, only the resulting toxicity of the brewed potion counts.

### Notes

  * Even though you can mix White Gull with identical secondary substances (like [Rubedo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rubedo "Rubedo")), it will not carry the effect over to the potion you have mixed. So you best mix it with those ingredients that lack any secondary substance, unless you plan to simply drink it, then you can benefit from a dominant secondary substance. But considering its medium toxicity you may want to use a low toxicity potion like [cat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cat_\(potion\) "Cat \(potion\)") for this purpose.
  * Try to have a small stack of White Gull in your inventory while you play. Sometimes you will find yourself in need of a potion that needs high or top quality alcohol. Especially in the later parts of the game, a small stack can be both a time and life saver since you may not always find the needed ingredients and alcohol.
  * A stack of White Gull tends to take up less inventory space than the often three or four different alcohols you might otherwise be carrying. It may be useful to convert any available potion bases to white gull immediately to save space, even those that would normally serve as top quality bases themselves.
  * There are two potions of White Gull in the armory on the second floor of [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") in the Prologue.
  * White Gull is sometimes used by locals as a palliative potion, to ease the suffering of the sick and dying. An old woman beggar in the Outskirts makes such a request of Geralt. If he obliges, she rewards him with the [Eternal Fire signet ring](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire_signet_ring "Eternal Fire signet ring").
  * Be careful when using alchemy as the game may sometimes use White Gull to brew more White Gull, potentially wasting perfectly usable inventory.

### Tips

  * It takes 43 White Gulls to brew all "Superior" recipes, so it's best to stock on the ingredients as early as possible. [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard") is in fact an excellent place to acquire all ingredients during the prologue, as [arenaria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arenaria "Arenaria") is abundant there, and [Elsa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elsa "Elsa") sells all three required alcohols. After the prologue she will no longer be available, so it's best to deal with her while you still have the chance. The [innkeep](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Innkeep_\(Seven_Cats_Inn\) "Innkeep \(Seven Cats Inn\)") at the [Seven Cats Inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Seven_Cats_Inn "Seven Cats Inn") sells all three as well. 
    * [Redanian herbal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redanian_herbal "Redanian herbal") can be found as random loot so if you can't afford to buy all three, you can skip it.
    * [Mandrake cordial](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mandrake_cordial "Mandrake cordial") and [cherry cordial](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cherry_cordial "Cherry cordial") cannot be found as random loot anywhere in the base game. They can, however, be found sometimes in [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint") with the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion. So unless doing a standalone playthrough, purchasing them is the only real option. [Innkeepers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Innkeeper "Innkeeper") in [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") sell them at the cheapest price, and there are two who sell both - [Jonas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jonas_the_Innkeep "Jonas the Innkeep") in [Kaer Trolde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde "Kaer Trolde") and [the one](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Innkeep_\(Harviken\) "Innkeep \(Harviken\)") in [Harviken](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harviken "Harviken").
    * Shop inventories can be refreshed by meditating if you need to buy more.

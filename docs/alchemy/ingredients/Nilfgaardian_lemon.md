## Item ID

Nilfgaardian Lemon

**Nilfgaardian lemon** is considered an [alchemy ingredient](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and thus can't be consumed like regular [drinks](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_food_and_drink "The Witcher 3 food and drink"). It's needed to craft [alcohest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alcohest "Alcohest"). 

One bottle is received during the main quest [Lilac and Gooseberries](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lilac_and_Gooseberries "Lilac and Gooseberries"), when [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") talks with [Gaunter O'Dimm](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gaunter_O%27Dimm "Gaunter O'Dimm") at the [White Orchard inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard_inn "White Orchard inn"). It can also be found as random loot, guaranteed in many places in [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), or purchased from the following [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"):

## Notes

  * Innkeeps in [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") sell alcohol at the cheapest prices, but there's one exception in the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion: Hugo Monnart who buys and sells everything at base item values. Prices listed in the infobox don't include him.
  * Similarly to the [roasted chicken leg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roasted_chicken_leg "Roasted chicken leg"), there is an unsellable version of this item (ID: Free nilfgaardian lemon), originally intended to be received from Gaunter O'Dimm. This version also shows as having 10s potion duration, indicating that Nilfgaardian lemon was also initially intended as a regular alcoholic drink. However, it only exists in the game files, likely for practical reasons as nothing can be done with it.

Vespula, a human laundress, was briefly romantically involved with Dandelion during his time in Novigrad. She was one among the numerous young women Dandelion had a fleeting romantic interest in.

**How did Vespula react when she discovered Dandelion's infidelity?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Vespula reacted to Dandelion's infidelity with shock and aggression. She encountered Dandelion at a market and struck him on the head with a frying pan, causing him to lose consciousness. Unbeknownst to her, she had actually hit Dudu, a doppler impersonating Dandelion. Witnessing Dudu revert to his original form, Vespula was further shocked and fled the scene.

Vespula plays a disruptive role in the pursuit of Dudu. She emerges in the market where the search party, consisting of Geralt, Dandelion, and Dainty, is looking for the doppler. Vespula attempts to assault Dandelion with a frying pan, causing the group to disperse. She later catches Dandelion off guard in the same market.

## Biography

Sometime in the [1260s](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1260 "Timeline"), she and Dandelion had been in a relationship, but as was typical of the [bard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bard "Bard"), he was caught running after other women. Angry at this behavior, Vespula started yelling at the bard while throwing everything out her window at him, including his lute, but Dandelion was able to catch it. 

Later on, while [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), Dandelion, and [Dainty](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dainty_Biberveldt "Dainty Biberveldt") were trying to catch [Dudu](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dudu "Dudu"), a [doppler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Doppler "Doppler") that'd been impersonating the latter, she appeared in the same market the trio were searching. Dandelion, who felt he wouldn't do much good for the group, began to give a small show at one of the stalls and Vespula, armed with a frying pan, tried to make her way over to him to hit him upside the head, but he and the others scattered. 

A short time later, she spotted Dandelion once more in the same market and, catching him unawares, hit him on the head with the frying pan, knocking him out. However, she'd actually hit Dudu, who'd taken on Dandelion's image, and the doppler began to change to his natural form. Shocked at what she was seeing, Vespula screamed and ran off. 

### Later years

* * *

The following is considered game canon only and may contradict [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")'s works.

* * *

In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games") she worked in a [laundry](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vespula%27s_laundry "Vespula's laundry") in the [Farcorners](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Farcorners "Farcorners") district of Novigrad and Dandelion convinced her to take him back. However, after spending a week asking her questions about her job at [Sigismund's Bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse") and about the other washer women, she saw him at the [harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harborside "Harborside") with a [blonde woman](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priscilla "Priscilla") and promptly threw the bard out once more. 

Dandelion described her in his [planner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion%27s_Planner "Dandelion's Planner"): 

    _Though timid in looks, no adventure did skip her.  
My heart melts when she asks, "Ever had a Big Dipper?"_

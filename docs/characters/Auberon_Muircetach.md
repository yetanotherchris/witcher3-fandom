**Auberon Muircetach, King of the Alders** , was the [king](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") of the [Aen Elle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Elle "Aen Elle") [elves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elf "Elf"), an [Aen Saevherne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aen_Saevherne "Aen Saevherne"), and ruler of [Tir ná Lia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tir_n%C3%A1_Lia "Tir ná Lia"). He was determined to "take back" what had been stolen from the Aen Elle by [Cregennan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cregennan_of_Lod "Cregennan of Lod") when he took [Lara Dorren](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lara_Dorren "Lara Dorren"), Auberon's daughter. To do this, he had [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), a descendant of the two lovers, brought to him and groomed to produce an heir. He was notably patient and never brutal, but nor would he be dissuaded from his goal. 

He died before he was able to impregnate Ciri; killed, possibly unwittingly, possibly not, from an overdose of an aphrodisiac or performance enhancer at the age of over 650 years. The drug was supplied by [Eredin Bréacc Glas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eredin_Br%C3%A9acc_Glas "Eredin Bréacc Glas"), who viewed himself as a more suitable ruler. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What was Auberon Muircetach's relationship with Ciri in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Auberon Muircetach, the Alder King, had a strained relationship with Ciri in the Witcher series. They were part of a deal orchestrated by Avallac'h, where Auberon was to father a child with Ciri. Despite his attempts, Auberon's age and contempt for humans hindered the process. Eredin's intervention with an aphrodisiac led to Auberon's untimely demise. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why was Auberon Muircetach determined to 'take back' what had been stolen from the Aen Elle?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Auberon Muircetach, the former king of the Aen Elle world, was motivated by loyalty and his perception of justice. In particular, he wished to reclaim what he believed was stolen from his people, the Elder Blood gene, owing to Lara Dorren's "betrayal" of falling in love with a human. 

Provided by: Community 

**How did Auberon Muircetach, the Alder King, die?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Auberon Muircetach, the Alder King, met his end through poisoning by Eredin Bréacc Glas, who subsequently assumed the throne. His demise was potentially due to an overdose of a performance enhancer provided by Eredin. At the time of his death, Auberon was weary and bitter from over six centuries of existence. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Who was Auberon Muircetach's daughter in the Witcher universe?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher universe, Auberon Muircetach, also referred to as the Alder King, fathered Lara Dorren. Lara's lineage extends to significant characters like Riannon and Ciri. Her mother was Shiadhal, and she had four siblings, one of whom was Morenn. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What was Auberon Muircetach's role as the king of the Aen Elle elves?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Auberon Muircetach, recognized as the Alder King, was the sovereign of the Aen Elle elves, ruling the distant realm of Tir ná Lia. His reign spanned over 650 years, and he was instrumental in the formation of the Red Riders, designating Eredin as their commander. His rule ended when he was treacherously poisoned by Eredin, who subsequently assumed the throne. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

Auberon Muircetach appeared only in a dream summoned by [Corinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Corinne_Tilly "Corinne Tilly") showing the moment shortly before his death. This vision was used to convince [Ge'els](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ge%27els "Ge'els") to betray Eredin. 

### Journal entry

    _Auberon Muircetach, also known as King of the Alders, was ruler of the far-off land of Tir ná Lia - the world which is home to Avallac'h and the Wild Hunt. He died after being poisoned by Eredin Bréacc Glas, who then declared himself king. At the time of his death Auberon had been exhausted and embittered by over six hundred and fifty years of living._

## Trivia

  * Auberon used the drug [fisstech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fisstech "Fisstech").
  * In medieval and renaissance literature, [Oberon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oberon "wikipedia:Oberon"), or Auberon, is the King of [Fairies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/fairies "wikipedia:fairies"). He is a prominent character in _[A Midsummer Night's Dream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Midsummer_Night%27s_Dream "wikipedia:A Midsummer Night's Dream")_ by [William Shakespeare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare "wikipedia:William Shakespeare").
  * Another reference to classical literature may be Auberon's sobriquet "King of the Alders" which is a literal translation of _[Erlkönig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erlking "wikipedia:Erlking")_ \- a mythical king of the fairies from the German and Scandinavian folklore. He is the antagonist of [Johann Wolfgang von Goethe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Wolfgang_von_Goethe "wikipedia:Johann Wolfgang von Goethe")'s 1782 [poem of the same name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Der_Erlk%C3%B6nig "wikipedia:Der Erlkönig").
  * Auberon Muircetach's name was possibly inspired by the medieval Irish name Muirchertach, shared by multiple famous historical kings of Ireland.

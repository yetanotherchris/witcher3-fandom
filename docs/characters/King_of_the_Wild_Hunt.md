# King of the Wild Hunt

The **King of the Wild Hunt** leads a horde of specters - omens of misfortune and death - across the skies of the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"). 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What powers does the King of the Wild Hunt possess?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The King of the Wild Hunt, a mysterious figure, is connected to destiny's forces. He carries a scythe, a death symbol, and wears parts of an old suit of armor. He commands a horde of specters, representing misfortune and death, across the Continent's skies. His presence usually foretells upcoming calamity. He pursues specific individuals' souls, even fighting for them. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Who is the main adversary of the King of the Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The protagonist, Geralt, is the main adversary of the King of the Wild Hunt. The King persistently oppresses Geralt, reminding him of past sorrows and instigating challenging dialogues. His presence is tied to Destiny, lingering on the fringes of the main narrative. The King of the Wild Hunt consistently taunts Geralt, distorting the witcher's actions to induce self-doubt and question his motives. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does the King of the Wild Hunt determine the outcome of a clash?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The King of the Wild Hunt, a pivotal and enigmatic figure, influences clash outcomes by exploiting vulnerabilities. He is tied to destiny's forces, using his might to oppress and cause distress. His weapon of choice is a scythe, symbolizing death, and his presence typically signifies impending catastrophe. He often delegates his wraiths to battle, taking advantage of his adversaries' weaknesses, such as when he used Leo's specter against Geralt. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of the specters led by the King of the Wild Hunt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The King of the Wild Hunt leads the Wild Hunt, a group of ghostly riders seen as omens of death and misfortune. His appearances, often to Geralt, are marked by taunts and twisted memories. The King uses his specters to exploit opponents' weaknesses and his presence usually signals impending disaster, as evidenced in the village of Murky Waters near Vizima. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What role does the King of the Wild Hunt play in Geralt's journey?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Eredin Bréacc Glas, the King of the Wild Hunt, is a pivotal figure in Geralt's journey. He serves as an oppressor, linked to destiny and often reminding Geralt of painful events. He taunts Geralt, causing him to question his actions and motives. The King is seen pursuing Geralt in several locations, including the forests near Kaer Morhen and the Outskirts.

## Appearance(s)

### Voice actor

[William Roberts](http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1005254/)

No enemy is more terrifying than the **King of the Wild Hunt** , who leads a [horde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") of ghastly riders across the sky. Doom incarnate, he fears no opponent, for he can always decide that his adversary is doomed, which usually determines the outcome of any clash. Over the course of the game, [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") has several opportunities to meet the King, as his every step is dogged by the specter. 

Over the course of the game, the [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") taunts Geralt each time they meet by twisting everything the witcher has done in an attempt to have our hero doubt himself and his own motives. 

It is heavily implied that the Wild Hunt is pursuing Geralt as he runs through the forests near [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") in the Prologue. In Chapter I he appears in the [Outskirts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Outskirts "Outskirts") along with the ghost of [Leo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leo "Leo"). In Chapter IV, Geralt must be careful _not_ to summon him while completing a quest for the [Hermit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hermit "Hermit"). These encounters culminate at the end of the game and true to form, the witcher is faced with another dilemma, as the King has come to claim the soul of [Jacques de Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jacques_de_Aldersberg "Jacques de Aldersberg").

During his final conversation with [Letho](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letho "Letho"), Geralt recalls his forgotten memories, remembering that Eredin found him and [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") at the Isle of Avalon after Ciri rescued the two from the riot that took place in Rivia. The Hunt attacked Geralt and Yennefer soon after, taking the sorceress captive after laying waste to the island. 

Geralt had began searching for Yennefer, along the way he gathered the witchers of the [Viper School](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Viper_School "Viper School") and engaged the Wild Hunt in an attempt to rescue her but the Hunt far outnumbered them. So Geralt offered his soul in exchange for Yennefer's, to which Eredin agreed without hesitation.

_Developer[CD PROJEKT RED](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/CD_PROJEKT_RED "CD PROJEKT RED")'s characterization of the King of the Wild Hunt taken from the monsterbook, which was enclosed with the Collectors Edition of the computer game _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)") _for Poland, Hungary and the Czech Republic:_

"This powerful being leads a retinue of wraith raiders in their mad rush across the sky. The Wild Hunt, like a comet, is considered an ill [omen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Omen "Omen"), a harbinger of disaster and war. The King of the Wild Hunt is an unusual opponent, if only because he is from another world — a fact well known to Sapkowski's fans. 

"When we decided that the King of the Wild Hunt would appear in the game, we knew he had a serious part to play. From beginning to end, this nightmarish ruler appears on Geralt's path as an oppressor and portent of misery. The King of the Wild Hunt is linked to the forces of Destiny: he hovers just outside the main narrative, only appearing to remind the witcher of painful events or to provoke difficult discussions. 

"Our protagonist's most powerful opponent is thus one of the game's most important and mysterious characters. The King of the Wild Hunt wields a scythe — an attribute of death. Beneath his ragged robes, he wears elements of an ancient suit of armor. The ornaments on his clothing and crown evoke associations with flesh—eating maggots."

Beneath their raised visors glowed the specters' bluish eyes. Their tattered banners rustled. ## Behind the scenes

Legends and myth about the Wild Hunt have origins in ancient Northern and Central Europe folklore, in our real world. According to myth, the Wild Hunt is a hunt or chase of spirits and other supernatural beings, all completed with hounds, horses and huntsmen. 

It represents an omen of danger, misfortune or catastrophe, or simply the personification of human fear. It's also cited in cinema, music and literature.

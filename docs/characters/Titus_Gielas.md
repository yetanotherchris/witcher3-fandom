## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Titus Gielas** was a [fisstech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fisstech "Fisstech") dealer who in [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), was nearly killed during a deal gone wrong with a [priest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priest "Priest") of the [Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire"). 

## Biography

The priest of the Eternal Fire had been a customer of Titus', buying fisstech off him three times without a hassle. That changed one day though, where the priest decided to silence Titus completely and had his thugs attack him, then left him for dead, hoping the [necrophages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Necrophage "Necrophage") in the area would kill him. 

_If Geralt saves Titus then questions him:_ The priest hired [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") to burn the bodies in the area, claiming that it'd prevent the spread of disease and necrophages. However, Geralt found Titus fighting a pack of [ghouls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ghoul "Ghoul") and jumped in to help him out. Afterwards, Titus revealed what had transpired beforehand before deciding he had enough of the area and was going to leave. 

_If Geralt saves him but doesn't question him:_ Having enough with the area and nearly being killed for it, Titus proclaimed he was leaving, even if it meant traveling all the way to [Poviss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poviss "Poviss"). 

_If Geralt doesn't save him:_ Unfortunately for Titus, he was killed by the ghouls in the area and thus all evidence of the priest's crimes disappeared.

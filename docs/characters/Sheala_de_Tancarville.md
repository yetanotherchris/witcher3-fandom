**Sheala de Tancarville** or **Síle de Tansarville** ,[1] also known as the **Koviri Loner** , was a [sorceress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") from [Creyden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Creyden "Creyden") and one of the founding members of the [Lodge of Sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses"). She was among the most powerful and respected mages in the [North](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"), even inspiring fear in some of the other members of the Lodge.[2]

Unlike her colleagues, Sheala was not known to interfere in politics and instead dedicated her time to research, even resigning her seat with the [Council of Wizards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Council_of_Wizards "Council of Wizards") prior to its fall because it interfered with her work.[2]

## Biography

### Formation of the Lodge

On August 5th [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), Sheala was summoned via a [projection](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Telecommunicator "Telecommunicator") spell to begin the first true meeting of the Lodge of Sorceresses, mainly around recruitment and their purpose, along with [Philippa Eilhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Philippa_Eilhart "Philippa Eilhart"), [Triss Merigold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold"), [Margarita Laux-Antille](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margarita_Laux-Antille "Margarita Laux-Antille"), [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz"), [Assire var Anahid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Assire_var_Anahid "Assire var Anahid"), [Sabrina Glevissig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sabrina_Glevissig "Sabrina Glevissig"), and [Francesca Findabair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Francesca_Findabair "Francesca Findabair"). Despite Sheala's reluctance due to her scholarly work, and the initial disagreements over the events of Thanedd, they agreed to work together to protect the safe practice of magic. Backed by Margarita's evidence of women performing better with magic (based off comparing pupils from [Aretuza](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aretuza "Aretuza") and [Ban Ard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ban_Ard "Ban Ard")), they agreed women would only be allowed to join, Sheala also being the one to suggest the name "the Lodge".[2]

### Meeting Ciri

Later that year, the [king](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") of [Kovir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kovir "Kovir"), [Esterad Thyssen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Esterad_Thyssen "Esterad Thyssen"), experienced a bad dream, he began seeking the aid of some kind of [astronomer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Astromancy "Astromancy") or fortune teller, when his wife, [Zuleyka](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zuleyka "Zuleyka"), mentioned that Sheala happened to be in [Lan Exeter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lan_Exeter "Lan Exeter"), though the king refused to seek her help.[3]

In fact, Queen Zuleyka was Sheala's informant and it was the sorceress who had influenced the king's dreams. The two women had made a deal in which the queen was to provide personal information regarding the king's thoughts on the secret loan that [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") obtained from Kovir, in exchange for Sheala's magical aid in helping [Prince](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") [Tankred](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tankred_Thyssen "Tankred Thyssen") to distance himself from the company he had been keeping, as well as to stop "chasing skirts" until he was introduced to [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") of [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra").[3]

Sheala and Philippa planned to introduce Ciri to Prince Tankred, so that she could become his lover and give him a child. The child was to ensure the future and status of the Lodge, and, after the birth, Ciri was to become the Lodge's new member. They needed to invent a new surname for Ciri, so that their plan could work. Sheala offered Ciri to take hers, in that case she would be known as "Cirila de Tancarville", but she refused, preferring to be called "Cirilla of Vengerberg." Then the Lodge decided whether or not to allow Ciri meet [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") one last time, Sheala voting against it to teach Ciri discipline.[4]

## Appearance and personality

For the formation of the Lodge, Sheala dressed in a strict, black velvet gown trimmed with pearls, her neck draped with silver fox furs.[2]

## _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_

[![Sile de Tansarville - 01](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/35/Sile_de_Tansarville_-_01.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20110522082009)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/35/Sile_de_Tansarville_-_01.png/revision/latest?cb=20110522082009) [![Sile de Tansarville - 01](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/35/Sile_de_Tansarville_-_01.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20110522082009)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/35/Sile_de_Tansarville_-_01.png/revision/latest?cb=20110522082009) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Sile_de_Tansarville_-_01.png)

Síle appears in _The Witcher 2_. In the English version of the game, her name is spelled "**Síle de Tansarville** "; Síle is the traditional Irish spelling of the name. Geralt meets up with her in the town of [Flotsam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flotsam "Flotsam") where he learns that she has been hired to rid the port of the dreaded [Kayran](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kayran "Kayran"). 

[Triss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold") and Geralt meet her after she successfully repelled the Kayran from the Flotsam harbour. The witcher discovers Síle knows him and notices the tension between the two sorceresses. They agree on hunting the beast together. In the meantime, the sorceress hires [Zoltan Chivay](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zoltan_Chivay "Zoltan Chivay") to polish some diamonds she could use to build herself a megascope, an apparatus performing many complicated magical operations and enabling her to communicate with other sorceresses. 

When it comes to fighting the beast, Síle lures the kayran to the shore and helps Geralt slaying it. She then harvests some rare ingredients from the corpse. As Philippa later claims, they were needed to cure [Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt")'s impotence and thus make the king trust a sorceress and allowing Síle to his court. 

It is no wonder then that after leaving Flotsam she is found by the king's side and attends the negotiations between prince [Stennis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stennis "Stennis") and the [Dragonslayer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Saskia "Saskia"). When the mist of [Sabrina's curse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Blood_Curse "The Blood Curse") descends, de Tansarville defends the Kaedweni king and manages to escape with him and [Dethmold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dethmold "Dethmold") back into their [camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaedweni_camp "Kaedweni camp"). 

During the quest "[Enter the Dragon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Enter_the_Dragon "Enter the Dragon")", she attempts to teleport away from [Loc Muinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loc_Muinne "Loc Muinne"), unaware that her megascope has been sabotaged by [Letho](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letho "Letho"). Geralt can choose to remove the flawed diamond to save her life, or leave it alone causing her to be torn to pieces.

#### Journal entry

    _Geralt and I both knew Síle de Tansarville, the Recluse of Kovir, the once proud member of the Lodge of Sorceresses who in her day plotted, persuaded and spellcast the world into a shape of her liking. Síle always claimed she acted only out of concern for the good of magic and mankind, yet the blood of a great many people stained her hands, including that of kings who stood in the way of her aims. She took part in countless conspiracies and herself devised many intrigues in which the witcher often figured as an unwitting accomplice, obstacle or incidental victim._
    _Thus Geralt's prior meetings with the sorceress were stormy at best. On at least one occasion it could be said that Geralt saved Síle's life, but this did not mean he felt very warmly about her._
    _Be that as it may, the sight of the tortured and maimed sorceress in King Radovid's dungeon made the witcher's cold heart swell with sorrow and sympathy. Perhaps it was these feeling, or perhaps it was simply respect for a defeated former enemy now suffering at the hands of cruel fanatics, that made Geralt take pity on her. Aware of what fate awaited her if she lived, Síle de Tansarville was grateful to be given a dignified death, one free from torment and humiliation._

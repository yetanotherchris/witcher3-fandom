**Sigismund Dijkstra** (b. [1219](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1219 "Timeline")) was the head of [Redanian Intelligence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redanian_Secret_Service "Redanian Secret Service"). He was a physically imposing man; nearly seven feet tall and bald, with a rather noticeable stomach that was said to give the impression of a scrubbed pig.[4]

Preferring flamboyant clothes with bright colors, he didn't dress the least bit like a [spy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spy "Spy"). However, he was an exceptionally clever man, having studied at [Oxenfurt Academy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt_Academy "Oxenfurt Academy"),[5] and was said to be very influential in [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania").[4] While he was referred to as a [count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") in public, this was merely a cover that [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Vizimir II](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II") ordered him to use so nobody would know that Dijkstra was a mere commoner.[6]

## Biography

### Letter to Vizimir

As a minor spy in the Secret Service's [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") cell, Sigismund somehow managed to obtain documents proving [Michael de Noailles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Michael_de_Noailles "Michael de Noailles")' incompetence, pertaining to his ignorance of the state being infiltrated by foreign intelligence. In [1248](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1248 "Timeline/Games"), he wrote a letter to King Vizimir II, addressed _For Your Royal Eyes Only_. The king, enraged, ordered to imprison [Count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") de Noailles and elevated Dijkstra to the position of the Royal Security Assignee. In a few years, Dijkstra managed to overhaul the service's structures, replacing moles with faithful men and expanding the area of operations from [Kovir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kovir "Kovir") to the [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire").[7] He then moved his quarters to [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"), to the rented academy's rooms he called the "Faculty of Most Contemporary History".[4] Around this period, he romanced [Philippa Eilhart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Philippa_Eilhart "Philippa Eilhart") for three years, who frequently cooperated with the spymaster from then on.[5]

### Northern War I

The biggest challenge Dijkstra faced was the [Nilfgaardian Military Intelligence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Military_Intelligence "Nilfgaardian Military Intelligence"). The web collaborated among state officials and aristocracy, disposing of enormous, hard-to-infiltrate sums of money, barely touched by his agents prior to the [First Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_I "Northern War I") in [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1263 "Timeline"). Then, just after reports from [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") ceased to reach the Oxenfurt quarters, Nilfgaardian agents began unleashing hell throughout Redania. Military warehouses were burned, cavalry [horses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse") were poisoned, and many commanders were found dead with knives in their throats. Sigismund Dijkstra, the spymaster, had learnt a hard lesson in humility from his opponent, [Vattier de Rideaux](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vattier_de_Rideaux "Vattier de Rideaux"). 

This startle did not last long as shortly after the initial shock, the service's agents throughout the kingdoms started to take out persons long suspected of collaboration with the Empire. The traitors' blood flowed like rivers, though mistakes were made and certain innocents, especially [nonhumans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nonhuman "Nonhuman"), were killed as well. The few Nilfgaardian informants who managed to avoid [Drakenborg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drakenborg "Drakenborg") fled to Kovir and [Hengfors](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hengfors_League "Hengfors League"), but even there the knife and the garrote awaited them. 

Before Dijkstra could trace the traitors among nobles, however, the war ended, and terms of the truce were required to end the bloodshed. Unable to eliminate the core of the imperial spy web in Redania, he was back near his starting point.[7]

### Interwar period

In [1266](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1266 "Timeline"), he recruited [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion") as his informant and asked [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") to protect the bard when he was captured by [Rience](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rience "Rience") in a town near [Bleobheris](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bleobheris "Bleobheris"). The sorceress saved Dandelion and ordered him to hide under Dijkstra's wing. Later, Sigismund managed to calm the tension when [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and [Olsen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Olsen "Olsen") killed Temerian agents who, while they were legit agents, had at the time been acting on their own in pursuit of Geralt and [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri") to try and claim Rience's reward for the pair. Together with Philippa, the spymaster asked Dandelion about Geralt's current whereabouts; Dijkstra was surprised when Philippa mentioned Ciri as well, perceiving it as a hasty move.[4]

In late June [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), he was informed that [Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") had learnt of the planned [Aedirnian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn") and [Lyrian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lyria "Lyria") staged attack on [Glevitzingen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Glevitzingen "Glevitzingen"), and the [Imperial Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army") now had moved into [Dol Angra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dol_Angra "Dol Angra"). After receiving a message from King [Demavend III](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Demavend_III "Demavend III") about the attack, he sent word via a royal messenger, [Aplegatt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aplegatt "Aplegatt"), to inform Demavend to not go through with the staged attack as now Nilfgaard knew about their plans. However, he learned too late this message was never delivered as the messenger had been killed in an isolated incident.[6]

### Thanedd coup

On the 30th, he joined Philippa on [Thanedd Isle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island") as part of their plan to [take out the Nilfgaardian supporters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup") before they could put their own coup in action. At the banquet he talked for some time with Geralt before heading off. The next day, in the early hours just before dawn, he began to help [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz") round up the Nilfgaardian supporters with the assistance of his soldiers. As they were arresting [Artaud Terranova](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Artaud_Terranova "Artaud Terranova"), Geralt stumbled upon them and Dijkstra had the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") detained before discussing with the mages on what to do before taking Geralt to Philippa to decide. As he felt Geralt wouldn't cause them too much trouble, Dijkstra didn't bind the witcher, which he would regret later. 

On the way, Dijkstra, in his ever sarcastic demeanor, informed Geralt what was going on and asked where Yennefer was. Geralt, not truly understanding the gravity of the situation, told him last he checked she was sound asleep in bed, pissing the spymaster off as the plan had been only for Nilfgaardian supporters to go to bed while the rest made their move (not realizing Yennefer, like many others, had actually remained neutral). After talking with Philippa, she gave Dijkstra part of their deal: a list that'd been on [Vilgefortz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilgefortz "Vilgefortz") that had names of Redanians who'd sold out to Nilfgaard. Satisfied with the list, Dijkstra then had Geralt follow him through the towers, intending to head to [Loxia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loxia "Loxia") as the spymaster knew, despite rumors, Ciri, the sole heir and [princess](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Princess "Princess") of Cintra, was alive and in the school, but the rectoress would not hand Ciri over to anyone except Geralt. 

When it became clear to Geralt that the spymaster planned to use Ciri as a political tool, intending to send her to Redania's capital, [Tretogor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tretogor "Tretogor"), the witcher punched the spymaster and Dijkstra, along with the few soldiers he had with him, tried to fight the witcher but were all knocked out. Dijkstra then threatened to get his soldiers from the ship but Geralt, intent on finding Yennefer and Ciri without distractions, summarily broke one of the spymaster's ankles, causing the man to howl and faint from the pain.[6]

### First phases of Northern War II

It was later revealed that the previous night, King Vizimir had been assassinated and, with the start of the [Second Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_II "Northern War II"), the country was ruled by a [Regency Council](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Regency_Council "Regency Council") headed by Dijkstra and Philippa (even though technically [Queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Queen "Queen") [Hedwig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hedwig_of_Malleore "Hedwig of Malleore") was supposed to be the ruler).[6] Incensed by the failed coup on Thanedd and how quickly Nilfgaard was taking over other kingdoms, Dijkstra began to hang people left and right in Redania, though he wasn't able to walk for two weeks, and even then required help from Philippa to do so.[5]

A few weeks later, he was informed by [Jan Lennep](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jan_Lennep "Jan Lennep") that Geralt was recovering in [Brokilon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brokilon "Brokilon"). However, Dijkstra ordered him to leave the witcher alone, though that wasn't to say he forgave the witcher for breaking his ankle, he just didn't have the time and was professional enough to not use his own men to settle a private matter. He then sent out correspondence to Philippa, still intent on finding information that Yennefer had sided with Nilfgaard. He was later visited by the Nilfgaardian ambassador, [Shilard Fitz-Oesterlen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shilard_Fitz-Oesterlen "Shilard Fitz-Oesterlen"), and the two conversed about politics before turning to what they both really wanted: Dijkstra believed Nilfgaard was hiding Vilgefortz and Yennefer and wanted them, while Shilard believed Redania had [Cahir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cahir_Mawr_Dyffryn_aep_Ceallach "Cahir Mawr Dyffryn aep Ceallach"), a Nilfgaardian knight that was wanted for treason. However, neither actually had the people in question and Shilard left, now considered _persona non grata_ within the kingdom, his life safe only via diplomatic immunity. Dijkstra then sent another letter to Philippa, piecing together that the earlier news they heard about Nilfgaard having Ciri was false, or rather, a [fake Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/False_Ciri "False Ciri"), meaning the real one was still out there somewhere. To confirm these suspicions, he later received important information from a [prisoner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nazarian "Nazarian") who was about to be hanged, confirming that it was a fake Ciri.[5]

Still intending to find Vilgefortz, as now Dijkstra and Philippa believed it was likely Ciri was with the rogue sorcerer, the spymaster put Redania's Secret Service to use and found two of Vilgefortz's hangouts, but no sorcerer or Ciri. However, they did find evidence that Vilgefortz had been experimenting on pregnant women, killing them in the process.[5]

### Late autumn armistice

Later, in late October, he made his way to [Lan Exeter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lan_Exeter "Lan Exeter"), the winter capital of [Kovir and Poviss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kovir_and_Poviss "Kovir and Poviss"), to try and ask for a loan of a million [bizants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bizant "Bizant") to raise an army in time for spring, when Nilfgaard would start up its assault on the North once more. However, Kovir's king, [Esterad Thyssen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Esterad_Thyssen "Esterad Thyssen"), refused on the grounds of the kingdom's neutrality and an old agreement that meant he couldn't back Nilfgaard's enemies. Despite this, the king's wife, [Zuleyka](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zuleyka "Zuleyka"), gave him advice that showed him a loophole and was thus able to supply the spymaster indirectly. Dijkstra, having struck up an unlikely friendship with Esterad, ended up staying a little bit longer and the two had philosophical debates. On their last conversation before Dijkstra left, Esterad warned the spymaster to stop all the bloodshed in Redania, as it'd bring nothing but assassins after him sooner or later. Following the meeting with Esterad, Sigismund had growing suspicion towards Eilhart and [her close circle of sorceresses at Montecalvo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses").[1]

During the autumn and winter, apart from preparations to raise troops and in secret from Philippa, Dijkstra and his personal assistant [Ori Reuven](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ori_Reuven "Ori Reuven") aided [Jacques de Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jacques_de_Aldersberg "Jacques de Aldersberg") in his mission to reform [Order of the White Rose](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_of_the_White_Rose "Order of the White Rose") into [Order of the Flaming Rose](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Order_of_the_Flaming_Rose "Order of the Flaming Rose") with aim to balance the rising position of sorceresses and aid the forces fighting Nilfgaard with a strong knightly order. Dijkstra was aware of the dangers that hosting the Order in Redania might bring, thus ensuring that its headquarters would lie south to the [Pontar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_\(river\) "Pontar \(river\)"), in [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria").[8] It was also apparently during this period when he, under the alias of **Sigi Reuven** , co-founded the [Syndicate](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Syndicate "Syndicate"), an underground alliance of criminal bosses and church officials that was to end the period of instability in Novigrad streets. Taking information out of drunken [Walter Veritas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Walter_Veritas "Walter Veritas"), he was able to capture and eliminate the Nilfgaardian agent [Doadrick Leumaerts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Doadrick_Leumaerts "Doadrick Leumaerts") and later recruited Walter to the Syndicate.[9]

In January [1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline"), Dijkstra received a letter from Geralt, informing him that Vilgefortz's hideout was located in [Stygga Castle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stygga_Castle "Stygga Castle"), [Ebbing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ebbing "Ebbing").[10] Unable to operate that far south of the [Yaruga](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yaruga "Yaruga"), he traded this information to Vattier de Rideaux in exchange for details on Vizimir's assassination learned by Nilfgaardian Intelligence. 

### Peace of Cintra

As the de factor ruler of Redania, Queen Hedwig, was still indisposed, Dijkstra joined the other kingdoms' rulers in Cintra to discuss the [peace terms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_of_Cintra "Peace of Cintra") in April [1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline"). However, due to being a non-royal, many of the rulers scoffed at him being there and the [Hierarch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch "Hierarch"), [Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cyrus_Engelkind_Hemmelfart "Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart"), even noted to himself that they must do away with the spy. After peace was officially declared, a celebration was held in Novigrad and the spy informed Philippa that he'd learned the [half-elf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Half-elf "Half-elf") that assassinated Vizimir hadn't been acting alone. Sometime later, he walked through Tretogor Castle one last time, saying goodbye to his personal assistant, Ori Reuven, noting that he'd said one word too many to one person too many but, having been tipped off by Vattier de Rideaux and Emhyr var Emreis, knew assassins sent by Philippa were coming for him, and fled the country. 

He later appeared in [Elskerdeg Pass](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elskerdeg_Pass "Elskerdeg Pass") and met [Isengrim Faoiltiarna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Isengrim_Faoiltiarna "Isengrim Faoiltiarna") and [Boreas Mun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Boreas_Mun "Boreas Mun"), two fugitives on the run for their own reasons. An unlikely party, the three agreed to travel east together, to [Zerrikania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zerrikania "Zerrikania") or [Hakland](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hakland "Hakland"), with Dijkstra taking on the name Sigi Reuven once again.[10]

### Return in the North

[![Dijkstra in ](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/de/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_sigismund_dijkstra.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20190901092055)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/de/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_sigismund_dijkstra.png/revision/latest?cb=20190901092055) [![Dijkstra in ](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/de/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_sigismund_dijkstra.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/180?cb=20190901092055)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/d/de/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_sigismund_dijkstra.png/revision/latest?cb=20190901092055) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gwent_cardart_syndicate_sigismund_dijkstra.png)

Dijkstra in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad")

Sometime later Dijkstra, still using the alias Sigi Reuven, returned to the Northern Kingdoms accompanied by Isengrim and [Bart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bart "Bart"). He went to Oxenfurt in order to help his daughter Iriana. To accomplish this task he enlisted the help of Dandelion who was a professor there at the time. While Dandelion, with the help of Bart and Isengrim, helped out Iriana, Dijkstra hid in an old safehouse of his. The former home of Count van Troffke, who Dijkstra had arrested for high treason but later offered a deal: exile instead of execution. When Iriana and Dandelion came to him for help he provided them with old documents of the Redanian intelligence. Later he gave his daughter the key to her old house and to help her look for Count van Troffke as a way to make amends.[3] He then went to Novigrad where he bought a [bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse")[2] and completed the Syndicate's code of conduct.[3] In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games") he and three other most influential gang leaders were known as the [Big Four](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Big_Four "Big Four"). 

After the outbreak of the [Third Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Third_Northern_War "Third Northern War"), Dijkstra, not approving of how Radovid was "destroying" everything his father, Vizimir II, had built up, began to work with the Temerian loyalists [Vernon Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vernon_Roche "Vernon Roche"), [Ves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ves "Ves"), and [Thaler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thaler_\(spy\) "Thaler \(spy\)"), in their plan to assassinate King [Radovid V](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") and make Temeria a vassal state to Nilfgaard while allowing the rest of the North to be swallowed up by the empire.[2]

_If Geralt doesn't complete Reason of State quest (default):_ The assassination however was never carried out, thus allowing King Radovid to win the war against Nilfgaard and gather again the Northern Kingdoms under the silver eagle blazon. Dijkstra's fate remained unknown. 

_If Geralt completes Reason of State quest:_ The assassination was successful but, having despised surrendering Redania in favor of Temeria's semi-independence, the former head spy turned on the Temerians and planned to kill them, as he knew they wouldn't go along with his plans. 

    _If Geralt defends the Temerians:_ Dijkstra underestimated Geralt's resolve to remain neutral though, and in turn Dijkstra was summarily killed.
    _If Geralt doesn't defend the Temerians:_ After Roche and the others were all killed, Dijkstra took over Redania, becoming the Chancellor of the country and brought in a new era of manufacturing to the region.

## _[The Witcher Adventure Game](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_Adventure_Game "The Witcher Adventure Game")_

    _Sigismund Dijkstra, better known simply as Dijkstra, is a master spy and head of special forces for the kingdom of Redania, a wealthy country located in the North. Extremely tall, balding, and big boned, the man could be easily mistaken for one of those wealthy royal entourage types who do nothing but roam around courts with a glass of wine, oblivious to the brutal political dynamics playing out in the background. Like any spy, Sigismund won't hesitate to use any manner of dirty trick to achieve his goals or forward his agendas. Dijkstra is known to dislike Geralt of Rivia: the men crossed paths in the past and, as a result of that encounter, the former ended up temporarily crippled, which—combined with the witcher besting the spy—added injury to insult. However, Geralt and Dijkstra, cold professionals both, also harbor respect for each other's achievements in their respective fields of work._

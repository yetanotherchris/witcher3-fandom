**Philippa Eilhart** (Polish: _Filippa Eilhart_) was a [sorceress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorceress "Sorceress") and [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Vizimir II](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II")'s advisor, who remained a powerful figure at [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania")'s court even after his death, for which she was likely responsible. She was one of the few mages with the rare ability to [polymorph](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Polymorphism "Polymorphism"), and was able to turn at will into a large grey owl.[4]

She led the [Thanedd coup](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup") against the [Brotherhood of Sorcerers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers"), intending to neutralize suspected traitors allied with [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire").[5] Later, she initiated the founding of the [Lodge of Sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses") and was considered its leader. The all female organization secretly met at [Montecalvo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Montecalvo "Montecalvo"), Philippa's home base.[6]

She was one of the victims of the [Witch Hunts](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunts "Witch hunts") in the [1270s](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1270 "Timeline"), having been tortured and killed by Archpriest [Willemer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Willemer "Willemer")'s men. Many years later, she was exonerated and canonized as Martyr Saint.[1]

## Biography

### Early life

Philippa studied [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") under the auspices of [Tissaia de Vries](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tissaia_de_Vries "Tissaia de Vries"),[5] probably at [Aretuza](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aretuza "Aretuza"), the magical school for sorceresses on [Thanedd Island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island"). She would later claim the position of a royal advisor at the court of Vizimir II, King of Redania, as well as a seat on the [Council of Wizards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Council_of_Wizards "Council of Wizards"), one of the two ruling bodies of the [Brotherhood of Sorcerers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers"), which governed and guided magical use and practice in the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms").[4]

### First Northern War

In [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1263 "Timeline"), during the [First Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/First_Northern_War "First Northern War"), Philippa travelled with the Brotherhood of Sorcerers to [Lower Sodden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lower_Sodden "Lower Sodden"), where they fought alongside [northern](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms") armies in the [Battle of Sodden Hill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Sodden_Hill "Battle of Sodden Hill"), against the [Nilfgaardian Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Army "Nilfgaardian Army").[4]

### Hunt for Rience

In [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), she had a strong relationship with [Sigismund Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra"), head of the [Redanian Intelligence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redanian_Secret_Service "Redanian Secret Service"), the two having previously been lovers for three years,[6] and was present at his meeting with [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion") in [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"), discussing the location of [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"). She pushed Dandelion for information on [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), under the pretense of being worried for her safety, though Dijsktra considered this a hasty move. 

Later that night, as an owl she followed Dandelion back to [Wolfgang Amadeus Goatbeard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolfgang_Amadeus_Goatbeard "Wolfgang Amadeus Goatbeard")'s house, a friend of the bards, where she found Geralt, Dandelion and the medic [Shani](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shani "Shani"). They discussed Geralt's position of danger in Oxenfurt, and then when the topic of [Rience](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rience "Rience") came up, Shani revealed she had met him three days previous, at the shop of a local merchant [Myhrman](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Myhrman "Myhrman"). The four went to the shop, and at the gate Shani lured the man out so he would let down the drawbridge and Geralt knocked him out. After Shani and Geralt attempted to persuade him, Philippa grew angry and turned to torture, using painful magic that led them to the location of an amulet that could summon Rience. 

At dawn, after Geralt had found Rience and incapacitated the four [Michelet brothers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Michelet_brothers "Michelet brothers"), Philippa found [Toublanc Michelet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toublanc_Michelet "Toublanc Michelet") laying dying on the ground. Whispering words of comfort, she was able to deduce that [Vilgefortz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilgefortz "Vilgefortz") had recommended the brothers to Rience, revealing the corruption in the brotherhood. She mercilessly finished him off with a stiletto and ran after Geralt and Rience. The two were fighting and as Geralt went to stab Rience, Philippa paralyzed the witcher, allowing Rience to escape through a [portal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Portal "Portal"), and partially explained her reasoning.[4]

### Thanedd coup

On the last night of June, she travelled with Dijkstra to [Thanedd Island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island") for the [Grand Mage's Conclave](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup"). That evening, at the banquet, they briefly talked with [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") and Geralt about [Sabrina Glevissig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sabrina_Glevissig "Sabrina Glevissig") and her alliance to [Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt"). Later she found Geralt again and they spoke about politics until interrupted by [Keira Metz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Keira_Metz "Keira Metz"). 

In the early hours of the morning, Philippa rallied several of the Northern supporters and began to arrest those allied with Nilfgaard. After they were all rounded up, Dijsktra and Keira brought a magically blinded Geralt to Philippa, who restored his eyesight and gave Dijkstra a list of traitors within Redania, found in Vilgefortz's office. The traitors were then led by [Radcliffe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radcliffe "Radcliffe") to [Garstang](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Garstang "Garstang") to face trial. Within the palace, [Tissaia de Vries](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tissaia_de_Vries "Tissaia de Vries") let out the Nilfgaardian traitors and a fight broke out between both sides. After Philippa managed to flee the conflict, she polymorphed into an owl and found Ciri being attacked by [Artaud Terranova](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Artaud_Terranova "Artaud Terranova"), whom she scratched his eyes deep with her talons, and Geralt finished him off. Believing herself in debt to Geralt for the escape of Rience, she allowed the two of them to flee without the council following, and then fled Thanedd herself back to Redania.[5]

After returning to Tretogor with several other mages left displaced by their rulers newfound hostility, such as [Triss Merigold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold"), Keira Metz, and [Margarita Laux-Antille](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margarita_Laux-Antille "Margarita Laux-Antille"), she learnt of Vizimir's assassination the same night as the coup, and so attempted to assist Queen [Hedwig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hedwig_of_Malleore "Hedwig of Malleore") in gaining control of the kingdom from her castle, [Montecalvo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Montecalvo "Montecalvo").[5][6]

### Formation of the Lodge

On August 5th, the four used a [projection](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Telecommunicator "Telecommunicator") spell to begin their first true meeting, mainly around recruitment and their purpose, summoning [Assire var Anahid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Assire_var_Anahid "Assire var Anahid"), [Sabrina Glevissig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sabrina_Glevissig "Sabrina Glevissig"), [Sheala de Tancarville](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sheala_de_Tancarville "Sheala de Tancarville"), and [Francesca Findabair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Francesca_Findabair "Francesca Findabair"). Philippa led the meeting and helped diffuse the tension created after Francesca joined, between her and Sabrina. Despite initial disagreements over the events of Thanedd, they agreed to work together to protect the safe practice of [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"). Backed by Margarita's evidence of women performing better with magic (based off comparing pupils from Aretuza and [Ban Ard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ban_Ard "Ban Ard")), they agreed women would only be allowed to join, and also the sorceresses would not have to be apolitical.[6]

### Death

Between 1272-1276 Philippa was later killed as a result of torture during the [Witch Hunter Raids](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_Hunter_Raids "Witch Hunter Raids"), becoming the last victim. 

## Appearance and personality

Philippa was described as attractive but intimidating by Dandelion, who thinks of her (perhaps using his signature hyperbole) as being "no less than three hundred years old" by [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"). She had dark hair and black eyes, and was known to favour black agate jewelry. She often wore practical traveling outfits when the need required,[4] however to more to formal events, such as the Thanned banquet, she wore a dress lined with diamonds and ermine fur. She also used crimson coloured lipstick, and cinnamon and muskroot perfume.[5]

Philippa was widely known throughout the Northern Kingdoms for both her political influence at the court of King Vizimir II, as well as for her prodigal magical powers, as even Tissaia De Vries considered her to be one of her best students.[5]

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

[![Philippa Eilhart's wanted post, as it appears in Novigrad](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/03/Wanted_noticed_posters_01.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20160104203323)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/03/Wanted_noticed_posters_01.png/revision/latest?cb=20160104203323) [![Philippa Eilhart's wanted post, as it appears in Novigrad](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/03/Wanted_noticed_posters_01.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20160104203323)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/03/Wanted_noticed_posters_01.png/revision/latest?cb=20160104203323) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Wanted_noticed_posters_01.png)

Philippa Eilhart's wanted post, as it appears in Novigrad

Philippa reappeared, having survived Loc Muinne, she fled to [a hideout](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Est_Tayiar "Est Tayiar") in southern Redania. In the quest [Return to Crookback Bog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Return_to_Crookback_Bog "Return to Crookback Bog"), Geralt found a [doll](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_magic_doll "Black magic doll") with obvious signs of black magic performed by the [crones](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crones "Crones") used to control the person this doll represents. The feathers on this doll immediately reminded Geralt of Philippa, but there was no definitive proof that the doll in fact represented her. 

She contacted [Margarita Laux-Antille](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Margarita_Laux-Antille "Margarita Laux-Antille"), explaining her plan to regrow her eyes by cultivating tissues on precious stones, just as [Vilgefortz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilgefortz_of_Roggeveen "Vilgefortz of Roggeveen") did, and to revive the Lodge. Then she went to [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") to seek help from her former lover, the sorcerer [Arthur de Vleester](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arthur_de_Vleester "Arthur de Vleester"). Having humiliated him in the past, de Vleester convinced her she'd be safer in her owl form, and then attached a [dimeritium](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dimeritium "Dimeritium") band around her ankle. Following de Vleester's execution during the witch hunts, Philippa fell into the possession of [Zoltan Chivay](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zoltan_Chivay "Zoltan Chivay"), who affectionately named her Poppy. 

He later lost her to [Sigismund Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra"), who made the mistake of removing her dimeritium band, and she returned to human form and attacked. Only Geralt's intervention put an end to her rampage. Philippa was then recruited to help stop the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") in exchange for amnesty from Emperor [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis"). After Margarita was rescued from [Oxenfurt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oxenfurt "Oxenfurt"), she and Philippa met with Ciri to offer her equal partnership in the Lodge, to which she declined. 

    _If Geralt choose to participate in the conspiracy to assassinate Radovid:_ Philippa spied on a meeting between Geralt, Dijkstra, Roche, Ves and Thaler in her owl form. Subsequently, she changed to human form and spoke with Geralt when he left the meeting, telling him that Radovid won't take his word that he had her captive and provided him Vizimir's ring to legitimize his story. Following the deaths of Radovid's escort by Geralt and the [Temerians](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria"), Radovid banged on a nearby door, only to find Philippa waiting for him. She blinded him with magic dust as payback for taking her own eyes before stabbing him in the back, killing him, after which she polymorphed into an owl and disappeared.

Philippa later assisted Geralt in seeking out the [Sunstone](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sunstone "Sunstone") to help in their plan to lure out the Wild Hunt. While searching through the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") caves, Philippa criticised Geralt for having an overprotective attitude towards [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), stating that the Lodge were not a pack of wolves he could scare away with his sword, to which the witcher insisted that he could because they were. Once they found the Sunstone, Geralt assumed that she would take the artifact for herself, but Philippa insisted that that was not the case. She then revealed her intentions: to take [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg")'s place at Emhyr's court and become Ciri's advisor, and hoped Geralt would take Yennefer with him and leave everything to her.

### Journal entry

    _Philippa Eilhart, one-time advisor to King Vizimir II, called the Just, member of the Council of Mages and later founder of the Lodge of Sorceresses, had played a momentous part in the history of the world as well as in Geralt's life._
    _There could be no denying her talent, yet neither could one claim she did not also at times demonstrate sickly ambition. Very trustworthy reports indicated she had had a hand in King Vizimir II's death, and, as a member of the Lodge, was implicated in the murder of two other monarchs as well - Demavend of Aedirn and Foltest of Temeria._
    _For this reason the one-time "Jewel of the Court at Tretogor" was now a wanted woman, the quarry of special forces from the North and Nilfgaard alike._
    _Vizimir's son and successor, Radovid V, was particularly eager to capture her. During their last encounter in Loc Muinne he had had Philippa's eyes gouged out, yet she had fled and he wanted nothing more than to stick the head of "Tretegor's Jewel" on a spike above the city gates._
    _Thanks to her mastery of the difficult art of polymorphy, Philippa had been able to evade her pursuers by transforming into an owl. Then she had holed up in a hideout outside Novigrad to wait out the worst._
    _Everything seemed to indicate that Philippa had not only been investigating magic cures to her lost vision, but had also taken concrete steps towards the reactivation of the Lodge of Sorceresses. Her current location, however, remained unknown._
    _Imagine our surprise when we learned Philippa had been right under our noses the whole time! During her flight from her pursuers she had sought shelter with an old lover and fellow mage. He must've still born a grudge, however, for he tricked her and imprisoned her in her owl form. After the with hunters carted him off, his house was plundered and the owl wound up in the hands of... our dear friend Zoltan. Sadly Triss discovered this right after Zoltan had lost the owl in a game of cards - meaning our search had to continue._
    _Philippa's new "owner"? Dijkstra. Was it a coincidence that she was now in the hands of her spurned-ex-lover and hated enemy? Triss certainly didn't think so, instead suspecting the former spy had woven a gwent-centered web of subterfuge in order to settle some old score._
    _Luckily for Philippa, Geralt got things under control and saved her from whatever delights Dijkstra was planning._
    _Though the Lodge's prior actions were highly controversial, Philippa stubbornly worked toward its reconstitution, offering Ciri the chance to join it as a full and equal member._
    **If Geralt chooses to complete Reason of State:**

    _One can call historical justice the fact that Philippa Eilhart, Radovid's old tutor, ended the crazed monarch's life. In doing so she also exacted revenge for her blinding and the death and persecution of her fellow mages, so many of whom had died in agony on Radovid's orders._

In _[The Lady of the Lake](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Lady_of_the_Lake "The Lady of the Lake")_ , Philippa is wearing a sardonyx cameo brooch which, according to [Fringilla Vigo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fringilla_Vigo "Fringilla Vigo"), was a gift from her girlfriend. 
    * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , Sigismund Dijkstra also mentions it in [Now or Never](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Now_or_Never "Now or Never"), stating how he once tried to woo the sorceress, but she made it clear she preferred women. In _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ , [Yarpen Zigrin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yarpen_Zigrin "Yarpen Zigrin") tells Geralt that Philippa was once involved with Dijkstra to learn the plans of the [Redanian Secret Service](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redanian_Secret_Service "Redanian Secret Service"), despite the fact that her lack of interest in men was widespread knowledge. It is made clear that the two had an affair before in the novels.

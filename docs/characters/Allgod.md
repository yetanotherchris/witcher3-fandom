**Allgod** was a [sylvan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sylvan "Sylvan") worshiped by some villagers in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"). His [cult](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cult_of_Allgod "Cult of Allgod") was initiated in the [12th century](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#12th_century "Timeline/Games") after Allgod inadvertently "revealed" himself to Roslav of [Oreton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Oreton "Oreton"), as he'd made his lair in a cellar below.

In The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, Allgod is a sylvan worshipped by villagers in Velen. His cult started in the 12th century after being found by Roslav of Oreton. Villagers in Velen worshipped Allgod, a sylvan posing as a deity. Around 1170, Allgod convinced Roslav of Oreton of his divinity, leading to the formation of a cult. He demanded food and wine offerings, even in difficult times, and lived off these tributes. The villagers believed in his divine powers, unaware of his true nature.

Allgod, a sylvan, had convinced the locals of his divine status and demanded food sacrifices, growing fat off their offerings. Geralt finds Allgod living in a hidden cellar and has the option to expose him, demand he accept humble offerings, or kill him.

## Biography

Living in relative comfort for almost a century, Allgod secretly lived in the hidden cellar off of his worshipers' offerings, soon growing fat off the amount of food he demanded. 

_If Geralt comes along and discovers Allgod:_ In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") discovered locals trying to appease Allgod with their meager offerings, as the [war](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_War_III "Northern War III") had ravaged their food source. Annoyed at the lack of food, Allgod claimed he'd bring bad luck down on the locals if his demands weren't met. 

    _If Geralt killed the sylvan:_ Allgod soon learned he'd bitten off more than he could chew and the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") summarily killed him for his greed.
    _If Geralt convinced the sylvan to accept any offerings:_ Geralt convinced the sylvan that the scraps that the local peasantry were offering would be sufficient. Allgod conceded but explained he would be back to good meals after the war was done.
    _If Geralt couldn't persuade the sylvan:_ Allgod made Geralt tell the peasantry that he would not take any scraps, which led to the peasants sacrificing even more of their food.

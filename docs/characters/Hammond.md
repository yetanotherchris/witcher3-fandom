**Hammond** was a [slaver](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Slavery "Slavery") and former member of [Jad Karadin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jad_Karadin "Jad Karadin")'s gang, along with [Selyse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Selyse "Selyse"), [Lund](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lund "Lund") and [Vienne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vienne "Vienne"). He was considered to be Karadin's right-hand man.

**How did Hammond become a chief pirate in Faroe?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Hammond rose to the position of chief pirate in Faroe by establishing a slave trade in Trottheim, Skellige, and commanding a formidable fleet. His operations across the Skellige islands earned him bounties and respect from the jarls. Previously, Hammond was a key figure in Jad Karadin's gang, which contributed to his reputation.

**Why did Hammond start a slave trade in Skellige?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Hammond, once part of Jad Karadin's gang, initiated a slave trade in Skellige's Trottheim after the gang split. He rose to become the chief pirate of Faroe, commanding a strong fleet and earning bounties across Skellige. His actions earned him respect from the jarls but also put him at odds with Holger Blackhand. In 1272, Geralt of Rivia confronted Hammond near a slave market for information on Karadin. Hammond, loyal to Karadin, attacked Geralt and was killed.

**What role did Hammond play in Jad Karadin's gang?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Hammond played a crucial role in Jad Karadin's gang as Karadin's right-hand man. He participated in the killing of the witcher Aiden from the Cat School. Following the gang's breakup, Hammond relocated to Skellige, where he became a slaver and the leading pirate of Faroe.

**What was Hammond's connection to the witcher Aiden?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Hammond was involved in the death of the witcher Aiden as a member of Jad Karadin's gang, which also included Selyse, Lund, and Vienne. ## Biography

As a member of Jad Karadin's gang he was one of the people responsible for the death of the witcher [Aiden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aiden "Aiden") of the [Cat School](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cat_School "Cat School"). After the gang disbanded, Hammond travelled to [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") and started a slave trade business in [Trottheim](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Trottheim "Trottheim"). He built his reputation as the chief pirate of [Faroe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Faroe "Faroe"), commanding a mighty fleet, gaining bounties in every island of Skellige and the respect of the jarls. He was in constant conflict with [Holger Blackhand](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Holger_Blackhand "Holger Blackhand"). 

_If Geralt goes after him:_ In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), he was meditating at an altar near the slave market when [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") approached him, chasing information about Jad Karadin. Unwilling to betray Karadin's trust, Hammond attacked the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") and met his end. Geralt later tells Lambert that Hammond was in business with Karadin, the two having remained in contact despite appearances.

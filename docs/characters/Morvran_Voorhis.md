**Morvran Voorhis** was an [Imperator](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Imperator "Imperator") of the [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") from the Voorhis family who ascended the throne sometime around the end of the 13th century. As a relative of [Emhyr var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") through both paternal and maternal lines, his position was always secured. After his death, [Jan Calveit](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jan_Calveit "Jan Calveit") succeeded him. 

Prior to ascending the throne, Morvran was a member of the [Guild of Merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Guild_of_Merchants "Guild of Merchants"), commander of the [Alba Division](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alba_Division "Alba Division") and, thanks to his [father](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince_Voorhis "Prince Voorhis"), a prominent member of the Nilfgaardian nobility and favorite of opposition. 

## Biography

### Conspiracy against Emhyr

In [1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline"), during a time when he was still a child according to imperial law, the original conspirators - [Joachim de Wett](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Joachim_de_Wett "Joachim de Wett"), [Ardal aep Dahy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ardal_aep_Dahy "Ardal aep Dahy"), Count [d'Arvy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/D%27Arvy "D'Arvy"), [Berengar Leuvaarden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Berengar_Leuvaarden "Berengar Leuvaarden"), [Count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") [Broinne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Broinne "Broinne"), and [Stefan Skellen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stefan_Skellen "Stefan Skellen") \- planned to overthrow Emhyr and place someone who would become their puppet. Originally, Morvran's father was proposed but later they decided to put young Morvran on the Imperial throne. Before they could take any real steps, however, Leuvaarden betrayed them and they were subsequently sentenced to death.[1]

### Commander of the Alba Division

Sometime after the death of [Tibor Eggebracht](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tibor_Eggebracht "Tibor Eggebracht"), Morvran was appointed as the commander of an armored [Alba Division](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alba_Division "Alba Division") and also joined Berengar Leuvaarden's [Guild of Merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Guild_of_Merchants "Guild of Merchants"). With this high ranking position and his pure-blood nationality, his status in society went up. He was also the most important general during the [Third Northern War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Third_Northern_War "Third Northern War").[2]

In May [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), Morvran's men were killed by the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") while escorting [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") to [Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima"). During Geralt's stay in the occupied capital of Vizima, Morvran interviewed the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") on his past while he was being shaved for his audience with Emhyr. When he had time to spare, he would either enjoy the company of [Baroness](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Baroness "Baroness") [Maria Louisa La Valette](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maria_Louisa_La_Valette "Maria Louisa La Valette") in her [villa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Baroness_la_Valette%27s_Villa "Baroness la Valette's Villa") or go to see races near [Vegelbud Residence](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vegelbud_Residence "Vegelbud Residence"), as he believed horses to be "far more honest than men."[2]

### Governing the Empire

As Emperor, he ordered the conviction of coadjutor of the Empire [Peter Evertsen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peter_Evertsen "Peter Evertsen"), a former imperial treasurer of the crown and chief chamberlain of the army, who died in [Winneburg Castle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Winneburg_Castle "Winneburg Castle") in [1301](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1301 "Timeline").[3]

Morvran Voorhis was fond of saying he could win a war without a single sword. There was much truth in that: Morvran's strategy revolved around the shrewd use of spies and saboteurs. This might not have earned him much honor, but it did earn him a great many victories. He was also known to recognize no rules in warfare. To vanquish the enemy, he was willing to make alliances with anyone, whether they be renegade witchers, war criminals, or rebellious mages. Only one thing mattered: victory.[4]

He liked to keep close tabs on his enemy's activities. To this end, he would write them numerous lengthy letters; the contents were pure drivel, flowery formulations saying nothing at all, but the emissaries he sent to deliver these letters kept their eyes wide open, serving him as an extension of [Secret Service](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Secret_Service "Nilfgaardian Secret Service"). He also cultivated both a wide range of skilled spies working on the Empire's behalf, as well as special [Nauzicaa Brigade](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nauzicaa "Nauzicaa") officers devoted to dealing with agents foolish enough to betray the Empire.[4]

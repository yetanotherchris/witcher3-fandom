**Tellico Lunngrevink Letorte** , alias _Penstock_ , but to his friends, **Dudu** , is a [doppler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Doppler "Doppler") who first appears in the short story "[Eternal Flame](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Flame "Eternal Flame")" in _[Sword of Destiny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sword_of_Destiny "Sword of Destiny")_. 

In "Eternal Flame", Dudu ultimately faces [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") by taking on the witcher's own shape. This gives Geralt a very good idea of what fighting himself would be like and it is not at all certain at the outset if the "true witcher" will prevail. It all comes down to a question of conscience. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What is Dudu's real name in the Sword of Destiny?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Sword of Destiny, Dudu is actually Tellico Lunngrevink Letorte, also known as Penstock. As a doppler, Dudu can mimic others, and in 'Eternal Flame', he assumes Geralt's form, providing a unique combat experience for the witcher. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does Dudu's ability to shape-shift affect his interactions with Geralt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Dudu, a doppler, uses his shape-shifting ability to impersonate Dainty Biberveldt, a friend of Geralt, to leverage Dainty's contacts for his business. Geralt finds Dudu when he reverts to his natural form after being knocked out while disguised as Dandelion. Geralt protects Dudu's identity when they are confronted. Dudu's ability also aids in Geralt's search for Cirilla, as he is discovered during a theatre play in Novigrad. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**In which installment of the game trilogy does Dudu appear?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Dudu is featured in The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, the last installment of the game trilogy. Geralt, in his quest for Cirilla, finds Dudu in a theatre play in Novigrad, with the help of his friend Priscilla and a clever ruse. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How does Dudu's confrontation with Geralt in 'Eternal Flame' end?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Geralt locates Dudu during a theatre play in Novigrad, aided by a trick and his friend Priscilla. Post-confrontation, Geralt is allowed to leave, suggesting the duel's conditions were met. Subsequently, Geralt and Dandelion devise their next steps and pause at a temple where Nenneke replenishes Geralt's elixir supply. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Dudu** appears in final installment of the game trilogy and is sought after by Geralt during his hunt for [Cirilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"). With a trick and a little help of his friend [Priscilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priscilla "Priscilla"), it's possible to find Dudu during a theatre play in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). 

### Journal entry

    _Geralt and I had known the doppler Dudu for years - from before the moment we had actually met him, in fact._
    _You see, we first unwittingly talked to him when he was in Novigrad impersonating another friend of mine, a halfling merchant named[Dainty Biberveldt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dainty_Biberveldt "Dainty Biberveldt"). Dudu had assumed Dainty's form to use the merchant's network of contacts for some business endeavors of his own._
    _Dainty was infuriated at first at his inability to locate the impostor who had inserted himself into his dealings, but when Dudu's investments began generating sizable returns, the halfling changed his tune. He took the doppler on as a partner, introducing him to everyone as his cousin, Dudu Biberveldt._
    _Geralt learned that Dudu had crossed[Whoreson Junior](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Whoreson_Junior "Whoreson Junior"), one of Novigrad's most ruthless crime bosses. That Dudu extracted himself from this predicament while only losing one eye should be considered a fortunate turn._
    _Ciri, Dudu and I had all embroiled ourselves in quite the fiasco, but unlike me, the doppler was not caught by the temple guard._
    _Dudu was a theater aficionado and frequent attendee of[Irena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Irina_Renarde "Irina Renarde")'s [[_sic_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "wikipedia:Sic")] mummers' performances. Apparently he had even stepped in to replace indisposed mummers on several occasions - and, given his special abilities, proved himself a uniquely convincing understudy._
    _Finally, after a great deal of trouble and thanks to a clever ruse, Geralt was reunited with his old friend - and a certain imprisoned poet's only hope._
    **If Geralt chose to kill Whoreson Junior:**

    _To end Dudu's story, let me just say that, as usual, he found a way to land on his feet. Dudu disguised himself as his one-time adversary, Whoreson Junior, and took over his business, announcing it was going clean and investing all of its ill-gotten wealth in a legal (and very profitable) overseas trading company._
    _In a way, you could say he was living the "Novigrad dream."_

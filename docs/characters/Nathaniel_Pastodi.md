**Nathaniel Pastodi** was a [reverend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reverend "Reverend") of the [Eternal Fire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eternal_Fire "Eternal Fire") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), and the supervisor of the city's [morgue](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad_Morgue "Novigrad Morgue"). 

## Biography

Once, Nathaniel Pastodi worked as a torturer, and reportedly took delight in torturing women. Later, Pastodi was appointed to supervisor of the city morgue by Hierarch [Hemmelfart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cyrus_Engelkind_Hemmelfart "Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart"), in which position he was tasked with overseeing cremations, performing funeral masses on [Temple Isle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Isle "Temple Isle"), and cooperating with the [Temple Guard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Guard "Temple Guard").[2]

In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), Nathaniel Pastodi first met [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and [Joachim von Gratz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Joachim_von_Gratz "Joachim von Gratz") in the city morgue, while they were investigating the serial killer who had attacked [Priscilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Priscilla "Priscilla"). The reverend reprimanded the coroner [Hubert Rejk](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hubert_Rejk "Hubert Rejk") for allowing Geralt and Joachim access to the morgue without authorization. 

Later, after finding a note made of [human](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") skin on [Patricia Vegelbud](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patricia_Vegelbud "Patricia Vegelbud")'s body, the murderer's trail led Geralt to [Crippled Kate's](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crippled_Kate%27s "Crippled Kate's") where Pastodi was busy torturing [Sweet Nettie](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sweet_Nettie "Sweet Nettie") with a hot [poker](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poker_\(weapon\) "Poker \(weapon\)"). 

_If Geralt kills Pastodi:_ Geralt decided that Pastodi was the serial killer and ended his life, not pursuing the matter forward. 

_If Geralt hears him out:_ from the information that Pastodi told him, Geralt deduced that the actual killer was Hubert Rejk, who attempted to steer him onto Pastodi's trail. The [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") may then have decided to kill Pastodi anyway for torturing women as a pastime, used the [Axii](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Axii "Axii") [sign](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sign "Sign") on him, or left him be. 

## Journal entry

    _Reverend Nathaniel, supervisor of the city morgue and cleric of the Church of the Eternal Fire, was a man with a vile character – and a stormy past. Before donning the frock he had served for years as the city torturer._
    _One would be hard pressed to find a more inferior candidate for a pastor than a former hangman – but, as the saying goes, in Novigrad anything is possible._
    _To say Geralt caught Nathaniel red-handed would be an ill-considered pun. Yet though the situation Geralt found him in clearly proved Nathaniel was a cruel sadist, it did not prove he was a serial killer. Geralt kept a cool head and decided the priest was not the man he was after._
    **If Geralt kills Nathaniel:**

    _Nevertheless, Geralt still felt Nathaniel deserved death. At times I have thought Geralt too eager to play judge, jury and executioner – but this time, I took no issue with his actions._
    **If Geralt spares Nathaniel but didn't catch the real murderer:**

    _Afterwards, however, a new victim's body was found and Nathaniel's name was cleared - well, of the killings, that is, for nothing could absolve this priest of his countless other sins._
    **If Geralt realizes Nathaniel is not the murderer:**

    _Not tarrying any further, Geralt raced off to catch the true perpetrator._

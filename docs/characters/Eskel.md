**Eskel** , born as **Esau Kelly Kaminski** ,[1] was a [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") of the [School of the Wolf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Wolf "School of the Wolf"), trained by Master [Vesemir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vesemir "Vesemir") at [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen"), and active during the [13th century](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#13th_century "Timeline"). As the [sorceress](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorceress "Sorceress") [Triss Merigold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold") noted, Eskel emitted a strong [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") aura. Though not as famous as his peer [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), Eskel earned a solid reputation as a professional, reliable witcher, known for his kind and easygoing nature.[2]

Eskel and Geralt, the White Wolf, were like brothers. They grew up together, shared in the Trials, and enjoyed countless moments of camaraderie. A close friend to the younger [Lambert](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lambert "Lambert") and [Coën](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Co%C3%ABn "Coën") of [Poviss](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Poviss "Poviss"), Eskel viewed Vesemir not only as a mentor but also as a father figure. Every winter, he returned to Kaer Morhen to exchange stories and rejuvenate before heading back out on [The Path](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Path "The Path").[2]

## Biography

### Early life

Born in the mountains, Eskel hailed from a family of hill-folk. His mother used to sing him an old hiking tune about a [hen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hen "Hen"), which would later become the only memory he retained of her.[3]

Under unknown circumstances, the Witchers' Settlement in the [Blue Mountains](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blue_Mountains "Blue Mountains") eventually became Eskel's home. It was there that he befriended Geralt, a peer who would become almost like a brother to him. Together, they indulged in youthful mischief. On one occasion, Eskel and Geralt captured a large forest bumblebee and tied it to a jug, watching its frantic movements with fits of laughter until Vesemir caught them and punished them with a [leather](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leather "Leather") strap.[4]

Tirelessly studying all manner of creatures, the young trainees often heard their teachers repeat crude jokes about virgins, [unicorns](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Unicorn_\(creature\) "Unicorn \(creature\)"), and [basilisks](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") and mirrors—jokes that quickly lost their charm. The trainees found the most primitive of these tales to be variations on how a young witcher might shake hands with a female [dragon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon").[5]

To become full-fledged witchers, the youth underwent the excruciating and dangerous [Trial of the Grasses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Trial_of_the_Grasses "Trial of the Grasses"), which altered their physiology, granting them abilities such as [cat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cat_\(creature\) "Cat \(creature\)")-like vision in the dark. Eskel feared for Geralt's life when his friend's hair turned white as a side effect of the trial. Despite this, both managed to survive the ordeal together. They trained on [The Trail](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Killer "The Killer"), a dangerous course filled with obstacles that was infamously known as "The Killer."[2]

### The Child Surprise

[![Eskel propped by Lambert, moments after receiving distorting scars.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/48/Cutscene_PoN_involved_2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20191121195607)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/48/Cutscene_PoN_involved_2.png/revision/latest?cb=20191121195607) [![Eskel propped by Lambert, moments after receiving distorting scars.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/48/Cutscene_PoN_involved_2.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20191121195607)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/4/48/Cutscene_PoN_involved_2.png/revision/latest?cb=20191121195607) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Cutscene_PoN_involved_2.png)

Eskel propped by Lambert, moments after receiving distorting scars.

No earlier than 1229,[N 1] Eskel saved a prince of [Caingorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Caingorn "Caingorn") during a fierce struggle with a [werebbubb](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Werebbubb "Werebbubb") gang. As a reward, the prince offered Eskel anything he desired. However, unable to think of a specific request, Eskel recited the fabled [Law of Surprise](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Law_of_Surprise "Law of Surprise") that he had heard from Vesemir over the years: "Give me that which you find at home yet do not expect." 

This is how Princess [Deidre Ademeyn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Deidre_Ademeyn "Deidre Ademeyn"), born during an eclipse, became Eskel's surprise child. Eskel, reluctant to accept responsibility, went to great lengths to avoid returning to Caingorn. He took long, convoluted routes to distance himself from the kingdom, hearing tales of Deidre maturing into a highly unpredictable young woman. She had also discovered an innate gift for blocking [magic](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic"), which many superstitiously interpreted as the [accursed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse") [mutation](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mutant "Mutant") of the [Black Sun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sun "Black Sun"). To understand what this all meant, Eskel carried [a collective work](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Curse_of_the_Black_Sun_\(book\) "The Curse of the Black Sun \(book\)") about the phenomenon. He suspected that the [Council of Wizards](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Council_of_Wizards "Council of Wizards") had meddled too much in state affairs, causing the situation to spiral out of control. Had they not sent [Sabrina Glevissig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sabrina_Glevissig "Sabrina Glevissig"), Deidre might not have been ostracized. 

Around late 1240s or early 1250s,[N 2] Deidre sought asylum at Kaer Morhen. Vesemir took her in, while Eskel worked on a [kikimore](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kikimore "Kikimore") hatchery in an old mine outside the keep, all while trying to devise a solution to the growing crisis. Geralt found Eskel there, and the two discussed the situation. Eskel cared for Deidre and was unwilling to send her to Glevissig, who, driven by unproven conclusions, sought to kill and study the princess. 

After returning to Kaer Morhen, Vesemir called his pupils together to present ideas. Unsure of the best course of action, Eskel turned to Geralt for guidance. 

_If the witchers chose to help her:_ The witchers decided that they should aid Eskel's surprise child. Unfortunately, Eskel became involved in a scuffle between Deidre and her younger brother, [Merwin Ademeyn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merwin_Ademeyn "Merwin Ademeyn"), a misguided ally of Glevissig who sought to rule. During the confrontation, the princess accidentally slashed Eskel across the face. Deeply regretful, she left, resolving the situation. Several years later, she sent Eskel a letter sealed with her royal seal. Eskel, however, burned the letter without reading it and never spoke of her again. 

_If the witchers chose not to help Deidre:_ The witchers decided not to help Deidre, which led to a furious outburst from her. In her rage, Deidre nearly killed Eskel before fleeing. Sabrina intervened, healing Eskel's wounds, though she could not remove the deep scars from his face. She insisted that the witchers leave Deidre to her, promising to find and deal with her herself. It is unclear whether the witchers allowed this, but for the next two years, Deidre became a notorious outlaw, terrorizing southern Kaedwen. Eventually, she was hunted down and killed, either by Sabrina and her allies, who tortured and examined her for mutations (finding none), or by a mysterious scarred man who refused to collect the bounty on her head. 

Although the witchers of Kaer Morhen never spoke of Deidre again, none of them ever forgot her. Each of them carried her memory in their heart. The bard [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion") would later compose a ballad about the events.[6]

### Training Ciri

In early autumn of [1264](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1264 "Timeline"), Eskel returned to Kaer Morhen for the winter. One day, while he was closing the main gate to preserve the warmth inside, Geralt arrived with Ciri. Eskel embraced Geralt, though he was initially skeptical of the idea of training Ciri. Nonetheless, he agreed to her training and welcomed the girl to Kaer Morhen, taking her luggage. When she asked who else was at the keep besides Vesemir, Eskel introduced her to Lambert and [Coën](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Co%C3%ABn "Coën"), their comrade from the [School of the Griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Griffin "School of the Griffin").[2][7]

Over the following months, Eskel helped Ciri with her training. However, he grew increasingly concerned about the trances she fell into after accidentally drinking [White Gull](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Gull "White Gull").[2]

The third trance, in particular, caused great distress among the witchers. They gathered together in silence, each unsure of how to handle the situation. Unable to endure the tense quiet, Eskel swore, stood up, and went to fetch a strong drink. As Coën and Geralt discussed the issue, Eskel poured himself some vodka. Initially, Vesemir had been furious about the alcohol, but after a moment, he beckoned Eskel to pour him a pint as well. The silence continued, until Eskel, seeking a solution, suggested summoning a [witch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") for help. 

When Lambert mockingly commented on Ciri's latest prophecy—suggesting that witchers might die by monster teeth, or perhaps by dental gangrene—Eskel added, in a seemingly serious tone, that this could be another possible interpretation. Vesemir immediately hushed both of them, reminding them that such matters should not be taken lightly.[5]

They ultimately decided to summon [Triss Merigold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Triss_Merigold "Triss Merigold"), who arrived near [Midinváerne](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Midinv%C3%A1erne "Midinváerne") in [1265](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1265 "Timeline"). Upon meeting her, Eskel, aware of his own potent magical aura, bowed and politely kissed her hand. At first, he was suspicious of Triss's intentions, fearing she might reveal the witchers' secrets to the [Brotherhood of Sorcerers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brotherhood_of_Sorcerers "Brotherhood of Sorcerers"). However, she quickly convinced him that she had no such plans. Eskel had a generally good rapport with Triss, listening carefully when she shared her thoughts on how the witchers were treating Ciri. 

After discussing the situation regarding Ciri's trances and prophecies, Triss informed the witchers about the broader issues affecting the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"). Eskel, who had been asking several questions at first, fell silent as the conversation shifted into a heated debate about neutrality, which he chose not to engage in further.[2]

### On the path

[![Eskel wintering at the keep.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e0/Witcher-3-7.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20160708072629)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e0/Witcher-3-7.png/revision/latest?cb=20160708072629) [![Eskel wintering at the keep.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e0/Witcher-3-7.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20160708072629)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/e/e0/Witcher-3-7.png/revision/latest?cb=20160708072629) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Witcher-3-7.png)

Eskel wintering at the keep.

Before [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), [Yennefer of Vengerberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") visited Kaer Morhen, and Eskel had the chance to meet her. 

Despite never achieving Geralt's level of fame, Eskel was equally skilled and experienced, with a reputation as a reliable and efficient witcher. One of his notable deeds was attempting to save a little girl from a basilisk. He successfully split the creature open, but by the time he reached her, she had already been half-digested. Eskel had been in the area hunting a [zeugl](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zeugl "Zeugl") at the time. He did not shy away from more adventurous encounters either—he once slept with a [succubus](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Succubus "Succubus") and indulged in [fisstech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fisstech "Fisstech") with her. At some point, he also invoked the Law of Surprise again, gaining a foal which he named Scorpion, and which later became the horse he rode.[3]

During the warmer months at Kaer Morhen, Eskel would venture out to drive away dangerous [harpies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Harpy "Harpy"), helping to keep the area safe.[8]

### Two attacks on Kaer Morhen

#### Salamandra burglary

[![Eskel during the Salamandra heist.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/12/Tw-screenshot-eskel.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/210?cb=20111015125838)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/12/Tw-screenshot-eskel.png/revision/latest?cb=20111015125838) [![Eskel during the Salamandra heist.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/12/Tw-screenshot-eskel.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/210?cb=20111015125838)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/1/12/Tw-screenshot-eskel.png/revision/latest?cb=20111015125838) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw-screenshot-eskel.png)

Eskel during the Salamandra heist.

One day before [Belleteyn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Belleteyn "Belleteyn") of [1270](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1270 "Timeline/Games"), Eskel and Lambert were at Kaer Morhen, tasked with repairing a staircase. However, they postponed the task. They were also training the latest witcher student, [Leo](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leo "Leo"), an orphan Vesemir had brought to Kaer Morhen. The following day, while Eskel and Vesemir were out hunting, a magical storm swept through the area. They discovered an unconscious Geralt—who had been presumed dead—near the keep and loaded him onto a cart. As they traveled back, Eskel reassured the amnesiac Geralt that everything would be fine, as he was among friends now. 

Shortly after Geralt's return, [Azar Javed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Azar_Javed "Azar Javed") and the mysterious [Salamandra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Salamandra "Salamandra") attacked. Eskel, along with his comrades, defended the keep. Vesemir assigned Eskel and Lambert to assist him in fighting [Savolla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Savolla "Savolla")'s [frightener](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Frightener "Frightener"), but the bandits gained the upper hand. The witchers retreated to the upper courtyard, where Merigold chastised Eskel and Lambert for not repairing the staircase, which could have helped with the defense. When she speculated that Salamandra might be after witcher mutagens, Lambert was skeptical, but Eskel supported her theory. Although Eskel helped defeat the frightener, the bandits managed to escape with the mutagens after killing Leo. After the fight, he spent some time in the keep's armory and also practiced fistfighting with Geralt. 

After the witchers burned Leo's body on a funeral pyre, they split up to gather information. Eskel headed west, where he had contacts and preferred the coastal climate.[9]

#### Bounties in 1271

Executing a forktail.

In the year following the defeat of [Jacques de Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jacques_de_Aldersberg "Jacques de Aldersberg"), the mastermind behind Salamandra, Eskel undertook several contracts. One of his notable contracts involved dealing with a hypnotic [samovila](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vila "Vila"). He was hired to free a peasant farmer from its influence but left when the farmer, who had fallen in love with the samovila, asked Eskel to trap her for him afterward. Eskel refused, recognizing the danger of entangling the farmer further in such a predicament. 

Around six months before the witchers' final clash with the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt"), Eskel was ambushed by a [Manticore](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Manticore "Manticore") in the forests of [Creyden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Creyden "Creyden"). The beast moved so quickly that it didn't trigger his medallion. Eskel was injured in the skirmish and received a sizable scar, but he managed to kill the Manticore and take its head to the nearest village elder. However, there was no contract on the creature's head, and the elder swore he had never heard of such a monster before, meaning Eskel didn't receive any payment for his trouble. 

On another contract in [Aldersberg](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aldersberg "Aldersberg"), Eskel was tasked with hunting a [katakan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Katakan "Katakan"), an ancient vampire that had been killing drunkards. He spent a month stalking the creature before devising a plan to catch it at a masquerade ball held by local nobles. Eskel used the event to his advantage—he had made an agreement with an alchemist: she would load herself with magic mushrooms, vodka, and fisstech and act as bait. During the ball, Eskel enjoyed the company of several ladies before dealing with the vampire. The katakan took the bait and drank his fill of the alchemist's blood. Fighting became an afterthought, allowing Eskel to kill the vampire. He took its body back to Kaer Morhen for further study, as it had been a stronger one than he had encountered before. He believed that a proper examination of its body parts might lead to a new witcher method for determining age based on the vampire's footsteps.[3]

#### Clash against the Wild Hunt

In the fall of 1272, Eskel was hunting a [forktail](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Forktail "Forktail")—a draconid that needed to be removed as Yennefer lacked the organs necessary to distill ingredients for disenchanting [Uma](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Uma "Uma"). After Geralt tracked him down, Eskel agreed to let the White Wolf join the hunt. The two caught up on recent events in their lives as they tracked the forktail to its lair and ultimately killed it. Afterward, Eskel mounted his horse, and the two engaged in a friendly race back to Kaer Morhen. 

Once inside, Eskel showed Geralt the katakan specimen he had recently obtained and began its autopsy. Later, when Yennefer started the transformation process on Uma, Eskel was visibly troubled by Uma undergoing the Trial of the Grasses. He eventually walked off, but returned with some rags to help clean up after Yennefer, who had been vomited on during the process. After Yennefer successfully broke Uma's curse, she went to rest, and Eskel sat down with Lambert to share stories and drink. When Geralt joined them, they all began drinking together. 

_If Geralt opts to get drunk with the rest:_ The three of them became increasingly drunk as they swapped tales, played a drinking game, and even dressed up as the [Lodge of Sorceresses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lodge_of_Sorceresses "Lodge of Sorceresses") in a playful attempt to invite them to Kaer Morhen. While fiddling with Yennefer's [megascope](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Megascope "Megascope"), they briefly made contact with [Hierarch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hierarch "Hierarch") [Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cyrus_Engelkind_Hemmelfart "Cyrus Engelkind Hemmelfart") of [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), which led to Yennefer storming in to stop the charade. Eskel had to return the clothes they had taken in jest. 

[![Eskel goes toe to toe with Caranthir.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Caranthir_battling_Eskel.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20151207010657)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Caranthir_battling_Eskel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20151207010657) [![Eskel goes toe to toe with Caranthir.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Caranthir_battling_Eskel.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/245?cb=20151207010657)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/0/0e/Caranthir_battling_Eskel.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20151207010657) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Caranthir_battling_Eskel.jpg)

Eskel goes toe to toe with Caranthir.

In the wake of Ciri's arrival at Kaer Morhen, [Eredin Bréacc Glas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eredin_Br%C3%A9acc_Glas "Eredin Bréacc Glas") and the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") launched an attack. Eskel defended the inner courtyard of the keep, where Caranthir opened a [portal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Portal "Portal") and brought more riders through. Eskel held his own in a duel with Caranthir, managing to fight him expertly until Caranthir used his teleporting abilities. This almost cost Eskel his life, but Ciri intervened and saved him. Caranthir, called away by orders, retreated, though not before commanding his warriors to kill Eskel and Ciri. The two witchers fought back, killing the Hunt's warriors and continuing to defend the keep. 

As the battle progressed, the defenders were pushed back to the keep's courtyard, where suddenly a freezing cold tore through the gate, freezing Eskel and the others in place. He remained frozen until Eredin and the Aen Elle warriors vanished, driven off by Ciri's burst of power following Vesemir's death. Eskel's ears had bled from the shock of the magical release, though he was freed once the battle was over.[3]

### Departure south

In the aftermath of the battle, Eskel bid a final farewell to his valiant master, Vesemir. Struggling to imagine Kaer Morhen without him, Eskel decided to leave, with his sights set on Lormark. Recently ravaged by war, Eskel believed the region would provide ample work opportunities for a seasoned witcher like himself. Although Eskel would take over the responsibility of watching over the witcher's keep.[3]

The Wolf witcher had a menacing, metallic voice that sounded like a [dog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dog "Dog")'s barking to Ciri when she first heard it. The most prominent feature of his visage was a long, hideous, semi-circular scar running from the corner of his mouth across the length of his cheek up to the ear. It got itchy when he was nervous or embarrassed. Minus the scar and hair color, Eskel and Geralt resembled one another like true brothers. Eskel's hands emanated powerfully with magic, with more [Power](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Power "Power") than the White Wolf's. This triggered pleasant, piercing vibration in mages.[2]

Eskel was calm, patient, stable, thoughtful and polite. Even when he disagreed wholeheartedly with a topic, he feigned politeness and interest in the matter. What betrayed him in such situations was avoiding eye contact. He enjoyed drinking and laughing with friends. Quite self-aware, he had no delusions about his appearance but that didn't hinder him from smiling. His fitting, mature behavior, especially when it came to Ciri compared to the other witchers endeared him to Triss Merigold.[2] Vesemir prized Eskel's reliability.[10]

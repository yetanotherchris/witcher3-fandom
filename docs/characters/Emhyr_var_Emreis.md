**Emhyr var Emreis, Deithwen Addan yn Carn aep Morvudd** ([Nilfgaardian language](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_language "Nilfgaardian language"): _The White Flame Dancing on the Barrows of his Enemies_), also known to a few under his alias as **Duny** , the **Urcheon of Erlenwald** (Polish: _Jeż z Erlenwaldu_) was [Emperor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emperor "Emperor") of the [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire"), Lord of [Metinna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Metinna "Metinna"), [Ebbing](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ebbing "Ebbing"), [Gemmera](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gemmera "Gemmera"), and Sovereign of [Nazair](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nazair "Nazair") and [Vicovaro](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vicovaro "Vicovaro") from [1257](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1257 "Timeline") until his death sometime in the late [13th century](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#13th_century "Timeline"). He also became the [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") of [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") after marrying a [false Cirilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cirilla_Fiona_\(imposter\) "Cirilla Fiona \(imposter\)") in [1268](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1268 "Timeline").[2]

His rule of Nilfgaard was highly aggressive, often pursuing expansionist policies similar to those of his predecessors. This led to the outbreak of [two wars](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Wars "Northern Wars") against the [Northern Kingdoms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Kingdoms "Northern Kingdoms"), both of which he lost. Emhyr var Emreis was an intelligent and brilliant ruler. He chose his people well and crushed many plots against him. He was ruthless toward traitors and moved towards his goals with great determination. 

He was publicly favorable to the [Elder Races](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Races "Elder Races"), in stark contrast to monarchs of the North.

## Biography

### Childhood

At the age of 13, Emhyr's father [Fergus var Emreis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fergus_var_Emreis "Fergus var Emreis"), the emperor of Nilfgaard at the time, was deposed from his title, imprisoned and tortured after a coup led by an influential aristocrat only known as the [Usurper](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Usurper "Usurper"). 

[![Transformed into a hedgehog](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c4/Gwent_cardart_nilfgaard_braathens.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/174?cb=20200630131005)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c4/Gwent_cardart_nilfgaard_braathens.png/revision/latest?cb=20200630131005) [![Transformed into a hedgehog](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c4/Gwent_cardart_nilfgaard_braathens.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/174?cb=20200630131005)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c4/Gwent_cardart_nilfgaard_braathens.png/revision/latest?cb=20200630131005) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gwent_cardart_nilfgaard_braathens.png)

Transformed into a hedgehog

A [sorcerer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sorcerer "Sorcerer") named [Braathens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Braathens "Braathens"), under the service of the Usurper, turned Emhyr into a humanoid hedgehog form, partially as a joke, as the word "Eimyr" in Nilfgaardian dialect meant "urcheon", an old name for a hedgehog. This served as a way to break Fergus and force him to grant legitimacy to the coup, though the plan failed and the Usurper killed Emhyr's father. Having no more use for Emhyr, he was then released into the woods with [dogs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dog "Dog") set on him. Despite this, Emhyr survived and learned the sorcerer hadn't even done a thorough job on the curse: every night Emhyr regained his human form. 

In his escape, Emhyr counted on a few loyal acquaintances for help while hiding at [Ardal aep Dahy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ardal_aep_Dahy "Ardal aep Dahy")'s place. He met with an astrologer [Xarthisius](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Xarthisius "Xarthisius") who led him to the North, "beyond the [Marnadal Steps](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marnadal_Stairs "Marnadal Stairs")", to look for a cure for his curse.[2]

### Journey to Cintra

After some time, Emhyr finally arrived in the North and used the moniker "[Duny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duny "Duny"), Urcheon of [Erlenwald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Erlenwald "Erlenwald")". In [1237](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1237 "Timeline"), while prowling Cintra's forest region, he ran into a wounded and helpless King [Roegner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roegner_of_Ebbing "Roegner of Ebbing") and after saving him invoked the [Law of Surprise](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Law_of_Surprise "Law of Surprise"). 

15 years later, he presented himself before [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe") during [Pavetta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pavetta "Pavetta")'s 15th birthday and the [princess](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Princess "Princess")' would-be suitors in [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra_\(city\) "Cintra \(city\)"). Although Princess Pavetta was expected to be married off when she was 15, Duny had already started seeing her a year before and they secretly fell in love so when the [queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Queen "Queen") hosted a feast for Pavetta's birthday, he took the opportunity and announced that from her birth, the princess had belonged to him, and that he had now come for her. 

Duny then declared that Pavetta was to be his through the Law of Surprise he'd enacted when he rescued Calanthe's deceased husband, the former King Roegner. After a long and heated exchange, Duny was fooled by Calanthe into removing his helmet before midnight, exposing his monstrous form, yet Pavetta stated her approval of Duny nonetheless. This caused an uproar and the queen's vassals and the would-be suitors tried to attack Duny, with [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and [Mousesack](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mousesack "Mousesack") trying to protect Duny. However, one of the suitors managed to stab Duny and Pavetta's sorcerous abilities suddenly broke loose, sending furniture and people flying and threatening to collapse the castle itself. 

After Geralt and [Mousesack](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mousesack "Mousesack") silenced Pavetta, Duny, now presenting himself as [Prince](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prince "Prince") of [Maecht](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Maecht "Maecht") and a son of [Akerspaark](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Akerspaark "Akerspaark"), regained human form as it had struck midnight. After a discussion with Calanthe, who finally gave her approval for Duny and Pavetta to be married, Emhyr's curse was lifted. After thanking Geralt, he and his future wife were surprised with the news that Pavetta was pregnant, and Duny was soon to be a father.[3]

When the child was born, they named her [Cirilla](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"). 

### Reclaiming His heritage

In the 1250s, after Ciri's birth, the sorcerer [Vilgefortz](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vilgefortz "Vilgefortz") appeared before Duny at Cintra as an ally, wanting to acquire wealth and power only the Nilfgaardian Imperator could provide. When Vilgefortz disclosed with him the [prophecy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_White_Frost "The White Frost"), Duny decided to return to Nilfgaard to reclaim his heritage with Ciri in tow. However, under the watchful eye of Calanthe, Duny and Vilgefortz thought of a ploy to disappear without a trace by faking Pavetta's, Ciri's, and his own death on a sinking ship while Vilgefortz secretly prepared a way for them to survive. However Pavetta, aware of her husband's plans, smuggled Ciri out of the ship in time, leading to a scuffle between Pavetta and Duny as the former was pushed overboard and drowned at sea. Vilgefortz then teleported the ship from a raging maelstrom with Duny being the only survivor. Though distraught from inadvertently murdering his wife, and despite not holding genuine feelings towards her, Emhyr was successful in faking his death as "Duny" and was able to return to his homeland and lead a revolt against the Usurper. One of the first things he did was that he rewarded Xarthisius with a [tower](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Xarthisius%27_tower "Xarthisius' tower") in the capital city for his aforementioned services.[2]

Following all the power struggles around the throne, Emhyr had all of his dead political enemies disinterred and used their gravestones to pave his ballroom, earning him the nickname "_The White Flame Dancing on the Barrows of his Enemies"_ or in Elder Speech, _Deithwen Addan yn Carn aep Morvudd_.[4]

### First Northern War

In [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1263 "Timeline"), as the Emperor of Nilfgaard, Emhyr commenced the [First Nilfgaard-Nordling War](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/First_Nilfgaard_War "First Nilfgaard War") with the aim of expanding his empire and began by [defeating](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Marnadal "Battle of Marnadal") the [Cintran Army](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintran_Army "Cintran Army") at the [Marnadal Stairs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marnadal_Stairs "Marnadal Stairs") and holding [a siege against Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Slaughter_of_Cintra "Slaughter of Cintra"), where his daughter was held. While Nilfgaard's forces invade Cintra, Ciri managed to escape and Emhyr soon sent his agents to locate and capture her.[5]

After several successful campaigns against the North, Emhyr's invasion was put to a halt after the utter defeat of his forces during the [Second Battle of Sodden Hill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Sodden_Hill "Battle of Sodden Hill"), marking the end of the First Nilfgaard-Nordling War. In response, Emhyr had the commanders that failed him on Sodden either executed or replaced by younger and more determined officials to lead his armies next time around while readying his forces for the [next major war](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Second_Nilfgaard_War "Second Nilfgaard War").[5]

### The search for Ciri

After the war, in [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1267 "Timeline"), Emhyr received a report from Field Marshall [Menno Coehoorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Menno_Coehoorn "Menno Coehoorn") that the rulers of the North - specifically [Vizimir II](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizimir_II "Vizimir II") of [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania"), [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") of [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria"), [Demavend III](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Demavend_III "Demavend III") of [Aedirn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Aedirn "Aedirn"), [Henselt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Henselt "Henselt") of [Kaedwen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaedwen "Kaedwen") and [Meve](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meve "Meve") of [Lyria and Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lyria_and_Rivia "Lyria and Rivia") \- had a secret council at a castle in [Hagge](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hagge "Hagge"). Emhyr tasked the field marshall to disperse this information among the [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") and members of the [Chapter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Chapter_of_the_Gift_and_the_Art "Chapter of the Gift and the Art"). Receiving no further news regarding [Rience](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rience "Rience"), Emhyr asked Coehoorn on his thoughts about Cintra. Coehoorn described it as 'gloomy' to which Emhyr followed by stating that it was different once and would be different again, promising Coehoorn a 'beautiful, happy' Cintra, though he would leave governorship to someone else, and wanted Coehoorn stationed at [Dol Angra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dol_Angra "Dol Angra") after he had quashed the [rebellion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra_rebellion "Cintra rebellion") within Cintra. At Dol Angra, Emhyr tasked Coehoorn to not allow himself and his fellow officers to be provoked by Meve or Demavend's attacks and only allow themselves to be attack immediately when ordered to do so. Before the marshall could leave, a messenger walked in to report to Emhyr the success of suppressing the rebellion - despite a few escapees - and the capture of its leader, [Duke](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duke "Duke") [Windhalm of Attre](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Windhalm_of_Attre "Windhalm of Attre"). Pleased by the news, Emhyr ordered Windhalm's public and 'spectacular' execution as an example to frighten others and demanded Coehoorn's presence there as [Governor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Governor "Governor") of Cintra and proxy for the emperor. Furthermore, Emhyr - impatient for Rience's report - tasked Coehoorn to have his mages set up a telecommunication link to Redania and eventually Rience, commanding him to immediately dispose of the witcher Geralt while leaving the sorceress [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") untouched. Afterward, a [count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") by the name of [Cahir](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cahir_Mawr_Dyffryn_aep_Ceallach "Cahir Mawr Dyffryn aep Ceallach"), appeared before the emperor and the field marshall. Emhyr reminded the count of his blunder involving capturing Ciri two years prior, and the consequences he would face if he repeated it as he was being provided with a chance for redemption. Commanding the attention of both officers, Emhyr tasked them with an important mission. They learnt that the Emperor decided to arrange what would go down as the [Thanedd coup](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_coup "Thanedd coup").[5]

He knew Ciri would be on the [Thanedd Island](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thanedd_Island "Thanedd Island") and so he gave Cahir a second chance to capture her for him. Cahir, however, failed a second time and Ciri escaped through [Benavent's portal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Benavent%27s_portal "Benavent's portal") on the highest floor of [Tor Lara](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tor_Lara "Tor Lara"). Around this time, Emhyr learned that Vilgefortz betrayed him, when in fact he was never on his side and had been playing his own game. Thus, Emhyr ordered for Ciri, Vilgefortz and Cahir to be sought out by his [Secret Service](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Secret_Service "Nilfgaardian Secret Service"). Soon, a detachment led by half-elf [Schirrú](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Schirr%C3%BA "Schirrú") brought him Ciri, but when he saw her, he realized that he was given an imposter. Nevertheless, he pretended to believe it, while still having his secret police seek out Ciri with the ongoing war being in favor of the Empire. Also, as it turned out, a man he sent out to find Ciri, [Stefan Skellen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stefan_Skellen "Stefan Skellen"), was a conspirator who wanted to overthrow the Emperor and initiate a coup in Nilfgaard. At the time, Aedirn and Lyria and Rivia were already defeated, with only Kaedwen, Temeria, and Redania standing in the Empire's way.[6]

After a short time, both Geralt and Emhyr found Vilgefortz and Ciri at [Stygga Castle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stygga_Castle "Stygga Castle"). Skellen, who worked with Vilgefortz, was captured by [Impera Brigade](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Impera_Brigade "Impera Brigade") officers who arrived at the castle together with their Emperor. At a meeting, Geralt alone had discovered that Emhyr and Duny are merely two names for the same person. Emhyr then told Geralt about his plans. Hearing about his incestuous plans with Ciri, just because of [some prophecy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ithlinne%27s_Prophecy "Ithlinne's Prophecy"), Geralt calls the emperor a monster and asks him not to delay his and Yennefer's death. By order of Emhyr, the witcher and the sorceress were given time to stay together, while he left the castle with Ciri. As Ciri leaves the castle, she breaks down in inconsolable tears over the loss of her adoptive parents. Seeing his daughter cry so heartbroken makes Emhyr realize he broke his promise to Yennefer not to make her cry. This causes Emhyr to abandon his plan with the prophecy and decide to release her, but not before seeing and embracing her without directly telling her he is her father. Finally, he leaves without her, ordering his troops not to touch her as they depart, allowing Ciri to go to Gerart and Yennefer before they attempt suicide. 

Parallel to these events is the decisive [battle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battle_of_Brenna "Battle of Brenna") of the Second War with Nilfgaard at [Brenna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Brenna "Brenna"), where the army of Nilfgaard loses to the combined forces of the North under the leadership of [Constable](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Constable "Constable") [Jan Natalis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jan_Natalis "Jan Natalis"). But the war still ended successfully for the Empire: all the lands south of Yaruga, including Cintra, were left under the banner of the Golden Sun. To achieve this end, Emhyr married the false Ciri that Schirrú had brought him, who he grew to love. As a result, an agreement was signed in the capital of the conquered country, which became known as the [Peace of Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_of_Cintra "Peace of Cintra").[2]

### Assassins of kings

In 1271, a mysterious group of witcher murderers killed Demavend III and Foltest, with a witcher Geralt being blamed for the latter's assassination. This group called themselves the [Kingslayers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kingslayers "Kingslayers") and were witchers of the [School of the Viper](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/School_of_the_Viper "School of the Viper"), led by [Letho of Gulet](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letho_of_Gulet "Letho of Gulet"). When Letho and Geralt finally met face to face, the Emperor was revealed as Letho's patron, having supported and funded the regicides as a precursor to his plans to destabilize then invade the Northern Kingdoms. As an incentive, he had promised Letho a land within his Empire where Witchers could be free to live without the hatred and prejudice they typically experience. He never fulfilled this, however.[7]

In 1272, Emhyr and his troops crossed the Yaruga for the third time and, due to chaos caused by the Kingslayers, quickly conquered Temeria, Aedirn, Lyria and Rivia, as well as part of Kaedwen up to the source of the [Lixela](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lixela "Lixela"). The remainder of his enemies, Redanians, Temerians, Aedirnians, Kaedwenis and others, gathered under leadership of King [Radovid V](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") of Redania, who managed to stop the Nilfgaardian Army on the [Pontar river](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pontar_\(river\) "Pontar \(river\)"). Emperor Emhyr, certain of a quick and sure victory, started to call himself Ruler of the North and South. However, his war was not popular with Nilfgaard proper, leading to trade corporations and scheming nobles to begin plotting against him, which would force Emhyr to deliver a swift victory, lest his subjects rebel against him. He also decided to offer his daughter, Ciri, the throne as his successor. However, he required help finding her. Having previously captured Yennefer along with the witchers of the Viper School, he made her and Geralt an offer in exchange for them finding Ciri. 

After Geralt chased down leads in [Velen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Velen "Velen"), [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), and [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), Emhyr sent his troops to retrieve him for a progress report. The witcher had brought with him [Uma](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Avallac%27h "Avallac'h"). Geralt explained to the emperor that he and Yennefer suspected that the creature may be a transformed Ciri or maybe the key to finding her. Emhyr turned to Yennefer, asking if she was able to lift the curse on Uma. She stated that she believed she could, and Geralt added a phrase in Elder Speech that Ciri told [Dandelion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dandelion "Dandelion"), which would be key to accomplishing it. 

When Geralt chose to seek help against [Eredin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eredin "Eredin"), he spoke with Emhyr, explaining that he had found Ciri's location but, as soon as he would get to her, the Wild Hunt would attack, so he created a plan to bring the battle to [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen"). Emhyr agreed to lend Geralt Nilfgaardian troops to help with the battle, but only if [Morvran Voorhis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morvran_Voorhis "Morvran Voorhis") would lead them. Unwilling to allow this, Geralt backed down, and the two parted ways. Later on, a second battle against the Wild Hunt ensued, taking place on the shores of [Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik"). This time, Geralt was more or less forced to agree with Nilfgaardian support and was given an army under the leadership of [Duke var Attre](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duke_var_Attre "Duke var Attre").[8]

_If Radovid or Dijkstra rule the North (default):_ Emhyr is assassinated by internal conspirators for losing the war. 

_If Radovid and Dijkstra are killed:_ Emhyr takes over the North and wipes out all those who opposed him. 

_If he won the war and Ciri visited him and survived:_ Emhyr abdicates the throne to his daughter.

### Later reign

In 1290, Emhyr granted a title of an imperial treasurer of the crown to his confidant, [Peter Evertsen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peter_Evertsen "Peter Evertsen"). In the final period of rule, he even further extended Peter's prerogatives by raising him to the rank of coadjutor of the Empire, which could suggest he was no longer able to be the sole ruler of the Empire. He eventually died no later than 1301.[6]

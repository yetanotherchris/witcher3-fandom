**Bran Tuirseach** was jarl of [An Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Skellig "An Skellig") who ruled as the [King of the Skellige Isles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King_of_the_Skellige_Isles "King of the Skellige Isles") in the 1250s before the ascension of his brother [Eist Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eist_Tuirseach "Eist Tuirseach") and again from [1267](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1267 "Timeline/Games") until [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"). 

Though initially a marriage of his nephew [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite") and Princess [Pavetta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pavetta "Pavetta") had been planned, King Bran managed to form an alliance with [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") by marrying Eist off to Queen [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe") in [1252](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1252 "Timeline"). With crown passed to Eist, the bond elevated into a personal union. 

After an interregnum following King Eist's death, Bran regained the iron crown of [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") and ruled for a few more years. Once he felt too old, Bran set out to hunt a [bear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear "Bear") and died. 

## Biography

### Early life

Born in [1192](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1192 "Timeline/Games"),[N 1] Bran had two siblings, brother [Eist Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eist_Tuirseach "Eist Tuirseach") and a [sister](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach%27s_mother "Crach's mother") who later gave birth to his nephew [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite").[2] As a member of [Clan Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tuirseach "Clan Tuirseach"), Bran eventually inherited the title of the jarl of [An Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Skellig "An Skellig") and thereby the clan's leadership. 

After the previous [Konung](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King_of_the_Skellige_Isles "King of the Skellige Isles") died, Bran submitted his candidacy and proved himself worthy by slaying a [dragon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dragon "Dragon") named [Evren](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Evren "Evren"). The display of valor won the hearts of the jarls, and Bran was given the crown.[1]

### Primary reign

King Bran united the [clans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clans_of_Skellige "Clans of Skellige") through the way unknown to his predecessors: compromise. He invited his rivals to banquets and with sweet words, coin, and barrels of mead persuaded most to join his side.[3] During one such feast, warrior [Uve Jabberjaw](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Uve_Jabberjaw "Uve Jabberjaw") got drunk and insulted the king. Angered, Bran let Uve live but ordered him to tear out his tongue and therefore retain honor. 

At some point, Bran had met the love of his life [Birna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Birna "Birna") who, although not his first nor last lover, had always been closest to him and had become the mother of his firstborn son, [Svanrige Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Svanrige_Tuirseach "Svanrige Tuirseach").[1] To keep rivals in check, King Bran was aided by his wife, who told him every suspicious rumor or deed that she had noticed during the feasts.[3] Aside from her, King Bran had [druid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid") [Mousesack](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mousesack "Mousesack") as an advisor and confidant. His brother Eist who by then rose to be the [jarl of Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jarl_of_Skellige "Jarl of Skellige"), commander of the armed forces, was an advisor as well.[2]

When [Queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Queen "Queen") [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe") of [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra") set up [Pavetta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pavetta "Pavetta")'s 15th birthday feast in [1252](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1252 "Timeline"), would-be suitors arrived. Bran sent his nephew Crach an Craite with Eist, Mousesack and [Draig Bon-Dhu](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Draig_Bon-Dhu "Draig Bon-Dhu") as an entourage but the event turned out unexpectedly. While Crach didn't win Pavetta's hand, Eist married the queen.[2] Bran was pleased with the result as it seated the remote islands at the same table as the mightiest dynasties of the [Continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent").[4]

### Pause and continuous reign

Despite his wife's repeated requests, the King refused to champion their son Svanrige as rightful heir to the throne. He perceived the concept of hereditary monarchy as preposterous and believed that the boy would earn the crown himself was he worthy.[3] By [1257](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1257 "Timeline"), the crown has been bequeathed to Eist while Crach became the new jarl of Skellige.[5] The reign of his brother and Queen Calanthe, titled _Ard Rhena_ (High Queen) on Skellige, lasted for over ten years until they died fighting the [Nilfgaardian Empire](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire").[6]

After a brief interregnum during which Jarl Crach an Craite was effectively the steward of the Isles, leading the Skelligers against the Black Ones,[7] Bran was reinstated as the king.[8] For the next few years, Bran continued to rule as before, keeping the clans under him and leading them on profitable raids. Nevertheless, he never united the jarls as one massive fleet which could more seriously threaten Nilfgaard.[1]

### Last hunt

Bran on his final voyage.

Bran lived long and reached 80 years of age in [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"). When his hands began to tremble and his ideas wandered off mid-sentence, Bran decided to go into the woods of An Skellig to hunt a great [bear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bear "Bear") armed with only a knife and dressed in delicate cloth, leaving the [world](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/World "World") on his own terms.[3]

His body was put on a [drakkar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drakkar "Drakkar") in [Kaer Trolde Harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor") by Tuirseach warriors with all items he could need in an afterlife. When everybody bid him farewell, [his young concubine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran%27s_unidentified_concubine "Bran's unidentified concubine") willingly lay next to his body as the ship was set out to sea and set ablaze by his nephew, Jarl Crach. One shepherd sacrificed his best mare for the king, while a group of An Craite warriors offered Bran their manes, swearing to remain bald until a new king had been chosen.[1]

### Legacy

[![The specter of Bran confronts Uve Jabberjaw and his companions.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3e/Tw3_cave_of_dreams_Bran.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/215?cb=20160822104511)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3e/Tw3_cave_of_dreams_Bran.png/revision/latest?cb=20160822104511) [![The specter of Bran confronts Uve Jabberjaw and his companions.](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3e/Tw3_cave_of_dreams_Bran.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/215?cb=20160822104511)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/3/3e/Tw3_cave_of_dreams_Bran.png/revision/latest?cb=20160822104511) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_cave_of_dreams_Bran.png)

The specter of Bran confronts Uve Jabberjaw and his companions.

On the day of his burial, a wooden bust of Bran with detailed trimming was carved out by the harbor's artisans to be put up on the shore of [Larvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Larvik "Larvik") where, the islanders believed, its fierce gaze would protect them from Nilfgaard. At the wake in [Kaer Trolde citadel](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_citadel "Kaer Trolde citadel") that ensued straight after the funeral, Bran was recalled as a hero by all present. [Holger Blackhand](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Holger_Blackhand "Holger Blackhand") remembered him fondly for keeping Birna in line and hoped for the next king to be his equal. With toasts raised to his memory, there were still likes of [Cerys an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cerys_an_Craite "Cerys an Craite") who felt Bran did little to resolve old quarrels. 

To prove himself a worthy candidate to the crown, [Blueboy Lugos](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blueboy_Lugos "Blueboy Lugos") set out to best his fears in the [Cave of Dreams](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cave_of_Dreams "Cave of Dreams"). He took along [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and two raiders, Uve Jabberjaw and [Jorulf the Wolverine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jorulf_the_Wolverine "Jorulf the Wolverine"). Inside, Uve saw specters of Tuirseach warriors and Bran. As his fear of insulting Bran once more manifested, Uve had to defeat the ghost warriors before they could proceed.[1]

Inspired by their visit to the isles sometime prior to [1275](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1275 "Timeline/Games"), two brothers from [Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), Counts [Igor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Igor_Monnier "Igor Monnier") and [Martin Monnier](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Martin_Monnier "Martin Monnier"), invented a [Skellige Gwent deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige_Gwent_deck "Skellige Gwent deck") and commemorated Bran on a leader card.[9]

Unlike the other jarls and konungs of Skellige, Bran knew how to use compromise to his advantage. In his early years, hospitality and friendly demeanor enabled him to easily win over any opponent and turn him into his vassal.[3] However, by the end of his reign, memory began to fail the elderly Konung and he could no longer unite the clans into one great force which may seriously endanger to the Nilfgaardian Empire.[1]

## Notes

  * In _[A Fractured Land: Tales of the Northern Realms](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Fractured_Land:_Tales_of_the_Northern_Realms "A Fractured Land: Tales of the Northern Realms")_ , a sentence above Bran's entry mistakenly attributes the cognomen of "the Conquerer" to him. This, however, not only doesn't fit with the character of his reign but also refers to [another Tuirseach ruler](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran_Tuirseach_\(the_Conqueror\) "Bran Tuirseach \(the Conqueror\)") mentioned in the 3rd game.

## Trivia

  * In Middle Welsh, _brân_ means "crow". 
    * Within the universe, his name may derive from the [Elder Speech](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Elder_Speech "Elder Speech") word _breinne_ which aptly translates to "royal".[1]

## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

**Vigi the Loon** was a boatwright and [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") warrior from [Clan Tordarroch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Tordarroch "Clan Tordarroch") that joined [Hjalmar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hjalmar_an_Craite "Hjalmar an Craite")'s expedition to kill the [ice giant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ice_giant "Ice giant") [Myrhyff](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Myrhyff "Myrhyff") in hopes of changing his nickname to "the Unfearing". 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What was Vigi the Loon's original profession before becoming a warrior?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Vigi the Loon, prior to his warrior life, was a skilled boatwright. He is a part of Clan Tordarroch, renowned for his swordsmanship. He is also known for participating in Hjalmar's expedition to defeat the ice giant Myrhyff. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Why did Vigi the Loon join Hjalmar's expedition to kill the ice giant Myrhyff?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Craving glory and driven by the desire to protect his home isle from the Ice Giant, Vigi the Loon convinced Hjalmar to embark on an expedition to Undvik to kill the ice giant Myrhyff. However, the journey turned perilous, resulting in Vigi becoming a captive in the Ice Giant's cave. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What was the nickname Vigi the Loon was hoping to earn?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Vigi, a Skellige warrior, aimed to be known as 'Vigi the Unfearing'. Yet, his daring actions on an expedition to Undvik to protect his home from the Ice Giant earned him the title 'Vigi the Loon', a nickname he embraced. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What actions led to Vigi earning the nickname 'the Loon'?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The nickname 'the Loon' was bestowed upon Vigi following his audacious actions. He persuaded Hjalmar to embark on an expedition to Undvik to protect their homeland from an Ice Giant. This perilous journey led to numerous crew fatalities and Vigi's imprisonment in the Ice Giant's cave. Upon his release by Geralt, Vigi's conduct affirmed his moniker. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## Journal entry

    _Even for Skelligers, who value courage above all else, Vigi's fearlessness went far beyond the pale of bravery, deep into the realm of suicidal madness. This Clan Tordarroch warrior had strived to earn the right to be called Vigi the Unfearing. Instead, his actions earned him the nickname Vigi the Loon – and it seemed this outcome suited him just fine._
    _It was Vigi who, craving glory and wanting to save his home isle from the Ice Giant, had convinced Hjalmar to brave the journey to[Undvik](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Undvik "Undvik"). Many of their crew were killed during this expedition, and Vigi himself wound up a prisoner in the Ice Giant's cave._
    _The second[Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") freed Vigi from his cage, the Skelliger lived up to his moniker and awoke the sleeping giant with a hearty kick. To be fair, afterwards he fought bravely and effectively against the [monster](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster"), proving he had the making of a mighty warrior behind his madness._
    _That was not the last time Vigi an Geralt fought side by side. Vigi answered Geralt's call and went with Hjalmar to[Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen") to fight the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt"). Though once again he fought with skill and courage, this time he fell in battle._
    _News of his heroic death reached Skellige, where in ballads he has earned the title that eluded him in life: Vigi the Unfearing._

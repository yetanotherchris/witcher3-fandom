### Quick Answers

**What is the significance of the School of the Cat in Gaetan's life?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Gaetan, a member of the School of the Cat, faced a life-threatening situation when the people he was safeguarding attempted to kill him post the execution of a perilous contract. Consequently, he retaliated, leading to their deaths. Furthermore, Gaetan fought valiantly with his comrade, Cedric, in a bid to defend their school, but met his end at the hands of soldiers.

**What happened to the School of the Cat in 1272?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The School of the Cat suffered a major blow in 1272 when witchers Axel and Cedric were killed during a caravan attack, with the fate of witcher Schrödinger left uncertain. Certain members were also banned from witcher resting places like Kaer Morhen. The decline continued in 1274 with Lexandre's betrayal, marked by a murder and theft of the Grandmaster's diag. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Who was Joël and what was his relationship with Gaetan?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Joël, a Witcher from the School of the Cat, corresponded with Gaetan via letters. He informed Gaetan about the hostile takeover of their school by soldiers, leading to the demise of Axel and Cedric. He was unsure about the status of Schrödinger. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**How did Gaetan navigate his life after the downfall of the School of the Cat?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Post the School of the Cat's downfall, Gaetan, a witcher of the same school, faced controversy for killing those he had pledged to safeguard. This incident transpired when they deceitfully tried to end his life following the completion of a perilous contract. Gaetan was saved from execution, not by judicial intervention, but by Geralt, a fellow witcher. His subsequent life remains unspecified.

## Biography

As a child, Gaetan had a younger sister, and even after being taken to the School of the Cat and becoming a witcher, he still remembered her fondly. 

While on the path, Gaetan received a [letter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Letter_to_Gaetan "Letter to Gaetan") from fellow witcher [Joël](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jo%C3%ABl "Joël"). It stated that the School of the Cat had been overrun by soldiers, leaving at least two witchers dead and the fate of another uncertain. Joël advised Gaetan to avoid cities or main roads, as bounties had been placed on both their heads. Nevertheless, Gaetan ventured into [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") to purchase something from [Marcus T.K. Hodgson](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Marcus_T.K._Hodgson "Marcus T.K. Hodgson"), giving him a [conserved shaelmaar trophy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Conserved_shaelmaar_trophy "Conserved shaelmaar trophy") as collateral. 

One day, Gaetan accepted a contract to eliminate a [leshen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leshen "Leshen") that was terrorizing the village of [Honorton](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Honorton "Honorton") in Velen. After successfully killing the creature, the village's ealdorman refused to pay the agreed-upon sum, offering Gaetan only 12 crowns—a paltry amount for such a dangerous task. Gaetan protested, and the ealdorman, along with a few other villagers, claimed they had hidden the rest of the payment in the barn, leading him there to retrieve it. 

However, this was a trap. The group attempted to murder Gaetan, and he narrowly avoided being fatally impaled by a pitchfork at the last moment. Wounded and enraged by the betrayal, Gaetan went on a rampage, killing every villager in sight, whether they were involved in the plot or not. He only spared a little girl, [Millie](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Millie "Millie"), as she reminded him of his sister, making her the sole survivor of the massacre. 

_If Geralt investigates:_ [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") eventually found Gaetan not far from the village, still recovering from his injuries. After hearing Gaetan's account of the events, Geralt implied that this might not have been the first time something like this had happened. Gaetan, however, refused to confess to anything else. 

    _If Geralt chooses to spare him:_ Geralt reflected on his own experience, often being referred to as the [Butcher of Blaviken](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Butcher_of_Blaviken "Butcher of Blaviken"), and noted that sometimes "heads just roll." Given that the ealdorman did try to kill Gaetan, Geralt couldn't fully blame him for acting in self-defense. Gaetan, grateful, thanked Geralt and provided him with the location of his hideout, where he had some valuable items stored as a reward.

    _If Geralt chooses to kill him:_ Deciding that Gaetan's regrets were insufficient for the atrocity he committed, Geralt fought and killed him on the spot, ending the cat witcher's life.

## Notes

  * If you approach Gaetan while wearing the [Cat School Witcher Gear](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cat_School_Gear "Cat School Gear"), he will comment on it before you engage him in conversation. Saying "Well well, what have we here? Feline armor, wolf's head medallion - a crossbreed?".
  * In Gaetan's hideout, he has a wall of trophies he collected which Geralt wonders why he still has, and believes he may have been cheated of his reward in the past and kept them as mementos.

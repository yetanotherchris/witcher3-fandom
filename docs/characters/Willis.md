**Willis** had been the local [armorer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Armorer "Armorer") of [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard") for 50 years when one night, in [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), an unknown [arsonist](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Napp "Napp") burned his [workshop](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Willis%27_forge "Willis' forge") down. 

## Biography

When [Temeria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temeria "Temeria") fell and [Nilfgaardian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") soldiers began to occupy White Orchard, Willis treated them like any other customer, taking any work they gave him as long as they gave him the necessary supplies for it. Having no strong loyalty feelings for one side or the other, many of the locals began to despise the [dwarf](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Dwarf "Dwarf"), incorrectly believing he was profiting off their misery and soon culminated into his workshop being burned down. 

_If Geralt helps him out:_ Willis offered what little he had left if the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") could help him find the arsonist and bring him to justice. 

    _If Geralt takes Napp's bribe:_ Unfortunately for Willis, Geralt couldn't find the culprit.

    _If Geralt turns in Napp:_ Willis berated Napp, whose late mother the armorer never charged for his services. Having gotten fed up with the locals, he then called over the Nilfgaardians to turn Napp in and who was summarily hung for his crimes. However, this deepened the rift between the locals so much that they began to travel half a day's ride to another armorer to have work done, even though the other armorer did shoddy work.

## Biography

Sometime prior to 1270, Thaler was already the head of Temerian Intelligence, where he helped with various spy activities as well as more unusual tasks, such as sending some of his men out to hunt down [catoblepas](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Catoblepas "Catoblepas") for their [meat](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Catoblepas_meat "Catoblepas meat"), as it was [Adda the White](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Adda_the_White "Adda the White")'s favorite dish.[1]

During this time a young [Shani](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Shani "Shani") happened to be in [Vizima](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima "Vizima") and, taking a liking to her, Thaler asked her out, but hid who he really was. However, one night while they wandered the city, Shani noticed two spies following them and eventually the inept men accidentally revealed that Thaler was, in fact, the head of Temerian Intelligence, ending their short relationship.[1]

### Meeting Geralt

Eventually in 1270, Thaler began to pose as a fence in the city to gather intel and was visited by [Coleman](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Coleman "Coleman"), who sold him some [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") equipment he'd found. Thaler eventually sold this off to the guards, not realizing it'd soon lead to him crossing paths with the witcher [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), who was also looking for the [owner of the sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Berengar "Berengar"). During this time, [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Foltest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Foltest "Foltest") also left the city for a bit and personally put Thaler in charge while he was away. 

Shortly after, Geralt visited and, after Thaler revealed what he knew about Berengar, the two parted ways only to meet up again later during an important reception for high class society. However, this time Thaler was now using his real identity, Bernard, and revealed to Geralt who he really was. He eventually asked a favor for a favor and had Geralt retrieve a mysterious letter, not revealing he believed someone was forging documents as Foltest. With the newest letter in hand though he was finally able to confirm it, as the letter was supposedly from Foltest stripping Thaler of his position as chief but was missing the king's signature. 

As the party winded down, Thaler went outside but soon got into an argument with [Roderick de Wett](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roderick_de_Wett "Roderick de Wett"), and while the [count](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Count "Count") remarked Thaler was just mad about being fired, Thaler noted de Wett knew more than he was letting on. 

_If Geralt defends Thaler:_ Thanks to the witcher stepping in, Thaler was saved from being killed by de Wett and his men. He then asked Geralt to meet him back in the [inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_New_Narakort "The New Narakort") to talk and Thaler revealed all the evidence he'd gathered on the forged documents. 

_If Geralt kills him or walks away:_ With no one else to protect him and outnumbered, Thaler was quickly killed. _Game continuation:_ Note that this is one of the few times in the games where your choice has no effect and Thaler will appear alive in the third game, even if killed here and you imported saves all the way through.[1]

### Third Nilfgaardian War

After [Nilfgaard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") invaded Temeria in late [1271](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1271 "Timeline/Games"), they quickly dismantled the kingdom's secret service and Thaler barely escaped with his life. Not knowing what else to do, he proceeded to drink away his sorrows until his "liver screamed bloody murder" then decided he had to do something about the situation. Shortly thereafter he got into contact with [Vernon Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vernon_Roche "Vernon Roche") and [Sigismund Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra") and the three decided something extremely drastic had to be done: King [Radovid V](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") of [Redania](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Redania "Redania") had to die.[2]

Using his experience as Temeria's former spymaster, he began to recruit new spies and took on the role of a traveling cobbler by the name of **Clogs** , noting that cobblers didn't attract any attention while still being able to see and hear all the people around them while they worked. Beginning plans for Radovid's murder, he planned to meet up with one of his co-conspirators, though was left waiting. He travelled to [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard"), and used an old lady living on the [Ismena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ismena "Ismena")'s hut and pan-soot to write [letters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Burned_papers "Burned papers") to them, forgetting his [monocle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Silver_monocle "Silver monocle") in the process.[2]

_If Geralt takes part in[A Deadly Plot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Deadly_Plot "A Deadly Plot"):_ While relatively safe from being harassed by soldiers, Thaler found himself instead attacked by a group of [rock trolls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rock_troll "Rock troll") who wanted shoes of their own. Geralt eventually found and extracted Thaler from them and Thaler made it safely to the [Seven Cats Inn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Seven_Cats_Inn "Seven Cats Inn"). 

    _If Geralt takes part in[Reason of State](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reason_of_State "Reason of State"):_ He later met up with his co-conspirators to tell them of the information they'd been waiting to hear about and included Geralt in on their plans, who was needed to lure Radovid off his ship and into an ambush. As Thaler and Dijkstra weren't very adept fighters like Geralt, Vernon, and [Ves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ves "Ves"), he and Dijkstra remained at the [butcher's yard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Butcher%27s_Yard_theater "The Butcher's Yard theater") in [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad") to wait on the outcome of the attempted assassination. 

    _If Geralt protects the Temerian patriots:_ Thanks to Geralt, Thaler survived Dijkstra's betrayal and was able to continue negotiations with Nilfgaard for Temeria to become a vassal state to the Empire and made plans to return to Vizima once [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") cleared out.
    _If Geralt lets Dijkstra kill the Temerian patriots:_ Unfortunately Thaler didn't foresee Redania's former spymaster turning on them and was subsequently killed with Vernon and Ves.

## _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_

During Chapter II, Thaler can be found in his [house](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thaler%27s_place "Thaler's place") and sometimes walking around through the streets of the [Temple Quarter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_Quarter "Temple Quarter"). His house doubles as his shop, in the southeastern section of the Temple Quarter, just around the corner from the [dungeon](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vizima_dungeon "Vizima dungeon"). There he can be called upon to buy and sell a variety of goods. He also plays [dice](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_dice_poker "The Witcher dice poker") and offers a [quiet drinking atmosphere](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drinking_contest "Drinking contest") and a free [place to stay](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Meditation "Meditation"). 

Thaler will buy and sell: 

  * [weapons](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:Weapons "Category:Weapons") of any kind
  * [weapon upgrades](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blade_enhancement "Blade enhancement")
  * [jewelry](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Jewelry "Jewelry")
  * some [books and scrolls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Book_prices "Book prices") (he will only sell these)
  * [flint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flint "Flint")

## Trivia

  * Both Thaler's real and fake name reference currency. A ducat and thaler (also spelled "taler") were coins once used throughout Europe.
  * "To swear like a cobbler" is idiom in Polish (_"kląć jak szewc"_) and several other Slavic tongues for unrestrained profanity, comparable to "swear like a sailor" in English.

## Notes

  * Initially Thaler's role in _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ was supposed to be much smaller. After the team decided to cut out half of the characters during development though, Thaler inherited the role of the removed spymaster, as the idea of an educated man pretending to be a fence was considered a very interesting idea for a character.[3]
  * Only in the English version of _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ does he switch accents (from lower class to upper class).
  * Regardless of whether or not he died in _[The Witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_\(game\) "The Witcher \(game\)")_ , he still plays a role in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , even with saves imported throughout the series.
  * In _[The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_2:_Assassins_of_Kings "The Witcher 2: Assassins of Kings")_ , one of Thaler's agents will deliver a package to Geralt near the market square in [Flotsam](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flotsam "Flotsam") and contains [Dragon's Dream formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Formula:_Dragon%27s_dream "Formula: Dragon's dream"). 
    * If Thaler was saved in the first game, the package may also contain [The Arachas - A Study](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Arachas_-_A_Study "The Arachas - A Study") and [Reinforced leather enhancement](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Reinforced_leather_enhancement "Reinforced leather enhancement").
  * In the third game, it's implied that he's the stranger that borrowed the [pan](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Frying_pan "Frying pan") during the secondary quest [A Frying Pan, Spick and Span](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Frying_Pan,_Spick_and_Span "A Frying Pan, Spick and Span").
  * In the third game, he has a [gwent card](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Thaler_\(gwent_card\) "Thaler \(gwent card\)") that's part of the [Northern Realms deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Realms_Gwent_deck "Northern Realms Gwent deck").

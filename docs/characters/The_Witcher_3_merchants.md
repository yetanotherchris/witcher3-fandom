# The Witcher 3 merchants

[![Gwent cardart syndicate townsfolk](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/77/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_townsfolk.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/249?cb=20190629235857)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/7/77/Gwent_cardart_syndicate_townsfolk.png/revision/latest?cb=20190629235857) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gwent_cardart_syndicate_townsfolk.png)

**Merchants** in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ can buy and sell: [food and drinks](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_food_and_drink "The Witcher 3 food and drink"), all [alchemical ingredients](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_ingredients "Category:The Witcher 3 ingredients") (except [loot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Loot "Loot") from [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_bestiary "The Witcher 3 bestiary") such as [mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens") and [trophies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_trophies "Category:The Witcher 3 trophies")), and [junk items](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_junk "The Witcher 3 junk"). 

Other (junk) items can be sold to merchants at higher prices compared to other types of shopkeepers, except [keys](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Category:The_Witcher_3_keys "Category:The Witcher 3 keys") and [currencies](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Currency "Currency"). Most merchants have preferred items and will offer higher prices for them, such as blacksmiths preferring weapons, armor, and runestones. Merchants of the same type have a few set tiers for purchasing your gear, ranging from offering 20% to ~41.5% of an item's face value. Generally, but not always, merchants in villages on the continent offer the least, those in large cities (those you cannot gallop within) offer the most, and Skellige merchants typically offer 35% of the item's face value. 

Waiting or meditating for 5 whole days will refresh a merchant's cash supply.

## Bugs

  * All merchants' (regardless of type) icons stay on the map at the last place where they are seen, even if they are no longer present at the location. In most cases, this is fine as it resets when they reappear or are seen at another location, but there are some who never appear again, yet have their icon permanently displayed. 
    * For wandering merchants in particular, if loading a save that has [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") near one of them, a second icon will appear on the map, at the position where the merchant is loaded from the save, and there doesn't seem to be a way to get rid of the false one.
  * Some merchants (and particularly all wandering ones) have different auto-generated appearances each time they're met, likely for a wider variety effect. However, sometimes they may change appearances instantly upon being interacted with and, when that happens, they get broken and trading with them is impossible. This can be fixed by meditating for a couple of hours, but only temporarily and it may happen again the next time they're met.

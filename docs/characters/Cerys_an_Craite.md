**Cerys an Craite** , aka "Sparrowhawk", was the daughter of [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite"), and [Hjalmar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hjalmar "Hjalmar")'s younger sister. The young woman was a courageous [Skelliger](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") like her father and had inherited all his stubbornness. She was very self-confident and always ready to prove she could fulfill any task as good as a man, which caused her and Hjalmar to have a constant, but good-natured, rivalry. However, unlike her brother, she appeared more levelheaded, choosing to investigate and think things through first before jumping to conclusions. 

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What is the relationship between Cerys an Craite and Hjalmar?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Cerys an Craite and Hjalmar an Craite, characters from the Witcher 3: Wild Hunt, are siblings and the children of Crach an Craite. In the game's narrative, the player as Geralt can assist either Cerys or Hjalmar in their bid to rule Skellige. Hjalmar, the elder sibling, mirrors his father in many ways. Cerys, on the other hand, is a brave islander, inheriting her father's stubbornness and constantly striving to demonstrate her capabilities. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What does the name 'Cerys' mean?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Cerys is a Welsh name that signifies 'love' or 'loved one'. In the Polish context, she is affectionately called 'Przepióreczka', a term that translates to 'Quail'. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What are the consequences of helping Cerys or Hjalmar in their quests?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Assisting Cerys leads to her crowning as Queen of Skellige, focusing on the Isles' prosperity. Helping Hjalmar results in him becoming King, intensifying raids against Nilfgaard. Cerys commits to helping you after the coronation, and if she is queen, she offers Hjalmar's assistance.

## Biography

In [1272](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline/Games#1272 "Timeline/Games"), eager to prove her merit as the worth successor to the throne, Cerys sailed to [Spikeroog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Spikeroog "Spikeroog") to free [Jarl Udalryk](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Udalryk "Udalryk") from a supposed [curse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Curse "Curse"). 

_If[Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") helped her:_ thanks to Geralt's assistance, her endeavour was successful and the [hym](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hym "Hym") possessing Udalryk was eventually expelled, ending his afflictions. 

Later on, she was present at the [feast](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King%27s_Gambit "King's Gambit") at [Kaer Trolde](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde "Kaer Trolde"), which was soon turned to a massacre by [vildkaarls](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vildkaarl "Vildkaarl"). 

_If Geralt decides to help her investigate:_ Cerys adopted a methodical approach and ultimately succeeded in uncovering the culprit: [Birna Bran](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Birna_Bran "Birna Bran"). For her actions, she was subsequently chosen to be the next [Queen of the Skellige Isles](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Queen_of_the_Skellige_Isles "Queen of the Skellige Isles"). Under her rule, Skellige prospered and became a far more peaceful place. Later, as allies, Geralt approached her to ask for assistance in the upcoming battle against the [Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wild_Hunt "Wild Hunt") at [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen"); while she did not go herself, she sent Hjalmar to help. 

## Journal entry

    _While in Skellige Geralt finally had the chance to meet Cerys an Craite, the younger of Crach's two children. Known as Sparrowhawk to her friends, she was as fierce and swift as this name would indicate._
    _Cerys was an islander through and through and the spitting image of her father, having inherited all his courage, resolve and stubbornness. Dauntlessly she sought to prove at every turn that there was no task she could not fulfill as well as - or better than - any man in the islands, including her older brother Hjalmar._
    _Though Cerys' decision to stake a claim to Skellige's throne came as a shock to everyone else, she had thought it over quite thoroughly beforehand. Striving as always to outdo her brother, she intended to perform a great deed that would earn her widespread recognition and respect._
    _That is why she had sailed to Spikeroog to free Jarl Udalryk from the strange affliction that seemed to curse him._
    **If Geralt helps Cerys in Possession:**

    _Cerys did what she set out to do - with the[witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher")'s help she freed [clan Brokvar](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Clan_Brokvar "Clan Brokvar")'s leader from the wraith that had tormented him for years._
    **If Geralt sides with Cerys in King's Gambit:**

    _Once again Sparrowhawk gave proof of her cunning, this time by unmasking Birna's plot and cleansing the shame from her clan's name after the infamous "bloody banquet" at Kaer Trolde._
    _All these feats convinced the Skelligers that Cerys was fit to be queen. Though it was clear it would take some time before the young ruler could step entirely out of her father and brother's shadow, the greater part of the islanders were pleased and proud with their choice of ruler._
    **If Geralt doesn't side with either Cerys or Hjalmar in King's Gambit:**

    _Sadly Crach's daughter died alongside her brother during a failed attack on the Nilfgaardian fleet. The inhabitants of Skellige long mourned the brave Sparrowhawk's premature death._

## Notes

  * In the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion, there is a gwent card that [bears her image](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cerys_\(gwent_card\) "Cerys \(gwent card\)") and name as part of the [Skellige deck](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige_deck "Skellige deck").
  * In the original version of the plot, Cerys' true identity was [False Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cirilla_Fiona_\(imposter\) "Cirilla Fiona \(imposter\)"), which she tried to keep a secret.[1]
  * In the Polish (original) version her nickname is _"Przepióreczka"_ which is an affectionate form of _"Przepiórka"_ which means "Quail" in English.
  * Cerys is a Welsh name, meaning love or loved one. However, in welsh, the name is pronounced with a hard C. Instead of being pronounced, ser-is, as it is in the game.

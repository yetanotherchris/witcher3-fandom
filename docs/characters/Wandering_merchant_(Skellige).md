## Appearance(s)

### Games

_[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

Similarly to on the [continent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Continent "Continent"), there are some merchants who will walk the [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige") roads, covering sparsely populated areas. They are effectively clones of the roadside camp [merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(roadside_camp\) "Merchant \(roadside camp\)") south of [Kaer Trolde Harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor"), buying and selling the same things, but their appearance is dynamic. 

They only walk around [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"), as the other isles are much smaller and hardly have roads between remaining settlements to cover. While there are a couple of wandering merchants who are always available, others need their trade routes to be cleared of danger before they will consider the road safe. 

## Road near Kaer Trolde

This merchant walks from the northern side of the wooden bridge above [Kaer Trolde Harbor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Trolde_Harbor "Kaer Trolde Harbor") (eastern side), going south until the second crossroads, where he turns northeast and continues following the road until it reaches the harbor, but this time under the bridge, where the [notice board](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Notice_board_\(Kaer_Trolde_Harbor\) "Notice board \(Kaer Trolde Harbor\)") is. He is supposed to turn around at either end of that almost full circle, but tends to stay in place instead. At night, he rests by a fire at either of the two points. 

There are no prerequisites before he appears, but he does panic near the pirates in the woods near the crossroads, and due to a bug not accounting for elevation levels, also by the [wraiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wraith "Wraith") from [Peace Disturbed](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Peace_Disturbed "Peace Disturbed"), as a couple of them are located directly underneath the eastern tip of the route (there are some prisoners of war there). 

## Between Rannvaig and Rogne

This merchant appears after the [sawmill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sawmill_\(an_Craite\) "Sawmill \(an Craite\)") northeast of [Rannvaig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rannvaig "Rannvaig") is liberated, as it is on the road between the two settlements. At night, he will rest by a fire in either village. 

## Between Rannvaig and Kaer Nyssen

Before this merchant appears, there are three prerequisites before the trade route is considered safe, even though none of the enemies are significantly close to the road: 

  * Clearing the [bandit camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bandit_camp "Bandit camp") directly east of Rannvaig
  * Completing the small quest [Armed Assault](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Armed_Assault "Armed Assault"), taking place at a hut closer to [Kaer Nyssen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Nyssen "Kaer Nyssen")
  * The first encounter with [Morvudd](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morvudd "Morvudd"), until the [fiend](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fiend "Fiend") blinds [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") and runs away

He will then start walking the road between the small crossroads east of Rannvaig and to a point just before reaching the ruined fortress. There are small cage-like crates on both ends, which the merchant uses as a stool to rest on during the night. 

## Between Sund and Arinbjorn

Somewhat oddly, instead of one merchant walking the entire road, there are two appearing after the [sawmill](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sawmill_\(Drummond\) "Sawmill \(Drummond\)") south of [Sund](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sund "Sund") is liberated. One walks between Sund and the sawmill, while the other between the sawmill and [Arinbjorn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Arinbjorn "Arinbjorn"), each taking night rest by a fire at whichever destination was closer to them. Both also tend to stop and stay in place at either destination even while they aren't resting, though they are supposed to turn around. 

They share a single trader's icon on the map (i.e. only one of them will have it), but can be simultaneously seen in relative proximity. Interestingly, the Arinbjorn one isn't dependent on [Never Trust Children](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Never_Trust_Children "Never Trust Children") or [Call of the Wild](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Call_of_the_Wild "Call of the Wild"), even though the white [wolves](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_\(creature\) "Wolf \(creature\)") can easily kill him. 

## Between Arinbjorn and Holmstein

Before this trade route is considered safe, Geralt has to clear the [bandit camp](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bandit_camp "Bandit camp") north-northwest of [Holmstein's Port](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Holmstein%27s_Port "Holmstein's Port"). After that, a merchant will service the entire almost the entire road between the bandit camp and Arinbjorn, as long as it is, turning around just short of reaching either location. He has 2 small stools placed on sides of the roads where he sits to rest at night - one west of the crypts near Arinbjorn and one almost by the bandit camp. 

## Between Blandare and Palisade

This merchant is bugged and can virtually never be seen actually walking the road, at most only seen take a few steps before he sits down, as he is permanently in rest mode. He will however appear sitting on roadside stools on either end of his route - at the crossroads south of [Blandare](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blandare "Blandare"), and then on the other side of [Kvalheim Isle](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kvalheim_Isle "Kvalheim Isle"), by a large tree just before the next crossroads south.

## Biography

When Tomira was 18 years old, she was taken in by Mother [Nenneke](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nenneke "Nenneke") to become a healer. However, there was a young man named Goslav that all the novices, including Tomira, couldn't keep their eyes off of at work and Tomira ended up leaving the [temple](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Temple_of_Melitele_\(Ellander\) "Temple of Melitele \(Ellander\)") for him. The two spent a summer together but then Goslav left and when Tomira tried to return to the temple, Nenneke refused to take her back. Her own parents refused to say a word to her as well and simply gave her a travel cloak and a small coin pouch. 

Tomira traveled for a long time, trying to find a place that was both safe and where she felt she was needed and finally ended up in White Orchard. At one point she befriended another villager in the area, [Claer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Claer "Claer"), and the two became close friends until Claer's untimely death. 

In 1272, during the [Nilfgaardian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") invasion, White Orchard had its own problems in the form of a [griffin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Griffin_\(creature\) "Griffin \(creature\)"), which was attacking people on the roads. One such woman, [Lena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lena "Lena"), was gravely injured and brought to Tomira's [hut](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Tomira%27s_hut "Tomira's hut") to try and heal her. Unfortunately, there wasn't anything else Tomira could do for Lena other than try to make her as comfortable as possible until she succumbed to her wounds. 

_If Geralt helps Lena:_ she thanked the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") "for giving a damn" and gave him a small gift in return. Later, if the witcher returned and asked about Lena, she explained that Nilfgaardian soldiers showed up and took her but doesn't know where to or why. 

_If Nilfgaard or Dijkstra wins the war:_ Tomira lived out her days in White Orchard as she supplied herbs to the local peasants under Temeria's new soverign rule under the Nilfgaardian Empire or Dijkstra. 

_If Redania wins the war:_ [Radovid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Radovid_V "Radovid V") hunted down all the [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage"), herbalists, and [alchemists](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alchemist "Alchemist") and killed them. When [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") came back to White Orchard, he found Tomira's hut ablaze and her tied to a stake, prepared to be burned by a group of [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter"). 

    _If Geralt intervenes:_ Geralt saved Tomira, and she traveled far away, to escape Radovid and his wild groups of witch hunters.
    _If Geralt doesn't intervene:_ Geralt chose to not step in, leaving Tomira to be burned at the stake.

## Notes

  * If you speak to her after completing the quest, Contract: Devil by the Well, and ask her about Claer, she'll reward you with some [saltpeter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Saltpeter "Saltpeter") as well as other [alchemical ingredients](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients").
  * If Geralt already possesses some buckthorn, he will not be asked to visit Tomira in order to advance the quest, The Beast of White Orchard.
  * In the [leaked materials](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_cut_content "The Witcher 3 cut content"), originally Claer and Tomira were supposed to be twin sisters but this was later re-written.
  * If you never visit Tomira at first and come back after Lena dies, you can ask what happened to Lena and Geralt will mention the choice to not give her [swallow](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Swallow "Swallow"), even though he never made it
  * There is a notice in the village (pinned to a tree between the tavern and forge) put up by Tomira, saying that she will pay top coin for [honeycombs](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Honeycomb "Honeycomb"), and she does indeed buy them at a higher price.

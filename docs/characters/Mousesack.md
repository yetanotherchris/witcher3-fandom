**Mousesack**[1] (Polish: _Myszowór_), also known as **Ermion** , was a skilled and intelligent [druid](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Druid "Druid"), and also a good friend and advisor to [King](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/King "King") [Bran Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bran_Tuirseach "Bran Tuirseach") of [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige"), as well as [Queen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Queen "Queen") [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe "Calanthe") of [Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Cintra "Cintra"). He had a thickset and robust appearance and wore a bushy beard.

**Who were Mousesack's parents in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher series, Mousesack's parents were an unidentified man from Skellige and a witch named Manissa. Manissa served Fregenal and was killed in self-defense by Korin, Geralt's future father. Mousesack's father departed from Manissa during Mousesack's early childhood.

**How is Mousesack connected to the character Geralt?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Mousesack, also known as Ermion, is a skilled druid with a robust friendship with Geralt. He assisted Geralt during a Witcher Tournament massacre. As an advisor to King Bran Tuirseach of Skellige and Queen Calanthe of Cintra, his ties also extend to Ciri, with him participating in the search for her in Brokilon. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the significance of Mousesack's alternate name, Ermion?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Mousesack, alternatively Ermion, is a proficient druid and counselor to Skellige's King Bran Tuirseach and Cintra's Queen Calanthe. The name Ermion was devised for non-Polish translations of the game. In the game, Mousesack adopts the name Ermion, serving as the hierophant of Ard Skellig's druid circle. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What was Mousesack's relationship with Queen Calanthe of Cintra?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

Mousesack served as the court druid of Cintra, maintaining a close relationship with Queen Calanthe. After Eist Tuirseach and Calanthe's marriage, Mousesack remained in Cintra, aiding the injured and assisting Pavetta in managing her power. He was frequently seen with Pavetta's daughter, Cirilla. In 1263, he revealed to Geralt that Calanthe had called off Ciri's engagement to Prince Kistrin of Verden. During an attack on Cintra, Mousesack conjured a magical barrier to safeguard the queen and the city.

## Biography

### Early life

* * *

The following is considered comics canon only and may contradict [Andrzej Sapkowski](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Andrzej_Sapkowski "Andrzej Sapkowski")'s works.

Mousesack was the son of an unknown man from Skellige[2] and [Manissa](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Manissa "Manissa"), a [witch](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch "Witch") in the service of [Fregenal](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Fregenal "Fregenal"), who was killed in self-defence by [Korin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Korin "Korin"), the future father of [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"). Mousesack's father left Manissa when he was a small child. Not knowing that the warrior killed his mother, the young Mousesack befriended Korin and the druid [Visenna](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Visenna "Visenna"), who took him to the druid circle of [Mayena](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mayena "Mayena").[3]

Even though the Mayena Circle was destroyed due to the plots of [mages](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mage "Mage") and kings, Mousesack and two older druids were invited by the king to a [Witcher Tournament](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher_Tournament "Witcher Tournament") which was to take place in a special arena. When the tournament turned into a massacre of witchers of the [Wolf School](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Wolf_School "Wolf School"), Mousesack helped Geralt, his childhood friend, escape.[4]

### Mania of Mad Etibald

Mousesack was opposed to the arguments of [Eltibald](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eltibald "Eltibald") and [Stregobor](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Stregobor "Stregobor") during a council of wizards in which he took part when they argued to consider the children born in the year of the [Black Sun](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Black_Sun "Black Sun") monsters.[5]

### Princess Pavetta's coming of age

Mousesack was present at the feast organized by Calanthe to celebrate [Pavetta](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Pavetta "Pavetta")'s coming of age, accompanying [Eist Tuirseach](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Eist_Tuirseach "Eist Tuirseach") and [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite") as part of the delegation from Skellige. He communicated secretly with Geralt by manipulating breadcrumbs on their plates to form runes. Both men felt the impending trouble of that evening — [Duny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Duny "Duny")'s return and Pavetta's power being unleashed — though neither knew the exact details beforehand. 

When everyone at the interrupted feast finally arrived at accepting Duny's claim — thanks to Geralt's and Mousesack's intervention in the heated argument — and letting Pavetta decide whether she would fulfill it, Mousesack warned Geralt of the grave danger he felt mounting in the room through breadcrumbs again. When Pavetta's power was uncontrollably unleashed by seeing Duny being injured by [Rainfarn](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rainfarn "Rainfarn"), Mousesack and Geralt used their [magical](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Magic "Magic") powers to try and put a stop to the rapidly escalating destruction. 

When everything had settled again, in the early morning, Geralt and Mousesack did not draw attention to the fact that, despite the sun having risen, Duny had not yet transformed back, instead waiting to see when he'd notice it himself and amicably laughing when he did.

### Searching for Ciri in Brokilon

In [1263](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Timeline#1263 "Timeline"), Mousesack was searching Brokilon forest for Ciri, when he came across her and Geralt, who were being attacked by mercenaries; he helped them defeat the men. 

Later, while the trio were resting under a tree, Mousesack informed Geralt that Calanthe had cancelled Ciri's engagement to Prince [Kistrin](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kistrin "Kistrin") of [Verden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Verden "Verden"), and upon their return to Cintra with Ciri, he would also encourage her to pick her own partner. Mousesack urged Geralt to come with them to Cintra and take Ciri under his guidance, as the druid believed their fates were intertwined by the [Law of Surprise](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Law_of_Surprise "Law of Surprise"). Geralt, who did not believe in [destiny](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Destiny "Destiny") and thought that one is only destined to die, declined the offer. 

Mousesack reminisced the old prophecy, "the Sword of Destiny has two edges". He explained that like a two-edged sword that can either serve its bearer or harm them, the destiny of Ciri and Geralt could be pure or sinister. He further explained that the bond between them could be pure, but only if Geralt connects with her as a guardian or it could manifest itself differently in a negative or destructive way if he abandoned her. 

Ignoring the persuasions of Mousesack, Geralt got on [his horse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Roach "Roach") and rode away into the dark cold forest, still believing the only destiny he knew: the destiny of death.

### Further fate

[![Gwent cardart skellige ermion](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c7/Gwent_cardart_skellige_ermion.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/174?cb=20191101120724)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c7/Gwent_cardart_skellige_ermion.png/revision/latest?cb=20191101120724) [![Gwent cardart skellige ermion](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c7/Gwent_cardart_skellige_ermion.png/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/174?cb=20191101120724)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/c/c7/Gwent_cardart_skellige_ermion.png/revision/latest?cb=20191101120724) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Gwent_cardart_skellige_ermion.png)

Mousesack eventually survived the [Slaughter of Cintra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Slaughter_of_Cintra "Slaughter of Cintra") and fled. In 1272, he and Geralt met again; this time at [Ard Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ard_Skellig "Ard Skellig"), where Mousesack, known here as Ermion, managed to become the hierophant of the local circle of druids. It turned out that he did not want to let [Yennefer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yennefer_of_Vengerberg "Yennefer of Vengerberg") go to the destroyed part of the forest, which she wanted to explore. He eventually allowed it only after consent from [Crach an Craite](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Crach_an_Craite "Crach an Craite"), even though he was strongly upset by the sorceress because she, with the help of Geralt, stole the Mask of Ouroboros from his laboratory. His anger reached its zenith when Yen used the mask. This rendered the mask useless and also created a huge storm. He calmed down a little, though, when he learned that it was necessary to find [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), but he was still very angry.[6]

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

### Journal entry

    _Most druids on Skellige differ from the deeply-rooted Continental stereotype of a graybearded old man in white robes bowing before sacred oaks, taming wyverns and pestering local lords with petitions to add yet another species to the list of protected beasts. Ermion, Geralt's old acquaintance, was no exception in this regard._
    _This stiff-bearded, spindly man was the leader of the Druids' Circle in Skellige. He was also Jarl Crach an Craite's advisor on matters of magic and the mystical, as well as on any and all issues requiring more refinement than a well-timed uppercut or a hard-swung axe._
    _Ermion was known to be obstinate and to have a tendency, quite rare in Skellige, to consider all possible consequences of a particular course of action before it was undertaken. All this meant that conversing with him demanded a considerable amount of time and patience._
    _Given all this, one can hardly be surprised that Yennefer and Geralt's "rash" and "highly irresponsible" actions regarding the magic anomaly – stealing a priceless artifact from a private chamber being first among them – put Ermion quite out of sorts._
    _However, despite his difficult character and his somewhat testy relationship with Geralt, Ermion agreed at once to travel to Kaer Morhen and help defend Ciri from the Wild Hunt's forces._
    _When persuasion and reason failed, Ermion would, as a last resort, turn to violence - and then he became a foe to be reckoned with._
    _Madman Lugos found this out personally when, in a fit of rage, he attacked Ermion and Geralt. As they had in the past, witcher and druid stood side by side and fought a common enemy._

  * In _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ , [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") can play [gwent](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Gwent "Gwent") with him and win the unique "[Leshen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leshen_\(gwent_card\) "Leshen \(gwent card\)")" gwent card, and at a later stage of the game, Ermion can be recruited to help with the defense of [Kaer Morhen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Kaer_Morhen "Kaer Morhen").   * In _[The Hexer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Hexer "The Hexer")_ TV series, Mousesack appears in 2 episodes: [Calanthe](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Calanthe_\(episode\) "Calanthe \(episode\)") (episode 6) and [Rozdroże](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Rozdro%C5%BCe "Rozdroże") (episode 8).   * He appears in the non-canon short story _[Something Ends, Something Begins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Co%C5%9B_si%C4%99_ko%C5%84czy,_co%C5%9B_si%C4%99_zaczyna "Coś się kończy, coś się zaczyna")_.
  * The word "[myszowór](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/pl:myszow%C3%B3r "wikipedia:pl:myszowór")" is actually the Polish name of [phascogale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/phascogale "wikipedia:phascogale") or wambenger, an Australian mouse-like marsupial; the "wór" (sack) part refers to the animal's pouch.

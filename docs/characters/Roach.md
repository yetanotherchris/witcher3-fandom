**Roach** (Polish: _Płotka_) is the name that [Geralt of Rivia](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia"), the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher"), gives to all of his [horses](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse "Horse"). His preference is for mares.[1]

### Quick Answers

![fandom logo](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/8941553e-df6a-4310-a080-b9da56fdcbdd)

**What is the significance of the name 'Roach' in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The name 'Roach' in the Witcher series is used by Geralt of Rivia for all his horses, particularly mares. The rationale behind this choice remains unclear, as Geralt often sidesteps the question. It's suggested that 'Roach' could have been a spontaneous choice. Nonetheless, the horses appear to readily accept this name. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Does the name 'Roach' have any connection to the character Vernon Roche in the Witcher?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher series, 'Roach' is not directly connected to Vernon Roche. 'Roach' is a term for a European freshwater fish and is the moniker Geralt uses for his horses. The resemblance between 'Roach' and 'Roche' is incidental, with no evidence of a relationship. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**What is the origin of the term 'roach' as it is used in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

The term 'Roach' in the Witcher series originates from the French word for 'rock'. In Polish, the horse is called 'Płotka', a smaller version of 'Płoć' which translates to 'roach'. This term is feminine, reflecting Geralt's preference for female horses. It's unrelated to the insect cockroach, the French name 'Roche', or the character Vernon Roche. 

Provided by: Fandom 

**Does Roach, the horse, die in the Witcher series?** ![toggle section](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/36002e46-06fe-4633-9d99-27d96cf5bcdc)

In the Witcher series, Roach does indeed meet her end. She sustains severe injuries in a battle with a chernobog, leading Geralt to euthanize her. Later, Geralt acquires a new horse from Yarpen Zigrin, which he continues the tradition of naming Roach. 

Provided by: Fandom 

## Name

The name refers to the [roach (Rutilus rutilus)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_roach "wikipedia:Common roach"), a common European fresh water fish. It has nothing to do with the cockroach, and nothing directly to do with the either the French given name "Roche" or the character [Vernon Roche](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Vernon_Roche "Vernon Roche"). Though the English term for the fish ("roach") does in fact share its origin with "Roche": both terms come from the French word for "rock". 

It's worth noting that the original name for the horse in Polish is "Płotka", a diminutive form of the word "Płoć" (meaning "roach"). Diminutives are endearing in Polish, so a more direct translation would have been something along the lines of "Roachy" or "Roachie". 

Moreover, the Polish word is female gendered which corresponds with Geralt's preference for mares - it would be odd for a stallion to be called "Płotka". The tender tone as well as the gender of the name is lost in the translation. 

However, "Płotka" has another meaning which is "small fry". In the Polish version of the third game, this is brought up during [Broken Flowers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Broken_Flowers "Broken Flowers") if Geralt beats [Morvran Voorhis](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Morvran_Voorhis "Morvran Voorhis") in a race, who will praise him by saying _"Impressive rider skills from someone for whom every horse is a small fry."_ In this meaning the name becomes gender neutral, thus can be used for a stallion as well. 

## _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_

[![Horse combat](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a9/Tw3_roach_combat_promo.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20170604225750)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a9/Tw3_roach_combat_promo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170604225750) [![Horse combat](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a9/Tw3_roach_combat_promo.jpg/revision/latest/scale-to-width-down/220?cb=20170604225750)](https://static.wikia.nocookie.net/witcher/images/a/a9/Tw3_roach_combat_promo.jpg/revision/latest?cb=20170604225750) [](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/File:Tw3_roach_combat_promo.jpg)

Horse combat

The horse Geralt owned throughout the game is named Roach. There is a number of [equipment](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Horse_equipment "Horse equipment") available for Roach: saddles, saddlebags, and horse blinders. Saddles let Roach gallop full speed longer, saddlebags increases Geralt's [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory") limit, and the blinders decreases the rate that Roach's fear level rises. 

In a contrast to the previous games, trophies gained from contracted monsters appear tied to Roach's saddle instead of on Geralt's person. If Geralt visits [Emhyr](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Emhyr_var_Emreis "Emhyr var Emreis") after he finds [Ciri](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ciri "Ciri"), and does not accept the coin offered, when he leaves he is granted a black Pure Bred [Nilfgaardian](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nilfgaardian_Empire "Nilfgaardian Empire") thoroughbred stallion. After this event, whenever Roach is called, a black horse appears instead of the original bay mare.

### Journal entry

    _A horse is more to a witcher than merely a means of locomotion - just ask any bandit who has taken a well-aimed hoof to the head during combat. Additionally, many a witcher has talked over the nuts and bolts of his current contract with his horse while staring at the stars shining above the lonely road, though few would ever admit to this._
    _Geralt named his every mount Roach, though no one really knows why or what Geralt had in mind with this name. When asked, Geralt would dodge the question or give an evasive answer. Perhaps this had just been the first word that came to his head? Roach, for her part, seemed to accept the name with no reservations._
    _Geralt would grow annoyed and curse whenever Roach panicked and tossed him in the middle of a battle, as well as when she would suddenly turn a different direction that he wanted while he was riding at full gallop towards some urgent destination. In truth, though, he was very attached to her and would never trade her for any other horse. Not even one which, when summoned, would never stand helpless in front of a seemingly easily surmountable obstacle, such as a low fence or stray piece of timber. Nor even one which would sometimes, in some incomprehensible fashion, wind up dancing on some peasant's roof. "Well," Geralt would say with a shrug. "A witcher's horse isn't a normal animal. Constant contact with magic beverages and Signs must have left a mark."_
    _While completing a contract in[Toussaint](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Toussaint "Toussaint"), Geralt had the chance to find out exactly how serious Roach took her role and how well-versed in the arcana of the witcher's trade she was. He also discovered she was an entirely pleasant conversation partner._

### Bugs

  * During the quest [Beyond Hill and Dale...](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Beyond_Hill_and_Dale... "Beyond Hill and Dale..."), Geralt has the option to ride a white unicorn. It is possible to save the game and create a [New Game +](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/New_Game_%2B "New Game +") over the save once you discover the unicorn, which will change your mount from Roach to the white unicorn and is your new permanent mount throughout the base game and expansions.
  * If you enter a location that has galloping forbidden (e.g. a city) while riding Roach, then fast-travel somewhere and call her, you still wouldn't be able to gallop, regardless of where you are. Loading a save or leaving a city with her fixes the issue.

## Trivia

  * [Roach (Rutilus rutilus)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_roach "wikipedia:Common roach") is a species of small, fresh water fish, common in many of Poland's lakes and rivers. It's perfectly edible but many people dislike it due to its relatively small size and quite a lot of fish bones.

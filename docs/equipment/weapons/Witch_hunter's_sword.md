# Witch hunter's sword

**Witch hunter's sword** is a [steel sword](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_steel_swords "The Witcher 3 steel swords") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and comes in the following forms: 

  * Witch hunter's sword (crafted)
  * Witch hunter's sword (basic)

## Item ID

Inquisitor sword 1

Dropped by [witch hunters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter") and the [Prison Warden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Prison_Warden "Prison Warden") during the [main quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_main_quests "The Witcher 3 main quests"), [The Great Escape](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Great_Escape "The Great Escape"). It is also available as random loot starting at level 27 and can be purchased from the following [merchants](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_merchants "The Witcher 3 merchants"): 

  * All [blacksmiths](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blacksmith "Blacksmith") in [Skellige](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Skellige "Skellige")
  * [Merchant](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Merchant_\(Isolated_Hut\) "Merchant \(Isolated Hut\)") at the [Isolated Hut](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Isolated_Hut_\(Hindarsfjall\) "Isolated Hut \(Hindarsfjall\)") on [Hindarsfjall](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Hindarsfjall "Hindarsfjall")

With no level restrictions, one is guaranteed to appear along with [Diagram: Witch Slayer](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Diagram:_Witch_Slayer "Diagram: Witch Slayer") on a [witch hunter](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witch_hunter "Witch hunter")'s corpse by a [drowner](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Drowner "Drowner") [nest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster_nest "Monster nest") on the swampy coastline far west of [the Orphans of Crookback Bog](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Orphans_of_Crookback_Bog "The Orphans of Crookback Bog"), further southwest from [Leslav](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Leslav "Leslav").

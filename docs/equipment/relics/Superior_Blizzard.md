### Effect(s)

Four of these can be crafted at any one time and placed in the consumables slot.

Although the descripton states that 3 Adrenaline Points are needed in order to perform stamina depleting actions without consuming it during the slowdown time, only 1 full adrenalie bar **and** a full stamina bar is necessary. This has been tested prior to [Patch 1.62](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.62_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.62 \(The Witcher 3\)"). Also, the period of slowdown time is 3 seconds in the Superior version of the potion, and time slows down **only** for enemies, while [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") remains in his normal speed of actions. In short, it slows down enemy actions and not time. 

## Tips

  * Combining superior Blizzard with a 42% slowdown [Yrden](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Yrden "Yrden") makes for a 102% slow status effect on enemies.
  * Frenzy from the [Alchemy](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_abilities/Alchemy "The Witcher 3 abilities/Alchemy") skill tree slows down time for Geralt the same as it does for enemies, so it might not synergize well with this potion.
  * The freezing slowdown from [Zoria](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Zoria_runestone "Zoria runestone") [runestones](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Runestone "Runestone") on swords, [Northern Wind](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Northern_Wind "Northern Wind") bombs, and [Piercing Cold](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Piercing_Cold "Piercing Cold") [mutation](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mutations_\(Blood_and_Wine\) "Mutations \(Blood and Wine\)") (when then don't fully freeze) work well with Blizzard, but note that they remove Yrden effects in order to be applied.

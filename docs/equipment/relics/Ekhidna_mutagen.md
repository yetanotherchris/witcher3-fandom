## Item ID

Lamia mutagen

**Ekhidna mutagen** is an [alchemy ingredient](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that is used to craft [ekhidna decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ekhidna_decoction "Ekhidna decoction"). Like all [special mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens"), they can only be dropped by their corresponding [creature](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Creature "Creature"). They act like [lesser blue mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lesser_blue_mutagen "Lesser blue mutagen") when placed in the diamond-shaped slots in the character panel. 

[Melusine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Melusine "Melusine") will always drop this mutagen during [Contract: Here Comes the Groom](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Contract:_Here_Comes_the_Groom "Contract: Here Comes the Groom"). It is hard to obtain without facing her, as [ekhidnae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Ekhidna "Ekhidna") prefer water over land, and [monsters](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Monster "Monster") killed in water rarely drop anything. However, there are a few [locations](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_locations "The Witcher 3 locations") where it's possible to farm ekhidnae like in the western parts of [An Skellig](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/An_Skellig "An Skellig"), near the secondary quest [The Tower Outta Nowheres](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Tower_Outta_Nowheres "The Tower Outta Nowheres"). 

## Notes

  * In an undocumented change for [Patch 1.08](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.08_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.08 \(The Witcher 3\)"), all [mutagen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Mutagen "Mutagen") icons were changed. However, there was still a bug for those on Steam and Origin that didn't reflect this and was later fixed in [Patch 1.22](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.22_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.22 \(The Witcher 3\)"). Despite this, some may still see the original icons instead, even with a fully patched game.

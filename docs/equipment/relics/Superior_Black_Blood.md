## Item ID

Black Blood 3

**Superior Black Blood** is the most advanced form of the [potion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_. Five of these can be crafted at any one time and placed in the consumables slot. 

It returns 10% more damage compared to the [enhanced](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Enhanced_Black_Blood "Enhanced Black Blood") version, and adds [bleeding](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bleeding "Bleeding") that only requires proximity to trigger. The bleeding effect is very powerful, even capable of finishing off strong monsters such as [quest](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine_quests "Blood and Wine quests") boss [bruxae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bruxa "Bruxa") in the _[Blood and Wine](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Blood_and_Wine "Blood and Wine")_ expansion.

## Tips

  * Like most "punishment" effects, the damage returned depends on the damage [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") receives. Which means that when running a tank build the effect will be minute or if the damage is negated by using [Quen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Quen "Quen"), the returned damage will also be 0. 
    * However, the bleeding effect will always work the same unless the enemy is immune.
  * It is most effective against [bruxae](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Bruxa "Bruxa") and [alps](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Alp "Alp"), as they may jump on Geralt and drink his blood, in which case they will receive considerable damage. 
    * Geralt will comment that he should have drank Black Blood if such a thing occurs when he is not under the effects of the potion.

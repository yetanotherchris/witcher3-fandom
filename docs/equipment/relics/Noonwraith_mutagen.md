## Item ID

Noonwraith mutagen

**Noonwraith mutagen** is an [alchemy ingredient](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and is used to craft [noonwraith decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith_decoction "Noonwraith decoction"). They act like [lesser green mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lesser_green_mutagen "Lesser green mutagen") when placed in the diamond-shaped slots in the character panel. 

Like all [special mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens") they can only be dropped by their corresponding monster. This one however, is the easiest and earliest that can be obtained in the entire game, through the [Contract: Devil by the Well](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Contract:_Devil_by_the_Well "Contract: Devil by the Well") quest in [White Orchard](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/White_Orchard "White Orchard"). If for some reason you wish to skip that, there's also the [The White Lady](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_White_Lady "The White Lady") outside [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"), and any regular [noonwraith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Noonwraith "Noonwraith") is guaranteed to drop it as well. You can even just wait for daytime if a [nightwraith](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Nightwraith "Nightwraith") is encountered somewhere, and it will be swapped out. 

## Notes

  * In an undocumented change for [Patch 1.08](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.08_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.08 \(The Witcher 3\)"), all mutagen icons were changed. However, there was still a bug for those on Steam and Origin that didn't reflect this and was later fixed in [Patch 1.22](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.22_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.22 \(The Witcher 3\)"). Despite this, some may still see the original icons instead, even with a fully patched game.
  * This mutagen can technically also be dropped by [plague maidens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Plague_maiden "Plague maiden"), if any such could be killed. However, the Pesta during [A Towerful of Mice](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/A_Towerful_of_Mice "A Towerful of Mice") is the only such monster in the game and she is scripted to escape or be freed, never slain.

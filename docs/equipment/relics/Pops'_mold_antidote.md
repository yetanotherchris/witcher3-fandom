## Item ID

Pops Antidote

**Pops' mold antidote** is a [potion](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_potions "The Witcher 3 potions") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ that grants immunity to the pops' mold in the [sewers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad_sewers "Novigrad sewers") beneath the [bathhouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund%27s_Bathhouse "Sigismund's Bathhouse"). 

It is given to [Geralt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Geralt_of_Rivia "Geralt of Rivia") ready-made, along with the [formula](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Manuscript_page:_Pops%27_mold_antidote "Manuscript page: Pops' mold antidote"), while the [witcher](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") investigates the theft of [Dijkstra](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Sigismund_Dijkstra "Sigismund Dijkstra")'s treasure, and it can be replenished at any time afterwards.

## Notes

  * Prior to [Patch 4.0](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_4.0_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 4.0 \(The Witcher 3\)"), whenever a new stack of the potion is added to the [inventory](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_inventory "The Witcher 3 inventory"), its toxicity was assigned a random value in the range of 25-30. Therefore, dropping the potion and re-crafting it could cause its toxicity to change.
  * The antidote works for non-[witchers](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Witcher "Witcher") as well, but it's so distasteful it makes [humans](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Human "Human") prone to vomiting.

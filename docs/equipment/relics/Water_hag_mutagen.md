## Item ID

Water Hag mutagen

**Water hag mutagen** is an [alchemy ingredient](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and is used to craft [water hag decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water_hag_decoction "Water hag decoction"). They act like [lesser red mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lesser_red_mutagen "Lesser red mutagen") when placed in the diamond-shaped slots in the character panel. 

Like all [special mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens") they can only be dropped by a corresponding monster. However, despite the name, regular [water hags](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Water_hag "Water hag") can't drop this mutagen, and it is only dropped by their stronger variation, bilge hags. [Abaya](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Abaya "Abaya") during [Contract: Muire D'yaeblen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Contract:_Muire_D%27yaeblen "Contract: Muire D'yaeblen") is the one guaranteed source, while regular bilge hags are otherwise only seen around a few [guarded treasures](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Guarded_treasure "Guarded treasure"), the treasure hunt quest [Battlefield Loot](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Battlefield_Loot "Battlefield Loot"), and one during [Carnal Sins](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Carnal_Sins "Carnal Sins"). 

## Notes

  * In an undocumented change for [Patch 1.08](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.08_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.08 \(The Witcher 3\)"), all mutagen icons were changed. However, there was still a bug for those on Steam and Origin that didn't reflect this and was later fixed in [Patch 1.22](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.22_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.22 \(The Witcher 3\)"). Despite this, some may still see the original icons instead, even with a fully patched game.

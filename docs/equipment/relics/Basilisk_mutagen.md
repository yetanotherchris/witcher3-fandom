## Item ID

Basilisk mutagen

**Basilisk mutagen** is an [alchemy ingredient](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_alchemy_ingredients "The Witcher 3 alchemy ingredients") in _[The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3:_Wild_Hunt "The Witcher 3: Wild Hunt")_ and is used to craft [basilisk decoction](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk_decoction "Basilisk decoction"). It acts like a [lesser blue mutagen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lesser_blue_mutagen "Lesser blue mutagen") when placed in the diamond-shaped slots in the character panel. 

Like all [special mutagens](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens") they can only be dropped by their corresponding monster. However, unlike others they are guaranteed to drop from any [basilisk](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Basilisk "Basilisk") encountered in the wild. One relatively easy to kill by making it drop in the water is on the island next to the [Flying Stag](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Flying_Stag "Flying Stag") shipwreck, southeast of the [Lighthouse](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Lighthouse "Lighthouse") outside [Novigrad](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Novigrad "Novigrad"). 

## Notes

  * In an undocumented change for [Patch 1.08](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.08_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.08 \(The Witcher 3\)"), all [mutagen](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/The_Witcher_3_mutagens "The Witcher 3 mutagens") icons were changed. However, there was still a bug for those on Steam and Origin that didn't reflect this and was later fixed in [Patch 1.22](https://witcher.fandom.com/wiki/Patch_1.22_\(The_Witcher_3\) "Patch 1.22 \(The Witcher 3\)"). Despite this, some may still see the original icons instead, even with a fully patched game.
